## CHAPTER 10 — LA LLEGADA (1 PAGE)

**GOAL**: Enter the city → meet the Keepers → witness the first city-scale supernatural death

**SCENE FLOW**

1. **Entry into the City**

* Buenos Aires, 1821 — *cabildo abierto*, *gauchos* in town, post-1810 air
* Inocencia's boarding house on Calle Belgrano (Mercedes's safe house)
* Mercedes notices the "dust from the pit" being beaten from players' jackets (3b)

2. **The Streets**

* The mate seller mutters *"Dadálah"* as the carriage passes
* A man in the crowd Marked PCs cannot quite see; the eye keeps trying to find a feature

3. **The University & First Glimpse of the Vault**

* Manzana de las Luces — Jesuit construction, 1610
* Keeper meeting house: Saráchaga's *casa chorizo* in the Barrio del Alto

4. **The Keeper Meeting** — Six NPCs in one room

* **Saráchaga**: 71, white-haired, dissenter, *"Mientras esté abajo, está. El movimiento es lo que él quiere."*
* **Albarrán**: 54, Franciscan, abstains, *"Cuando me conteste, voy a saber. Esta noche todavía no sé."*
* **Galíndez**: 49, retired cavalry, operational floor (15 men, 2 routes, 1 ship)
* **Ana Bermúdez**: 26, secretary, asks one quiet question Quirce told her to ask
* **Mercedes**: argues for moving the book — *before he forces them to*
* **Lacasa**: empty chair (Mercedes will not name him)

5. **The Streets at Night**

* Galíndez walks the players home; Cabildo strikes eleven (should have been twelve)
* Cabildo clock skipped a strike (tarnished-copper runner)

6. **Dawn — Saráchaga is Dead**

* Third-patio well; body in his chair, no wound
* Iron Keeper-ring on the floor (he tore it off — it was burning him)
* Witness-stone *hot, not warm*
* Final notebook entry: *"Caliente, no tibia. Mercedes tenía razón. Movámoslo."*
* Lacasa's spurred boot prints in the patio

**KEY DIALOGUE**

* Saráchaga: *"Hasta que la piedra esté caliente, no tibia."*
* Final entry: *"Caliente, no tibia. Hay menos tiempo del que pensé."*

**ATMOSPHERIC RUNNERS**

* Vibration faint in Buenos Aires; strongest in San Ignacio cloister
* Tarnished copper: gas-lamps, the Cabildo skip
* Dust still on the players' jackets

**END**

* Body discovered. Conversion-evidence on the desk. C11 starts immediately.

**GM NOTES**

* Hand out a one-page Keeper roster + table seating diagram
* Saráchaga's death must be *discovered*, not narrated
* If players refuse the meeting: Mercedes goes alone; Saráchaga still dies; harder C11
* El Patrón is strictly off-stage in C10. Hand only.

## CHAPTER 11 — LA DECISIÓN (1 PAGE)

**GOAL**: Open the vault → confess → meet El Patrón → plan the transport

**SCENE FLOW**

1. **The Vault, Finally**

* Saráchaga's iron key opens the 1610 Jesuit vault cleanly
* Inside: a chest with a *foreign* lock — installed 1810, by Quirce, to fool the Keepers
* Mercedes notices fresh dust near the chest — she is unsettled
* Visual handout: `assets/dual-diagram.svg`

2. **Tracing Saráchaga's Last Hours**

* Kitchen → well → boot prints → Lacasa's town house empty
* Strongbox: Quirce's letter + the *E. Dadálah* deed at Areco
* **Lacasa is the traitor. He has fled.**

3. **Albarrán Returns Wet from the Well**

* *"Dios me contestó. Lo movemos."*
* The vote is over before it is taken

4. **Ana Bermúdez Confronted**

* Six months of correspondence with Quirce; ciphers; books as payment
* She did not know what she was. She does now.
* Player choice: trust her with false intel, or wall her off

5. **The Cursed Gaucho at the Tannery**

* Tribal allies have joined him
* His terms: *don't take El Patrón from me*
* Hands over the *cuchillo de campo* as collateral

6. **Mercedes, Alone — The Cloister Confession**

* In four checkpoints, with pause:
  * (1) She copied the Necronomicón at 24 in 1781
  * (2) Through Tiburcio Lacasa → Salamanca cult → El Patrón
  * (3) Don Eusebio's manuscript, Quirce's mediumship, the Coypac alignment — all downstream
  * (4) The parchment has *two pages*: destroy-ritual outer; binding section inner
* **READER-PATH (2c)**: she gives the parchment to the Reader-PC; explains the inner page is theirs to read

7. **Midnight at Inocencia's — El Patrón's Parlor Visit**

* Polite. Bows. Uses *usted*. Sits where invited.
* The deal: leave the book in the vault; players walk away with silver
* If players threaten or shoot: he smiles, bows, walks out; door closes on its own; SAN 1/1D6
* He leaves a sharpened pencil on the chair (tarnished brass ferrule)
* Inocencia falls asleep at the kitchen table; does not remember him

**KEY DIALOGUE**

* Mercedes: *"He leído partes del Necronomicón. No fui forzada. Era joven, y curiosa. Llevo cuarenta años pagándolo."*
* Mercedes (to Reader): *"Yo lo copié hace cuarenta años; vos podés."*
* El Patrón: *"Hagan eso, y al final de mañana yo los dejo vivir."*

**END**

* Plan finalized. Cordon assigned. Transport in 18 hours.

**GM NOTES**

* Run Mercedes's confession as four pauses, not a speech
* El Patrón is *polite*. The horror is that the players will want to take the deal.
* If players accept: campaign ends here. Bad ending. Honor it.
* If they refuse Mercedes's parchment: Destroy ending unavailable; Hide is default

## CHAPTER 12 — EL LIBRO (1–2 PAGES)

**GOAL**: Run the transport → reveal → choice → aftermath

**STRUCTURE: 4 ACTS**

| Act | What | Real-time |
|---|---|---|
| 1 — Transport | Carriage rolls; cordon walks; first attack | 90 min |
| 2 — Reveal | Cursed Gaucho's stand; dock; El Patrón on stage | 90 min |
| 3 — Choice | Hide / Use / Destroyed; player decision | 90 min |
| 4 — Aftermath | Survivors; the Land; the Final Image; annual dream | 60 min |

**ACT 1 — THE TRANSPORT**

* Three Weak Points: University Exit / Belgrano-and-Defensa / Riachuelo Dock
* Each is a *test of whether the cordon holds*. Run separately, not as one combat.
* Visual handout: `assets/city-grid.svg` — mark each completed leg as it happens
* Observable consequences (D5): street-lamps flicker ahead of the carriage, dogs go silent at corners, temperature drops at uncovered ambush points, gas-lamps dim 30% city-wide at the moment the geometry locks

**WEAK POINT 2 — THE STAND**

* The Cursed Gaucho stops the carriage at Belgrano and Defensa
* Wounds El Patrón with the *cuchillo*. Dies.
* If player-terms agreed in C11: hands the *cuchillo* off, says *"No me lo lleves al barco. Lo dejo a vos."*
* If solitary: knife found on body

**ACT 2 — THE REVEAL**

* Dock geometry: the diagram has closed around the players
* Mercedes asks: ***"¿Lo guardamos, lo usamos, o lo destruimos?"***
* Sixty seconds. No Keeper override.

**ACT 3 — THE THREE ENDINGS**

* **HIDE**: Albarrán arrives at the dock; speaks *"Hic locus est. Hic manet."*
  * Cost: whoever speaks the Latin dies within 24h. Default: Albarrán. Player request only: Mercedes.
  * Book sealed in new chest; sails north on *Carmen del Pilar*; 20 years of peace
  * El Patrón withdraws

* **USE** *(Reader-path required: C5 + C8 + C11)*
  * Hand the Reader-PC's player a physical card with: *"Hic vincio nomen tuum loco. Hic vincio locum nomini tuo. Verbum dico, et iam dictum est."*
  * Reader names self → unfolds inner page → reads aloud at the table
  * Cost: full SAN track + Mythos in one round; permanent passives; one-way receiver in reverse
  * El Patrón bound back into the avatar's body; body dies; avatar contained, *not gone*

* **DESTROYED**
  * Player-distributed cost-table among Marked PCs:
    * **Anchor** (dies, like Saráchaga) / **Pillar** (POW + 1 sense) / **Wall** (1d10 SAN + tic) / **Witness** (one year of dreams) / **Bystander** (one warm pulse, then nothing)
  * 5+ Marked PCs: all tiers; fewer PCs: drop tiers from the bottom
  * The *cuchillo* through the chest (or Mercedes's substitute *facón* if unavailable; PC who draws their own blood takes one tier higher)
  * Book burns by withdrawal — ink rises as smoke
  * El Patrón is **gone**, not bound; body of Eligio Méndez collapses to its 1798 weight

* **BAD ENDINGS**: Reader refuses (Mercedes attempts at dawn, fails — Hidden-but-worse) / Players accept the deal in C11 / All flee the dock

**ACT 4 — AFTERMATH**

* **The dust lifts.** Off cuffs, seams, hatbands; rises in a single coherent layer; gone. (3b resolution)
* **The vibration resolves** per ending (3c)
* Per-NPC epilogues — one line each, around the table at dawn
* Per-location epilogue: La Esperanza / San Ruiz / Jesuit ruins / cordillera / Buenos Aires
* Final Image: La Luz Mala flickers once at the edge of frame; then *(per ending)* descends / relocates / ends
* **Annual dream (5d)**: once a year, on the same calendar night, one surviving PC dreams of the pit. Concepción at the centre, fourteen, fifteen, sixteen — humming a different tune each year. They will not remember the dream. They will write the date in their journal.

**KEY DIALOGUE**

* Mercedes: *"Lo guardamos, lo usamos, o lo destruimos?"*
* Albarrán: *"Hic locus est. Hic manet."*
* Galíndez: *"Pasé treinta años pensando que sabía cómo se libraba una guerra. Hoy aprendí que no se. Ustedes saben."*

**GM NOTES**

* Pre-print the Latin card. Practice the cadence before C12.
* Run all three endings with equal commitment; do not signal a "right" one in advance
* Cost-table conversation in Destroyed: real five-to-ten minutes at the table; no Keeper facilitation
* The dust lifting must be *seen* by the players — show, don't say

## ARC 4 SNAPSHOT

* **Theme**: The avatar negotiates. The choice is yours, with cost.
* **Emotions**: Cold Resolve → The Long Hour → Choice → Aftermath
* **Seeds**: All sequel hooks (Lacasa at Areco, Saúl's hand-copy, the Network, the Reader, the avatar's return in Hide, the annual dream as framing device)
* **Rule**: The campaign ends where the players choose to end it. Honor the choice. Run the cost.
