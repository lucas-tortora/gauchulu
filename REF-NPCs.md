# NPC REFERENCE — LA TIERRA MALA
### *Quick-access portraits, hooks, and locations for all twelve chapters (Arcs 1–4)*

---

## HOW TO USE THIS DOCUMENT

Each NPC entry includes:
- **Appears in**: which chapters they are present or active
- **Location(s)**: where they can be found
- **Role**: their function in the story
- **Quick Description**: physical snapshot for immediate use at the table
- **Voice**: a phrase or two capturing how they speak
- **Core Motivation**: the single thing driving their decisions
- **The Truth They Hide** (where applicable)
- **Key Hook**: the most useful roleplaying lever
- **Key Lines**: 2–3 dialogue fragments for the table

This document is the *long* NPC reference. For the *short* version — one or two lines per NPC, embedded in the per-chapter scene flow — see the per-arc cheatsheets:
- `01-la-tierra-mala/cheatsheet.md`
- `02-la-salamanca/cheatsheet.md`
- `03-the-great-sleeper/cheatsheet.md`
- `04-buenos-aires-and-the-heist/cheatsheet.md`

Use this dossier when an NPC enters a scene and you need their voice. Use the per-arc cheatsheet when you need to remember *what they do this chapter*. The two are designed to sit open side-by-side at the table.

### DON EUSEBIO VALDEZ
**Appears in**: Chapters 1–9 (canonically); optionally referenced in C10–C12
**Location**: Estancia La Esperanza (C1–C6); The Pit camp (C7–C9); ruined or aftermath at the estancia (C10–C12, off-stage)
**Role**: The client. The culprit. The necessary presence in the closing.

> **[THIS TABLE — 2026-05-29] PRIMARY CO-ANCHOR CANDIDATE.** With Kuyen and Nahuel dead (C3), Don Eusebio is the **only remaining candidate for the C8/C9 center anchor** — the man who opened the wound, closing it from inside. Dramatically correct, mechanically the weakest anchor (lower POW than Nahuel's; the entity presses him hardest, and the Round 2 offer hits as *"the land will be ordinary again and you will have to live in it knowing what you cost it."*). He also becomes the **primary pre-ritual interlocutor in C7** (the Nahuel/Kuyen evening scenes do not run) and the one who **sets the closing's timeline** in C6 ("Tomorrow night") since Kuyen is not there to drive it. His Scene 4 in C8 (the outer-ring "No") runs unchanged regardless. See `TABLE-STATE.md` and `assets/arc-review-plan.md`.

**Quick Description**: 62 years old. Silver hair, old-leather tan, yellow-white mustache. White linen shirt, bombachas de campo, polished boots. Ink stains spreading across his right hand — index finger (C1), full palm (C4 onward). Smells faintly of tobacco and struck flint. Receives visitors standing. Taps his right index finger twice on the table when changing the subject.

**Voice**: Deep, deliberate. Long pauses before important words. Accustomed to being believed.

**Core Motivation**: Preserve what he built. The estancia, the land, the identity of a man who succeeded by his own means. He opened the scar because he thought he was saving his land. He has been lying to himself about the cost ever since.

**The Truth He Hides**:
- He performed the ritual himself, from a manuscript found in the walls
- He watched at least three people walk south at night and did nothing
- He sent Silvio to check the outer markers at night — he didn't send him *into* the pasture, but he sent him there
- He has been maintaining the diagram in modified form, believing it creates a pressure valve
- He contacted Aldao Quirce, whose response contained knowledge Don Eusebio had never shared

**Arc Through the Campaign**:
- **C1**: Employer, confident liar, directing investigators away from the truth
- **C2–3**: Watches from a ridge. Investigators become threats.
- **C4**: Exhausted, partially honest. Confrontation with the manuscript breaks his denial.
- **C5–6**: Resistance, bargaining, Silvio as the mirror that cracks him. Agrees to stand in the ring.
- **C7**: Confession — Concepción, the nine weeks of reasoning, what he thinks the entity will do to him
- **C8**: Faces the entity's offer at his position. Stays or falters.
- **C9**: Speaks the word "No." The geometry completes.

**Key Hook**: He wants someone to witness his choices as choices — not as results. He wants to be accountable to a person, not just to a consequence.

**Key Lines**:
> *"I told myself it wasn't my concern. I didn't stop them."*
> *"He works."* (Said flatly, about Silvio.)
> *"There were nine more weeks of that reasoning."*
> *"No."* (Phase Four. One word. Final.)

**Tell**: Taps right index finger twice on table → changing subject / deflecting. Looking toward the study door → he's protecting the manuscript. Looking east → he's thinking about the south pasture.

### TOMÁS ARREDONDO
**Appears in**: Chapters 1–2, available C3–6
**Location**: Estancia La Esperanza
**Role**: The afraid foreman. A partial truth-teller. The person most likely to crack under genuine kindness.

**Quick Description**: 43, mestizo, stocky. Cracked lips, shifting dark eyes. Faded red chiripa, facón on his belt that hasn't been drawn in years. Right hand trembles when thinking about the south pasture.

**Voice**: Low, fast when nervous. Speaks in bursts and stops mid-sentence, as if hearing something behind his own words.

**Core Motivation**: Keep his family safe (wife and two children in the workers' quarters). Loyal to Don Eusebio by economic necessity, not affection.

**What He Knows** (if made to feel genuinely safe):
- The tracks in the south pasture don't behave right
- He saw La Luz Mala three weeks before Chapter 1
- The pit predates the cattle deaths — he found it a year ago
- Don Eusebio kept him away from the study for two weeks last spring; he heard scratching sounds
- He will not call La Luz Mala by name — he calls it *"ella"*

**Key Hook**: Treat him as a skilled worker whose knowledge matters — not a servant, not a superstitious fool. If investigators earn genuine trust, he presses a flat river stone (the vizcachera stone) into their hands. It has the same angular symbol scratched on it as the hide. *"I found this near the pit. Months ago. Before everything started."*

**Key Lines**:
> *"I don't go out at night."* (Absolute. Final.)
> *"I don't know who told Don Eusebio that."* (Re: tribal blame. His bravest near-contradiction.)
> *"They just stop. In the middle of open ground. They just stop."* (About the tracks.)

**Tell**: Crosses himself automatically when not watched. Spits before entering south pasture territory. Goes still at any mention of "she."

### CONCEPCIÓN
**Appears in**: Chapter 1 (background); Chapter 7 (referenced)
**Location**: Estancia La Esperanza → Town of Azul (sent away C2)
**Role**: The servant girl Don Eusebio protected. Evidence that he is not wholly lost.

> **[THIS TABLE — 2026-05-24] DEAD.** Concepción did not survive C1. Don Eusebio's C7 confession shifts: he sent her away injured, she died anyway. He volunteered her. He knows it. He stands in the outer ring in C8 without being asked — settlement, not redemption. The quiet victory callback in C9 is gone. See `TABLE-STATE.md`.

**Quick Description**: 13 years old, quiet, large eyes. Brings water without being asked. Leaves without meeting anyone's gaze.

**Core Motivation**: Obedience; survival. She doesn't fully understand what she experienced.

**The Hook**: She is not present for most of the campaign — Don Eusebio sent her to her cousin's house in Azul after she walked toward the south pasture one night. Her one line, if investigators find out about her:
> *"It's very big down there, Patrón. Don't be afraid."*

This line is not spoken to investigators directly — Don Eusebio quotes it in his Chapter 7 confession. It is one of the most chilling moments of the campaign.

### KUYEN
**Appears in**: ~~Chapters 3–9; if alive, Chapters 10–12~~ — **[THIS TABLE] DEAD. Died C3 holding the eastern line.**
**Location**: ~~Dry creek bed encampment (C3–C5); On the move (C5–C6); The Pit camp (C7–C9); Inocencia's boarding house and the Riachuelo cordon (C10–C12)~~ — Creek bed. Body at the eastern ochre line.
**Role**: The true defender. The ritual leader. The voice of consequence. **At this table: the only person who understood the full closing sequence. Her death is the campaign's central complication from C4 onward — the investigators must find another path to the pit closing without her.**

> **[THIS TABLE — C3 — 2026-05-24] KUYEN IS DEAD.** Died on the eastern line in the final moments of R4. Moved to hold the position herself when no one else did in time. Completed a partial symbol. Took the full distortion load from the Hound's passage. Something closed — not enough, but something. She did what she said she would do. The creek bed is where she is.
>
> **[THIS TABLE — C3 — 2026-05-29] THE TRIBE IS DEAD.** Kuyen's people did not survive C3 — the whole line is gone, not only its leader. **The only survivors who carry any of the tradition's geometry are the Wrong Returned (Héctor and Marta), and that by exposure, not teaching.** Consequences: no tribal allies travel to Buenos Aires in C10–C12 (only Wrong Returned, if alive); the "Kuyen's people / surviving tribal allies" beats throughout Arcs 3–4 do not trigger; the closing's tradition-knowledge now lives only in Saens's manuscript (written), Mercedes (reader), Marta's body (alignment), and the Marked PCs (perception). In the C12 Destroyed ending the tradition has no elder left to mourn its own inheritance — Mercedes delivers the line in Spanish, from memory. That loss is itself a campaign cost.

**Quick Description**: 52, Mapuche. Silver-grey hair in two braids. Face that is not hard but *still* — the stillness of someone doing something difficult for a long time without stopping. Heavy wool, riding pants, worn boots. Small pouch of ground pigments at her belt. Hands always available for symbol work — she does not gesticulate.

**Voice**: Low, controlled. Speaks at the pace information requires. When frightened, voice drops lower, not higher. Does not perform calm — she sustains it because there is no alternative.

**Core Motivation**: Complete the binding. Protect her people, especially Nahuel. Close what was opened. She has been doing this for eight months.

**What She Knows**:
- The full binding ritual and its history in her tradition
- The scar is older than her people's time in this land
- Don Eusebio opened it — she assumed ignorance, not malice
- The entity learns the geometry as they deploy it; they need to be faster than it is
- The Wrong Returned are structural anchors — not victims, not sacrifices, necessary

**Key Hook**: She does not ask for help. Concrete specific offers (*"I'll hold the eastern mark"*) get precise instructions. Vague offers get: *"Then don't interfere."*

The most powerful moment: if an investigator says *"We were working for the man who did this. I'm sorry."* — She is still. Then: *"I know. Now be useful."*

**Key Lines**:
> *"Not all rituals are the same."*
> *"We were stopping it."*
> *"You helped it grow. You understand that."*
> *"It will scar over. Land does. It won't be what it was."*

**Tell**: Eyes always partly on the ritual circle, even in conversation. Says *"No."* once when disagreeing, simply, then explains. Never gestures with hands.

### NAHUEL
**Appears in**: ~~Chapters 3–9; if alive, Chapters 10–12~~ — **[THIS TABLE] DEAD. Died C3 at the ritual center.**
**Location**: ~~Dry creek bed encampment → Pit camp → Ritual center~~ — Creek bed. Center of the ochre diagram.
**Role**: The ritual focus. The most vulnerable person. The entity's intended pressure point. **At this table: died when the working collapsed. Had been holding the focus for 23 days. The entity was using him as a classification point — it found him interesting. His last line: *"I thought it would feel like being erased. It just felt like being categorized."***

> **[THIS TABLE — C3 — 2026-05-24] NAHUEL IS DEAD.** Died at center when the working collapsed at end of R4. The entity did not kill him the way weapons kill — the geometry simply stopped being organized around him. 23 days as ritual focus. He did not crack; the working cracked around him. The C8 co-anchor slot is now vacant. Don Eusebio is the only remaining candidate.

**Quick Description**: 24 years old. Lean, angular features. Dark circles so deep they look bruised. Kneeling at the symbol center, arms at sides, eyes fixed on something not present. Breathing shallow, dangerously controlled. Ochre geometric patterns traced on his palms — smudged from constant redrawing. Shadow falls in the wrong direction from the firelight.

**Voice**: Thin, slightly echoed, as if arriving from a short distance. Speaks in present tense about things that happened in the past.

**Core Motivation**: Complete the ritual. Not crack. Protect Kuyen. He will go past his limit — which is exactly the problem.

**The Trap**: He believes the entity has been moderating its pressure, keeping him functional because it wants the closing attempted — with him as focus — so it can fail at maximum pressure and the breach will be total. He may not be the right sole focus.

**Signs of Strain**:
- Shadow falls wrong
- Blinks in synchrony with other circle members (not consciously)
- Ochre marks on palms are redrawn dozens of times
- Has been the focus for three weeks before the final night

**Key Hook**: He needs an anchor — something physical to hold — to ground the focus at critical moments. An investigator offering to hold his hands in Phase Five is the most direct sacrifice available in the campaign.

**Key Lines**:
> *"Ask me after."* (Whether he's all right.)
> *"Twenty-three days."* (Precise. He has been counting.)
> *"Cold. In the angles."* (What he feels.)
> *"It looked at me. When the circle broke. I thought it would feel like being erased. It just felt like… being categorized."*
> *"I can still feel the corners. They're quiet now."* (Aftermath.)

### ROSA
**Appears in**: Chapters 2, 5 (message), 9 (aftermath)
**Location**: San Ruiz village — last house on the east end
**Role**: The last witness. The guardian of memory. A partial guide to truth.

**Quick Description**: 71 years old. Mapuche-Tehuelche descent. Short, wide, face like carved ironwood. Black dress, wool shawl in the heat. Small silver ornament at throat — four lines meeting at angles, not quite a cross. Hands always busy: mending, grinding, weaving. Works while talking.

**Voice**: Low, measured. Waits through silences that make others uncomfortable. Mixes Spanish with occasional Mapudungun words she does not translate — not to obscure, but because some things don't exist in Spanish.

**Core Motivation**: Be heard. Pass on what she knows to someone who can act on it correctly. She stayed in San Ruiz because someone needs to remember.

**Hidden Knowledge**: The complete binding symbol scratched from memory under her grinding stone. She shares it only with those who have proven trustworthy.

**Key Hook**: She responds to respect and genuine listening. If investigators rush her or dismiss her, she gives the minimum. She will not name Don Eusebio — naming the guilty is not her role. Finding the shape is.

**Her Final Word** (after the chapel encounter):
> *"Now you know what you're in the middle of."*

**Key Lines**:
> *"Someone needs to remember. If I leave, it is as if it never happened. The land forgets faster than people do."*
> *"The Valdez man told you that."* (Not a question.)
> *"Close it anyway."*
> *"I'll plant something here. Over the pit. Something that grows slow."* (Aftermath.)

### SILVIO MÉNDEZ
**Appears in**: Chapters 1 (background, unnoticed); 4–9 (active)
**Location**: Estancia La Esperanza (C1–C6); The Pit (C6 onward)
**Role**: The Wrong Returned within the estancia. Present since before investigators arrived. The most immediately uncanny NPC.

**Quick Description**: 38, looking 50. Lean from forgetting to eat. Skin dry and cracked in summer. Eyes open a fraction too wide — lids don't fully close even when blinking. Faint mineral smell, same note as the pit. Shadow falls wrong in certain light. Works constantly without breaks or rest. Has been found standing in the dark outside the south pasture fence, facing inward.

**Voice**: Polite. Slightly displaced — answers what you asked three sentences ago, running behind the conversation. Present tense about everything.

**Core Motivation**: Complete the work. He holds himself back from the pit with what remains of his will.

**What He Knows**:
- Can feel where the geometry is, as spatial fact
- Knows there are two others like him (Héctor and Marta) coming from the south
- *"The shape is getting louder."*
- After descending the pit voluntarily (C6): *"It showed me the inside. Not in pictures. In angles."*

**His Best Line** (Chapter 7, sitting at the pit's edge at 3 AM):
> *"It doesn't want things. It moves. The opening is a place where it can move more easily. We opened it wider. Now it has too much of itself here and it can't go back the way it came."*

**The Aftermath Line**:
> *"I've been thinking about going back to my family's house in Mendoza. I don't think I've thought about that in months."* *(He looks surprised by this.)*

**Key Hook**: He is the horror of the ordinary. He does his job. He answers questions. The wrongness is in the details. Let investigators decide whether he is a victim to help or a danger to avoid — he is both, incompletely.

### HÉCTOR RÍOS
**Appears in**: Chapters 4–9; if alive, Chapters 10–12
**Location**: San Ruiz (origin); Tres Lagunas (post-evacuation); Estancia fence line (C4); Road east (C5); Jesuit ruins (C5); Pit camp (C6–C9); Inocencia's boarding house and the cordon (C10–C12)
**Role**: The Wrong Returned. Anchor. Farmer who was shown a map of what he'd been trying to describe. In Arc 4: a partial-geometry anchor along the transport route — his alignment is *exactly* what reads city geometry.

> **[THIS TABLE — 2026-05-29] LOAD-BEARING SURVIVOR.** Kuyen and Nahuel are dead (C3) and the tribe is gone — so the **Wrong Returned (Héctor and Marta) are the only non-PC personnel who carry the geometry in their bodies.** If Héctor lives through C9, he is a de facto cordon lead in C11/C12 alongside Marta, holding the eastern/partial-alignment positions the closing now depends on. His survival is structurally significant in a way the default run did not require.

**Quick Description**: 54, wide-shouldered, graying. Former farmer from San Ruiz, considered steady. Now precise and stripped of social texture — no small talk, no roundabout approaches. Just answers. Eyes track geometry before people. Shadow falls at wrong angles.

**Voice**: Direct, stripped of gaucho dialect he used to have. Like listening to someone read from a document they didn't write.

**Core Motivation**: Be at the geometry when it closes. He walked three days back from Tres Lagunas because something was finishing and he needed to be there.

**What He Knows**:
> *"It isn't underground. It's in the angles. It lives in the places where one surface meets another."*
> *"There are others like us. Not many."*
> *"This is where the shape lives. The pit is just where someone tore a hole in it."*

**Key Hook**: He goes directly to the carved stone at the Jesuit ruins and places his hands on it. He is the only person who touches it without hesitation. He is the character most fully at peace with what he is now — and that peace is more unsettling than Marta's grief.

### MARTA RÍOS
**Appears in**: Chapters 4–9; if alive, Chapters 10–12
**Location**: Same as Héctor; Jesuit ruins (C5, special scene); Ritual center (C9, possible); Inocencia's boarding house and the cordon (C10–C12)
**Role**: The Wrong Returned. Anchor. Village teacher and midwife who was shown something vast noticing something small. In Arc 4: the most articulate Wrong Returned at the cordon, the one most able to talk Mercedes through what is happening when the geometry shifts.

> **[THIS TABLE — 2026-05-29] DE FACTO CORDON LEAD (C11/C12).** With Kuyen dead, Marta is the **only person who can draw the complete dual diagram from direct experience** (she traced it from memory in the dark, C5/C9). If she survives C9 she **leads the Buenos Aires cordon's panel placement** — "I don't know the words Kuyen used. But I know where the corners need to be. My body knows the right angles." Improvised, less stable than Kuyen's, but real; a Mandinga-Warding PC can substitute their ward for one panel leg at a weak point. In C8/C9 she also fills the **western anchor** and, if rapport was built, co-anchors the center (see corrected note below). Her survival is load-bearing for Arc 4.

**Quick Description**: 43, Héctor's wife (no blood relation — the surname coincidence was a village joke). Former teacher and midwife. Calm by profession, calmer now in a different way. Occasionally a moment of the old Marta surfaces in her expression and is gone. Touches her own face sometimes, as if checking it's still there.

**Voice**: More present than Héctor. Occasionally slips into warmth before the flatness returns.

**Core Motivation**: Close the geometry. *"Close it anyway."* — She doesn't know if she comes back from it. She decided it doesn't matter.

**The Optional Scene** (Chapter 5, 2 AM at the ruins): She traces the complete dual diagram in the air from memory, in three dimensions, eyes open. Afterward:
> *"Fourteen people went to the field at night. Seven came back changed. Five of those didn't survive the change. Two did. I'm one of the two."*

**The Closing** (Chapter 9, if rapport was built): She offers to co-anchor the center ring ~~with Nahuel~~, using her partial geometric alignment to hold it steady from outside. This is her choice. Investigators cannot make it for her. ⚠️ **[THIS TABLE]** Nahuel is dead — there is no trained center anchor for her to support. At this table she co-anchors with **Don Eusebio** (or the Marked PC who volunteered for center), and her alignment is needed more, not less.

**The Aftermath**: If she co-anchors, her partial geometric alignment burns off at once — she spends the night unable to speak or move, then wakes hungry for the first time in fourteen months.

**Key Lines**:
> *"I don't feel pain the way I used to. I'm not sure if that's better."*
> *"Padre Saens was right about one thing — it isn't the devil. But it's not a wound. It's a door. The wound is what we did to it."*
> *"Close it anyway."*

### ALDAO QUIRCE
**Appears in**: Chapters 6–11 (off-stage in C9–C10, surfaced in C11)
**Location**: Estancia La Esperanza (C6); Pit camp (C7–C9); Buenos Aires — somewhere in the city, never at the meeting house (C10–C11). Excluded from Keeper meetings; suspected of being El Patrón's intermediary.
**Role**: The observer turned operator. In Arc 2–3, a network "scholar" with a hidden agenda. In Arc 4, the third-key holder — the man who installed the 1810 lock on the chest, the leak who fed Ana Bermúdez disinformation, and Lacasa's correspondent. He never appears on stage in Arc 4; his reach does.

**Quick Description**: 48, city-built, soft hands, good boots. Dark suit, no jewelry. Leather satchel of excellent make. Neat hair, precisely trimmed mustache. The face of a man whose profession requires trustworthiness at first glance, who has practiced it for decades. Carries: brass measurement instruments (angle gauges, theodolite), a cipher notebook, and a reproduction of the Saens manuscript with annotations not present in the original.

**Voice**: Warm, precisely modulated, educated Buenos Aires. Gives the impression of genuine delight in meeting you — until it starts to feel like a performance of delight.

**Core Motivation**: Observe the closing. Document what a successful seal looks like from the outside. This information is valuable to his network of scholars, engineers, and wealthy enthusiasts who study geometric anomalies. He views deaths as "field casualties — regrettable but significant."

**The Cover**: Here to help. Knows more than anyone. His instruments will improve accuracy. His copy of the manuscript fills the damaged sections.

**What Breaks the Cover**:
- **Psychology**: performing warmth. Tracking variables, not people.
- His annotated manuscript contains sections the "damaged" original lacks — he had the answer prepared when asked, but it's too smooth
- His diagram "correction" introduces an asymmetry that creates a peak of maximum entity-field exposure in the outer ring at the moment of closure
- Catching him with brass markers placed in Don Eusebio's study to extend the geometry's observable field

**The Moral Failure**: Not malice. A profound mismatch in what counts as harm. He didn't consider what two seconds of entity-field does to a human body. The geometry and the people standing in it are different categories of concern.

**Key Hook**: He looks at the corners of rooms before the people in them — the same thing the Wrong Returned do. Point this out to players who notice.

**Key Lines**:
> *"Field geometry. The study of how certain spatial configurations persist in land-use areas. Quite academic, I assure you."*
> *"It creates an observation window. A moment of maximum pressure before the seal closes. No one was going to be harmed by it — it's a two-second differential."*
> *"I owe a debt here. I know I do. The question of how to pay it will take time to answer."* (Aftermath.)

### DOÑA MERCEDES SOLÍS
**Appears in**: Chapters 4–12 (by letter C4–C6; in person C7–C12)
**Location**: Buenos Aires (her home base, off-stage C4–C6); the pit camp (C7–C9); Buenos Aires — the Manzana de las Luces, the Keeper meeting house, Inocencia's boarding house, the dock (C10–C12)
**Role**: The teacher. The moral and intellectual center of Arcs 3–4. The Keeper who advocates for moving the book and pays for it.

> **[THIS TABLE — 2026-05-29] ASSUMES THE RITUAL-DIRECTOR ROLE.** With Kuyen dead (C3), Mercedes inherits the **director / panel-caller function for the C8/C9 closing** and the **Phase 5 lead chant** (the "*Antu kishu, lan amukey*" line — she leads it; the untrained join and the geometry teaches them). This is grounded, not improvised: her dossier already makes her the only NPC who can both *read* the geometry and *explain* it. She translates **Saens's written procedure** into spoken direction — the campaign's resolution to "who knows the rite now that Kuyen is dead." Say it once through her in C7: *"Saens wrote down how. He could not do it alone. That was his only mistake we are not about to repeat."* In C11/C12 she also reads the destroy-page and delivers the tradition's closing line herself, in Spanish, since no elder survives. See `assets/arc-review-plan.md` (Blocker 1).

**Quick Description**: Mid-60s (born 1757; copied the Necronomicón at 24 in 1781), slight, white hair pulled back. Plain dark dress, silver crucifix she does not wear ostentatiously, ink stains on her right index and middle fingers. Gold-rimmed reading glasses she pushes up onto her forehead when listening. Walks with a cane she does not lean on. The face of a woman whose job has been to read carefully for fifty years.

**Voice**: Quiet, precise, educated Buenos Aires Castilian. Uses Latin when she is genuinely emphatic. Pauses where other people hurry.

**Core Motivation**: Atonement. At 24 she copied a section of the Necronomicón out of personal curiosity; that copy traveled, in fragments, into the manuscripts the Salamanca cult later read. She has been teaching, recording, and binding ever since to balance the ledger she made.

**The Truth She Hides** (until C11):
- She copied a section of the original Necronomicón forty years ago
- That copy is the reason El Patrón has been hunting her for thirty years
- The Salamanca cult's partial knowledge descends, in part, from her hand
- She believes destroying the book may be the only true atonement, and she carries the means to do it (the folded parchment she gives the players in C11)

**Arc Through the Campaign**:
- **C4–C6**: A correspondent. A voice in letters. Her judgment of Quirce is the players' first reliable Keeper-frame.
- **C7**: Arrives at the pit camp, ~~embraces Kuyen (thirty years of friendship visible for one breath)~~, names the Necronomicón as the book Saens copied from. ⚠️ **[THIS TABLE]** Kuyen is dead — there is no one for the thirty-year embrace. Mercedes arrives, reads the camp in an instant, and walks to the fire; the absence is the players' weight to carry. She still recognizes the pit-dust on a player's cuff (the dust runner).
- **C8–C9**: Present at the closing; carries the Coypac dispatch east; rides for Buenos Aires.
- **C10**: Sponsors the players to the Keepers; brings them into the meeting house.
- **C11**: Confesses what she copied. Hands over the folded parchment. *"No te pido que perdones."*
- **C12**: At the dock. Calls the moment of choice. Pays the cost per ending — burned hands if Used, ash if Destroyed, an old woman walking home if Hidden.

**Key Hook**: She is the only NPC who can both read the geometry *and* explain it. The players' relationship with her is the campaign's clearest model of how knowledge survives an entity-contact.

**Key Lines**:
> *"He leído partes del Necronomicón. No fui forzada. Era joven, y curiosa. Llevo cuarenta años pagándolo."*
> *"Tenemos que mover el libro antes de que él nos obligue a moverlo."*
> *"No te pido que perdones. Te pido que cuando ese hombre nos diga mañana de noche que yo soy la que abrió la puerta, ustedes ya lo sepan por mí. No por él."*
> *"Bueno. Mañana hacemos lo que planeamos."*

### THE CURSED GAUCHO
**Appears in**: Chapters 7–8, 11–12
**Location**: Pit camp (C7–C8, peripheral); Buenos Aires — the tannery on the Riachuelo, then a corner of Belgrano and Defensa (C11–C12)
**Role**: The vengeance hunter. The man whose family died at Coypac. The campaign's chosen martyr — if the players let him be.

**Quick Description**: Mid-thirties. Sun-blasted, lean. A clean diagonal scar from earlobe to chin on his left side. Black *bombachas*, *vicuña* poncho, wide-brimmed hat. Two knives in plain sight (a *facón* on his back, a *cuchillo de campo* on his belt) and an unloaded flintlock pistol that he carries for what it represents, not what it does. A *zaino* horse pushed hard for two days at every appearance.

**Voice**: Northern *gaucho* Spanish, the consonants softened, the vowels long. He speaks in short sentences. He does not raise his voice. He has carried his grief for three years and learned to speak it without inflection.

**Core Motivation**: Reach Eligio Dadálah — El Patrón — and put a knife in him before he dies. Every other action is logistical.

**Backstory** (told around the camp fire in C7): Three years ago, in a valley at the foot of the cordillera the locals call Coypac, a Cult of the Great Sleeper raiding party killed his wife, his four-year-old son, and his father. His mother survived but hanged herself the same week. The Cursed Gaucho has been hunting the cult — and the figure behind it — ever since. He has learned to read the cult's geometry well enough to find them before they find him.

**Arc Through the Campaign**:

**Key Hook**: He is the campaign's clearest case of *honoring a dying man's terms*. The players' decision in C11 — agree, refuse, or hedge — directly shapes whether his death buys them anything.

**Key Lines**:
> *"Tres años. Voy buscando. Mato cuando encuentro."*

> She does not follow the party — she *precedes* them. She is always already where they are going. No one sees her arrive. She makes no sound. She does not eat, does not bleed if cut, does not react to fire or gunshot. Her pupils do not dilate. She cannot be lost, tied off, or left behind.
>
> **She can harm the immaterial.** Mandinga's creature, she exists at the threshold between physical and non-physical. She can strike psychic remnants, displaced entities, and the Hound's edge-geometry — not destroy them, but disrupt their form and create distance. First demonstrated at San Ruiz when she headbutted La Llorona mid-turn and sent her lurching east. She acts on her own judgment; she cannot be directed or commanded. One disruption available to the party at all times, timed entirely by her.
>
> **The deal**: When a PC is effectively dying, she approaches, gets very close, and the dying PC receives an understanding — not words — of an offer. *You live. You close the pit with your hands. That is all.* If accepted, the wound closes and the goat attends that PC specifically from then on. The contract sits in them like a splinter: present, not painful, keeping count. If refused, she steps back and the dying proceeds. *(At this table: first deal opportunity at San Ruiz departure, if gravely injured PC is at threshold. See chapter-02, THIS TABLE closer.)*
>
> **Enforcement**: The life was a loan. If the PC moves away from the closing, the wound begins to return — gradual, visible, a clock. Mandinga will not let them die of anything *else* before the debt is paid; the body closes around injury against the PC's wishes. If they default entirely and die without closing the pit, the debt survives death. They come back wrong. See `TABLE-STATE.md` — The Deal and Enforcement.
>
> **Do not explain until Arc 2.**

### MANDINGA
**Type**: Folk supernatural / displaced territorial power
**Appears in**: Ambient from C2 (via the goat); implied presence in Arc 2 (La Salamanca is his domain); possible direct manifestation C5-6
**Full notes**: `TABLE-STATE.md` — The Goat Thread

**What he is**: The gaucho devil — the folk name for the Devil in the Río de la Plata tradition. He runs La Salamanca, the underground school where forbidden arts are taught. He rides at midnight. He takes the form of black animals. He makes deals but can be outwitted. He is the master of the morally compromised: gamblers, midnight riders, the man who sold his shadow at a crossroads. He is not an abstraction — he is a specific local power with territory, history, and grievances.

**The displacement**: The entity pushing through the pit is something Mandinga has no frame for. It does not acknowledge his territory, does not negotiate, does not even register him as a party. For the first time in the history of the pampas, Mandinga is not the most dangerous thing in the land. He is being overrun in his own house. He knows it.

**La Salamanca**: The Great Wizard of Salamanca believes he serves Mandinga's tradition. He does not — El Patrón has been steering him for forty years, operating within Mandinga's domain without permission. Mandinga has been used. He knows by whom. Investigators entering La Salamanca in C5-6 are walking into the territory of an entity with every reason to want the elder god gone and no clean way to act on it.

**How he operates**: He does not fight. He does not appear directly unless the conditions are exactly right. He works through markers, through deals, through the people already in play. He will not offer a clean alliance — he will offer a transaction, bounded and specific. He wants acknowledgment that his territory was violated, and the entity's removal from it. He and the investigators want the same outcome for entirely different reasons.

**The deal mechanism**: When a PC is dying and the goat is present, Mandinga acts through her. The terms are simple: *you live, you close the pit with your hands.* Not the soul — *"el alma no me sirve de nada si no hay pampas donde gastarla."* Just a placement at the closing. The deal is not generous; it is efficient. He needs the pit closed and he needs someone bound to close it. The dying PC is a resource, not a guest. See `TABLE-STATE.md` — The Deal.

**Quick description if he manifests**: A dark figure on the edge of firelight — could be a man in black, could be a black horse standing where a man should be. He is the last thing in the room that moves. His voice arrives slightly before he does. He smells of sulfur the way the land smells of grass: as a fact, not a warning.

**Key lines** *(if he speaks)*:
> *"Eso que está abajo no es mío. Nunca fue mío. Y tampoco es de ustedes."*
> *"Mi trato es sencillo. Lo sacan. Me devuelven lo que me deben. No les cobro el alma — el alma no me sirve de nada si no hay pampas donde gastarla."*

> **[THIS TABLE — 2026-05-24]** The goat is his marker, placed in the one ungeometric space in San Ruiz. The investigators removed it. He is now watching them directly. The La Salamanca encounter in Arc 2 is when this thread pays off.

### EL SABUESO (The Hound)
**Type**: Geometric entity / dimensional intruder
**Appears in**: Chapters 3 (full manifestation); 7–9 (active at the pit, pressing through geometry)
**Full notes**: Chapter 3, The Entity section; Chapter 8–9

**Quick reference**: Exists in peripheral vision and at angles. Never fully described. Large, scale wrong for the space. Occupies corners and edges — where surfaces meet — not open space. Moves toward complex geometry. Does not hunt; catalogues. Deaths it causes are incidental, a side effect of presence. Cannot be killed — can be displaced by sealing the geometry. Fire causes temporary retraction from surfaces. Low continuous vibration at the lower limit of hearing; eyes water in its presence. SAN 1/1D6 first manifestation.
