# LA TIERRA MALA — THE CAST AT A GLANCE

*One-page summary of the main actors, their motivations, and where the players fit in. For the GM at the table, or for a player-facing handout if you redact the lines marked **(GM only)**.*

---

## THE OPPOSITION

**EL PATRÓN — Don Eligio Dadálah** *(avatar of Nyarlathotep; off-stage C1–C10, on-stage C11–C12)*
Wants the Necronomicón. **Cannot** simply take it: the vault under the Manzana de las Luces is a 1610 containment that *holds him out* as much as it holds the book in. **The book has to move** — only in transit can it be intercepted. Forty years of arranging this carriage ride: through Don Eusebio's hand, through the Salamanca cult, through Quirce, through Lacasa. **(GM only:)** he occupies the body of Eligio Méndez, a Salta cattle merchant who disappeared in 1798. **Crossing the players:** he is the reason every faction in the campaign is moving. His on-stage moment is C12.

**THE CULT OF THE GREAT SLEEPER — fronted by The Great Wizard of Salamanca**
Believes that waking the entity below the pampas will refound the *campaña* outside the Spanish-Catholic frame. The Great Wizard is sincere, learned, and **wrong**: every fragment he has "discovered" was placed for him by El Patrón's network over forty years. The cult is a tool that thinks it is a hand. **Crossing the players:** the visible enemy of Arcs 2–3, the failed awakening at Coypac (C8–C9), and the assassins at the Riachuelo in C12.

**DON EUSEBIO VALDEZ** *(C1–C9)*
Found a Jesuit manuscript in a wall cavity three years before the campaign opens, performed a partial *opening* believing it would make his land fertile, and has been lying about it — to investigators and to himself — ever since. He does **not** serve El Patrón knowingly; he is a useful tool who chose his own enslavement. **Crossing the players:** the false client of C1, the man who hires them, the man who confesses to Concepción in C7 and stands in the outer ring in C8.

**ALDAO QUIRCE** *(C6–C11, mostly off-stage)*
*Porteño* medium and amateur occultist. Channels two voices — El Patrón's and the Great Wizard's — and believes he is using both. He is being used by both. He is the conduit through which Mercedes' compromised copy reached the Salamanca cult, and the operator who turned Ana Bermúdez and Don Bartolomé Lacasa. **Crossing the players:** the half-finished letter on Eusebio's desk in C1, the medium they meet in C6, the absent Keeper in C10–C11.

---

## THE DEFENDERS

**DOÑA MERCEDES SOLÍS** *(C4 by letter, C7–C12 in person)*
A Keeper of Buenos Aires. **At twenty-four, in 1781, she copied a section of the Necronomicón out of personal curiosity.** That copy traveled in fragments through forty years of transmission and is the **reason** El Patrón has been hunting her — and the reason the Salamanca cult has any knowledge at all. She has been teaching, binding, and recording for forty years to balance that ledger. She advocates moving the book; she carries the parchment that can be used or destroyed. **Crossing the players:** their teacher in Arc 3, their sponsor with the Keepers in Arc 4, the woman who confesses in C11 and pays the per-ending cost in C12.

**THE KEEPERS (LOS CUSTODIOS)** *(institution; on-stage C10–C12)*
Three centuries old. Maintain the Manzana de las Luces vault and the city's geometric containment grid. **Saráchaga** (the institutional dissenter, killed C10 dawn the night he changes his mind), **Padre Albarrán** (the Franciscan swing vote, *"Dios me contestó. Lo movemos."*), **Captain Galíndez** (the practical hand, fifteen armed men, two decoy routes, one ship), **Ana Bermúdez** (junior, used by Quirce, not yet a traitor), **Don Bartolomé Lacasa** (the empty chair; compromised; flees north). The Keepers cannot solve the C12 choice for the players — they can only run the operation around it.

**THE INDIGENOUS LINEAGE — Kuyen, Nahuel, Héctor, Marta** *(C2–C9)*
Heirs of the Mapuche / Tehuelche curanderas who maintained Padre Saens's *partial* binding for sixty years after the priest sacrificed himself in 1761. **Kuyen** is the keeper of the counter-chant; **Nahuel** is its unstable focus. They are not the enemy: they are the reason there has been time at all. **Crossing the players:** the false target of C1's hunt (this is the lie that begins the campaign), the genuine ritualists of C3, the people who close the pit alongside the players in C8–C9.

**THE CURSED GAUCHO** *(C7–C8, C11–C12)*
Mestizo, Marked, hunting El Patrón's network alone for three years since the cult killed his wife, his four-year-old son, his father, and (indirectly) his mother. Asks the players one thing in C11: *don't take El Patrón from him.* The campaign's chosen martyr if the players honor that request. **Crossing the players:** the dangerous outsider who saves them in C7, the man who buys them fifteen minutes on Calle Belgrano in C12.

**EL POMBERO** *(C4–C5; ambient C6–C12)*
The folkloric guide. The most honest party in the campaign. Will not lie, will not elaborate, will not accept payment, will not enter rooms. Whistles before someone enters into something significant. **Crossing the players:** if treated as a colleague, gives one or two precise sentences in moments that matter; if treated as a threat, leaves and does not return.

**PADRE SAENS** *(deceased 1761; present in his bone chamber and in his page 79)*
The Jesuit who tried to close the wound in the land sixty years before the campaign. Achieved a *partial* binding by using himself as the geometric center; bought ~60 years and a counter-ritual lineage. His failure is the reason the players have to finish the work. **Crossing the players:** the bone chamber in C5, his page 79 of the Necronomicón that one of the PCs reads aloud — and so begins the **Reader** path of C12.

## WHERE THE PLAYERS FIT IN

The players begin as **hired hunters** chasing the wrong enemy: tribes blamed for cattle deaths Don Eusebio caused himself. They witness the pit in C1, but they are not yet Marked. **The Mark is dispensed in C3** — and only contingently: any investigator wounded by the Hound of Tindalos during Kuyen's counter-ritual *and surviving* takes a Mark. A clean-observer table can leave C3 with no Marks at all; most tables end with one or two Marked PCs. From C3 onward, Marked PCs **perceive geometry the way other people perceive grammar** — they become the only entities in the campaign who can read the geometry *from inside it*. They are not Keepers. They are not gauchos in the campaign's eyes anymore either.

What this makes them, by Arc 4, is the **only people who can make the C12 choice** — because they are the only people in the room with both the perception (the Mark, where it landed) and the moral standing (they didn't put the book in the vault, they didn't copy from it, they didn't strike the bargain). The Keepers are too compromised. Mercedes is the leak. The Cursed Gaucho is too narrow. The cult is the enemy. **The players are the swing.** *(If no PC took a Mark in C3, Mercedes and the Pombero pick up the perceptive load and the choice still lands on the players — by moral standing, not by perception.)*

The choice in C12 is theirs alone:

- **HIDE** — the book stays in a new vault. The Keepers' three-century policy continues. Cost: Albarrán's *Hic manet*, the carriage ride, the Cursed Gaucho's fifteen minutes.
- **USE** — the **Reader-PC** (the player who first read Saens's page 79 aloud, in C5) speaks the Latin sentence on Mercedes's parchment and binds El Patrón outside the vault. Cost: layered, distributed, named at the table.
- **DESTROY** — the book is unmade, with the Cursed Gaucho's *cuchillo* through page 79 at the Reader's hand. Cost: the players choose who pays it (Anchor, Pillar, Wall, Witness, Bystander), publicly, around the table.

There are no hidden right answers. The campaign's argument is that **the people who broke the binding are the ones who get to choose how it ends, and live with what they chose.** The Mark gives them the perception; the choice is what they do with it.
