# ILLUSTRATION BRIEF — C3 — EL RITUAL ROTO
*Visual descriptions for image generation. Argentine pampas, 1821. Call of Cthulhu.*

---

## SETTING — THE CREEK BED

A dry creek bed cut into the pampas. The banks rise two meters on each side — raw earth, dry grass, exposed clay, a few flat river stones. The bowl is roughly thirty meters across: wide enough to feel like a stage, enclosed enough to feel like a trap. No sight lines from outside. The sky above is the only opening.

Night. Or the last hour of dusk — the sky is deep indigo-black at the east, still faintly amber at the western horizon behind the bank. There are no clouds. The stars are hard and close.

The air inside the bowl is visibly cooler than the surrounding land — not fog, but a quality of stillness that doesn't belong to the season. Four small fires burn at the cardinal points, north/south/east/west, each one carefully controlled: low, precise, no smoke. They do not flicker in the wind because there is no wind inside the bowl.

The floor of the creek is cleared earth. At its center, a large ritual diagram drawn in white ash and ochre pigment: geometric, precise, roughly five meters in diameter. It has the structure of a closed shape — arcs, radial lines, small spiral motifs at the four diagonal positions — but the eastern arc is visibly incomplete. The lines end mid-curve. The ochre there is thinner, more hesitant, drawn and re-drawn over weeks. The rest of the diagram glows faintly amber in the firelight.

**Atmosphere**: Sacred, strained, almost-complete. Like holding your breath. The kind of quiet that has been maintained through enormous effort.

---

## THE RITUAL DIAGRAM

Top-down view, lit from below by the four cardinal fires.

**Shape**: A large circle with internal geometry — not decorative, structural. The arcs divide the circle into a closing pattern. Eight anchor markers at the outer ring. Four diagonal spirals at NW, NE, SW, SE. Animal-track marks at each cardinal: guanaco (north), puma (south), condor (west). The eastern cardinal has no animal mark. The marks there are erased — you can see where they were drawn, scraped away, drawn again, and abandoned.

**The eastern gap**: The eastern arc simply stops. The lines that should complete the circle end mid-curve on both sides, leaving an opening roughly a meter wide. The ochre lines near the gap are eaten-looking — not smudged, but *consumed*. The certainty of the lines fades as they approach the gap. Where they should meet, there is bare earth.

**Color**: Ochre and white ash on dark clay. Warm amber near the fires, cooler grey-white toward the center. The diagram catches light differently than the surrounding earth — it reads as deliberate, organized, alive.

## NPC PORTRAITS

### NAHUEL — RITUAL FOCUS (CENTER)
*24 years old. Lean, angular face. Mapuche. Kneeling at the exact center of the diagram.*

His arms hang at his sides — not relaxed, held. His spine is straight. His eyes are open, fixed on a point just above the horizon: not looking at anything, looking *through* everything. His breathing is shallow, measured, mechanical. He has been doing this for twenty-three days.

The dark circles under his eyes are extreme — not tired, *used*. His face is still in the specific way a face is still when the person behind it is doing something enormous and cannot afford to do anything else.

His hands, resting at his sides: the palms have geometric patterns traced in ochre — binding marks, anchor-shapes. They are smudged from being redrawn many times. The skin is dry and cracked from weeks of pigment work.

His shadow falls in the wrong direction from the nearest fire.

**Clothing**: Heavy wool poncho, worn through at the elbows. Riding clothes. Nothing decorative.

**Expression**: Not serene. Contained. There is a difference.

---

### KUYEN — WEST EDGE
*52 years old. Mapuche. Standing apart from the circle, west side of the bowl.*

Silver-grey hair worn in two braids. A face that is not hard but *still* — the stillness of someone who has been doing something difficult for a long time without stopping.

She is in motion even when standing: her attention moves constantly between the diagram, the fires, Nahuel, and the darkness at the east. Her hands are at waist height, lightly open — available. A small cloth pouch of pigment is at her belt.

Her clothing: heavy wool, riding pants, boots worn through at the toe. Practical. She dressed for work, not ceremony.

**Expression**: Controlled fear. She is not calm. She has suppressed panic so completely it has become something almost indistinguishable from calm, and the difference is visible only in the way she holds her hands — fingers spread, ready, not resting.

She does not look at the investigators unless she is speaking to them. Her eyes belong to the circle.

---

### THE TRIBE — CARDINAL AND DIAGONAL POSITIONS
*Seven people in total, arranged in the diagram.*

North, south, west: one person each, standing at their position within the outer ring. They are mid-thirties to mid-fifties, Tehuelche and Mapuche. Dressed practically — wool and leather, no ceremony. They stand with the particular stillness of people in sustained concentration: not rigid, not relaxed, *held*.

Their mouths move in the chant — a single repeated line, barely audible, felt more in the sternum than heard. They have been doing this for hours.

NW, NE, SW, SE: the diagonal positions, kneeling at the spiral motifs.

**The east position: empty.** No fire marker. No person. The gap in the ritual.

---

### CATA — EASTERN ANCHOR
*Late teens. Wrists bound to ground stakes at either side of the eastern gap. Kneeling on the ochre line.*

She is on her knees at the precise point where the diagram's eastern arc ends on each side. The rope from each wrist runs to a short stake driven into the clay. The ropes are not tight enough to cut. They are tight enough to hold.

She has been here for hours. The initial fear has exhausted itself. Her face is not expressionless — it has several things in it at once: exhaustion, a residual readiness to be afraid, and something that has come after a long time of waiting alone, which is a kind of stripped-down clarity. She is watching the investigators arrive with eyes that know whether people are coming toward her or not.

She is not the ritual's victim in the visual sense — no dramatic distress. She is ordinary. She is specific. She is a person who should not be here, kneeling in a diagram, looking at the people who just arrived.

**What to communicate visually**: She is in the diagram. The ochre lines run to her wrists. She closes the gap geometrically. She looks at the investigators and the investigators look at her and the decision is not made yet.

---

### THE INVESTIGATORS — CAMP EDGE
*Arrived from the west. 3 able-bodied, 1 gravely injured.*

The injured investigator is at the western bank's edge — held at the circle's perimeter, excluded. They can see everything. They cannot enter.

The three able-bodied investigators stand at the western bank, watching. They have arrived as the ritual enters its critical phase. They are dressed in gaucho travelling clothes, trail-dusty. One has visible signs of recent injury.

**What to communicate visually**: They are outside something. They can see what is happening. They can see Cata. The moment of decision — whether to intervene, whether to take her place, whether to let it proceed — belongs to them and has not yet been made.

---

## THE ENTITY — EL SABUESO

*Never fully described. The horror is the peripheral. What investigators notice:*

It exists where surfaces meet: at the angle between the creek wall and the floor, at the boundary between firelight and shadow, at the edges of the ritual diagram. It does not occupy open space. It occupies corners.

**Scale**: Wrong for the space. Larger than it should be for where it is.

**Appearance**: The outline of something very large in dim light — you can see where it ends but not what it is. Its shape is geometrically consistent in a way that living things are not: no organic asymmetry, no variation. The angles of its edges are exact.

**Effect on space**: Where it is, the geometry is wrong. Walls that should be parallel are not. Shadows fall incorrectly. The ground level reads as uncertain. It is not dark — it is a shape made of spatial incorrectness.

**Movement**: Not predatory. It moves toward complex geometry — toward the diagram, toward clustered people, toward fire-shadow intersections. It catalogs. The death it causes is incidental — a side effect of what it is, not what it wants.

**Sound**: A low, continuous vibration at the lower limit of hearing. Makes the eyes water.

## SCENE ILLUSTRATIONS

### SCENE 1 — ARRIVAL
*Wide shot. Dusk. The investigators stand at the top of the western bank looking down into the creek bed bowl.*

The full ritual is visible from above: four fires, the ochre diagram on the dark earth, seven figures in their positions, Nahuel at the center. The scale communicates both the size of the undertaking and its fragility — seven people, four fires, a drawing in the dirt, holding something back.

The investigators are silhouettes at the bank's edge. Below them, Kuyen is the only one who has looked up. She is watching them. Not afraid. Assessing.

At the eastern gap: a girl on her knees, wrists bound to stakes, kneeling on the ochre line. Her face is turned upward toward the arriving investigators. She is the first thing in the diagram that reads as wrong from this angle — not geometrically wrong, humanly wrong. Everything in the circle is deliberate and she is the most deliberate thing in it.

**What to communicate**: The diagram is almost complete. The girl is the completion. The investigators are looking at a decision that has already been made on their behalf, and they have not yet decided whether to accept it.

---

### SCENE 2 — NAHUEL ALONE
*Close. Night. Nahuel at the center of the diagram, kneeling.*

The ochre marks on the ground around him are intricate and precise. The four fires are visible in the background as low warm points of light. Everything else is dark.

His face in partial firelight: still, used, twenty-three days deep. The ochre marker-lines on his palms visible. His shadow falls wrong.

**What the image should communicate**: This person is holding something that should take eight. He has been doing it alone for too long. The stillness is not peace — it is the physical face of an enormous, sustained, solitary effort.

---

### SCENE 3 — KUYEN AT THE WESTERN EDGE
*Medium shot. Her back is three-quarters to us; she is facing the diagram.*

The full ritual visible behind her. Her hands at waist height, open. The pouch of pigment at her belt. The investigators are blurs at the top of the bank behind her, arriving.

**What the image should communicate**: She is the only reason any of this is still working. She knows that. It is not pride — it is a fact she has incorporated the way a carpenter incorporates the weight of the wood they're carrying.

---

### SCENE 4 — THE EASTERN GAP
*Ground-level. Looking east along the diagram, across the gap.*

The ochre lines run toward us and end. Bare earth. Then continue on the far side, beginning again. The space between the two ends of the arc is roughly a meter of bare clay.

The eastern bank of the creek is visible beyond the gap: dark, steep, ordinary-looking. But the geometry of the diagram reads the gap as an *opening* — the shape is organized around it, converging on it from both sides. The gap is the entire problem.

In the distance (or just at the gap's edge): the geometric wrongness of the Hound's presence, barely visible. Not a shape — an *incorrectness* in the angles of the bank.

**What's missing**: A person. A body. Someone standing on that line.

---

### SCENE 5 — ROUND 1 CHAOS
*Wide. The circle breaks. Everything happens at once.*

The fires go out simultaneously — not blown, extinguished. The darkness is sudden and total. Four of the seven tribe members sit down at once, mid-chant, and do not move again. Their positions are wrong — not collapsed, but *stopped*, like a word cut off mid-sound.

Nahuel is on his side on the diagram. Not unconscious — he is trying to get up and cannot. Kuyen is already moving, low and fast, redrawing pigment lines on the ground with her hands.

At the eastern gap: the Hound is there. Not arrived — *present*, as if it had always been at the angle between the bank wall and the ground. The ochre lines near it are losing their edges.

The investigators are at the perimeter. Decisions are about to be made.

---

### SCENE 6 — CLIMAX (THE EASTERN LINE)
*Close. One investigator standing at the eastern gap, on the line.*

They are standing where no one has stood. Their feet are on the ground at the exact point where the ochre lines end on both sides. The gap is a meter wide. They are the closure.

The Hound is present behind them — not attacking, pressing. The spatial wrongness radiates from where they stand. The investigator's posture: not heroic. Planted. Absorbing. Holding something back the way you hold a door against pressure from the other side — with your whole body, without drama, because there is no other option.

Kuyen's hands are moving at the far end of the diagram, finishing the last lines. Nahuel is conscious, gripping someone's hand, holding the center.

**What the image should communicate**: The gap is being held by a person. Not by geometry, not by knowledge — by presence and will. The shape that needed eight anchors now has a human being at its most dangerous point. The cost of this is visible. So is the fact that it is working.

---

### SCENE 7 — AFTERMATH
*Quiet. After.*

The four fires have relit simultaneously — full, bright, steady. The diagram is complete: all lines intact, all arcs closed, the eastern segment finally drawn through. The ochre glows warm amber for a few seconds and then fades to ordinary pigment.

The Hound is gone. Not fled — simply no longer occupying the space.

Kuyen is sitting on the earth inside the circle. Not collapsed — deliberately sat down. She is looking at the four people who are sitting in positions that are wrong. She does not speak. She will not speak for a long time.

The investigators are where they were. The injured one is at the bank's edge. The one who held the east is standing on the completed line.

**What the image should communicate**: The cost is visible in the four still figures. The working held. These two things are both true and cannot be separated.

## VISUAL THROUGHLINES FOR ALL ILLUSTRATIONS

**Color palette**: Ochre, ash-white, deep indigo-black, amber firelight. No blue-greens. The cold is temperature, not color — the sky is dark, not cold-lit.

**The eastern direction**: Always darker, always wrong. In every image that includes an east bank or east edge, something about the geometry there should read as slightly off — the angle of the bank against the sky, the way shadow falls, the empty fire position.

**The diagram**: Always visible when the creek bed is shown. It is the organizational center of every image. Its incomplete eastern arc is the visual focal point before R3; its completed arc is the visual resolution after.

**The tribe members**: Never individually heroic. Present, sustained, professional. They have been doing this for months. The four who die should be shown in positions of stopped action, not agony — they were mid-work when they stopped.

**Kuyen**: Never lit dramatically. Her light source is always practical — the nearest fire, the chalk-white of the ground. She is not framed as a figure of power. She is framed as a person doing something extremely difficult.

**The Hound**: Always peripheral. Never centered. Always in the angle between things — where the wall meets the floor, where firelight meets shadow. If an image centers the Hound directly, something is wrong with the composition.
