# Geometric Perception Cues — GM Trigger Sheet

A per-chapter index of what Marked PCs *see* and what the world *does* when geometry is active. Print double-sided. Read across the table for the chapter you are running.

This sheet exists because the campaign's central failure mode is over-explanation: NPCs translating geometry to players. The fix is **the Mark = perception** (see `overview.md` *Mark of Tindalos* and `flowmap.md*). This sheet is the operational tool for running that rule.

---

## How to use this sheet

1. **Marked PCs**: read their column privately. Describe what *they* perceive without explaining what it means. Let the player translate to the table — or not.
2. **Observable consequences**: describe to *everyone* in passing, without rolls. These are environmental beats. The Marked PC sees *why*; the unmarked PC sees *that*.
3. **Trigger threshold**: the standard 2d6 / double-1 trigger applies. If a Marked PC actively uses geometric perception in a scene ("I look hard at the diagram"), raise the threshold for that scene to **double-1 OR double-2**. This is the cost of leaning on the Mark.

## What Marked PCs *cannot* do with the Mark

This is the load-bearing list. The Mark is geometric perception, not omniscience.

- **Read text or untranslated language.** The Necronomicón is a book; the Mark sees the ink as ink. Mercedes still has to read.
- **Identify intent.** A Marked PC sees a diagram is *opening* or *closing*. They do not see *who* drew it or *why*.
- **See through walls in any general sense.** They see the geometry of *what they are looking at*, including geometry layered into surfaces — but not what is behind a closed door.
- **Detect lies.** El Patrón's geometry is the most pristine in the campaign and reads as *clean*. He has no interior wrongness that the Mark can sense.
- **Predict the future.** They see *what is now*. They can extrapolate ("if this leg of the route is drawn, the next leg will be such-and-such") because that is reading shape forward — but they do not see futures.

## Trigger raising — the cost in detail

Each scene in which a Marked PC *actively* uses geometric perception:

- **First active use**: trigger raised from "double 1" to "double 1 or double 2" for that scene. *(2 of 36 outcomes, ~5.5%)*
- **Second active use in the same scene**: raised to "double 1, double 2, or double 3." *(3 of 36, ~8.3%)*
- **Third active use**: "any double." *(6 of 36, ~16.7%)* — the entity is paying close attention.
- **Fourth active use**: roll a Hard Cthulhu Mythos check; on failure, the entity manifests *immediately* without a 2d6 roll, regardless of stress level. The Marked PC has been *located*.

This is a deliberately steep curve. Players who lean on the Mark heavily will pay for it. The Mark is the campaign's central tool *and* its central risk. Both are real.

---

## When to *not* use this sheet

- Combat. Skip these cues; combat is fast.
- Player-to-player dialogue scenes. The Mark should not be hovering over every conversation.
- Scenes where the players are off the geometry (Buenos Aires cafés outside the cordon, Inocencia's kitchen, the *galera* on the road). Let the world rest.
