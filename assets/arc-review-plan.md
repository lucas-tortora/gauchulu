## TWO STRUCTURAL BLOCKERS — CONCRETE FIXES

The positions table above reconstructs *who stands where*. It does not resolve two prerequisites that the closing silently depended on Kuyen for. Both must be answered before C7, or the closing has no in-world basis to run on.

### BLOCKER 1 — Where does the closing *procedure* come from?

**The gap:** Kuyen was "the only person who understood the full closing sequence." The reconstruction names Mercedes as director — but Mercedes' competence is *book*-knowledge (she read the Necronomicón), which is not self-evidently the same as the Antu-Rayo binding *rite*. Left unaddressed, a player can rightly ask: "If the one person who knew the ritual is dead, how are we performing it?"

**The fix — declare a canonical knowledge stack.** The closing is runnable, *as an improvisation*, from the sum of four sources the party can actually possess. State this to yourself before C7 and hold it:

| Source | What it supplies | Where acquired | Status at this table |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Saens's manuscript** | The *written procedure* — Saens learned the counter-rite from Antu-Rayo and recorded it. This is the campaign's only **written** copy of the closing method. Page 79: "I could not close it alone." | C4 study / C5 ruins | Available — the manuscript is recovered |
| **Mercedes** | The **interpreter/director**. Her dossier establishes she is "the only NPC who can both read the geometry *and* explain it" — that is canonical, not improvised. She translates Saens's procedure into spoken direction. | C7 arrival | Available |
| **Complete binding geometry** | The *shape* the procedure draws (see Blocker 2) | C2 clay / stone-fragment | **At risk — see Blocker 2** |
| **A Marked PC** | **Live correction.** Geometry perception is the moment-to-moment substitute for Kuyen's fluency — they feel when a panel is wrong before it fails. | C3 onward | Available (2 Marked PCs) |

**The ruling:** Kuyen's unique contribution was **fluency and speed**, not secret knowledge no document held. Saens wrote the rite down; Mercedes can read it; a Marked PC can correct it live. Losing Kuyen makes the closing **slower, harder, and more failure-prone** — which is exactly the increased difficulty the C8 notes already assume — but **not impossible.** Saens failed for lack of a *second person*, not lack of knowledge; the party is the second person he never had. Say this out loud to the table through Mercedes once, in C7: *"Saens wrote down how. He could not do it alone. That was his only mistake we are not about to repeat."*

### BLOCKER 2 — How does the party get the complete symbol past a hostile Rosa?

**The gap:** The complete binding symbol is now **single-sourced behind a hostile gatekeeper**. Canonically it was quadruply redundant (Kuyen's memory, Rosa's under-stone scratch, Dolores's buried clay, the 75% stone-fragment). At this table: Kuyen is dead, Rosa is hostile, and the clay is buried under the rubble *the PCs created* — and Rosa controls access. The redundancy has collapsed.

**The fix — a three-tier retrieval path.** Run them in order; each is a real fallback so the closing is never hard-blocked by a single failed roll or a Rosa who stays cold.

**Tier 1 — Repair Rosa (best outcome). Routed through the geography: the party passes San Ruiz twice.** The creek bed (C3) is east of San Ruiz, which is east of the estancia. The westward return leg runs **creek bed → San Ruiz → estancia** — so San Ruiz bookends Arc 2 as a natural pass-through, no contrivance needed. Run it as two beats (see the staged scene under C4 below):

- **First pass (C3→C4, riding back to confront Don Eusebio):** the cold encounter. They return the **stone-fragment** (the piece Dolores wanted carried onward) and tell her **La Llorona is loose and moving east** — Rosa held the chapel *for* Dolores; learning Dolores is unanchored moves her more than any apology. She does **not** give the symbol yet. They are, as far as she can tell, still riding to deal with the man who opened the wound. She opens the door a crack and names her condition.
- **Second pass (pre-C7, riding to the pit to close it):** the payoff. Now their intent is true and present-tense — *we are going to the pit to finish what Dolores started.* Rosa lifts her grinding stone and gives them the complete symbol scratched beneath it — **as a transaction, not forgiveness.** Her line: *"No lo hago por ustedes. Lo hago por la que dejaron suelta."* — *"I don't do this for you. I do it for the one you set loose."*

This is the clean path and the most thematically earned one — the PCs repair, in microcosm, the damage they caused, and San Ruiz frames the whole of Arc 2 with the same face. If the party only passes once (they commit to the closing early, before C4), collapse both beats into a single handover.

**Tier 2 — Reconstruct without Rosa (fallback).** If Rosa stays cold or the party never returns to San Ruiz: the **chapel stone-fragment gives 75% of the diagram** (established C2), and a **Marked PC who has seen the dual-diagram stone at the ruins (C5) can complete the remaining 25% by perception** — a Hard INT or Cthulhu Mythos roll, *one step easier for the Marked* per the Mark rules, or automatic if they actively "look hard" (raising the 2d6 Hound threshold for that scene, per the cost rule). Saens's manuscript diagrams cover gaps the fragment lacks. This yields a **complete but slightly imprecise** symbol.

**Tier 3 — Proceed on a partial symbol (degraded, but not a dead end).** If both above fail, the closing runs on an **incomplete eastern segment** — which is *already the established C8 threat* (the uncorrected eastern asymmetry). This unifies cleanly: **the improvised symbol's imperfection IS the eastern gap the Hound attacks.** The closing can still complete via a human anchor holding the east physically (a PC or Don Eusebio at the eastern line, pure-decision, the C3 mechanic reused) or a Mandinga Warding placed there. The cost is higher; the closing is uglier; nobody dies *only* because a clue was missed.

**Net effect:** the symbol is never an unrecoverable hard-block. Tier 1 is a genuine subplot reward (repair Rosa → clean symbol), Tier 2 makes the Marked PCs structurally load-bearing (which is their design purpose), and Tier 3 routes a total failure into the existing eastern-asymmetry mechanic rather than a campaign stall.

> **Keeper decision — RESOLVED for this table:** Tier 1 is the plan. The party passes San Ruiz on the C3→C4 return leg (cold beat — see the staged scene under C4) and again pre-C7 on the ride to the pit (symbol handover). Keep Tier 2 warm as backup: make sure at least one Marked PC stands at the dual-diagram stone in C5, so the 75% fragment + perception path exists if the party never circles back. Tier 3 (physical eastern anchor / Warding) remains the floor — pre-decide who takes the east.
