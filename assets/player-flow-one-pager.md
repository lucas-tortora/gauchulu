# LA TIERRA MALA — THE PLAYER FLOW AT A GLANCE

*One-page overview of the expected player journey across twelve chapters. Each beat: **what they think they're doing → what they actually do → what they leave the chapter with.** Use as a GM compass; do not show the players.*

---

## ARC 1 — LA TIERRA MALA (C1–C3) · *The false premise breaks*

**C1 — EL CONTRATO → EL POZO** *(arrival, hire, the pit)*
*Think:* a paid job — hunt cattle-killing tribes for Don Eusebio at Estancia La Esperanza.
*Do:* tour the south pasture; find the radial cattle bodies and the pit; survive La Luz Mala; protect 13-year-old **Concepción** as she scrapes the geometry apart with her hands; learn that *breaking the geometry stops the manifestation.*
*Leave with:* a branded hide and Tomás's stone; an unfinished letter from Don Eusebio addressed to *"A. Quirce, Calle Florida, BA"*; Don Eusebio gone east. **(No Marks issued yet.)**

**C2 — LAS SEÑALES** *(San Ruiz; the burned chapel; the Llorona)*
*Think:* follow Don Eusebio's trail; talk to whoever knows what happened here.
*Do:* ride to abandoned San Ruiz; meet **Rosa Quiroga** at the burned chapel; hear what the villagers tried; recognize the **dual diagram** (opening + closing) on the chapel walls; follow La Llorona's pointing to the bones of **Padre Saens** (1761).
*Leave with:* Rosa's silver counter-binding ornament; the knowledge that someone tried to *close* this before; a name (Saens) and a date (60 years ago); the suspicion that the tribes are not the enemy.

**C3 — EL RITUAL ROTO** *(tribal camp; the first closing; the Mark is dispensed)*
*Think:* maybe the tribes know how to stop this — or maybe they're doing it.
*Do:* reach **Kuyen's** camp; witness the counter-ritual; choose posture — *interfere* (Hound manifests, ritual collapses), *assist properly* (Mythos / Occult check; ritual holds, Hound held off), or *observe without interfering* (ritual partially succeeds, no Hound, no Marks); aid the partial closing of the pit.
*Leave with:* **the Mark — for any PC wounded by the Hound and surviving** (mechanical: 2d6, double-1 → Hound for 1d3 rounds; perceptive: read geometry as felt wrongness, –1 difficulty step on Mythos rolls about pattern in real space); a tribal binding diagram; clarity that the cattle-deaths story was a lie; an alliance with Kuyen, Nahuel, and the curanderas. *(A clean-observer table leaves with no Marks; most tables end with one or two Marked PCs.)*

---

## ARC 2 — LA SALAMANCA (C4–C6) · *The world widens*

**C4 — EL PATRÓN** *(return; confrontation with Don Eusebio; the manuscript)*
*Think:* confront Eusebio — he hired us under false pretenses.
*Do:* return to La Esperanza; corner Eusebio; force him into partial honesty; find the Jesuit manuscript and the ritual diagram he has been recopying; receive **Mercedes's first letter** from Buenos Aires.
*Leave with:* the manuscript; the name **Doña Mercedes Solís**; Quirce's announcement letter (Latin, *sub signo magno*); a sense the campaign is much larger than one estancia.

**C5 — EL MANUSCRITO** *(the Jesuit ruins; the bone chamber)*
*Think:* find what Saens wrote; understand the closing.
*Do:* descend into the Jesuit ruins; **El Pombero** warns of three things; pass the whispering tunnels; reach Saens's bone chamber — his self-sacrifice as the geometric center; **one PC reads page 79 aloud** (this is the seed of the **Reader path** in C12).
*Leave with:* page 79; the dual-diagram in full; the realization that Saens did not finish — they will have to.

**C6 — EL VISITANTE** *(Quirce's mediumship; first explicit Necronomicón)*
*Think:* meet the man Eusebio was writing to; learn what the manuscript is.
*Do:* sit through Quirce's mediumship session; hear two voices speak through him (El Patrón's and the Great Wizard's); first explicit naming of the **Necronomicón**; first hearing of the name **Dadálah**.
*Leave with:* the cult is real; there is a **book** under the city; someone named El Patrón is moving the chess pieces; head west to find Coypac, then back to the pit.

---

## ARC 3 — THE GREAT SLEEPER (C7–C9) · *The first closing pays its cost*

**C7 — LA VÍSPERA** *(pit camp; Mercedes arrives; the Cursed Gaucho)*
*Think:* prepare for the closing with everyone we've gathered.
*Do:* **Mercedes** arrives and embraces Kuyen (thirty years of friendship); Don Eusebio confesses the **nine weeks of his reasoning** and Concepción's role; **the Cursed Gaucho** rides in uninvited with a captured cult priest and asks for a place in the outer ring.
*Leave with:* a working coalition (Keepers, curanderas, Eusebio, Cursed Gaucho); the knowledge that the cult is attempting an **awakening at Coypac** simultaneously; the closing scheduled for the next dawn.

**C8 — LA TRAICIÓN** *(the closing; the Coypac vision; Eusebio's "No.")*
*Think:* close the pit; stop the awakening.
*Do:* hold the outer ring; the Mapudungun counter-chant binds the pit; the **Coypac vision** shows the cult's failed awakening; the entity offers Eusebio his life and his land; he says **"No."** and stays in the ring.
*Leave with:* the pit closing (tier A–D depending on losses); Eusebio dead or transformed; the **dust** still on the players (the campaign's running motif — it does not lift here); an urgent ride east to Buenos Aires.

**C9 — EL CIERRE** *(aftermath; ride to Buenos Aires)*
*Think:* recover; report to Mercedes's people in the city.
*Do:* bury or ritually disperse the dead; receive Saúl Carrasco's body at the *paraje*; the goat at the San Ruiz well lifts her head and shows six pupils to a Marked PC; ride east through abandoned country.
*Leave with:* survivors; a small caravan (Mercedes, Kuyen, possibly Nahuel/Héctor/Marta, the Cursed Gaucho parallel); the dust still present; a city ahead they have not seen before.

---

## ARC 4 — BUENOS AIRES & THE HEIST (C10–C12) · *The choice*

**C10 — LA LLEGADA** *(the city; the Keepers; Saráchaga dies)*
*Think:* deliver Mercedes; meet the Keepers; rest.
*Do:* enter Buenos Aires (the **eleven-by-nine grid** is itself a binding); meet **Saráchaga, Albarrán, Galíndez, Ana**; touch the **witness-stone** (warm, not hot); attend the meeting; find Saráchaga dead at dawn — converted in his last entry, killed before he could speak.
*Leave with:* Saráchaga's notebook (*"Mercedes tenía razón. Movámoslo."*); the witness-stones mechanic; the empty chair (Lacasa) named; the vote forced.

**C11 — LA DECISIÓN** *(investigation; Mercedes confesses; the heist plan)*
*Think:* find Lacasa; understand who killed Saráchaga; plan the move.
*Do:* track Lacasa's spurred-boot prints to his strongbox (Quirce's letter, the *E. Dadálah* deed); receive **Mercedes's confession** and the **folded parchment with two pages** (destroy-ritual + Necronomicón binding section); meet **El Patrón** in Inocencia's parlor; bargain with the **Cursed Gaucho** at the Riachuelo tannery.
*Leave with:* the parchment; the Reader-PC named (the one who read page 79 in C5); a plan — fifteen armed men, two decoy routes, a brig, a midnight transport down Calle Belgrano.

**C12 — EL LIBRO** *(the transport; the choice; the dust lifts)*
*Think:* move the book to the brig.
*Do:* ride the carriage; survive the ambushes; **the Cursed Gaucho buys fifteen minutes on Calle Belgrano**; reach the dock; **make the choice** at the table.
*Leave with:* one of three endings, paid for in named costs.

---

## THE THREE C12 CHOICES — THE PLAYERS' ALONE

| Ending | What the players do | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **HIDE** | Albarrán speaks *"Hic locus est. Hic manet."* on the dock; the chest goes onto the brig and to a new vault. The Keepers' three-century policy continues. | The Cursed Gaucho's stand. Carriage and decoy losses. Albarrán, exhausted. |
| **USE** | The **Reader-PC** unfolds Mercedes's parchment, reads the Latin sentence aloud as a binding act, names themselves the anchor. El Patrón is bound *outside* the vault. | Layered cost on the Reader (Sanity, years, Mark deepens). Mercedes pays in burned hands. |
| **DESTROY** | The Cursed Gaucho's *cuchillo* (or the substitute *facón*) drives through page 79 at the Reader's hand; the book is unmade. | The table chooses who pays — **Anchor / Pillar / Wall / Witness / Bystander** — publicly, around the table. Mercedes pays in ash. |

In all three endings the **dust lifts** at the moment of the binding (Rosa's chapel, Inocencia's tin, the Marked PCs' cuffs — silently, simultaneously). The campaign's longest-running motif resolves in a single image. **The pit closes. The dust does not — until now.**
