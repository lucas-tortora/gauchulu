## CHAPTER 4 — EL PATRÓN

**GOAL**: Confront the man who lied → break his denial → reveal the manuscript

> ⚠️ **[THIS TABLE]** No clean "return." The party fled C3 in pieces — run **THE RETURN** (creek bed, dawn, SAN 0/1D4) then **SAN RUIZ — ROSA, FIRST PASS** (cold; stone-fragment returned; La Llorona news delivered; **symbol withheld**) before they reach the gate. They hold only the **75% chapel stone-fragment**, not the complete binding symbol (earned on the 2nd San Ruiz pass, pre-C7). **Kuyen, Nahuel, the whole tribe, and Concepción are dead.** Two PCs are Marked (permanent geometry perception).

**SCENE FLOW**

1. **The Estancia, Changed**
   * Yellowed grass rings, cattle in single file, dogs that won't enter the south pasture
   * Tomás won't meet eyes; **Concepción dead** (died at the pit, C1 — "sent to Azul" does NOT run; Tomás only knows she vanished, Don Eusebio can't say it)
   * Don Eusebio's right hand: ink stain spreading from index finger (C1) → full palm now

2. **The Confrontation**
   * He interrogates *them* first: what they saw, what Rosa said
   * Psychology: he's deciding whether to double down or confess
   * Cover story: "inspecting northern fences" — lie

3. **Silvio Méndez Arrives**
   * Marked seven months; felt the pulse from Don Eusebio's pit
   * Forces Don Eusebio's hand by *being there*; neither man wants to speak first

4. **The Locked Study**
   * Light leaks under the door at night; low continuous reading aloud
   * Manuscript: Padre Saens, S.J., 1761–1763. Latin + Guaraní + Mapudungun marginalia
   * Diagrams = same shape as pit, hide, chapel
   * **SAN 0/1** (reading the manuscript — weight of recognition, not gore)

5. **First Wrong Returned**
   * Appear at the fence line at chapter end. Slow. Quiet. Watching.
   * Villagers from San Ruiz (Héctor 50s, Marta 40s — walked back from Tres Lagunas)
   * **SAN 0/1**

**KEY DIALOGUE**

* Silvio: *"Tienen el polvo. Como yo."* (the first naming as kinship)
* Don Eusebio: *"He works."* / **[ES]** *"Trabaja."* (about Silvio, flat, an admission)

**ATMOSPHERIC RUNNERS (3a/3b/3c)**

* Dust on the players' cuffs — Silvio recognizes it
* Low vibration in the locked study; intensifies when the manuscript opens
* Tarnished-copper runner → **C5 Scene 3**: Larrea camped at ruins; chapel-facing copper blackened (5b)

**END**: Wrong Returned at the fence; Silvio agrees to stay; Don Eusebio admits *some* of what he did

**GM NOTES**

* More hostile than C1 — he's been outmaneuvered
* Treat the manuscript as the chapter's *object*, not an info-dump
* If they never enter the study, leak it: light, sound, manuscript pages on the wind

## CHAPTER 5 — EL MANUSCRITO

**GOAL**: Translate the manuscript → visit the Jesuit ruins → find Saens's bone chamber → plant the Reader-path foreshadow

**SCENE FLOW**

1. **The Wrong Returned at the Estancia**
   * They walk in formation — geometric. They don't attack; they watch and place themselves on the diagram.
   * **SAN 0/1D4** sustained presence

2. **The Road to the Ruins**
   * Landscape changes: fewer birds, plants thinning, the air heavier
   * **Kuyen is dead (C3)** — no road meeting. Her warning (*move to the pit, days not weeks; the east fails first*) arrives instead through the **goat's pupils** / **Saens's late note** (*"El nudo del este siempre cede primero…"*) / **La Llorona's eastward pull** felt by a Marked PC

3. **The Jesuit Mission Ruins**
   * Foundation stones, broken bell tower, walls chest-high
   * Bell (1749) — older than the mission; brought from elsewhere
   * Visual handout: the dual-diagram SVG

4. **The Carved Stone & The Descent**
   * Spot Hidden / Cthulhu Mythos: the stone moves
   * Bone chamber below: Padre Saens's body laid as the geometric center (non-human skulls at the outer ring — "the first people")
   * **Bone Vigil** (niche): `bone-vigil-rite.svg` — closing-aspect rite; 1 honest question answered; phrase → Mercedes C8
   * He achieved a *partial* binding — bought ~6 decades by becoming the anchor
   * **SAN 1/1D6**

5. **READER-PATH FORESHADOW (2a)**
   * Page 79 can be read aloud. **The PC who reads it aloud is the Reader.** Note the player.
   * Don't tell them. Mercedes will recognize them in C8.

**MANUSCRIPT KEY FACTS**

* Names eight anchors, four directions, one warning: *the entity will let the ritual fail at peak pressure*
* Marginal note: *"…it is the same figure as the Necronomicón."* (Library Use, Regular)
* Pages 60–80: the binding section; pages 80+: the *opening* section (do not read)

**ATMOSPHERIC RUNNERS**: dust still on the players (Saens's bones have it too); vibration in the chamber, ladder rungs hum; candles bend toward the stone

**END**: Mercedes's **reply** arrives (her first letter was C4): *"I have read parts of the Necronomicón… the closing will work for this opening but not the broader question. There is more than one scar."* / **[ES]** *"He leído partes del Necronomicón… el cierre servirá para esta apertura pero no la cuestión más amplia. Hay más de una cicatriz."* — warns Quirce is honest about observations, liar about intentions

**GM NOTES**

* Partial translation still gives the eight anchors
* If they burn the manuscript: improvisation in C8–C9; survival becomes the best achievable outcome
* If a PC reads alone at night: dream of Padre Saens, 1/1D10 SAN, deepest Don Eusebio understanding

## CHAPTER 6 — EL VISITANTE

**GOAL**: Meet Quirce → expose his agenda (without losing him as ally) → force Don Eusebio to commit → name El Patrón

**SCENE FLOW**

1. **Quirce's Letter Arrives** (5c)
   * Elegant Spanish; Latin signature *sub signo magno* (occult-network closing). He arrives the next afternoon.

2. **Aldao Quirce — The Man**
   * Late 50s, soft hands, walks like a scholar. Three armed men with him.
   * Annotated manuscript copy. Brass markers (8). Spot Hidden: one is *asymmetric* — northern panel, off by 3°.
   * Cthulhu Mythos: this turns the ritual from a *closing* into a *binding for use*

3. **Quirce's Mediumship Moment** (3c — the vibration *stops* for ten seconds)
   * He pauses mid-sentence. Different voice. Says: *"Gracias. — Eligio Dadálah."*
   * Then the voice shifts again; the Great Wizard of Salamanca speaks one phrase. **SAN 1/1D6.**
   * This is **El Patrón named for the first time.**

4. **Forcing Don Eusebio's Cooperation**
   * Quirce uses the manuscript phrase: *"I invoke the anchor of the first opening."* — *mechanical*, not social; Don Eusebio cannot refuse.
   * If players intervene physically: three armed men, this is not a negotiation.

5. **The Asymmetry Is Named** (Kuyen is dead — runs differently)
   * A **Marked PC** (geometry perception) or **Marta** (tracing the diagram in the dark) catches the asymmetric panel Quirce "corrected":
   * *"Esta línea no la dibujé yo… La dibujó alguien que sabe el dibujo desde adentro. No fue Quirce."* — *"This line I did not draw… it was drawn by someone who knows the drawing from inside. It was not Quirce."*
   * The asymmetry is the **Great Wizard's** correction, channeled through Quirce — and is **never corrected**, carrying into C8 as an active threat.

**KEY DIALOGUE**

* Quirce: *"I came because no one else would. The manuscript is not a closing manual."* / **[ES]** *"Vine porque nadie más lo iba a hacer. El manuscrito no es un manual de cierre."*
* El Patrón (through Quirce): *"Gracias. — Eligio Dadálah."*

**ATMOSPHERIC RUNNERS**: ranch hands mention Quirce moving furniture in the night; Don Eusebio confirms the ten-second pause — *"Otra voz. No era la suya."*

**END**: The group assembles at the pit at dusk — investigators, Quirce, Don Eusebio, Silvio, Héctor + Marta. **No Kuyen, no Nahuel** — with no trained director, **Don Eusebio** sets the timeline himself: *"Tomorrow night. It's not waiting for us."*

**GM NOTES**

* If players kill Quirce in C6: betrayal vector shifts to Don Eusebio in C8 (entity offers abundance; Psychology to hold him)
* Reward catching the brass-marker asymmetry — it makes C8 cleaner
* The mediumship moment must land. Drop the dialogue cold. No setup.
