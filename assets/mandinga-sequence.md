# MANDINGA SEQUENCE
### *La Oferta — The Offer*
*Run during or immediately after C3. Triggered by near-death. Usable once per campaign.*

---

## TRIGGER

One PC is at or near 1 HP. The others are not far behind.

No special setup required. The trigger is proximity to death and proximity to the goat.

The PC at 1 HP has been looking at the goat. The goat has been looking back. At some point — during the post-ritual recovery, or in the last moments before losing consciousness, or simply in the particular exhausted state of a person who has seen too much tonight and is not fully managing it — the light in the creek bed changes. The scene doesn't announce itself. It does not say *you are now dreaming.* It just becomes a different kind of present.

**Whether this is real is a question the Keeper does not answer.** Not at the table, not afterward. The wound closes. The spell knowledge arrives. Those are facts. Everything else is interpretation.

---

## THE FIGURE

He is already there when they notice him. He was not approaching.

A gaucho. Well-dressed — better than well-dressed, the way a man is dressed when clothing is vocabulary. Everything black: the chirripá, the jacket, the boots, the wide-brimmed hat tilted at the precise angle that communicates exactly what it intends to communicate. The silver catches the fire from everywhere: the facón handle, the rastra belt with its discs, the spurs, the buttons, the thread of a boleadora coiled at his hip. The silver is not decorative. It is definitional. It is how you know what you are looking at.

He is petting the goat with one hand. The goat is leaning into it — the easy lean of an animal with its owner, not an animal being handled.

He does not look at the investigators immediately. He is looking at the land.

---

## THE LINE

*(Unhurried. Not threatening. The register of something that has been the most dangerous presence on the flat land for two centuries and is, for the first time, aware that it is not.)*

> *"La tierra está alterada. Lo que está entrando no tiene nombre en ninguna tradición — ni en las mías."*

*(He looks at the investigators.)*

> *"Hay que volver a lo que funcionó. Hay cosas que se están olvidando que no se pueden olvidar."*

*(He extends a chalice — silver, dark inside, does not catch the light.)*

> *"Beban. Cúrense. Y usen esa sangre para defender la pampa — en mi nombre."*

*(A beat.)*

> *"O no beban. Y llévenme algo más útil."*

He waits. He has all the time that exists.

---

## THE CHOICE — EVERY PC

Mandinga does not assess who is dying and who isn't. He offers the chalice to the table. Each investigator present chooses independently.

**The chalice — SALUD:**

Drink. The wound closes — not cleanly, not fully, but decisively. The body that was losing stops losing. HP restored to roughly one third of maximum (Keeper's call — the fiction is that the wound closes the way a wound closes when something other than medicine made that decision). If the PC was Dying, they stabilize immediately.

**SAN 1D5.** Immediate, on drinking. The mind registers what it just accepted — not the devil's face, not the blood in the cup, but the specific horror of understanding that the deal is real and that something has now counted them.

The debt itself is not visible tonight beyond the SAN hit. The PC wakes with it sitting in their chest the way a splinter sits under skin — present, not painful, a reminder that something is keeping count. They owe a role in an ending. They don't know the specifics yet. They will.

**The gesture — PODER:**

Refuse the chalice. Mandinga sets it down without comment, makes a brief gesture with his free hand, and understanding arrives whole — not a lesson, not speech, just knowledge that is there when you reach for it. One spell, from the five below. The PC chooses which.

The cost is visible immediately — whatever the spell requires (see below). No additional debt beyond what the spell costs. Mandinga is not generous; he is fair.

**If a PC takes both — drinks and reaches for the knowledge:**

He looks at them. Not angry.

> *"Una cosa o la otra. Acá no se negocia dos veces la misma noche."*

The chalice or the gesture. Not both.

**If a PC refuses everything:**

He sets the chalice down in the grass. Looks at the land.

> *"Como quiera."*

No threat. No consequence visible yet. The goat steps away from that PC and does not approach them again before the pit closing.

---

## THE FIVE SPELLS

When a PC chooses poder, Mandinga's gesture opens a sense of five distinct things available — like standing at a crossroads with five roads visible. The PC reaches for one. **One only.** The others close immediately. This is not a menu. It is a door that opens once.

Each PC who chooses poder picks one spell. No two PCs may take the same spell in the same night — Mandinga does not distribute identical instruments. First to choose locks that option for the others.

---

### MENGUA / SHRIVELLING
*ES: Mengua — EN: Shrivelling*
*Source: Call of Cthulhu 7e Keeper Rulebook, Spells chapter*
*Alt. names: Shriving, The Withering Blast of Death, The Black Words*

> *"Hay que poder devolver lo que le mandan."*

**What it does:** Offensive. The caster invests as many MP as desired. Opposed POW roll against the target — on success, the target's flesh blackens and withers, losing 1 HP per MP invested. Instantaneous.

**Cost per use:** Variable MP (caster's choice) + SAN equal to half the MP spent (minimum 1).

**Range:** Line of sight.

**Casting time:** Instantaneous.

**Keeper note:** The SAN scales with investment — spend 6 MP, lose 3 SAN. The caster decides how much they're willing to pay before the roll. Against entities with no normal HP (the Hound, Wrong Returned), use the HP damage as a disruption threshold for CON checks rather than tracking HP directly.

---

### MARCA DEL DUEÑO / WARDING
*ES: Marca del Dueño — EN: Warding*
*Source: Call of Cthulhu 7e Keeper Rulebook, Spells chapter*

> *"La pampa tiene dueño. Recuérdeselo."*

**What it does:** Draw a circle 1 meter in diameter — ochre, ash, blood, salt, chalk, whatever is at hand. Spend 5 POW permanently to consecrate it. Within that boundary, entities must win a POW contest (against the caster's POW at time of casting) to enter or act. The circle does not move. It protects the ground it was drawn on, not the people in it.

**Cost:** 5 POW permanent + SAN 1/1D6. What you are claiming — territory consecrated in Mandinga's name — costs both the will to do it and something in the mind that understood the land as neutral.

**Circle size:** 1 meter diameter.

**Time to cast:** One full uninterrupted minute of drawing.

**Keeper note:** Drawing it during a crisis requires an uninterrupted minute — which is why Kuyen is always drawing before things happen, not during. The 5 POW cost is permanent and significant; a PC with POW 50 who casts this twice has lost 10% of their magical essence forever. Use it once, at the right moment.

---

### ESPANTO / IMPLANT FEAR
*ES: Espanto — EN: Implant Fear*
*Source: Call of Cthulhu 7e Keeper Rulebook, Spells chapter*
*Alt. names: Finger of Doom, Eibon's Jinx, The Evil Eye*

> *"A veces alcanza con correrlos."*

**What it does:** The caster sees the target and points a finger while invoking the curse. The target suffers soul-chilling dread — loses 0/1D6 SAN immediately and feels compelled to retreat to wherever they would normally feel safe and secure. Breaks concentration, disrupts actions, forces withdrawal. Instantaneous.

**Cost:** 12 MP + SAN 1D6. The fear belongs to the target — but you had to reach into them to place it, and the reaching leaves a mark.

**Range:** Line of sight; caster must be able to see and point at target.

**Casting time:** Instantaneous.

**Keeper note:** 12 MP is steep — a PC with MP 10 cannot cast this at full strength without being nearly drained. Time it carefully. Against the Hound: it does not fear, but the compulsion disrupts its angle-navigation; it withdraws toward the nearest available geometry rather than pressing toward center. Against humans, this ends confrontations cleanly. Displacement not destruction — very Mandinga.

---

### VOZ DEL PATRÓN / DOMINATE
*ES: Voz del Patrón — EN: Dominate*
*Source: Call of Cthulhu 7e Keeper Rulebook, Spells chapter*
*Alt. names: Command of the Wizard, The Chant of Possession, Baleful Influence*

> *"Lo que yo doy, lo doy entero."*

**What it does:** Opposed POW roll against the target. On success: the target obeys the caster's commands without exception until the next combat round concludes. The command must be intelligible to the target and cannot contradict their basic nature. The spell can be recast immediately and repeatedly — each recast at the same cost — allowing sustained control across multiple rounds. Range: 10 yards (roughly 9 meters). One target at a time.

**Cost:** 1 MP + 1 SAN per cast. Cheap to use once; cumulative across recasts.

**Range:** 10 yards. Target must be able to understand the command.

**Casting time:** Instantaneous. Recastable.

**Keeper note:** The SAN cost is 1 flat — not a roll, a certainty. Each use of this spell costs exactly 1 SAN. Sustained control for five rounds costs 5 SAN. That's the real price. Don Eusebio, Quirce, the Great Wizard, Borghi — everyone standing between the party and the pit has a POW stat. This is the political spell, and it is cheap enough to be dangerous in exactly the right way.

---

### CUERO / FLESH WARD
*ES: Cuero — EN: Flesh Ward*
*Source: Call of Cthulhu 7e Keeper Rulebook, Spells chapter*
*Alt. names: Protect Thee from Mighty Blows, Armour of the Will, Blood Shield*

> *"La pampa endurece a los que se quedan."*

**What it does:** Each MP spent grants 1D6 points of armor against non-magical physical attacks. This armor absorbs damage as it's dealt — if the ward has 10 points remaining and a hit deals 6, the ward drops to 4 and the caster takes nothing. The spell lasts 24 hours or until the protection is fully depleted. Cannot be reinforced or recast until the existing protection is used up entirely.

**Cost:** Variable MP (caster's choice) + SAN 1D4 per casting — paid once when cast, not per hit absorbed.

**Range:** Self, or one chosen target within reach.

**Casting time:** 5 rounds.

**Keeper note:** The 5-round casting time means this is preparation, not reaction — cast it before the fight, not during. Each MP buys a roll of 1D6 armor; spending 3 MP gives roughly 10 points of protection on average. The SAN cost is 1D4 once per casting, not per activation. The gaucho who walks away from a fight that should have killed him — the PC who takes this is the one planning to still be alive when it matters.

---

## EXIT

He does not say goodbye.

He looks at the land one more time — the look of a landowner assessing damage that someone else caused and that he is going to have to account for. Then the goat steps out from under his hand and is already somewhere else, and Mandinga is simply no longer there.

The fires are lower. The other PCs are asleep, or looking the other way, or it was dark and the light does things.

**The PC who drank:** The wound is closed badly — not the smooth close of a healed cut, but the abrupt close of something that ran out of options. It looks like a scar that wasn't there before. The debt is sitting in their chest. They don't have words for it yet.

**The PC who took a spell:** They know something they didn't know. It is complete knowledge — not theoretical, not partial. They could use it right now if they needed to. They don't know where the knowledge came from. They remember the figure, and the goat, and the gesture. The rest feels like something they already knew.

**Either way:** No one else saw anything. The goat is in her usual spot. She has not moved.

## DOWNSTREAM NOTES

| What the PC chose | What carries forward |
|---|---|
| Salud (chalice) | **SAN 1D5 on drinking.** Debt: must be present at the pit closing and must not obstruct it. Wound re-opens progressively if they move away from that obligation. See TABLE-STATE — THE DEAL for enforcement mechanic. |
| Poder (spell) | No debt beyond the spell's own costs. But Mandinga noticed them. At La Salamanca (C5–C6), the geometry will feel familiar in a way they can't explain. |
| Refused everything | The goat does not approach them before the pit. At La Salamanca, they will feel watched in a specific directional way — from below, and slightly to the left. Mandinga has not forgotten that they declined. |

**Two PCs drink:** Two debts. Both must be at the closing. Mandinga needed one instrument and now has two. He has not revised his expectations downward.

**One drinks, one takes a spell:** Clean. The instrument and the weapon. This is the combination Mandinga would have designed if he were running the party himself.

**Both take spells:** He let them. He is not sentimental about the healing. If they survive on their own, the pampa is defended on their own terms. He respects that more than he would ever say.
