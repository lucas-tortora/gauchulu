# EL SABUESO — ENTITY REFERENCE
*Keeper use only. At-table reference for C3 and C7–C9.*

---

## WHAT THIS THING IS

The canonical Hounds of Tindalos dwell in the deep past and travel through sharp angles in space. What manifests at the ritual is of the same order — angle-geometrically oriented, alien, drawn to complex spatial patterns — but older and less directed. It does not hunt. It catalogs. Deaths it causes are incidental: the side effect of a large presence in a small space with insufficient geometry to contain it.

**It is not evil.** It does not want to hurt anyone. It moves through angles the way water moves through cracks — automatically, without purpose in the human sense. The ritual drew it because the ritual *is* geometry. A complex binding diagram on an otherwise featureless plain is the equivalent of a lit lamp in a dark room: it illuminates itself to whatever is looking from the outside.

**It cannot be killed.** It can be displaced — sealed out by completing the geometry it was drawn to.

## THE PAMPAS PROBLEM

The Argentine pampas is one of the most geometrically barren landscapes on earth. Flat horizon in every direction. Grass. Sky. No architecture, no cliff faces, no irregular terrain. The Hound's standard entry mechanism — materializing through a sharp corner where two walls meet — has almost nothing to work with here.

This is not a defense. It is a delay.

**What the pampas offers the Hound:**

### Kinetic angles — moving bodies
A running human body leans forward at fifteen to twenty degrees from vertical. At the moment a running person's foot crosses a flat surface, the angle between their body, their stride, and the ground is acute — sometimes as sharp as 15°. Sharper than most wall corners.

The Hound can enter through this. A person running across a ritual line does not leave an empty gap. They create a moving angle at the moment of crossing, and the angle is enough.

**This is how the Hound enters if Cata escapes.** Her body closed the eastern arc while she knelt on it. When she runs through the ochre line, the geometry of her sprint across it is the entry point. The mist appears at her footprints. The Hound enters through the angle her body made with the ground in the moment of crossing.

She runs east. The entry point is behind her. The Hound is not pursuing her — it was drawn to the diagram, not to her. But the mist was at the base of the eastern bank. She ran through it on her way to the bank.

**Implication for the pampas generally**: Anyone running from the Hound across flat open ground is manufacturing angles with every stride. The flat pampas is not safe. It is just geometrically neutral until something moves fast across it.

### Shadow angles
The pampas is so flat that at dusk and dawn, the sun skims the surface at angles approaching zero. Any vertical object — a blade of grass, a fence post, a person, a horse — casts a shadow of enormous length. A rider at dusk casts a shadow thirty meters long. The shadow meets the ground at a shallow, acute angle: sometimes 15°, sometimes less. The Hound can use these. It travels along shadow-earth interfaces, riding the geometry where the shadow's edge becomes a surface.

At dusk — when the ritual runs — the pampas is covered in overlapping shadow-angles from every grass stem, every fire, every standing person. The geometry briefly multiplies. This is not accidental. Kuyen knows this. The ritual is timed for dusk partly because she can see the shadow geometry and read the Hound's approach vectors. Full darkness removes the light-cast angles. Full daylight makes them too short and shallow to enter through. Dusk is the window.

**What investigators notice first**: A single shadow on the flat ground that has no source. The sun is at a specific angle; every shadow in the space is falling a specific direction. One shadow is falling wrong — not opposite the sun, but angled 30° off. It is sharp-edged where all the others are soft. It lies on the earth as if something large is standing just outside the visible, and its shadow is the only part that has arrived yet.

### Light shimmer — the Zonda contribution
The Zonda is a hot dry wind from the northwest. At surface level, it creates a thermal shimmer above the grass — a permanent false mirage, a visual layer where the air itself is geometrically impure. Surfaces that should be flat appear to curve slightly. The horizon line wavers.

The shimmer creates apparent angles where no real surfaces meet: the visual impression of edges, of intersections, of planes meeting. These are not physically real, but the Hound does not need physical angles. It needs geometric relationships. The shimmer is enough.

The Zonda arriving early at San Ruiz was partly the Hound pressing from below — its proximity distorts the thermal geometry of the air above the flat land, which generates the shimmer, which generates the wind disturbance. The storm and the entity are feeding each other.

**What investigators notice**: The shimmer above the eastern grass is denser than elsewhere, as if the air is hotter there. It has the quality of a heat mirage seen through the wrong end of a telescope — too clear, too organized. The grass beneath it lies flat in a pattern that doesn't match the wind direction.

### The ritual diagram as an entry point
In the absence of architecture, a large, geometrically precise ritual diagram drawn on flat earth is extraordinarily visible to the Hound. It is the sharpest, most complex angle-structure for kilometers in any direction.

The diagram's eight outer anchor markers create corners. The four diagonal spirals create curves that terminate at angular intersections. The radial lines create sectors with vertex angles. The four fires create light-geometry: shadow cones extending outward from each fire, intersecting with the diagram's lines, forming dozens of secondary angles at the diagram's edge.

**The eastern gap** is the entry point not because it is empty but because it is the sharpest geometric feature in the diagram: two arc-ends that almost meet, creating the sharpest angle in the entire structure — an angle that approaches zero at its apex. A near-convergence is sharper, for the Hound's purposes, than a clean wall-corner. The gap draws it specifically.

## ENTRY SEQUENCE — AT THE RITUAL

*Run this as atmosphere across R1. Do not describe the Hound directly. Describe what changes.*

### Pre-entry (before R1 — while the working intensifies)

**Spot Hidden (Easy)** — an investigator watching the eastern grass notices the shimmer is moving. Not shifting with the wind. *Moving toward the creek bed*, slowly, like something very large walking in heavy fog. The shimmer has a leading edge.

**Natural World (Regular)** — the specific quality of the air changes east of the diagram. Not colder — *pressured*. The same quality as air in a room before a large animal enters it. The nearby horses (if any) go very still.

**The shadows from the eastern fire** — which should extend westward, into the diagram — have shifted. The shadow of the fire's post is pointing *east*, against the light source. This is not possible. It is happening.

### Entry — the mist

Just inside the eastern gap, at ground level, a dense mist appears.

It does not drift or blow. It is *placed* — an egg-shaped volume, roughly three meters by three meters by four meters, lying with its long axis along the eastern arc. It appears instantaneously, the way a word appears in print. It is wet and cold in a way that has nothing to do with the ambient temperature. It smells of something very old and mineral — the same note as the pit.

The ochre lines at the edge of the diagram, where the eastern arc ends on each side, *eat inward* from their terminations. Not erased — consumed. The line simply loses certainty, retreating from the mist as if the lines themselves are retreating from what they know is coming.

**This is the Create Mist of R'lyeh, deployed as entry mechanism.** The mist precedes the Hound the way smoke precedes a fire coming through a wall.

### Emergence

Out of the angle where the mist meets the earth — not out of the mist itself, but from the geometric line where the mist's base meets the ground surface, the sharpest real angle in the space — **the Hound arrives**.

*Describe this as the chapter-03 text instructs: peripheral. Not centered. The angle between the mist and the earth. The outline of something large in dim light. The scale wrong for the space.*

It does not step through. It is simply present, the way a shadow is present when a cloud passes in front of the sun — not arrived, always there, newly visible.

**SAN: 1/1D6 — first full manifestation.**

## HOW IT MOVES

**It does not occupy open space.** It moves along edges: where the mist meets the ground, where firelight meets shadow, where the ochre diagram meets the surrounding earth. When it moves, the geometry around it changes — surfaces that should be parallel read as converging. The ground level seems uncertain. Walls that should be vertical appear to lean.

**It moves toward complexity.** The ritual diagram is the most complex geometry in the space — it will move toward it. Clustered investigators create intersecting shadow-geometries — it will drift toward them. The four fires create light-angle intersections — it will navigate along these.

**It does not charge.** It flows. The difference between its R1 position (eastern gap) and its R3 position (pressing toward center) is not movement in the human sense — it is a gradual increase in presence, the way fog fills a room.

**Fire disrupts it.** The geometry fire creates — the precise cone of shadow behind an object, the sharp light-to-shadow terminator edge — is somehow inimical. A burning brand thrown toward its position forces it to retreat from that surface temporarily. It is not harmed. The geometry changes, and it cannot use that angle for one round. This buys a meter and thirty seconds.

**The goat** can strike it directly. Her headbutt disrupts its form — the outline staggers, the spatial incorrectness around it distorts briefly before restabilizing. One disruption available, on the goat's own timing.

## THE ICHOR

When the Hound makes physical contact with a living body, a bluish pus-like substance is left on the wound site. This ichor is alive and active.

**Each round it remains on the body: 2D6 damage.**

Removal:
- Wipe off with cloth: DEX roll, takes one action
- Rinse with water: removes automatically, takes one action
- Fire: kills the ichor but deals 1D6 burn damage to the target in the process

**The ichor does not feel like anything while it is happening.** No pain, no sensation. Investigators watching someone take ichor damage see the skin appearing to *resolve* — the underlying tissue becoming briefly, incomprehensibly visible through what should be intact skin. The victim will notice when they look down. They will not feel it until after.

---

## THE TONGUE ATTACK

Once per round the Hound can extend its tongue to form a deep, penetrating, bloodless hole in a target. No physical damage. The wound is painless and does not bleed.

**The victim permanently loses 3D6 POW.**

The wound is real — it closes over the next few hours, leaving a circular scar with no depth, as if the flesh was never anything but flat there. A Medicine roll (Hard) establishes that the tissue inside has been **reorganized** rather than destroyed. It is not missing. It has been *sorted*, the way a filing cabinet is sorted. The material is all there. Something has been done to its arrangement.

Investigators who have seen the entity cataloging behavior will understand what this means. It was not attacking. It was *classifying*.

## SPELLS

The Hound knows at least 1D8 spells. One is confirmed:

### Create Mist of R'lyeh
**Cost**: 2 magic points  
**Casting time**: Instantaneous

Creates a dense mist in an egg-shaped volume (10 × 10 × 15 feet). The long axis is always at right angles to the direction the caster faces. Obscures vision for 1D6+4 rounds, then evaporates without trace.

**At the ritual**: Used as entry mechanism — the mist appears at the eastern gap the moment before the Hound fully manifests. It is not cast as a tactical obscurement; it is part of the geometry of arrival. The mist fills the gap-space, providing the interior angles the Hound needs for full materialization in the flat pampas.

**Tactical use in later chapters (C7–C9)**: At the pit, the Hound has architectural geometry available — the pit walls, the carved arcs, the chalk markers in the outer ring. It may use the mist offensively there, obscuring Kuyen's symbol-work or covering a specific investigator.

## WHAT DRIVES IT OFF

| Method | Effect |
|---|---|
| Fire thrown at its position | Retreats 1m for 1 round — the geometry of the burning brand changes the angle. Not harm. Displacement. |
| Completing the binding symbol | Forces full displacement — the geometry is sealed, the angles it was using close. Permanent solution for the night. |

| Shouting, fire toward sky, noise | No effect. It does not perceive threats the way a living thing does. |

## READING IT AT THE TABLE

**Round-by-round descriptions should be short.** The Hound is never the subject of a paragraph. It is the subject of one sentence, delivered mid-description of something else.

*"The stone moved."*  
*"The shadow is wrong."*  
*"There is a sound at the lower limit of hearing."*  
*"The ground between Kuyen and the east is not level anymore."*

**Never describe it looking at anyone.** It does not have eyes in the human sense. It has attention, which is different. When it attends to someone, that person's peripheral vision fills with edge-geometry — not the Hound, but the spatial incorrectness its presence creates.

**The Marked PC's read** (if there is one by C7–C9): they see it geometrically — not as a body but as a set of angular relationships moving through the space, like watching a proof assemble itself. They know its position with precision before anyone else does. This is not comfort. A proof assembling itself toward you is worse.

## CAMPAIGN NOTES

This entity is not the canonical Hound of Tindalos exactly. It shares the angle-geometry entry mechanic and the curved-vs-angular taxonomy, but it existed at this location before the Hounds as described in the literature. It was here before Dolores, before Saens, before Antu-Rayo's people. The pit is not where it came from — the pit is where Don Eusebio made a hole in the surface of the place it already occupied.

It is not pursuing anyone. It is not hunting. It was resident and the ritual drew its attention. This is, if anything, worse — there is no personal grievance that can be appealed to, no deal to be made (unlike Mandinga), no mistake that can be corrected. It will simply be present here, increasing in presence, until the geometry seals.

**The closing at the pit** does not kill it. It removes the open angle — the hole Don Eusebio made. The entity remains, on the other side of the sealed geometry, the same way it was before. The land will scar over. Decades.

*"It still exists, but it cannot enter. Like sealing a room — it still exists, but it cannot enter. We are trying to seal the room."* — Kuyen, C3.
