# CAMPAIGN REVIEW — *La Tierra Mala*
### Plot Holes, Timing Fixes, Unclear Clues, Alternative Paths, and Missing Hooks

This document is a Keeper's audit of the nine-session campaign. It consolidates the fixes already applied to the session files and notes recommended adjustments for problems likely to emerge at the table. Sections marked **[FIXED]** have already been written into the relevant session file. Sections marked **[RECOMMENDATION]** are suggested adjustments the Keeper should make or improvise.

---

## 0. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY — What Changed

Three structural problems surfaced after running Session 1:

1. **Players did not recognize "break the geometry" as the solution** to the Luz Mala / reanimated cattle encounter. The scene was written assuming investigators would intuit the connection between the blood arcs, the body arrangement, and the hostile entities. They did not. The encounter stalled.

2. **Don Eusebio had no narrative reason to be absent between S1 and S4.** He was simply "at the estancia, doing nothing" — which creates a passive NPC and no forward pressure toward San Ruiz.

3. **The push toward San Ruiz in S2 was soft** — it relied on tracks pointing east, but no in-fiction character was *telling* the investigators to go east.

All three are now resolved by a single scene change at the end of S1:

- **Concepción enters the pit and starts breaking the geometry herself.** She is a thirteen-year-old orphan servant with a partial alignment to the entity. She knows, at a wordless level, what must be done. Her action triggers the cattle rising and the swarm condensing — they target *her*, not the investigators — turning the encounter from a puzzle into a rescue. The players now *watch* the solution happen, and protect Concepción while she finishes it. **Tomás follows her screaming, cannot enter the pit himself, and shouts the connection out loud once he sees the first cow drop** (*"¡Está borrando las marcas y las vacas se caen! ¡SIGAN BORRANDO!"*). This is the teaching moment.

- **Don Eusebio flees east on horseback during the pasture encounter,** leaving an unfinished letter to "Don A. Quirce" on his desk and telling no one where he is going. Tomás names the direction: east, toward an abandoned town called San Ruiz. His absence becomes the primary hook into S2. (He will return by S4, claiming he was "inspecting northern fences." This is a lie; he went to the ridge above the tribal camp to check on Kuyen's counter-ritual.)

These changes also set up a richer payoff in S7, when Don Eusebio confesses that the investigators — not he — are the reason Concepción lived. Fixed in all affected session files.

## 1. TIMING AND CONTINUITY FIXES

### 1.1 The "14 months vs 8 months" problem **[FIXED in S2]**

**The issue**: S2 establishes that San Ruiz was evacuated "approximately 14 months ago." S1, S4, and S5 establish that Don Eusebio performed the opening ritual "eight months ago." Why were people fleeing a pit that had not yet been opened?

**The fix**: The pit has always been there. The Jesuits (Padre Saens) sealed it in 1763, but the seal has been degrading for over a century. Rosa's community lived on top of a weakening seal and suffered gradually worsening manifestations (sleepwalking, livestock deaths, the Luz Mala at a low level) for years before the 14-month evacuation. Don Eusebio's ritual in month -8 did not *open* the pit — it widened a wound that had been opening on its own. This is now explicit in S2 as a Keeper note and can be conveyed by Rosa if investigators press her on the timeline.

**Implication for other sessions**: The "eight months" refers specifically to when the crisis became acute — when cattle started dying visibly, when the Luz Mala became a nightly presence, when people started walking south in their sleep. It is the point at which Don Eusebio's interference *amplified* everything to a crisis.

### 1.2 Concepción's presence in S1 vs her backstory in S7 **[FIXED in S1 and S7]**

**The issue**: S7's confession described Concepción's sleepwalking as past history, with Don Eusebio having "sent her to Azul the next morning" after catching her. But the original S1 only had her as a peripheral servant, and the investigators would have no reason to care.

**The fix**: S1 now centers Concepción as the person who breaks the geometry at the pit. She is no longer a background servant — she is the session's pivotal NPC. S4 correctly establishes that Don Eusebio has sent her to Azul (this was already consistent). S7's confession has been rewritten so Don Eusebio admits he rode east *to avoid watching her either die or save everyone*, not out of any active protection. He sent her to Azul only after the investigators had already saved her. **The honesty is the redemption; the action was cowardice.**

This also enables a Keeper adjustment in S7: if the investigators personally risked themselves to save Concepción at the pit, Don Eusebio should make eye contact with that investigator during the confession. The emotional weight of this moment scales with their investment.

### 1.3 Don Eusebio's state on return **[RECOMMENDATION for S4]**

**The issue**: S4 opens with Don Eusebio visibly changed — gaunt, haunted, spending nights in his study with a blanket over the window. The original version implied this was the result of two weeks of normal deterioration. But he has now been on the road for several days in the new continuity, and has returned *knowing the investigators have seen the pit activate and seen Concepción in it*.

**Recommendation for Keepers**: Play Don Eusebio's S4 behavior as that of a man who has been outmaneuvered and is trying to decide whether to double down or confess. He is more hostile to the investigators initially — he interrogates *them* about what they saw, what they heard from Rosa, what they know — before Silvio arrives and forces his hand. If an investigator asks him directly where he went for three days, his cover story is *"inspecting the northern fence lines with the capataz of the next estancia over."* A **Psychology** check reveals this is a lie with no more detail. Pressing further has him shut down. The truth — the ridge above the tribal camp — does not come out until S7.

## 2. SESSION-BY-SESSION REVIEW

### SESSION 1 — El Contrato

**Primary fix applied**: Concepción's intervention at the pit; Don Eusebio's flight east. See §0 above.

**Additional issues identified**:

- **The "INT (Hard) realization" that the whole pasture is a single diagram was the only linkage between geometry and defeat of the entities.** This is now redundant with Concepción showing the answer physically, but keep it — for investigators who figured it out ahead of Concepción, the insight should still be rewarded. If they figured it out and were already breaking the geometry when Concepción arrives, she joins them in the pit and doubles the work rate.
- **Tomás's shame is now a new through-line.** He did not enter the pit. He will avoid making eye contact with the investigators in S2 (when they return for horses or supplies) and in S4 (when the situation has deteriorated). An investigator who notices this and offers him absolution gains him as a fiercer ally in S6–S8. In S8, if Tomás has been treated kindly, he can act as a fourth melee defender against Quirce or the Wrong Returned. If he has been ignored or shamed, he stays at the estancia and locks the gate.

**Alternative paths**:

- **If the players attack the Luz Mala directly with firearms**: The light is incorporeal. Bullets pass through. The light briefly destabilizes (flickers) for one round but resumes. This is fine — let them try. It demonstrates that physical force is not the answer.
- **If the players flee the pasture entirely before the Awakening triggers**: Tomás comes running after them to say Concepción has gone missing from the estancia. They will find her at the pit. The Awakening triggers whether they want it to or not. This removes the "players refuse to engage" failure mode.
- **If the players kill Tomás or Concepción during the encounter** (accidental shots, aggressive restraint): Concepción's death causes the entity to enter a resonance mode — the pit will require twice the ritual effort to close in S8–S9. Tomás's death removes a potential ally and shifts Don Eusebio's behavior in S4 (he will be significantly more hostile and will not confess in S7). This is a hard consequence but not a campaign-killer.

### SESSION 2 — Las Señales → La Capilla

**Primary fixes applied**: Reframed the opening situation to make Don Eusebio's eastward flight the primary hook. Added alternate path for investigators who want to follow Don Eusebio directly (they glimpse him at the ridge spying on the tribal camp — this previews S3 and deepens the "he is not what he claims to be" theme). Added fallback hook (rural-police runner reports lights over San Ruiz bell tower) if players refuse to leave the estancia. Fixed 14-month timeline.

**Additional issues identified**:

- **Rosa's willingness to talk** is not adequately motivated in the original text. She is willing to talk because she is old, she believes someone needs to know, and the investigators — unlike the last people who came through — are carrying the pit's geometry with them (she can feel it on them). **[RECOMMENDATION]**: Have Rosa look at the investigators when they first sit down and say *"You've been there. I can smell it on you. You carry the mark of the dust."* This establishes her as someone with real sight and explains why she trusts them.
- **What if investigators are hostile to Rosa?** They might read her as a suspect — she is the only living person in a "cursed" village. This is a real risk. **[RECOMMENDATION]**: Rosa does not defend herself. She will take accusations calmly and answer every question truthfully, including *"Are you doing this?"* (*"No. But I know who is. You do too, though you do not want to."*). Her transparency disarms most hostile approaches. If players threaten her physically, she turns her back on them and continues grinding corn. Investigators willing to shoot a 71-year-old woman in the back are roleplaying themselves into an early failure state, not a failure of scene design.
- **La Llorona encounter — what if investigators refuse to engage?** The chapel scene assumes the investigators will enter the chapel and encounter the ghost. If they refuse (some groups will), Rosa can describe the manifestation and show them Elena's journal, which delivers the same information. Do not punish players for caution; give them the content through an alternate channel.

**Alternative paths**:

- **If investigators read the chapel symbols with occult knowledge before the ghost manifests**: Cthulhu Mythos or Occult success reveals these are counter-symbols — the binding shape. This gives them an early clue toward the S3 counter-ritual and makes them more receptive to Kuyen's work. Reward them.
- **If the Llorona damages an investigator beyond recovery**: Rosa can intervene with the small silver ornament at her throat (the four-line counter-glyph). She will give it up freely. She knows she does not have long anyway.

### SESSION 3 — El Ritual Roto → El Sabueso

**Issues identified**:

- **Kuyen's initial hostility is appropriate but may prevent players from ever hearing her reasoning.** The window between investigators arriving and Nahuel's critical ritual moment is short. If Kuyen refuses to speak and the investigators are proud, they may leave and miss the entire session. **[RECOMMENDATION]**: Nahuel himself — not Kuyen — should initiate contact with the investigators if Kuyen is stonewalling. He is nineteen, exhausted, and knows he is the one holding the pattern. He will approach an investigator at the fringe of the camp and say, quietly, *"She thinks we should not speak to you. I think if no one speaks to you, this will end tonight for nothing. So I will speak. Ask."* This gives investigators an entry point that does not require them to soften Kuyen first.
- **The "interference" list is clear, but "do nothing" is also an interference vector.** If investigators stay at the edge of the ritual and do nothing, the ritual should proceed and *partially* succeed — reducing the entity's reach but not stopping the crisis. Do not punish passive play. The partial success gives Kuyen's people a victory that is later undone by Don Eusebio's panic re-opening things in S4–S6.
- **What if an investigator attempts to join the ritual properly?** They cannot sustain it — they are not Marked — but a Cthulhu Mythos or Occult success allows them to *add* to the outer ring for one round without collapsing it. If they do this, Nahuel survives the ritual intact (because the load was shared). This is a significant mechanical reward for roleplaying.

**Alternative paths**:

- **If investigators attempt to kidnap Nahuel to prevent him from being consumed**: Kuyen will physically fight them. Nahuel, interestingly, will not — he has made his peace. This is a good dramatic moment but it will cause the ritual to fail catastrophically. The Hound arrives. Investigators who "saved" Nahuel have now caused the deaths of everyone else in the camp. This is a hard consequence; communicate clearly that Nahuel's participation is consensual before they commit to kidnapping.
- **If all investigators flee before the Hound manifests**: The ritual partially succeeds (see above) but no one is Marked. This removes the Mark mechanic from later sessions — a significant consequence — but it is a valid path and the campaign continues. Silvio's Mark in S4 becomes the only surviving example, and investigators learn about the Mark from him rather than from their own bodies.

### SESSION 4 — El Patrón

**Issues identified**:

- **The investigators' return to the estancia needs a timing cue.** The text does not specify how long has passed. **[RECOMMENDATION]**: Roughly 10–14 days since S1. Silvio has been traveling from his estancia (200km away). Don Eusebio has had time to destabilize further. This gives the Wrong Returned time to begin appearing at the estancia's margins.
- **The locked study scene** assumes investigators will try to enter. What if they don't? **[RECOMMENDATION]**: Have the study "leak" — light comes out from under the door at night, a low continuous sound of a man reading aloud in a language the investigators almost but do not quite recognize. This is a lure. Investigators who still ignore it can be hit with a Wrong Returned appearing at the kitchen door while Don Eusebio is in the study; the connection between the study (the manuscript) and the Wrong (the ritual's feedback) becomes undeniable.
- **Silvio's arrival needs clearer in-fiction justification.** Why does he come *now*? **[RECOMMENDATION]**: Silvio has been Marked for seven months (his brother died during his half-complete personal ritual). He felt the pulse from Don Eusebio's pit — it registered through his Mark — and deduced that someone nearby has been doing what he was trying to do. He came to confirm, and to warn. He did not know Don Eusebio was the one until he saw the estancia.

**Alternative paths**:

- **If investigators drive Silvio away before he can speak**: He will leave, return three days later with a letter he wrote explaining what he knows, and slip it under a door. S5 can still proceed from the letter. He will not appear again until S8, where he arrives with Quirce's party but stays at the back.
- **If investigators kill Don Eusebio in S4**: The manuscript is still in the study. Concepción's connection to the pit remains. The ritual can still be closed, but with reduced information — Don Eusebio's specific knowledge of what he did is lost, making the final ritual in S8–S9 riskier. Investigators must rely entirely on Kuyen's chants and Silvio's notes. This is a viable but harder path.

### SESSION 5 — El Manuscrito

**Issues identified**:

- **The translation/reading of the manuscript is a long information-dump scene.** It is in Latin and Guaraní with Mapudungun marginalia. If no investigator has the languages, the scene stalls. **[RECOMMENDATION]**: Silvio can translate the Latin portions (he had Jesuit schooling). Kuyen — if investigators have earned her trust — can be brought in to read the Mapudungun. The Guaraní is harder; an investigator with Library Use can deduce meaning from cognates in about a day. Build in failure tolerance: partial reading still yields critical information (the need for eight anchors, the names of the four "directions," the warning about the entity's capacity to let the ritual fail at peak pressure).
- **The Wrong Returned encounter at the Jesuit ruins** assumes investigators will visit the ruins. If they focus entirely on the manuscript and do not leave the estancia, the ruins scene can be relocated — the Wrong Returned come to them instead, at the estancia's perimeter, in a slow-moving group at dusk. Same information delivery, same horror.

**Alternative paths**:

- **If investigators burn the manuscript before finishing their reading**: The pit remains, but the closing knowledge is gone. The campaign pivots to a desperate improvisation in S8–S9 based on whatever Rosa, Kuyen, and Silvio can jointly assemble. Survival becomes the highest achievable outcome, with lasting environmental damage.
- **If an investigator reads the manuscript alone at night without guidance**: SAN check with higher stakes (1/1D10). Reward the reading with a dream about Padre Saens, which conveys the emotional weight of the opening and the regret the priest felt. This is the deepest route to understanding Don Eusebio's arc and can unlock additional roleplaying options in S6 and S7.

### SESSION 6 — El Visitante

**Issues identified**:

- **Aldao Quirce's arrival is sudden** if the investigators never encountered the unfinished letter on Don Eusebio's desk in S1. **[FIXED in S1]**: The letter is now explicitly placed on the desk for players to find (*"Urgent. Come quickly. It did not hold. —E."*). If they found it, Quirce's name is already familiar. If they didn't, Tomás or Silvio can mention the letter ("The patrón wrote to a man in Buenos Aires weeks ago. I saw it.").
- **Forcing Don Eusebio's cooperation** — the text assumes Quirce will coerce him into returning to the pit. But what if Don Eusebio's investigators step in to defend him? **[RECOMMENDATION]**: Quirce does not come alone. He has three men (one a former soldier, two hired in Azul). He will not let the investigators physically prevent him from addressing Don Eusebio. He is not here to negotiate; he is here to execute. An investigator who draws a weapon on Quirce has drawn a weapon on three hardened men and should be treated accordingly.
- **What if investigators kill Quirce in S6?** His men scatter. His horses and papers are inherited by the investigators — the papers contain his true intent (to bind the entity for his own use). The ritual in S8–S9 can still proceed, but the investigators now know Quirce's agenda and lose the "S8 twist" surprise. This is acceptable; it shifts the final act into a clean rescue/closing operation without the internal betrayal.

**Alternative paths**:

- **If Don Eusebio refuses to go to the pit**: He physically cannot refuse once Quirce addresses him with the specific phrase from the manuscript (*"I invoke the anchor of the first opening."*) — this is a mechanical effect, not a social one. The Mark on him, the opening he performed, compels him. Communicate this to players so they understand he is not free to choose.
- **If the investigators decide to take Don Eusebio away from Quirce entirely**: They can — for 12 hours. Then Don Eusebio begins walking south in his sleep. He will walk to the pit under his own power if they do not physically restrain him. The opening he performed is calling him home.

### SESSION 7 — La Víspera

**Primary fix applied**: Concepción confession scene rewritten to account for S1 events. See §1.2.

**Additional issues identified**:

- **This is a low-action session by design**, which is good, but the risk is that players — especially tired players after the intensity of S6 — will want to skip it. **[RECOMMENDATION]**: Make clear at the session start that the choice of *who anchors the focus* and *how the panels are arranged* are decisions being made tonight, and the wrong choice will kill people tomorrow. This gives the session concrete stakes even though nothing combative is happening.
- **Quirce's diagram error** is a critical puzzle. If investigators miss the Spot Hidden check, they proceed into S8 with a broken diagram. **[RECOMMENDATION]**: If the Spot Hidden fails, give an alternate check opportunity in S8 *before* the ritual begins — Silvio, who has been watching Quirce's preparations with Mark-enhanced attention, can quietly say to an investigator, *"Check the northern panel. Something about it looks off to me and I can't say what."* This is a safety net so the error is still catchable.

**Alternative paths**:

- **If an investigator volunteers to anchor the focus in place of Nahuel**: This is possible but suicidal without the Mark. If the investigator has the Mark (from S3), the chance of survival is low but not zero — treat it as a structured sequence of CON, POW, and SAN checks in S9. Reward the attempt dramatically even on death.
- **If Nahuel insists on anchoring and no one contests it**: He will almost certainly die. This is the default outcome and the campaign is written around it. Do not let anyone talk investigators out of feeling bad about it.

### SESSION 8 — La Traición

**Issues identified**:

- **Quirce's betrayal is pre-written, but some groups will have already killed Quirce in S6 or S7.** **[RECOMMENDATION]**: If Quirce is dead before S8, the betrayal vector shifts to Don Eusebio himself. At the critical moment, the entity attempts to repossess him — reminding him of what it *offered* him originally (prosperity, renewal, abundance), not what he *did* to earn it. He will waver. An investigator's Psychology roll can hold him in place; a failure means Don Eusebio attempts to reopen the outer ring with his bare hands. This provides the same dramatic beat — an ally turning — without relying on Quirce.
- **The ritual's pacing in S8–S9** is the most mechanically dense part of the campaign. **[RECOMMENDATION]**: Before running S8, the Keeper should list the anchors, the Marked, the panel assignments, and which investigator is where. A scratch diagram on a sheet of paper will prevent half the narrative confusion that arises during the actual ritual rounds.

### SESSION 9 — El Cierre

**Issues identified**:

- **The entity's final response** — attempting to complete the opening rather than accept the closing — is the campaign's core theme delivered as a mechanic. But if investigators have been very successful (all Marked alive, diagram correct, Nahuel anchored, Don Eusebio cooperating, Silvio present, Quirce neutralized), the final ritual can feel anti-climactic. **[RECOMMENDATION]**: Even in a "best case" scenario, *something* must cost the investigators. The entity cannot be closed without a cost. Pre-identify a specific cost for each investigator — something they care about, something the Keeper knows about from character creation and from the preceding sessions — and invoke one during the final ritual. If an investigator has a wife who was mentioned in passing in S4, have the ritual close require her name. If an investigator has been talking about their daughter in Buenos Aires, have the entity try to reach toward her. The ritual should feel like a price paid, not a puzzle solved.
- **The aftermath** assumes characters go home. Some will want to stay — Rosa may be dying, Concepción may need care, the land itself remains damaged. **[RECOMMENDATION]**: Write a one-year-later epilogue for each investigator. Who stayed? Who left? Who came back to check? This is the campaign's emotional landing and should not be rushed.

**Alternative paths**:

- **If the ritual fails catastrophically**: The pit reopens at full width. The scar becomes permanent. The region around the Estancia La Esperanza becomes unlivable for about 20 kilometers. Investigators who survive have a regional catastrophe on their conscience. This is a legitimate ending. Do not soften it.
- **If the investigators succeed but Don Eusebio dies**: He became an anchor in truth — the last useful thing he did. Concepción, if present, says quietly afterward, *"He was very afraid. But he stayed."* This is her final line in the campaign.

## 3. CROSS-SESSION CONTINUITY NOTES

### 3.1 Concepción's trajectory

- **S1**: At the estancia. Walks into the pit, begins breaking geometry. Saved by investigators. Falls asleep on the ride back.
- **S2**: At the estancia. Silent, withdrawn. Will speak only to the most gentle investigator, and only to say *"It's quieter now. But not gone."*
- **S3**: At the estancia. Asleep when investigators return.
- **S4**: **Sent to Azul.** Don Eusebio dispatched her to a cousin's house. She is not present. This is now consistent with her S1 role.
- **S5, S6**: Remains in Azul.
- **S7**: **Returns, unexpectedly, having walked back from Azul on her own.** She arrives the afternoon of the Víspera. She does not explain how she found her way. She sits near the fire and hums. Don Eusebio sees her and cannot speak. This is the setup for his confession later that night.
- **S8, S9**: She is present. In S9, she may ask to help anchor the focus. If the investigators let her, she will do so successfully — she is the only one in the entire group who is fully aligned enough to anchor without the Mark. If they refuse her and insist on protecting her, Nahuel anchors as originally designed.

**[RECOMMENDATION]**: Give Concepción a single line of agency at some point in each session she appears in. Keep her otherwise quiet. Her presence should feel like weather, not like a character with goals — until S9, when she has one.

### 3.2 Don Eusebio's arc

- **S1**: Lying patrón, manipulator of investigators, flees east when his control collapses.
- **S2**: Absent.
- **S3**: Glimpsed from a distance at the ridge above the tribal camp (if investigators tracked him).
- **S4**: Returned, deteriorated, defensive. Resists revelation but cannot stop it.
- **S5**: Periphery — he is in the study most of the day, reading. He will not accompany the investigators to the ruins.
- **S6**: Coerced by Quirce. Revealed as a man who gave up his agency months ago.
- **S7**: Confesses about Concepción. Most honest moment of his life.
- **S8**: Either cooperates or (if Quirce is dead) wavers and must be held in place.
- **S9**: Either survives diminished or dies as an anchor. Either ending is valid and thematically satisfying.

### 3.3 Tomás's arc

- **S1**: Foreman. Did not enter the pit. Ashamed.
- **S2**: Offers horses, guide, whatever help he can. Will not meet the investigators' eyes.
- **S3**: Not present.
- **S4**: At the estancia. Quietly reliable but diminished.
- **S5**: Can be asked about local history. Knows more than he lets on — his grandmother was from San Ruiz.
- **S6**: Will stand with the investigators against Quirce if they have treated him well. If they have ignored him, he stays out of it.
- **S7–S9**: Available as a fourth fighter in the final ritual if his arc has been nurtured. Capable, not extraordinary. If he fights and dies, he dies having redeemed the moment he did not enter the pit.

### 3.4 The Marks

- Investigators receive the Mark in S3 if they are hit by the Hound and survive.
- The Mark is not described fully in S3. **[RECOMMENDATION]**: The Mark's effects should be revealed in pieces across S4 and S5 — a glimpse of unnatural geometry where there should be none, an itch at the base of the skull when near the pit, an increased ability to read the manuscript's marginalia. These should feel like partial gifts, with later-revealed costs.
- The Mark's cost is fully paid in S9 if the investigator anchors. Even without anchoring, every Marked investigator ends the campaign with a permanent sensory alteration — they see angles slightly wrong for the rest of their lives, and certain geometries (church interiors, prison cells) cause them mild nausea. This should be roleplayed, not mechanically punished.

## 4. MISSING HOOKS AND ATMOSPHERIC RECOMMENDATIONS

### 4.1 Between-session hooks

The original campaign assumes sessions run back-to-back in fiction. In practice, most tables have a day or two of downtime between sessions, and players often want to "do something" on the off days. **Use these hooks**:

- **Between S1 and S2**: Dionisia, the kitchen woman, has a sister who married a San Ruiz man. She can tell them the husband never came back from the evacuation. This primes Rosa.
- **Between S2 and S3**: Rosa gives one investigator the silver four-line ornament at her throat, "in case you need it." She means: in case the Hound comes close.
- **Between S3 and S4**: A local rural-police sergeant passes through asking about "strange reports" from neighboring estancias. He offers to put an ear to the ground in Azul (setup for Silvio's identity verification).
- **Between S4 and S5**: A traveling merchant arrives at the estancia with broken equipment and must stay the night. In the morning, all of his copper items have tarnished black. No other metals were affected. This is environmental foreshadowing.
- **Between S5 and S6**: Investigators receive a short, formal letter from Aldao Quirce announcing his imminent arrival. The letter is written in elegant Spanish but the signature has a small notation under it in Latin: *sub signo magno*. A Latin speaker recognizes this as a signature style used in colonial-era occult correspondence.
- **Between S7 and S8**: Nothing. The night of the Víspera passes. Dawn comes. Nobody sleeps well.
- **Between S8 and S9**: These are the same night. No gap.
- **After S9**: One year later, on the same calendar night, one of the investigators dreams of the pit. Only a dream. But the same night. Every year.

### 4.2 Atmospheric details to salt in

- **The goat in the San Ruiz well.** Have Keepers remember it. It is still alive throughout the campaign. When investigators return to San Ruiz in S8 or in the epilogue, the goat is still there, still watching. It knows something. It doesn't bleed. Nobody ever brings it up directly.
- **The dust.** The pit releases a specific dust that settles on clothing and does not wash out easily. Investigators who have been to the pit carry it with them. Rosa notices it in S2 (suggested above). Kuyen notices it in S3. Silvio notices it in S4. This is a running motif of *the scene of the ritual following the investigators wherever they go*.
- **The sound.** A low vibration that is easier to feel than hear. Present near the pit, at the chapel in S2, during the counter-ritual in S3, in Don Eusebio's study in S4, and fully active in S8–S9. It is the entity's baseline. Players who describe their investigator noticing it should be rewarded with a small sanity buffer — awareness is protective, here.

### 4.3 Player-facing roleplaying hooks

- **Every major NPC has something they want the investigators to notice without saying it outright.** Don Eusebio wants them to ask the right question so he can confess without volunteering. Rosa wants them to sit down without pressing her so she can decide to trust them. Kuyen wants them to demonstrate they are not here to steal what her people know. Silvio wants them to challenge him so he knows they will challenge Quirce. Reward the investigators who ask good questions more than the investigators who make good rolls.
- **The three named prayers in the campaign** — *Por la tierra que fue buena*, the Mapudungun counter-chant, and Rosa's silent prayer at the chapel — should be named aloud by the Keeper when they happen. Players will remember names. These are atmospheric props.

## 5. PACING AND RUNTIME ESTIMATES

Based on the revised content, expected session length (for a group of 4 investigators, comfortable with CoC):

| Session | Estimated Length | Key Pacing Risk |
|---|---|---|
| S1 El Contrato | 3.5 – 4 hours | Pit scene now has a clear trigger and a clear escape condition; the stall that prompted this review should not recur. |
| S2 Las Señales | 3 – 3.5 hours | Rosa scene can drag if played too slowly — have her move the investigators to the chapel within 45 minutes. |
| S3 El Ritual Roto | 3.5 – 4 hours | The decision scene is the pacing hot-spot. Do not let it go longer than 20 minutes — the entity is waiting. |

## 6. OPEN QUESTIONS FOR THE KEEPER TO DECIDE BEFORE RUNNING

The campaign was designed with some deliberate ambiguities. The Keeper should decide these *before* session 1 and stay consistent:

1. **Is the entity malevolent or indifferent?** The text leans indifferent (it is a geometric phenomenon, not a mind). But the Keeper can play it as malevolent if the table prefers a clearer antagonist. Either is valid. The mechanics do not change.
2. **Does Concepción survive the campaign?** Leave this open. The pivotal moment is her offer to anchor in S9. If the Keeper decides beforehand that she dies, the campaign becomes bleaker; if she lives, the epilogue is lighter. Let the table's collective instinct guide the call.
3. **Is the land reclaimable?** The manuscript implies it can be, with time. The Keeper can choose whether the epilogue shows recovery or permanent damage. Both are thematically justified.
4. **What happens to Quirce's body/captured agents?** The Keeper should decide whether any of them carry forward — they could become antagonists in a sequel, or they could be quietly buried. The text does not assume a sequel.
