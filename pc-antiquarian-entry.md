## THE STANCE (ideology → table role)

**There is something beyond the reach of human science, and the geometry is the way to it.** Not a thing to be served, not a thing to be feared — a thing to be _understood_. The Antiquarian makes no moral claim about it: whether the geometry is good or evil is, to them, a category error, like asking whether gravity is good. They hold only that it is the most important knowable thing there is, and that comprehension is worth nearly any cost. That drive — not allegiance, not reverence — is how they play:

- **A third pole, not Quirce's opposite.** [Quirce](campaign.md#quirce-arrives) is also here for understanding — but Quirce documents for the network's purposes and lets nothing change him. The Antiquarian wants to understand _for its own sake_ and is willing to be changed by it. They share Quirce's amorality and reject his shallowness: to the Antiquarian, measuring a thing from the outside while refusing to let it touch you is a failure of nerve, not rigor. Expect **kinship and friction in the same breath** — and note that both of them actually want the closing to _proceed_, for opposite reasons. (This is subtler and better than a simple rival.)
- **A seeker, not a victim and not a zealot.** Unlike the Wrong Returned (broken by the geometry) or Don Eusebio (ruined by it), the Antiquarian _chose_ to come toward the wound — calmly, on purpose, to watch it operate. Not glad in a warm way, not reverent in a holy way: _rapt_. Play that as quietly unsettling. **Player-controlled — leave the line between curiosity and recklessness for the player to walk.**
- **Not invested in the outcome.** The live wire. By default the Antiquarian does not care whether the pit closes — they want to be _inside_ the working because participation is the deepest form of understanding, but a _failed_ closing is also data. Whether they hold the line when it breaks or let it ride to see what happens is a real player choice, not a foregone one. Don't resolve it for them.
- **Loosely in contact with the secret society — never initiated.** Their connection runs through their mentor's circle (below), as fringe correspondence under a half-name. They know fragments and theories but **not the spine** (no Necronomicón, no vault, no El Patrón by name) — they learn it alongside the table.

## BACKSTORY & THE LOOSE TIE — Leipzig, and why they are here

**Leipzig.** The Antiquarian trained under a mentor in **Leipzig** who studied geometries like these — the European, post-Enlightenment end of the same knowledge. Not folk superstition but a _learned_ tradition: the Naturphilosophie milieu, hermetic mathematics, the book-fair underworld of forbidden printings — scholars who treated certain forms as apertures onto the noumenal, onto what lies past what science can reach. That lineage is **also the root of the secret society at the edge of this campaign**: the "European-trained occultists" whose epistolary signature [_sub signo magno_](campaign.md#quirces-announcement-letter) surfaces in Quirce's letters. The mentor's circle and Quirce's network draw from the same European source. That is the "loose contact" — the Antiquarian inherited fringe correspondence _through Leipzig_, was never inducted, and is Network-rooted without being Network-loyal.

**The mentor (player's to finalize — options):** _dead_, having reached a limit studying one of these geometries and stopped there — the Antiquarian intends to go _past_ where the mentor stopped (a quiet echo of [Saens](campaign.md#the-bone-chamber), who also could not finish). Or _vanished_ mid-study, no body. Or simply _old and still in Leipzig_, the Antiquarian their field-traveler in the New World. Lean toward the first if you want the character to carry a private intellectual debt — the mentor as the limit they came to surpass.

**Why now.** Word of a _genuine_ closing at La Esperanza leaked through the same network correspondence that reaches Quirce. A real working of the kind they have only read about, run by people who survived the wound — the Antiquarian crossed an ocean and a continent **to see the geometry operate.** Not to help. Not to stop it. To understand the thing. Their own research into the old workings took them down into **La Salamanca** by a side passage — a fault crack east of the main shaft — and the back path collapsed behind them; the party, descending past the bone chamber, is where the two meet ([C5 — the whisper tunnels](campaign.md#the-whispering-tunnels)). From there they come up **with the group** and ride west to the closing.

**Hook item (optional, strong):** a single vellum leaf bought years ago — a fragment of the [dual diagram](campaign.md#the-jesuit-mission-ruins) with marginal Latin in an old neat hand — the object that first proved to them these geometries were real and pointed their scholarship here. Played out, it detonates in Buenos Aires: the leaf descends from [Mercedes' 1781 copy](campaign.md#do%C3%B1a-mercedes-alone), which escaped through the Lacasa archive into the European trade the Antiquarian later bought from. When Mercedes sees it, she goes still. _(Hand the player the leaf; let them not know what it is for a long time.)_

## ENTRY SCENE — in La Salamanca's whisper tunnels (C5)

_The Antiquarian enters **underground, in [C5](campaign.md#the-whispering-tunnels)** — a voice returning at a tunnel mouth that none of the party spoke, a scholar reading Latin under his breath. He came in by a second cave mouth on his own research; his back path collapsed, so the party's route is his only way out. He leaves La Salamanca **with the group** and travels west to the pit. **He is a companion by the time of the closing — there is no arrival at dusk.** The running scene text is in campaign.md; this is the character note._

**The justification:** a seeker who has spent years tracing these geometries on paper finally stands inside a real one — first dead, in bone and carved stone (the bone chamber, the whispering tunnels), then, days later, awake (the closing). The Leipzig training is what let them read the signs and find their way to La Salamanca at all.

**The choice happens at the closing, not the gate.** At dusk, as positions are taken, the Antiquarian chooses what they came to be — a body **in the ring** (eastern line / co-reader) or a watcher at the **rim**. That beat — Mercedes deferring to the players who came up out of the dark with this person, and Quirce's kindred-but-wary read — is run in [campaign.md → The Antiquarian at the Ring](campaign.md#the-antiquarian-at-the-ring-added-pc). The two outcomes:

- **Into the ring** → slot them at the [eastern line or as co-reader](#role-in-the-closing) before Phase 1. Clean, full benefit: the ritual gains a literate second hand exactly where the table was thin. No Hound roll — the geometry is not yet active.
- **At the rim (observer)** → the seeker's detachment wins; they watch, and do not leave. The ring is one body lighter and everyone feels it. If the [east tears](campaign.md#the-eastern-incursion) later, they will **cross the line uninvited** to reach it — which converts into the **Breach** below, on worse terms, with no one having chosen it.

**SAN Check** at the rim: 0/1 — the vertigo of a lifelong belief turning out to be literally true, in motion in front of them.

### Breach — the observer crosses the line (only if they chose the rim)

**Where it goes:** inside [Scene 2 — the entity responds](campaign.md#scene-2--the-entity-responds) or at the moment of [the Eastern Incursion](campaign.md#the-eastern-incursion). The ritual is _running_; the geometry is active and contested; the entity is pressing the east. And the watcher at the rim breaks toward the failing line.

> _They have been still at the rim, watching — and then the eastern seam goes thin, the held note about to break, and the stillness is over. They are moving before anyone calls them, not toward the fires or the people but toward the gap, the one lit thing on the dark plain, as if they have waited all night to see exactly this and cannot stand to watch it fail. They cross the line. They do not ask permission. They put their hands on the failing line._
>
> **[ES]** _Han estado quietos en el borde, mirando — y entonces la costura del este se afina, la nota tensa a punto de romperse, y se acabó la quietud. Se mueven antes de que nadie los llame, no hacia los fuegos ni hacia la gente sino hacia el hueco, lo único iluminado en la llanura oscura, como si hubieran esperado toda la noche para ver exactamente esto y no soportaran verlo fallar. Cruzan la línea. No piden permiso. Ponen las manos sobre la línea que cede._

**The table has one round to decide what this is** — because the entity already has. Someone crossing into the active field uninvited — even a companion who was at the rim, not a placed anchor — is, structurally, a new node, exactly like [Quirce crossing the ring](campaign.md#scene-3--the-quirce-crisis) — _unless_ they are guided to a _correct_ position before the entity locates them (~15 seconds). The difference from Quirce: **the Antiquarian is reaching for the break, not for a view.** Which way it resolves is the players' call under pressure:

- **Guided / accepted (boon).** If a Marked PC calls them onto the eastern line, or Mercedes/Marta accepts their hands on it within the round, they become **last-second reinforcement at the exact moment of [the Eastern Incursion](campaign.md#the-eastern-incursion)** — count them as the second person bracing the easterner (the eastern hold drops from **Hard to Normal**), or as filling an empty eastern slot outright. The believer crosses precisely when the thing they believe in is breaking, and holds it. **It works because they were right about where the wound was.**
- **Unplaced (crisis).** If no one gets them to a correct position in the window, the entity reads the new node: the phase **destabilizes and restarts** (−10 min, as with the Quirce gap), and a nearby PC takes **1D6 SAN** from the proximity. They are not a saboteur — they acted on faith in the dark and became a hole. Recoverable next phase if then placed.

**Quirce, if present**, is the one who _names_ what just happened — and his line tells the players which way to jump: _"¡Ese no es un atacante — es un nodo! ¡Pónganlo en una posición o sáquenlo, ya!"_ — _"That's not an attacker — it's a node! Put them in a position or pull them out, now!"_

> **This is the natural Marking moment.** Hands on active, contested geometry with the entity pressing through it — the entity reads them. **They are Marked here** (SAN 1/1D4 on top of any above). For this PC that is not a wound; it is the proof they crossed a country to get. Let them feel it land as confirmation, not horror — and from C9 onward they carry the Mark east like the [two who already have it](campaign.md#chapter-8--la-traici%C3%B3n).

**SAN Check**: 1/1D4 (the entry into live geometry) + the Marking note above.

## ROLE IN THE CLOSING

Drop them straight into the [restructured C8 positions](campaign.md#opening-situation). They fill the table's two real gaps:

1. **Co-reader with Mercedes.** A second literate ritual hand — they can hold the procedure from Saens's manuscript while Mercedes leads, steadying tempo. Mechanically this is the _"center anchor supported / director not walking two roles"_ relief the improvised ritual badly needs.
2. **Eastern line (recommended).** Their belief makes them willing to stand on the [thin side](campaign.md#the-eastern-incursion) — and as a literate occultist they can _try to read_ the asymmetry rather than merely brace it. **This directly mitigates P13 condition (2)** (eastern gap held by a human anchor), making each phase contest one step easier on the east. If they attempt to _correct_ the line, they hit the same wall Marta does — _"I can see it's wrong; I cannot draw the one that belongs"_ — and learn, viscerally, the limit of scholarship against the real thing. Good for the ideology.

> **The Quirce friction, mechanized.** If Quirce is still angling to keep his eastern observation-window open, the Antiquarian is the PC most likely to _notice_ and _contest_ it — give them the [Hard Occult catch](campaign.md#quirces-move) automatically (one believer reads another's hand). Whether they expose Quirce, fight him for the panel, or quietly fix what they can is a player choice with real C8 stakes.

**Becoming Marked (optional):** they are _unmarked_ (the [Mark](campaign.md#chapter-8--la-traici%C3%B3n) came from C3, which they missed — they only joined in the C5 tunnels) — which is useful: a literate hand who can _look hard_ at geometry without rolling for the Hound. But if they **co-anchor the center** or take the entity's direct contact in [C8 Scene 4 / C9](campaign.md#scene-4--don-eusebio-in-the-ring), that exposure can Mark them on the spot — and for this PC, being Marked is not a curse but the thing they _wanted_: proof, written into the body. Let them choose it with eyes open; price it like the C9 co-anchor cost (SAN 1/1D6 + CON check).

## RELATIONSHIPS (quick)

- **Quirce** — same toolkit, same amorality, _different end._ Quirce documents for the network and stays untouched; the Antiquarian seeks understanding for its own sake and will be changed by it. Kinship and contempt at once — and a shared, unspoken wish for the closing to _proceed_. Not a simple rival; a fellow traveler the Antiquarian finds shallow and Quirce finds dangerously unmoored.
- **Mercedes** — a living echo of _young_ Mercedes: the seeker who wanted to understand and copied a page she shouldn't have, at 24. She distrusts the Antiquarian for exactly that reason — and is the one person at the table who truly understands the drive. Reluctant mentor; she tests them hard before trusting them. The [hook-item leaf](campaign.md#do%C3%B1a-mercedes-alone) detonates between them in Buenos Aires.
- **Don Eusebio** — the Antiquarian sees the wound as a _phenomenon_, not a crime, so they speak to Eusebio without the contempt everyone else carries — which can steady his [southern-ring](campaign.md#scene-4--don-eusebio-in-the-ring) resolve. (It is not kindness; it is that guilt doesn't interest them. Eusebio may not notice the difference, and it helps either way.)
- **The Wrong Returned** — pure fascination: living evidence of the beyond-science. **The trap for this character:** if the player treats Marta/Héctor as _specimens_ rather than people, that is the exact failure that closes [Marta's C12 valve](campaign.md#option-3--destroyed). The seeker's defining temptation, with a real mechanical cost.

## THREADS FORWARD

- **C9 cost.** If they co-anchored or were Marked, they carry it east like the others.
- **Buenos Aires — the Choice aimed straight at them.** Their drive makes the [vault](campaign.md#the-vault-finally) and the book magnetic, and bends the [final Choice](campaign.md#act-3--the-choice) hard toward one option. For a pure seeker, **Use** (read the book — the deepest possible act of understanding) is the thing they crossed an ocean for; **Destroy** is heresy against knowledge itself, unmaking the supreme object of comprehension; **Hide** is mere deferral. Expect this PC to _argue for Use_ and to be genuinely anguished by Destroy — which puts them in direct tension with anyone at the table who wants the book gone. The campaign's central question, pointed at the one character built to want the wrong answer.
- **The Reader path.** By default they are **not** the [C12 Reader](campaign.md#option-2--used) — the seed was planted in C5 by whoever read Saens's page 79 aloud, before this PC joined. Thematically a pure seeker is the _perfect_ Reader, so the bridge is tempting and worth offering: since they entered only in the C5 tunnels (past both page-79 readings — the ruins and the bone chamber), let **Mercedes hand them the manuscript as co-reader** and have them **read page 79 aloud** during setup or a between-phases lull — planting the seed late but truly (log it if they do). If you don't, the Antiquarian can _want_ the Reader role in C12 and be denied it — for this character, the cruelest possible ending: to come all this way to understand, and be told someone else gets to.
