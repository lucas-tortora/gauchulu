# LA TIERRA MALA — CAMPAIGN FLOWMAP

A single-document GM reference for the full 12-chapter campaign, written to **this table's actual state** — the divergences from the published module are baked into the chapter files and summarized here, not flagged as alternates. Each chapter is a *narrative unit*, not a play session. Use this map to navigate threads, escalation, and decision points without rereading every chapter file.

> Full prep lives in `chapter-01.md` … `chapter-12.md`. NPC dossiers/status in `REF-NPCs.md`. The Mark = perception rule in `overview.md`. **Arc 4 city setting (1821 Buenos Aires) in `REF-BuenosAires-1821.md`.** Printable visual handouts in `assets/`. Per-session history of how the table got here: `SESSION-LOG.md`.

## CAMPAIGN AT A GLANCE

- **Setting**: Pampa argentina, 1821. Estancia (frontier *campaña*) → folklore underground (La Salamanca) → Andean cult (Coypac) → Buenos Aires (Rivadavia-era port, newly secularized Manzana de las Luces).
- **Premise**: Three gauchos hired as hunters discover the corruption is the *patrón's* own doing, then chase a wider conspiracy to its end at the Necronomicón beneath the University of Buenos Aires.
- **The real mover**: **El Patrón** (Eligio Dadálah / birth name Eligio Méndez), a Nyarlathotep avatar, has steered everyone for forty years to force the Keepers to *move* the book.
- **Length**: 12 chapters across 4 arcs.
- **Final question**: Should the Necronomicón remain hidden, be used, or be destroyed? Who pays the price? **At this table, Use is closed** (no Reader) — the dock choice is **Hide or Destroyed**.

## THE STATE LEDGER — what carries, chapter to chapter

This is the spine of the table's divergence. Every downstream beat assumes it.

- **Concepción is dead** (C1) — no Azul, no C7 reappearance; Don Eusebio's C7 confession is a settlement, and he stands in the ring unasked.
- **The chapel seal is destroyed** (C2) — the complete binding symbol survives only as buried clay under Rosa's control; the partial binding that held 14 months has failed.
- **La Llorona is loose** (C2) — displaced, enraged, eyes open, moving east toward the pit; she knows who broke the walls.
- **The goat travels with the party** (C2 → C9) — Mandinga's marker, pulled from the well; precedes the party everywhere; can disrupt the immaterial; **walks west from the pit at dawn in C9** and does not return.
- **The C3 ritual failed** — **Kuyen, Nahuel, and the whole tribe are dead.** No living counter-tradition remains; the closing must be improvised from Saens's manuscript + Mercedes + Marta + the Marked.
- **2 PCs are Marked** — permanent geometry perception; the campaign's interpretive engine. **2 PCs (outside the center) carry the Mandinga deal** — one traded for **health**, one for **power**; the price both accepted is *"their blood, offered to ensure the scar is closed"* — **due at the C8–C9 ritual** (palm to the panel line; withheld → the wound reopens / the gift inverts).
- **No Reader** (C5) — page 79 was never read aloud; **the C12 Use ending is closed** for the whole campaign. Do not run the C8 Mercedes-recognition or C11 cloister-bridge beats that assume a Reader.
- **La Salamanca chamber was looted** (C5) → **the malón for the maps** shadows the party home and **strikes at the C8 closing** (the C6 deal with one warrior did not call it off).
- **The Antiquarian** (added PC) joined at La Salamanca and stands the eastern line at the closing.
- **The eastern asymmetry** (Quirce's / the Wizard's correction) rides in Don Eusebio's recopied sheets; unless quarantined, it transfers to the C7 panels and is the entity's primary C8 attack vector.
- **Don Eusebio anchors the center** (C6 decision) with a **Marked PC beside him — currently at Sanity 10**; both survive the seal by default (C9), but the PC's COR is the night's thinnest thread.
- **The tarnished copper is in the geometry** (players' C6 decision) — Quirce's observation window is **two-way**: wider entity reach at the malón cull, an extra pressure vector at the Phase-5 press, and the fused copper leaves with Quirce afterward (Arc 4 thread).

## RECURRING THREADS — trace these across chapters

The goat, the dust, and the low vibration are the campaign's **three quiet atmospheric runners** — recurring motifs, not clues. Name them; let recognitions accumulate.

### The Marks (the Hound of Tindalos)
From C3 onward, **2 Marked PCs** *see geometry* — they perceive diagrams, alignments, and ritual shapes as felt wrongness or felt completeness, and do not need NPCs to translate. Mythos rolls about pattern in real space are one step easier; they read shape, not text. **This is the campaign's interpretive engine.** Per-chapter Hound-roll triggers are in each chapter's *Roll for Hound* section; the perception rule is in `overview.md` (*Mark of Tindalos*) and `assets/geometric-perception-cues.md`. After the C9 seal the Marks go permanently quiet at the pit site; in the city they communicate information, not manifestation. `assets/entity-hound.md` has the entity.

### The goat (Mandinga's marker)
Pulled from the San Ruiz well in C2, she follows the party for all of Arc 2–3: never eats, never bleeds, never reacts, always already where they are going. She can **disrupt** (not kill) the immaterial — a headbutt staggers an echo or an edge-entity, timed by her, never on command. She is Mandinga's territorial claim and the campaign's most patient witness. She reveals **six pupils** and walks **west** from the sealed pit at dawn in C9. Full notes: `REF-NPCs.md` (goat + Mandinga entries), `assets/npc-stats.md`, `assets/mandinga-sequence.md` (the deal mechanic).

### The dust from the pit
Fine grey particulate from the original carving's limestone, ground over centuries by the geometry; it settles on clothing and **does not wash out** because it is not, strictly, dust. Rosa smells it in C2; Marked-adjacent NPCs recognize it across the campaign; **Mercedes reads it off a jacket cuff seven hundred kilometers away** in C7 — the runner's most distant point. Per-chapter dust callbacks: `assets/geometric-perception-cues.md`.

### The low vibration
The campaign's continuous-presence sound — a low pressure behind the ears, felt in the sternum more than heard. First appears in **C1**; any investigator who *names* it aloud early is quietly rewarded (awareness is protective). It rises with the entity's ingress across Arc 1–3 and stops at the C9 seal. One of the three atmospheric runners.

### La Llorona (Dolores's echo)
An exhausted circuit until the players destroyed her chapel symbols in C2. Now displaced and enraged, eyes open, moving east toward the pit. A Marked PC near her path perceives a geometry unraveling outward (SAN 1/1D6). Full status: `REF-NPCs.md`.

### The Necronomicón — how it surfaces
Named obliquely by Mercedes (C5 letter), then in the marginalia of Saens's manuscript (C6), then by Mercedes in person (C7). A fragment sits at Coypac (C8 vision). The book itself is in the 1610 vault (C11). The dock choice is C12.

## THE THREE NAMED PRAYERS

Three folk prayers recur as *functional* ritual — geometry recognizes consistency. Name each aloud at the table the first time; players will remember.

### *Por la tierra que fue buena* — the gaucho's prayer
A folk-Catholic prayer with a pre-Christian root, said over land that has gone wrong. First named in **C1**; returns in **C8** (Don Eusebio in the ring) and in the **C12 Hide ending** (Padre Albarrán on the dock before the *Hic manet*).

### The Mapudungun counter-chant — *Antu kishu inchiñ ñi pewenmu / Antu kishu, lan amukey*
The tribe's closing line, first heard at the creek bed in **C3**. It returns at the **C8–C9 closing** — every NPC in the ring joins the line, including Don Eusebio and Mercedes, because the geometry teaches it to them under pressure — and in the **C12 Destroyed ending**, where **Mercedes delivers the tradition's longest-rooted line herself, from memory**, because the tribe that would have spoken it is dead.

### Rosa's silent prayer
Said daily over the buried clay at San Ruiz — silent, consistent, neither Mapuche nor Catholic. It anchored the partial binding until the walls came down in **C2**. A Marked PC who lingers at the chapel doorway afterward feels it as a felt geometry, not words.

## ENDING STATE

**Use is closed** — no PC read page 79 aloud (C5), so the Reader path was never earned and the parchment's inner page is unreadable to anyone present. The dock choice is:

- **Hide** *(default)* — the book goes back into a new sealed chest; the threat is deferred, not ended; **Mercedes loses an eye** over the following month. *Por la tierra que fue buena* on the dock.
- **Destroyed** *(always available — Mercedes carried the ritual forty years)* — the older chain ends the book and El Patrón both. The cost is paid in Marks. With only **2 Marked PCs** the tiers are **Anchor (dies) + Pillar (survives, sense-loss)** — unless the **Marta valve** is invoked (Marta anchors, both PCs drop a tier), offered only if the table treated the Wrong Returned as people. Mercedes delivers the tradition's longest-rooted line herself, from memory, because the tribe is dead.
