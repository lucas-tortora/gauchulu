# CHAPTER 9 — EL CIERRE
### *The Closing*

**Chapter Goal**: Complete the ritual. Pay the cost. Live with what remains.
**Emotional Arc**: Desperate Resolve → Sacrifice → Grief and Release
**Key Seed**: Not all wounds heal. The land will carry this. So will the people.

---

## OPENING SITUATION

The ritual is running. Phases completed in Chapter 8 carry forward. Two phases remain. The entity has mapped the geometry and is pressing hard. It is the deepest part of the night — approximately 3 AM — with three hours until dawn, after which the geometry's resistance weakens.

Everyone is in position. Everyone is exhausted. Nahuel has been the focus for the better part of the night and is pale in the firelight, his breathing measured and deliberate.

The pit below is the most active it has been at any point in the campaign. The carved walls pulse with the same light as the blood arcs in the soil. The geometry extends upward from the pit in three dimensions — a shape that was flat, becoming volumetric. The entity is more present here than it has ever been. Close to breach. Close to closing. Both conditions simultaneously possible.

---

## THE FIVE-PHASE STRUCTURE

The ritual has five phases. The first three (or fewer, if Chapter 8 had complications) are complete. What follows is the structure of phases four and five.

**General principle**: Each phase is a sustained action, not a single roll. Investigators hold positions, Kuyen works, Nahuel focuses. The entity contests. The outcome of each phase depends on the group's collective state — position integrity, Nahuel's condition, the outer ring composition, and specific player choices.

**Between phases**: 10–15 minutes. Time to speak, check conditions, move. The entity is active between phases but cannot reach through the partially closed geometry. It uses the gaps to press where the geometry is weakest.

---

## PHASE FOUR — THE DEEP CLOSE

> *Kuyen draws the fourth segment — the inner ring, the one that compresses the geometry toward the pit's center. As she draws it, the light in the pit changes color: from pale yellowish-white to something closer to amber. The entity's light and the binding's light are mixing.*
>
> *The sound deepens. Not louder — deeper. Lower than hearing, now. You feel it in the bones of the feet.*

**What investigators must do**: Hold positions. The entity tests each outer ring position in sequence — not simultaneously, in sequence, as if running through a list. Each test lasts approximately 30 seconds. The investigator at the tested position must make a choice: hold (automatic if their position is strong) or hold with effort (**CON check** at Normal difficulty if position was compromised in Chapter 8).

**The southern position — Don Eusebio**: When the entity reaches the southern position, it presses differently than on the others. For investigators: the test on Don Eusebio's position requires a **CON (Hard)** check or **POW** check if he is solo. If an investigator is physically with him (hand on his shoulder), the check is Normal.

**What happens at Don Eusebio's position**:

> *The entity finds the recognition it's been tracking all night. Don Eusebio. The opener. The geometry knows him the way a scar knows the hand that made it.*
>
> *He shudders once — full-body, as if struck. He grips the outer ring marker with both hands. His lips move without sound. Then he stops shuddering and becomes absolutely still.*
>
> *He says: "No."*
>
> *One word. Not dramatic. Final.*

**Keeper note**: This is Don Eusebio's actual redemptive moment — not a grand gesture, just the word *"No"* spoken with complete authority to something that has been his landlord for eight months. Whether it is the bravest thing he has ever done is between Don Eusebio and the investigators who witnessed it.

**End of Phase Four**: The fourth segment closes. The geometry is now 80% sealed. The pit's light has shifted entirely to amber. The entity's carved lines on the walls are being pushed down, compressing.

**SAN Check**: 0/1D4 — the beauty and wrongness of the near-sealed geometry, the amber light, the sound at the threshold of meaning.

---

## PHASE FIVE — THE FINAL SEAL

**Before Phase Five begins** — a 10-minute gap — Kuyen checks every position. She crouches in front of Nahuel and holds his face between her palms, looking at him.

> *"Can you hold the last one?"*
>
> *He says: "Yes."*
>
> *She says: "I know. Can you actually hold the last one?"*

He tells her the truth, whatever it is. If he cannot hold it alone, he says so now. This is the point where the focal anchoring decision becomes critical.

**Options for Phase Five focal support**:

1. **Nahuel solo, at limit**: He holds. But the final phase requires the focus to withstand the entity's maximum pressure — the moment of deepest resistance before the seal completes. At the limit, without support, a **POW (Hard)** check. On failure: he breaks concentration. The phase can be restarted (he recovers in 2 minutes) but the entity maps the failure point and the restart begins at higher pressure.

2. **Nahuel co-anchored by an investigator**: The investigator holds Nahuel's hands, placing themselves within the center ring. The contact grounds Nahuel's focus. He can sustain the final phase. The cost: the investigator takes direct geometric exposure — **SAN 1/1D6** and **CON check** (on failure: 1D6 damage from reality distortion). This is the volunteered sacrifice — not death, but payment.

3. **Marta steps into the center** (if investigators have built genuine rapport with the Wrong Returned): She can co-anchor from the outer ring by extending what the entity built into her — her partial geometric alignment — to hold the center steady from outside. This is Marta's choice; investigators cannot make it for her. She will offer it if she feels the investigators and Kuyen have treated her as a person rather than a resource. If she does: the closing has near-certain success in Phase Five. The cost to Marta — see aftermath.

---

### Phase Five — The Working

**Read aloud — beginning of Phase Five**:

> *Kuyen's hands lay the last section. The complete binding geometry is now present: outer ring, inner ring, the center Nahuel holds. The shape is complete in three dimensions, extending from the panels on the ground down into the pit's depth along the carved walls.*
>
> *The entity stops its testing. All the pressure that had been distributed across the outer ring positions collapses inward — to the center. To Nahuel. To the seal.*
>
> *It is not trying to escape anymore. It is trying to prevent the closing. It presses the center from every direction at once, and the geometry bends under it like a panel in a gale.*
>
> *Kuyen's chant has not stopped since the first phase. At this moment, with the geometry complete and the entity pressing inward, **every NPC in the outer ring joins her on the line.** Don Eusebio, who has never spoken Mapudungun in his life, finds the syllables in his mouth: **"Antu kishu, lan amukey."** Mercedes, who learned the language from books and never said it aloud, says it. The Wrong Returned (Marta, Héctor) say it. Quirce, if he is still in the geometry's range, says it — without consenting, without understanding. **Even Don Eusebio's foreman Tomás, if he is present, says it.** They have not learned it. The geometry is teaching them, this once, because the geometry needs them to.*
>
> *Players whose investigators are in the outer ring may, at the GM's invitation, **say the line aloud at the table.** No roll. The fiction recognizes the speaking as participation.*

> **Atmospheric runner — the prayers (4)**: This is the campaign's most powerful naming of *Antu kishu, lan amukey*. **Run it slowly.** Have the players say it themselves if they want to; it is harmless and it is the right ritual gesture for the table to make. Marked PCs who say it tonight will dream the chant for the rest of the campaign. (See `flowmap.md`, *The Three Named Prayers*.)

**Mechanically**: This is the 3-round climax of the final phase.

---

**ROUND 1 — THE PRESS**

The entity applies full pressure to the center. Nahuel's breathing goes ragged. The ochre marks on his palms glow briefly — the same color as the carved walls — and then fade. The geometry is holding but the center is visibly stressed.

**Player options**:
- **Co-anchor**: Step into the center ring with Nahuel. Take the cost (SAN 1/1D6, CON check). Stabilize the center. Phase Five continues smoothly from this round forward — skip Round 2's crisis.
- **Maintain outer positions**: Every investigator stays in their outer ring position. This prevents the entity from expanding the breach outward while Nahuel fights the center alone. Nahuel's **POW** check this round is at Hard.
- **Use the Marked investigator's knowledge**: If the Marked investigator identified the entity's primary approach vector in Chapter 7 (from the communication), they can call the direction — give Kuyen and Nahuel a moment of warning before the entity's hardest push arrives. This shifts Nahuel's check from **Hard** to **Normal** for one round.

**End of Round 1**: The center is holding. Barely. Kuyen's voice has not wavered.

---

**ROUND 2 — THE OFFER TO THE CENTER**

The entity shifts tactics. It repeats what it did to Don Eusebio at the southern position — but directed at Nahuel, who is the most geometrically connected person present.

What Nahuel experiences:

> *Not an offer. A revelation. The entity shows him — from the inside, the way it showed Silvio — what the closing will do. Not to the entity. To Nahuel. The geometry that has occupied him for six weeks, the part of him that is now partially aligned to the scar — it will close with the scar. The connection will not survive the sealing. He will be ordinary again, or something close to ordinary, or something he cannot currently imagine because he has been this for too long.*

The entity is not offering him relief. It is offering him the choice between two kinds of loss: lose the focus now and keep the connection (which is not a good thing, as Nahuel knows — it has been destroying him), or complete the closing and lose the connection (which may feel like losing himself).

Nahuel has one round to make this decision. He will make it. But if an investigator who co-anchored with him is there, holding his hands — that person can feel the offer being made and can, without words, answer it. The presence of a human hand in the center of that choice is itself a vote for the closing.

**End of Round 2**: Nahuel's decision is made. He holds. The geometry tightens.

---

**ROUND 3 — THE SEAL**

> *The final contraction. The outer ring holds, the inner ring holds, the center holds. The entity's counter-movement stops — not from retreat, but because there is no longer room. The geometry has closed around the point of entry and is compressing toward it.*
>
> *Kuyen draws the last line. One stroke, connecting the final gap in the outer ring's panel. The connection completes.*

**What happens**:

The pit seals from the bottom up. The carved lines on the walls compress and go dark in sequence — the deepest lines first, then the middle layers, then the surface lines. The blood arcs in the soil fade. The panels in the outer ring glow amber-bright for three seconds, then go dark.

The sound stops. Not fades — stops. The vibration in the chest ends. The silence that follows is not empty. It is the silence of a space that was occupied and is now not.

The pit is once more a meter deep. The extra two meters of carved wall are gone — not collapsed, gone, as if they never extended. The earth is undisturbed.

**The dust on the players, however, has not lifted.** The pale grit that has clung to seams, cuffs, and saddle-leather since C1 is still there. A Marked PC who looks at their own sleeves notices this — the dust did *not* leave with the entity. *"It's still on us,"* one of them may say. Kuyen, kneeling, looks at her own forearms and confirms it without surprise. *"Lo de acá se cerró. Lo de allá no."* — *"What was here is closed. What is there is not."* The pit sealed. The dust is the campaign's reminder that something else is still open. (Keeper note: this is the inline hook into Arc 4. The dust will not lift until C12.)

**SAN Check** (the seal completing): 1/1D6 — not horror. The shock of genuine resolution. Something was terrible for nine months and now it is over, and the mind does not know what to do with *over*.

---

## THE COST

### Nahuel

If no co-anchor: the closing worked, and Nahuel is sitting in the center of the geometry with his eyes open. He looks at his palms. The ochre marks are gone — washed away in the night's sweat. He looks up at Kuyen. He says: *"I can feel where the corners are, but I can't hear them anymore."* He closes his eyes. He breathes. *"It's like the difference between knowing where a room is and being in it."*

He will recover. The three weeks as focus, the six weeks of nightly ritual, the night of the closing — these will cost him recovery time, possibly months. He will have nightmares in which the geometry is the dreaming landscape. He will never be comfortable in rooms with precise right angles. He is, ultimately, alive.

If Marta co-anchored: Nahuel is substantially better — the shared load preserved more of him. He is exhausted, not broken.

### Héctor and Marta

The Wrong Returned feel the closing before anyone else does. In the moment the final seal completes, Marta flinches — a full-body flinch, the first genuine physical reaction she has had in months. Then she laughs. Not performed — involuntary. A sound that comes up from somewhere below calculation.

Héctor reaches across the outer ring and takes her hand.

> *"It's quiet,"* he says.*
>
> *"Yes,"* she says.*

**What the Wrong Returned become**: They are not instantly restored. The geometric alignment does not vanish — it fades, over weeks and months, like a dye washing out of cloth. They will always be different — people who were shown the inside of something vast and indifferent. But the constant pull, the sound in the angles, the shadow that falls wrong — these will go. Marta says, at the end: *"I think I'll be able to sleep again. Eventually."*

If Marta co-anchored the center: the cost to her is the acceleration of this process — the alignment that was fading gradually burns off at once. She spends the night after the closing unable to speak or move, not from trauma but from the adjustment of becoming, abruptly, entirely herself again. In the morning she is present, quiet, and hungry for the first time in fourteen months.

**Silvio**: He collapses when the seal completes. Not dangerously — he sits down hard on the ground, catches himself on his hands, and stares at the pit. He says: *"It stopped."* He looks at his hands — normal hands, normal shadow. He looks at the pit. He looks at Don Eusebio. He says: *"You did it."* Not as absolution. As statement of fact.

### Don Eusebio

He is still standing at the southern outer ring position when the seal completes. He does not move for a long time after. When he does, he turns slowly, as if reorienting, and looks at the estancia — visible on the horizon as a dark shape against the pre-dawn sky.

He says: *"What do I owe?"*

He does not mean spiritually. He means legally, practically, materially. He is asking the investigators what consequences he will face — for the deaths of the tribe members, for what happened to Silvio and the Wrong Returned, for what Dolores Quiroga died trying to stop alone. He is not asking to negotiate. He is asking because he wants to pay.

The investigators answer this. It is the last significant choice of the campaign.

**Possible outcomes**:
- He faces no legal consequence (his wealth and influence make prosecution unlikely in 1821 Argentina, where the *campaña* sits well outside any reliable courts). The investigators' role in his accountability is therefore social, moral, practical. He can be required to compensate Kuyen's people, support the Wrong Returned in their recovery, fund the repair or closure of the San Ruiz chapel. He can be made to meet Rosa. He can be told that Dolores's name should be known.
- He is already ruined. The estancia's cattle are gone, the land is unproductive, his reputation is damaged. The entity's withdrawal takes with it whatever productive abundance he believed it generated. The land will recover — Kuyen says *"it will take years, but it is just land again"* — but he may not.

---

### Quirce

He is at his theodolite on the rise, east of the pit, when the seal completes. He has been writing continuously since he left the ring in Chapter 8. When the closing happens, he stands up and looks south at the pit for a long moment. Then he closes his notebook.

He approaches the group before dawn. He says nothing about what he observed. He says: *"I owe a debt here. I know I do. The question of how to pay it will take time to answer."* He does not offer money. He does not offer an apology. He offers, obliquely, the possibility that his network's knowledge could be used differently — to close anomalies rather than observe them. Whether investigators trust this, whether they want anything from Quirce, whether they view him as a continuing threat or a changed person — this is theirs to decide.

He leaves before sunrise. His notebook goes with him.

---

## AFTERMATH — FINAL SCENES

### Rosa

If anyone rides to San Ruiz: Rosa is in her house, grinding corn. She looks up when they arrive. She does not ask what happened. She says: *"I felt it close. Around midnight."* She continues grinding. *"Sit down."*

If someone tells her what happened to Dolores — how her work was found, how Kuyen was told the eastern wall was incomplete, how the symbol in the clay was used — Rosa does not speak for a long time. Then she says: *"She was right all along. I knew she was right. I didn't help her enough."* 

*(A pause.)*

*"None of us did."*

She will, in the weeks after the closing, begin planting over the pit. Something slow-growing. She says: *"It will take years. But I want something alive there."*

---

### The Land

The south pasture in the weeks after the closing: the grass rings persist for several months, then fade. The metallic smell dissipates within days. The ground over the pit settles and firms. Scavengers return to the remaining carcasses. Normal decay resumes. Birds land in the south pasture for the first time in eight months.

The fig tree in the Jesuit ruins keeps growing, inexplicably, for years. Nobody removes it.

The Luz Mala never appears at the estancia again.

---

### Final Lines

**Kuyen, the morning after**:
> *"It will scar over. Land does. It won't be what it was."* She is already packing the leather panels — she will take them, and burn them, away from here.

**Nahuel, the morning after** (if alive and present):
> *"I still feel the corners. They're quiet now. I think I always will — feel them, I mean. But it's like knowing where a door used to be. Not like standing in it."*

**Rosa** (if contacted, later):
> *"Remember it. All of it. Even the parts you'd rather not. The ones who didn't make it deserve to be remembered by someone who was there."*

**Silvio** (sometime after, once the shadow is back in the right direction):
> *"I've been thinking about going back to my family's house in Mendoza. I don't think I've thought about that in months."* He looks surprised by this. *"That's—"* He stops. *"That's normal, isn't it."*

## GM NOTES

**Don't rush the aftermath**: The ritual's completion is not the chapter's end. The aftermath scenes are where the campaign's emotional payload is delivered. Give each NPC their moment. Don't skip Silvio. Don't skip Rosa. Don't skip what happens when Marta laughs involuntarily.

**Don Eusebio's question — "What do I owe?"**: This is the campaign's final moral question, and it belongs entirely to the investigators. Do not guide them toward an answer. Let them decide what justice looks like, what accountability looks like, in a situation where the law cannot reach and the harm was profound.

**The Marks on investigators**: The entity is gone from this location. The Mark — the 2D6 trigger from Chapter 3 — does not activate against something that is no longer pressing through the geometry. The Marks remain, but they are quiet. Like scar tissue. Occasionally, in the years after, an investigator will stand in a room with precise right angles and feel, briefly, as if the space is slightly wrong. It passes.

**Quirce's notebook**: Contains observations that are genuinely valuable and genuinely dangerous. What he does with them — whether his oblique offer of using the network for closing rather than observing is sincere — is for future stories. It can be ignored or it can be a hook. Either is valid.

**The campaign's final question**: Nine chapters have asked: *what do you owe for what you set in motion?* Don Eusebio set it in motion by opening it. The investigators set it in motion by working for the wrong side. Saens set it in motion by being the first one, in this cycle, to find the scar and touch it with human intention. The answer the campaign gives is not absolution — it is presence. You owe your presence in the consequences. You stay. You pay. You remember.

## FULL CAMPAIGN CLOSE — WHAT THE PLAYERS SHOULD FEEL

- Grief for the people who didn't make it — the four tribe members from Chapter 3, Dolores, the one person from San Ruiz who never came back
- Complicated relief — it is over, and it cost something, and the cost was real
- Respect for Kuyen and Nahuel, earned over nine chapters
- Complicated feelings about Don Eusebio — not forgiveness, but the recognition of a person rather than a villain
- The weight of the Marks they carry — not a curse, a memory
- Something like pride — they held the circle, they paid what was asked, they saw it through
- And beneath all of it, quietly: the land is just land again. It took work. It always takes work.

## EXPANSION NOTES — BRIDGING TO THE OVERVIEW FRAMING

The detailed prep above runs *El Cierre*: phases 4 and 5 of the closing, the seal, the cost, the aftermath. The `overview.md` framing of Arc 3 ends with the players believing the Necronomicón may be necessary. The existing prep already lands close to this — Mercedes' speech in C8 expansion seeded it; the Coypac thread (C7–C8 expansion) makes it operationally true. Use the scenes below to land Arc 3 *as a bridge* into Arc 4 instead of as a campaign finale.

### Reskinning summary

- The closing in the existing prep is *the campaign's final ritual*. In the new framing, **it is a partial closing**. The pit at La Esperanza closes successfully tonight. **The pits at Coypac do not close** — the cult succeeded, partially, in their awakening attempt; they retreated with their Necronomicón hand-copy intact; the pampas have one fewer scar and the cordillera has one new active site.
- The campaign does not end. The players ride east. Buenos Aires is the next destination.
- The aftermath scenes (the survivors, the land, the final lines) all hold; they need only a short *next-day* coda to reframe them as the *end of Arc 3* rather than the end of the campaign.

### NEW SCENE — THE COYPAC NEWS, AT DAWN

Insert this immediately after the ritual completes in the existing prep, in the dawn light, before the long aftermath sequence. A second runner arrives — not Saúl Carrasco (he is dead, his body found by the cult and left at a *paraje* on the post road as a warning; news of this comes in the days following), but a different rider, sent by Doña Mercedes' Buenos Aires colleagues by relay.

The runner carries a single-page report.

> *"Coypac está cerrado parcialmente. Un pozo cedió, el otro no. La cuadrilla se retiró antes del amanecer hacia el norte. Llevan un objeto de tamaño de un libro envuelto en encerado. Dos sacerdotes muertos, los demás vivos. La tribuna de Salamanca quedó marcada — la geometría se grabó en la roca de la cordillera durante la apertura parcial. Los Custodios están reuniéndose en Buenos Aires. Mercedes, quedate o volvete según veas. — Saráchaga."*
>
> *"Coypac is partially closed. One pit gave, the other did not. The group retreated before dawn to the north. They are carrying a book-sized object wrapped in oilcloth. Two priests dead, the rest alive. The Salamanca grandstand is marked — the geometry burned itself into the cordillera rock during the partial opening. The Keepers are gathering in Buenos Aires. Mercedes, stay or come back as you see fit. — Saráchaga."*

This is the campaign's first time the Buenos Aires Keeper named **Saráchaga** appears in writing. He is, in Arc 4, the senior dissenter; here he is just a name on a letter.

**Mercedes' response to the runner**: she reads the letter. She closes her eyes. She does not weep. She says, quietly, to the players:

> *"Saúl está muerto. Lo voy a saber con seguridad en una semana. Y el libro de los del Gran Durmiente sigue en sus manos, y lo van a llevar a Buenos Aires antes de que termine el año. Voy a Buenos Aires. Si quieren venir, ahora es cuando se prepara el viaje."*  — *"Saúl is dead. I will know for sure in a week. And the book of the Great Sleeper's people is still in their hands, and they will take it to Buenos Aires before the year is out. I am going to Buenos Aires. If you want to come, now is when the trip is prepared."*

### NEW SCENE — DON EUSEBIO, AFTER

In the aftermath, after his line *"What do I owe?"*, add a short scene that bridges him toward the new framing's continuing campaign.

Don Eusebio learns of the Coypac partial-failure with the same expression he had when Concepción walked into the pit. He does not collapse. He says:

> *"Voy con ustedes a Buenos Aires. No para defenderme — para ayudar. Si el libro completo está allá y hay forma de cerrarlo, voy a estar en el círculo otra vez. Es lo último que le debo a esta tierra."*  — *"I am going to Buenos Aires with you. Not to defend myself — to help. If the complete book is there and there is a way to close it, I will be in the circle again. It is the last thing I owe this land."*

This sets up Don Eusebio as a possible Arc 4 outer-ring participant in C12. In the existing prep, he is left at the estancia to face his consequences. In the expanded version, he chooses to ride east.

If the Keeper does not want to bring Don Eusebio to Arc 4: he says this, and the players talk him out of it (or the legal authorities arrest him before he can ride out). Either is valid. The line is offered to him; the choice of whether to use it is the table's.

### NEW SCENE — THE CURSED GAUCHO STAYS WEST, TEMPORARILY

The Cursed Gaucho leaves the camp before noon on the day after the closing. He is going west, not east. He says, to the players:

> *"Coypac es de donde vienen los que mataron a los míos. Voy a buscar el rastro de los doce sacerdotes y voy a alcanzar a los que pueda. Después voy a Buenos Aires. Yo llego antes que ustedes — los espero ahí. En la curtiembre del Riachuelo. La que tiene tres galpones. Los van a saber encontrar."*
>
> *"Coypac is where the ones who killed mine came from. I am going to find the trail of the twelve priests and reach the ones I can. Then I am going to Buenos Aires. I will arrive before you do — I will wait for you there. At the tannery on the Riachuelo. The one with three sheds. You will know to find it."*

He rides out alone. The next time the players see him is **Chapter 11**, in Buenos Aires. Between this chapter and that one, he has — off-screen — killed at least four of the twelve priests, lost a fight against three more, and ridden east on a stolen horse.

### NEW SCENE — THE RIDE EAST

The aftermath in the existing prep covers the survivors at the estancia and Rosa at San Ruiz. In the expanded version, add a brief **travel montage** at the very end of the chapter.

> *Three days later. The party rides east. Mercedes is in a hired carriage with Don Eusebio (if he came), and Saúl Carrasco's empty saddlebag tied to her own saddle. Kuyen and Nahuel ride parallel. Marta and Héctor are with them — they wanted to see Buenos Aires once before the geometric alignment finished fading from them. Silvio is back at the estancia, tending the corrals, healing.*
>
> *On the first afternoon east, the party passes through San Ruiz. Rosa is in the square, alone, sweeping the ground in front of the chapel. The goat is still in the well. As the party passes the well, the goat looks up. **A Marked PC who looks down at her sees, for the first time, that she has six pupils — three in each eye — arranged in a line.** No one else sees this. The Marked PC also realizes, in the same look, that the goat has been watching the campaign all along, and that she will be there when they ride back, and when their grandchildren ride back. Rosa, without looking up from her sweeping, says: "Adios, gente. Buen viaje." — "Goodbye, people. Safe journey." She does not stop sweeping. The party rides on.*
>
> *On the second day east, the party stops at the Jesuit ruins. The fig tree is still there. Marta walks to the chapel stone — the carved stone from C5 — and places her hand on it once. She says nothing. She walks back to the horses. She mounts. They ride on.*
>
> *On the fourth day, the smoke of Buenos Aires is on the horizon — saladero smoke, port smoke, the city already visible as a low brown haze before the buildings appear.*
>
> *Mercedes rides up beside whichever player has been most attentive to her since C7. She says, looking at the haze: "Esta es la parte difícil. Lo del campo era el principio. Lo de la ciudad es el resto." — "This is the hard part. The country was the beginning. The city is the rest."*

This image — the road east, the fig tree, the city haze — is the bridge into Arc 4 C10's *La Llegada*.
