# CHAPTER 8 — LA TRAICIÓN
### *The Betrayal*

**Chapter Goal**: The ritual begins. Quirce makes his final move. Don Eusebio faces the moment. The entity presses for breach. Establish what Chapter 9 will need to overcome.
**Emotional Arc**: Commitment → Crisis → Desperate Holding
**Key Seed**: The betrayal isn't just Quirce's. Don Eusebio has one more lie in him. Whether he tells it determines everything.

---

## OPENING SITUATION

Morning after the eve. The group prepares. Kuyen draws the final binding panel at dawn. Nahuel redraws his palm markings. Silvio, Héctor, and Marta take positions at the outer edge of the pit's boundary without being asked — they know where they need to stand.

The ritual is scheduled for dusk — the same hour Kuyen used for the partial bindings, when the light makes the geometry of shadows longest and the entity's ability to move through surface angles is slightly compromised. Six hours remain.

Six hours in which nothing should go wrong, and two things do.

---

## FIRST FRACTURE: DON EUSEBIO'S DEFECTION

Somewhere between noon and late afternoon, Don Eusebio disappears.

Not permanently. He does not flee. But an investigator who looks for him after the midday heat will find him: at the edge of the south pasture, standing at the fence line in the position Silvio used to occupy, gripping the topmost wire, looking south at the pit.

He is not in distress. He is considering.

**If not intercepted**: He will return on his own after an hour. He will be quieter and more resolved than before — in a way that could be resolution toward the ritual or resolution toward something else.

**If intercepted**: He will not lie about what he was thinking.

> *"I was thinking about leaving."* He says this plainly. *"Getting on a horse and riding north. Getting on a train. Not being here for whatever happens tonight."*

*(Pause.)*

> *"I didn't leave. I'm still here. But I wanted you to know I thought about it. So whatever I do tonight, you know I had the choice and I didn't take it. I want that to — I want it to mean something."*

This is, in its way, an act of courage. Not heroism. But the deliberate choice to be present in a moment he is terrified of, and the need to have a witness to that choice.

**Roleplaying hook**: The investigator's response here matters. If they acknowledge what he said without dismissal or false comfort — *"I know"*, or *"Then be there tonight"* — Don Eusebio will stand in that outer ring without flinching when it comes. If they dismiss him or respond with contempt, he will still stand in the ring, but he will be fighting himself as well as the entity's pressure, and Kuyen will feel that in the geometry.

---

## SECOND FRACTURE: QUIRCE'S MOVE

This happens at dusk, as the ritual begins.

### The Setup

Quirce has positioned his theodolite on a rise to the east of the pit — outside the effective geometry range, as he agreed. His notebook is open. He has been, by all appearances, keeping his word.

What he has not kept: the corrected asymmetry in the outer ring. If investigators corrected Kuyen's panel in Chapter 7, this is no longer a threat. If they did not, it is still present in the geometry and will activate at maximum closure pressure.

But Quirce has a second move that has nothing to do with the panels.

**What Quirce Does**: As the ritual reaches its second phase — when Kuyen deploys the eastern segment and Nahuel anchors the center — Quirce moves. He crosses inside the outer ring with one of his brass instruments. Not aggressively. Quietly. He steps over the chalked outer boundary and crouches at a position that puts him inside the geometry's effective range.

He is not trying to disrupt the ritual. He is trying to observe from the inside. He believes the difference in observational resolution between inside and outside the geometry at peak closure is significant enough to justify the risk. He has — from his perspective — calculated that his single instrument and his body mass will not affect the ritual's success.

**He is wrong**. A person inside the outer ring who is not an anchor, not the opener, and not an aligned Wrong Returned creates a structural inconsistency in the geometry — an unexpected node that the entity will immediately identify and exploit as an unguarded position. It is, mechanically, the equivalent of a gap in the outer ring.

**When investigators notice**: A **Spot Hidden** check when Quirce crosses the boundary. On success: *"He is inside the geometry."* The effective range is clear once you know it — the grass is slightly different inside the ring, the air temperature is one degree lower, the sound from the pit is louder. He crossed a line that, once seen, is obvious.

**The window**: Investigators have one round — perhaps 30 seconds of ritual time — to either pull Quirce out or compensate for his presence before the entity detects the inconsistency. Kuyen will not notice immediately (she is concentrating). Nahuel will know in seconds — and will say nothing, because disrupting his focus to speak would be worse.

---

## SCENE 1 — THE RITUAL BEGINS

### The Assembly

Kuyen leads everyone to their positions. The leather panels are laid in the pit's upper ring — the complete binding geometry in sections that interlock. As each piece is placed, the glow in the blood arcs below intensifies slightly, as if the new geometry is resonating with the existing pattern.

**Positions**:
- **Nahuel**: Center of the symbol. Kneeling. Arms at his sides. Eyes open.
- **Kuyen**: Mobile, moving between sections, maintaining the symbol, the working in her hands and voice.
- **Héctor and Marta**: Eastern and western anchor positions in the outer ring. They stand without instruction, as if they've been to a rehearsal the investigators weren't invited to.
- **Silvio**: Northern position. He stands with his back to the ritual, facing outward — he is not maintaining the symbol, he is listening for the entity's movements in the geometry and will call direction if it approaches.
- **Don Eusebio**: Southern outer ring position — closest to the pit's edge on the side where the opening is strongest. Kuyen has placed him there deliberately. He knows it. Before the ritual begins, while Kuyen is still moving the panels, Don Eusebio stands at his position. He removes his hat. He looks at the dead grass within the bowl — the same grass he has not been able to look at squarely for eight months. He says, quietly, as Tomás once said it at the rim of this same pit:

  > *"Por la tierra que fue buena, y por la tierra que se cansó. Que la tierra descanse..."*

  He stops. He cannot remember the third line. He stands for ten seconds with his hat in his hand, eyes closed. Then he puts the hat back on and takes his position.

  > **Atmospheric runner — the prayers (4)**: This is the campaign's second utterance of *Por la tierra que fue buena*. **Don Eusebio not finishing the prayer** is the point — he has not said it in twenty years (since his wife died) and the third line will not come back to him. The investigator who stood with Tomás at the pit-rim in C1 may finish it for him, silently or aloud. If they do, Don Eusebio will look at them across the geometry once during Phase 3 and nod. He stands without flinching for the rest of the closing.

- **Investigators**: Fill remaining positions as needed. If there are not enough investigators to complete the outer ring, Kuyen has prepared for this: she walks two positions at once, but her working suffers.

**Read aloud — the opening**:

> *The sun touches the horizon. Kuyen's voice begins — not loud, not melodic, the same angular chant from the creek bed weeks ago. She moves between the panels, and as she passes each section it brightens. The entity in the pit responds immediately: the vibration in the chest deepens. The carved walls below pulse with a light that is not warm.*
>
> *Nahuel closes his eyes and opens them and is somewhere else. Not gone — present, but the presence is directed inward, into the center of the shape. His breathing slows to something close to absence.*
>
> *The geometry above the pit begins to close. You can feel it — not with the body's normal senses, but with something underneath them. Something that knows angles even when it can't see them.*

**SAN Check**: 0/1D4 (the ritual beginning — the reality of it, the geometry responding)

## SCENE 2 — THE ENTITY RESPONDS

The entity does not wait politely.

Within three minutes of the ritual's opening, the vibration intensifies. The carved walls of the pit begin to produce something other than light: a slow movement of geometry, the lines extending upward along the walls as if being drawn by something below. The entity is countering — not disrupting the geometry, but adding to it, elaborating it, trying to convert the binding symbol into an opening symbol by extension.

Kuyen sees this. She begins counter-drawing faster.

**This creates the first major pressure point**: The geometry is being contested. It is not enough to have the right symbol — the right symbol must be held against an entity that understands it better than any human and can modify it from within.

**Mechanical effect**: At the end of each ritual phase (there are five — see Chapter 9), the entity makes a contest against the ritual. How well investigators have managed everything to this point determines the difficulty of that contest. If the outer ring is intact, if Nahuel has support, if the asymmetry was corrected, if Quirce is not inside the ring — the contests are manageable. If any of these conditions failed, add one level of difficulty to each contest.

---

## SCENE 3 — THE QUIRCE CRISIS

The moment Quirce crosses the outer ring boundary, several things happen:

1. Silvio turns around. He is facing inward now instead of outward. He says, very quietly: *"Someone is inside the ring who shouldn't be."*
2. Nahuel's breathing changes. The ochre patterns on his palms begin to blur — not wash away, blur, as if the geometry they represent is being distorted near the inconsistency.
3. The entity stops extending the carved lines and locates the inconsistency in approximately 15 seconds.

**If investigators act immediately** (pull Quirce out or physically replace him with a correct participant):
- The entity's attention is briefly on the gap, then returns to contesting the ritual. One extra difficulty level for this phase only. No permanent damage.

**If investigators cannot act immediately** (they're in positions, they'd have to break their position):
- The entity invades through the Quirce-gap. Not a full breach — more like a finger inserted into a wound. One investigator in a nearby outer ring position takes 1D6 SAN from the proximity. The phase fails and must be restarted, costing 10 minutes of ritual time. The entity is more deeply mapped into the geometry now.

**Quirce's response to being confronted or pulled**:

He does not resist. He steps out when instructed. He is also, if observers are paying attention, shaking — not from cold, from what he experienced inside the geometry for those 15 seconds. His notebook is open and he has been writing, rapidly, in cipher. Whatever he saw from inside the geometry has given him what he came for, and it has cost him something he didn't expect to pay.

He does not speak about what he experienced. Not this chapter.

---

## SCENE 4 — DON EUSEBIO IN THE RING

The most important scene in this chapter. Don Eusebio is in the southern outer ring position and the entity knows it. The entity has been aware of Don Eusebio since he first drew the geometry. He is, from the entity's perspective, the most significant node in the outer ring — the one who opened the wound, whose spatial recognition pattern is embedded in the scar's structure.

When the entity locates him in the ring, it shifts its effort. It stops contesting the geometry broadly and begins pressing the southern position specifically.

**What Don Eusebio experiences**:

> *Something is looking at him. Not from the pit — from everywhere at once, from the corners of the space, from the angles the fires make with the flat earth. It is the same attention he felt in the study, in the first months, when things were working and the land was producing and there was a sound from beneath the ground that he told himself was cattle. It is that attention, clarified. Stripped of the comfort of denial.*
>
> *And in that attention, an offer. Not in words. In certainty. The land will produce again. The cattle will be fat. The drought will end. All of it, available, if he simply steps out of the ring. Out of the geometry. Back to the version of things where he is the patrón and the land obeys.*

**Don Eusebio's decision**: This is his final test. He will hear the offer. He will stand there while it is made. Whether he steps out or stays depends on everything that has happened in the last six chapters — specifically whether investigators have treated him as a human being capable of choice, or as a tool to be managed.

**If the relationship has been built (investigators engaged with him honestly)**:

He stays. He grips the outer ring marker — a stone Kuyen placed — with both hands and does not move. His face is not heroic. It is the face of someone experiencing something terrible and not leaving. He says nothing.

**If the relationship was purely transactional or hostile**:

He falters. He takes one step back, one step out of his position. The outer ring geometry breaks. The southern segment of the binding fails. Kuyen shouts. Nahuel reaches his limit. The phase collapses.

**Recovery** (if he steps out): Don Eusebio can be returned to position by an investigator physically escorting him back — hand on his shoulder, clear eye contact, the command: *"Don Eusebio. Back."* He will go. But the position is now defined by his being held there by someone else rather than by his own will, and the geometry will reflect that — it holds, but the resistance to the entity at the southern position is weaker.

**SAN Check** (watching Don Eusebio experience the offer): 0/1D4 — not for the entity, but for the comprehension that this is what he's been running from, and he is not running now.

---

## END OF CHAPTER 8 — THE HOLDING POSITION

The chapter ends not at resolution but at a sustained crisis point. The ritual is running but not complete. One or more of the following situations is live:

1. **The ritual is behind schedule**: The entity has forced at least one phase restart. Time is running out before dawn, after which the geometry's efficacy drops.
2. **Nahuel is at the edge**: He has been the focus for six weeks total now. Tonight's working is the hardest he has faced. If no investigator co-anchored with him, he is at the point where another full phase may not be possible.
3. **The outer ring is intact but fragile**: Don Eusebio held (or was held). Quirce is outside. The Wrong Returned are in position. But everyone is exhausted and the entity has mapped the geometry's weak points.
4. **The phase count**: Whatever phases were completed this chapter carry forward to Chapter 9's total of five. If Chapter 8 completed phases 1–3, Chapter 9 begins at phase 4. If the entity forced restarts, the count may be lower.

**End image**:

> *The pit below is fully illuminated by its own geometry — a map of itself, glowing from the carved walls, the arcs in the soil, the sections of the leather binding symbol Kuyen has placed. The entity's counter-movement has slowed. Not stopped — but slowed. Something in the outer ring's integrity is holding it back.*
>
> *Kuyen looks around the ring. She counts. She nods — one small, precise motion.*
>
> *"Two more phases. Don't move."*

## GM NOTES

**Don Eusebio's decision is the moral hinge of the campaign**: Everything from Chapter 1 has been building to him standing in that ring and choosing whether to stay or leave. Make it real. Don't give him an easy out and don't make it impossible. The choice should feel genuinely uncertain.

**Quirce's breach is the final stress test of investigator preparation**: If they caught the asymmetry and corrected it, if they kept Quirce outside the geometry, they have done the work and the consequences are survivable. If they missed things or trusted him too much, the consequences are harder. This is the CoC principle from Arc 1 in its final form: preparation is not guaranteed, but it matters.

**The entity's offer to Don Eusebio should be vivid but wordless**: Describe what he feels, not what he hears. *"The land will produce again"* — but render it as a sensation of abundance, of the ground being fertile beneath his feet, of his life at the estancia restored to what it was before he understood what that cost. The offer is real. He can still take it. That's what makes it an offer.

**Nahuel's status is the mechanical pressure**: Track his condition carefully. If investigators co-anchored in Chapter 7, he has more reserves. If not, he is at the limit and Chapter 9 will require a choice about the focus.

## EXPANSION NOTES — BRIDGING TO THE OVERVIEW FRAMING

The detailed prep above runs *La Traición*: Don Eusebio's defection moment, Quirce's breach, the entity contesting the geometry, ritual phases 1–3. The `overview.md` framing wants the **Cult of the Great Sleeper as central threat**, the **failed ritual encounter outside Salamanca tied to awakening**, and **Doña Mercedes explaining the necessity of the Necronomicón**. Use the scenes below to interleave the parallel Coypac ritual into the existing pit-side action.

### Reskinning summary

- The chapter now runs *two rituals in parallel*: the closing at the burial pit (existing prep) and the awakening attempt at **El Pozo de Coypac** in the cordillera (new). The players are at the pit; news of Coypac arrives during the ritual via Saúl Carrasco's return (or his failure to return). The two events are mechanically coupled — failures at one site benefit the other.
- The **Cursed Gaucho** is in the outer ring tonight by Kuyen's decision (he asked, she agreed, with terms). He is paying attention to El Patrón / Dadálah specifically; if El Patrón manifests at the pit even briefly, the Cursed Gaucho will break formation to reach him.
- **Doña Mercedes** is present and active. She is the Keeper voice. She will, tonight, name the **Necronomicón** as a necessary tool for the *next* closing — not this one. Her speech here seeds Arc 4.

### NEW SCENE — SAÚL CARRASCO'S RETURN

Insert this between Phase 1 and Phase 2 of the existing ritual structure (before the ritual fully gathers steam, while a small breath is possible).

A horseman approaches from the west at full gallop. It is Saúl Carrasco. He arrived at Coypac before dawn yesterday, observed the cult's preparations, rode all night returning. He is at the limit of horse and rider. His message is short, delivered before he dismounts.

> *"Doce sacerdotes. Dos pozos en realidad — uno seco, uno con agua. Los están abriendo al mismo tiempo. El sacerdote principal es un hombre alto, viejo, vestido de ocre. Sabe el nombre de Dadálah. Lo dijo en alta voz cuando me vio."*  — *"Twelve priests. Two pits actually — one dry, one with water. They are opening both at the same time. The principal priest is a tall, old man, dressed in ochre. He knows the name Dadálah. He said it aloud when he saw me."*

Then Saúl realizes he has been seen. *"Pero me vieron. Ahora saben que yo vine. Y van a estar buscando a Doña Mercedes a partir de esta noche. Lo siento, doña."*  — *"But they saw me. Now they know I came. And they will be looking for Doña Mercedes from tonight on. I am sorry, doña."*

Mercedes places her hand on Saúl's shoulder. *"Bien hecho, Saúl. Quedate atrás del círculo. No quiero perderte esta noche también."* — *"Well done, Saúl. Stay behind the circle. I don't want to lose you tonight as well."*

**Mechanical effect**: knowing the Coypac ritual has *twelve priests, two pits, simultaneous opening attempt* gives Kuyen actionable information. She accelerates the closing tempo by one phase — what would have taken Phase 4 to anticipate now happens at Phase 3. **Phase 3's contest difficulty is reduced by one step** because of Saúl's intelligence. This is the mechanical payoff for sending the runner.

### NEW SCENE — THE COYPAC ECHO

Insert this in mid-Phase 2, when the entity is contesting the geometry. The Marked investigators experience something they did not in C7's communication: not a presence at the pit, but a *connection* between the pit and Coypac. A Marked investigator perceives the vision on a **POW** roll (Regular, *not* Hard — Marked PCs are now operating in their primary medium, see `overview.md` *Mark of Tindalos*):

> *Two pits, simultaneously, in the same vision. The pit you are at. The pit at Coypac. They are connected by a line that is not visible to anyone not Marked. The line passes through the cordillera. At Coypac, twelve figures are arranged around the dry pit — chanting in time with the entity's vibration. At the water pit, a single figure is standing waist-deep in the water, holding something the size of a brick wrapped in oilcloth. The Marked investigator does not need a Cthulhu Mythos roll to recognize what they are holding. It is a book.*

The cult has a *piece of the Necronomicón* at Coypac. Not the whole book — a section, hand-copied, carried east from somewhere. The brick-sized object is one of the cult's hand copies — possibly bound from pages copied by the Great Wizard of Salamanca over decades.

> **C12 redundancy (clue retrieval)**: Two guarantees so this critical Arc 3 → Arc 4 bridge does not depend on the POW roll alone:
>
> 1. **Difficulty lowered** to Regular POW (above). Marked PCs are operating in geometric perception now; this is the kind of thing the Mark exists to do.
> 2. **Mercedes confirms the Coypac vision after the ritual**. If no Marked PC made the POW roll, Mercedes will say at the bonfire after Phase 5 — looking west into the dark — *"Yo lo vi también. Doce sacerdotes alrededor de un pozo seco. Otro hombre en el pozo de agua, con un libro envuelto en encerado. ¿Lo vieron ustedes? — Para mí no es la primera vez."* — *"I saw it too. Twelve priests around a dry pit. Another man in the water pit, with a book wrapped in oilcloth. Did you see it? — It is not the first time for me."* Mercedes is not Marked; she has read enough to extrapolate. She is, in this moment, redundantly delivering the vision the players' Mark already gave them, *or* delivering it to them outright if they missed it. **Either way the table has the fact.**

**SAN Check**: 1/1D4 — the realization that *the awakening attempt is being conducted with text from the same book the players are riding toward in Arc 4*.

The Marked investigator can communicate this to Mercedes between phases. Mercedes' response, low:

> *"Si tienen una copia, la copia se va a quemar con esa pozo cuando cierre. Pero si no cerramos, se la llevan, y la próxima vez que aparezca esa copia va a ser en Buenos Aires. Hay que cerrar bien."*  — *"If they have a copy, the copy will burn with that pit when it closes. But if we do not close, they will carry it away, and the next time that copy appears it will be in Buenos Aires. We must close well."*

This is the campaign's most direct articulation that the Coypac ritual's failure is the Buenos Aires arc's necessity.

### NEW SCENE — THE CURSED GAUCHO BREAKS FORMATION

Insert this as Quirce's crisis (the existing scene where Quirce crosses the outer ring boundary). The Cursed Gaucho is in the western outer ring position. He has been still for two hours.

When Quirce crosses the line and the entity locates the inconsistency, the Cursed Gaucho turns his head — slowly, deliberately, *not toward Quirce*. He is looking past Quirce, into the dark beyond the ring. He sees something the rest of the party does not.

For five seconds, in the moment of geometric distortion the Quirce-gap creates, **El Patrón is briefly visible** at the western edge of the camp — perhaps thirty meters out, standing alone, watching the ritual. Not glowing, not radiating. Just standing. The same unremarkable face the players will recognize fully in Arc 4 C11.

**Only the Cursed Gaucho and any Marked PC see him** during the five seconds the gap is open. The unmarked players do not. Mercedes does. Mercedes does not move; she has seen him before.

The Cursed Gaucho breaks formation. He runs at El Patrón across the outer ring, through what is now a gap in the geometry. **This breaks the western position more thoroughly than Quirce's crossing did.** Kuyen is forced to compensate immediately or lose the phase entirely. Phase 2 fails and must restart.

The Cursed Gaucho reaches the spot where El Patrón was standing. El Patrón is gone — vanished in the same moment the geometric gap closes. The Cursed Gaucho stands in the dark, facón in hand, alone, breathing hard.

He returns to formation slowly. He says nothing. His face is locked. **He has confirmed, tonight, that El Patrón knows him by sight and is willing to come within knife range to remind him of it.**

**Effect on the ritual**: Phase 2 must restart. Loss of 10 minutes. Kuyen is angry but not surprised. *"Si vuelve a salir del círculo, no va a llegar al cierre. Lo perdemos."* — *"If he leaves the circle again, he doesn't make it to the closing. We lose him."*

**Effect on the player relationship with the Cursed Gaucho**: significant. He has chosen El Patrón over the closing, briefly. He has demonstrated the priority that will define his role in Arc 4: *if El Patrón is in reach, the Cursed Gaucho goes for El Patrón, regardless of cost*. Players who recognize this and plan around it will be ahead of him.

**SAN Check** on the players who saw El Patrón: 0/1D4 — for the casualness of his presence at the very edge of a closing ritual, watching.

> *"Lo que están haciendo Kuyen y los suyos — alcanza para esta noche. ¿Vos sentís lo mismo que yo? Que para mañana ya no alcanza."*

### Observable consequences (D5)

- **At the start of each phase**: The candles around the inner ring lean *inward* about 20° in the direction of the next phase's pressure. Players watching the candles can tell which side will be tested next without anyone announcing it.
- **When Don Eusebio takes his position in the outer ring**: His shadow on the ground is, briefly, *two shadows* — one his, one slightly larger and offset. The second shadow is gone within five seconds. Marked PCs see this clearly; unmarked PCs see something they will dismiss as the firelight flickering. (The entity is checking whether he will hold.)
- **When Quirce crosses the line**: The dust on the ground behind him does not settle. It hangs in air, in the shape of a footprint, for ten full seconds. The print remains visible until the next gust of wind disturbs it. *He has stepped through the geometry, and the geometry is keeping a record.*
- **During El Patrón's brief western manifestation**: The locust scouts above (audible since the Zonda chapter) *stop moving* in the air. They hover. For five seconds, no insect anywhere within fifty meters of the camp moves. When El Patrón is gone, they resume their flight in formation toward the cordillera.
- **At the climax (Phase 5 in the existing prep)**: Anyone with a fired bullet still in their body (Cursed Gaucho is the obvious candidate) feels the bullet *warm* — not hot, just no longer at body temperature, more like a coal that has been cooling for hours. The geometry knows where iron is. The Cursed Gaucho will note this without comment and roll his shoulder once.
