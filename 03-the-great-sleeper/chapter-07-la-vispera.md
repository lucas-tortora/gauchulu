# ARC 3 — EL CIERRE
## CHAPTER 7 — LA VÍSPERA
### *The Eve*

**Arc 3 Goal**: Close what was opened. Pay what the closing costs. Live with what remains.
**Arc 3 Theme**: Some scars don't heal — they become part of the land. What matters is what you do before the wound closes.
**Arc 3 Emotional Arc**: Desperate Resolve → Sacrifice → Grief and Release

---

**Chapter Goal**: The night before the final ritual. Preparations, revelations, last conversations. The entity at its most active. Everyone's last chance to be honest.
**Emotional Arc**: Anticipation → Dread → Bone-deep resolve
**Key Seed**: The pit at night, fully active, is not the same place it was in Chapter 1. It has been worked on from below.

---

## OPENING SITUATION

Everyone is camped near the pit. The investigators, Don Eusebio, Quirce, Silvio, Héctor, Marta, Kuyen, and Nahuel. Nine people around three small fires — the fires are not for warmth, it is summer, but for the geometry they create in the dark, for the way fire defines angles and pushes the shapeless back.

Kuyen is preparing: drawing the binding symbol in sections on cured leather panels that can be carried and assembled at the pit. She works by firelight without speaking. Nahuel sits near her and faces south. His hands rest on his thighs with the ochre patterns freshly drawn on his palms.

This chapter is about what happens in the hours before the ritual begins. It is also where the final fractures appear.

## THE PIT AT NIGHT — CHANGED

### Setting Description

> *The pit is different at night.*
>
> *In daylight it is a depression in the earth — ten meters across, a meter deep at the center, the collapsed layers of carcasses at the bottom dried and collapsed into something flat and dark. The blood arcs are faded but present in the soil. The geometry is visible if you know what you're looking at.*
>
> *At night, the geometry is visible to anyone. The blood arcs glow faintly — not in the way phosphorescent things glow, not biological, not fire. They glow the way something glows when it is the origin of the light rather than its reflection. From the pit's edge, looking down, you can see the complete outer circle and the inner arcs and where they intersect. It is beautiful in exactly the way that a very precise and very wrong thing can be beautiful.*
>
> *The pit is also deeper than it was. The investigators were in it in Chapter 1. It was one meter deep then. It is now three meters. The walls are not slumped — this is not erosion. The sides are vertical, and the walls show the same carved geometry as Saens's chapel stone, extending downward. Something has been carving from beneath.*

**SAN Check**: 1/1D4 — the glowing geometry, the additional depth, the carved walls.

**The Sound**: At the pit's edge, from 2 AM onward, a sound is audible — not heard with the ears, exactly, but felt in the chest. The vibration from Chapter 3 is here at its source and substantially stronger. A **Hard CON check** to sleep through it after 2 AM; on failure, the investigator is awake for the rest of the night. Not due to fear — due to the sound's effect on sleep architecture.

## SCENE 1 — LAST CONVERSATIONS

The night before the ritual is when characters say the things they haven't said. The Keeper should manage these conversations individually, giving each NPC time.

---

### Don Eusebio — The Confession

At some point before midnight, Don Eusebio will seek out an investigator — specifically one who has treated him with consistent honesty, not cruelty. If no such investigator exists, he seeks out Silvio (which is worse for him, and should be played that way).

He does not confess dramatically. He sits beside the fire and says:

> *"When the cattle started dying, the first ones — the land was producing like nothing I'd seen. Fat animals, deep grass, good water. I told myself the ritual was working but something needed to be calibrated. Not that I'd done something wrong. That I needed to adjust."*

He is quiet for a moment.

> *"There were nine more weeks of that reasoning."*

If the investigator listens without judgment, he continues:

> *"The first person I saw walking south at night — it was Concepción. The girl. Thirteen years old, an orphan my wife took in before she passed. She was walking in the grass, barefoot, in her nightdress, going south like she'd been called. I ran out and brought her back. Then I caught her the next week, and the week after that. And she was getting better at slipping past me. I told myself I would handle it. That I had time."*

He looks at the fire.

> *"Then you came. And you saw what I saw. You saw her go into the pit like she was going home. And you saw her start undoing the thing I'd done — with her little hands, with a rock, because she knew how. A thirteen-year-old girl knew how to close it and I didn't."*

*(A long silence.)*

> *"If you hadn't been there that afternoon, she would have died in that pit. The cattle would have reached her before she could finish. I knew it when Tomás came running to the house shouting her name and I understood what she had gone to do. And I rode east — not to help her. To get away from the sight of it. Because I couldn't bear to see whether you would save her or whether she would save you."*

He grips his knees.

> *"When I came back two days later and saw she was alive, I sent her to her cousin's house in Azul the next morning. I told myself it was for her education. It was because I could not look at her anymore."*

He stops. He looks down at his hands. The next sentence is the one he has been waiting six months to say to someone.

> *"She hums it. She hums the rhythm of the pit. Did you hear her, that first morning, when she brought you water? She doesn't know she does it. Her cousin in Azul wrote me last month — she still hums it at night, in her sleep. Her cousin asked me what tune it is. I told her it's an old country tune. I lied to her cousin. I lied to her cousin because I am a coward and because the truth is — there is nothing I can do for her. The pit has her tongue. It will have it for as long as she lives. That is what a thirteen-year-old girl carries because I needed eight more head of cattle."*

This is the **payoff for the hum that was first audible in C1** (Don Eusebio's foreman or one of the players catching her humming under her breath when she brought the *mate*; see Chapter 1, *The Hire*, GM notes). If the players noticed it in C1 — even as a passing observation — they should be told now: *that tune was the rhythm the pit beats against the soles of feet at night.* If the players did not notice it in C1, this is still where it lands; the fact that they *missed* it then is its own quiet horror.

This is the last significant reveal about Don Eusebio's character. He did not protect her before she was injured — the investigators did. He sent her away afterward out of shame, not virtue. The honesty is itself the redemption, such as it is. The girl was saved, but not by him. **And she carries the rhythm anyway.**

> **Optional callback (C9 aftermath)**: If the closing succeeds, the players who saved Concepción in C1 may want to visit her in Azul during the long aftermath. She is alive. She is going to school. She does not hum anymore. The night the burial pit closed in C9, her cousin says, *"se quedó callada de golpe — como si alguien hubiera cerrado una ventana."* — *"she went quiet all at once — as if someone had closed a window."* She does not remember any of it. **This is the quietest line of victory in the campaign.** Use it only if earned.

**Keeper note**: If one or more investigators risked themselves protecting Concepción in Chapter 1, Don Eusebio should look directly at that investigator when he says *"I couldn't bear to see whether you would save her or whether she would save you."* This is the moment where the Chapter 1 pit encounter pays off emotionally — the investigators *are* the reason she lived.

**If the investigators failed to save Concepción in Chapter 1** (she was badly injured, or worse): Don Eusebio's confession changes. The line becomes: *"And when I came back and heard what had happened to her — I sent her away anyway. To Azul, to her cousin's house. Even hurt. I couldn't look at her. I still can't."* If Concepción died in Chapter 1 — which is very hard to let happen, but possible — Don Eusebio's final ritual role in C8/C9 becomes grimmer: he volunteers to anchor without being asked, because he has nothing left that could be taken from him that hasn't been. Adjust accordingly.

**Roleplaying hook**: If an investigator asks *"What do you think will happen to you when you stand in the outer ring?"* — he looks at them and says: *"I think I'll find out why it let me keep doing it. Why it didn't stop me after the first time. I've been thinking about that. Something that can carve rock from beneath the ground and animate dead cattle for eight months — it could have stopped me. It didn't. A little girl nearly stopped it with her fingernails. Think about what that means."* He means this as something terrifying. It is. The entity let him complete enough of the opening that he couldn't undo it alone. He was used. And Concepción — untrained, unprotected, thirteen years old — was closer to being its equal than he ever was.

### Nahuel — What He Knows

If an investigator seeks Nahuel out directly, they find him sitting facing the pit from a distance, awake, still with the ochre marks on his palms:

> *"I keep feeling it measuring me,"* he says. *"Like — it checked me once, at the camp. When the circle broke. And it decided something about me. And now every time it brushes the geometry of the binding it checks me again, like it's confirming."*

If asked what it's confirming: *"I don't know. That I'm still here, maybe. That I'm still the focus."* A pause. *"I think it's been keeping the binding from collapsing completely. I think it wants me intact until the closing, so the closing has something to... push against. Something to fail against."*

This is the most important piece of information Nahuel can give: he believes the entity has been moderating its pressure on the binding because a complete collapse would mean Nahuel is no longer useful. The entity wants the closing attempted — and wants it done with Nahuel as the focus — because it believes the closing will fail at maximum pressure and the breach will be total and permanent.

**This is a trap**. The investigators must decide: is Nahuel the right person to be the focus? Can anyone else bear it? If Nahuel is the focus and the entity is right about the pressure point — the closing could fail at the worst possible moment.

**SAN Check**: 0/1D4 (the implication that the entity has been managing the situation all along, that it wanted them here, doing this, at this moment)

### Kuyen — What She Won't Say

Kuyen will not seek out investigators. If sought, she is working on the leather panels and will answer questions without stopping her hands.

If asked directly: *"What are the odds this works?"*

> *(She is quiet for a moment. Her hands keep moving.)* *"I've done the second-to-last approach three times. It gets harder each time. The entity learns the geometry as we deploy it — it maps our counter-moves. By the time we reach the closing, it will have seen this version of the ritual and will know where the pressure points are."*

*(Pause.)*

> *"This is why we need to be faster than it is. And why we need everything in the outer ring working. The wrong returned, the one who opened it, the marks on the focus — all of it. If any piece is absent or compromised, we lose the speed advantage."*

If asked about Nahuel specifically:

> *(Her hands stop.)* *"If there is someone in this group whose — condition — is resilient enough to share the focus, I want to know now. Not because Nahuel can't do it. Because three weeks as a focus is too long and what he is doing tomorrow requires someone to hold the center when it fights hardest."*

She is asking for volunteers without asking. The investigators can offer to co-anchor the focus with Nahuel — taking some of the geometric strain — by holding his hands, standing behind him, or (if they understand the geometry) positioning themselves at the inner ring positions. This reduces the concentrated pressure on Nahuel but distributes SAN and CON damage across those who volunteer.

### Silvio — The Last Thing He Saw

Silvio is the only one awake at 3 AM. He sits at the pit's edge and looks down. If an investigator approaches and sits beside him:

> *"When I went in — yesterday — it showed me the inside. Not in pictures. In angles. Like I could feel the shape of what it is from the inside out."* He watches the glowing arcs.* "It's been here a very long time. Longer than the carving. The carving is recent — maybe a few thousand years. The scar is — I don't have words for how old the scar is. It's like trying to say how old the dirt is."*

If asked what the entity wants:

> *"It doesn't want things. It moves. The opening is a place where it can move more easily. It's been using this place since the scar happened. We opened it wider. Now it has too much of itself here and it can't go back the way it came."* He pauses.* "That's what I think the closing does. Not expels it. Narrows the door back down. So it can still be here, but only the way it always was — in the corners, in the angles. Not... through."*

This is the most accurate description of the entity anyone in the campaign has offered. It doesn't want, it moves. The opening made it more present than it has been in centuries. The closing returns it to background noise rather than active intrusion.

**SAN Check**: 0/1 (not horror — the dawning comprehension of the entity's scale and complete indifference to human life)

## SCENE 2 — QUIRCE AT NIGHT

Quirce does not sleep. Around 1 AM, if any investigator is awake and watching, he is visible at the pit's edge with his theodolite, taking measurements of the glowing geometry. He is meticulous. He is also, clearly, awed — not afraid, but awed.

If confronted:

> *(He looks up from the theodolite without guilt.)* *"I knew you'd come eventually. Yes, I'm documenting. That is what I do."* *(A pause.)* *"I'm not here to stop your ritual. I want to see it work. I want to measure what a successful closing looks like from the outside. There are people who need to know that it's possible."*

He means this. The question of whether his presence and his instrument setup compromise the geometry is a real one — it's what investigators should be focused on. His correction to the outer ring's diagram (the asymmetry from Chapter 6) is still present in the leather panels Kuyen is preparing, unless investigators caught it and corrected it.

**If investigators haven't caught the diagram correction**: At some point this scene, a **Spot Hidden** or **Occult** check while looking at the assembled panels reveals the asymmetry. It is subtle enough to have passed Kuyen's review. Correcting it requires removing and redrawing one section of the leather panel — Kuyen can do this in an hour if informed.

**If investigators confront Quirce about the asymmetry**: He admits it immediately. *"It creates an observation window. A moment of maximum pressure before the seal closes. No one was going to be harmed by it — it's a two-second differential."* He does not appear to understand why investigators are angry. The geometry and the people standing in it are different categories of concern to him. *"The closing still works. I just wanted to measure the peak."*

This is the central moral failure of his character — not malice, but a profound mismatch in what counts as harm. He put a two-second amplification into the outer ring of a ritual where **Don Eusebio is standing**. He didn't consider what two seconds of concentrated entity-field does to a human body.

**Decision**: Can Quirce stay? Kuyen's opinion, if asked: *"He does nothing in the ritual. If his instruments are away from the geometry, he is harmless. If he insists on placing them inside the effective range, he is a problem."* This is a judgment call for the investigators.

## SCENE 3 — THE ENTITY SPEAKS

Specifically to the Marked investigators. At some point between 2 and 4 AM, any investigator who received the Mark in Chapter 3 experiences the following — not a dream, they may be awake:

> *The corners of the space are wrong. Not dramatically — two degrees, three. The angles of the campsite's tents, the angles of the night sky meeting the flat horizon, the corners where your blanket meets the ground — all of them are slightly off. The wrongness is consistent and complete: the whole space has shifted slightly, as if seen through glass that is not quite flat.*
>
> *In that wrongness, something is present. Not visible. Present the way cold is present — as a condition of the space rather than an object in it.*
>
> *It does not communicate in language. It communicates in geometry. What it expresses is not words but spatial relationships:*
>
> *You are in the right place. The shape is known. The attempt is expected. The outcome is known.*

**SAN Check**: 1/1D4 (the communication — it spoke to you, and what it said was that it has anticipated this)

**Keeper note**: The entity did not communicate *threat*. It communicated *certainty*. This is worse. Whether its certainty is correct — whether the attempt will fail as it expects — is what the next two chapters decide.

The Marked investigator can tell others what they experienced. Whether they do is up to the player. But the mechanical effect: they have been *informed* of what the entity expects, which means they also know exactly where the entity believes the attempt will fail. With a **Hard Cthulhu Mythos** check, a Marked investigator can identify the specific geometry point the entity most closely monitors. This information can be used to anticipate its moves in Chapter 8's combat.

## GM NOTES

**This chapter is about weight, not action**: The ritual doesn't happen yet. The combat doesn't happen yet. This chapter is conversations around fires, the geometry glowing below, the sound that prevents sleep after 2 AM. The horror is not in events but in preparation — in knowing what's coming and having to wait for it.

**Nahuel's revelation is critical mechanical information**: The possibility that the entity wants the closing to fail at maximum pressure — and has been managing Nahuel toward that outcome — needs to be communicated clearly enough that investigators can factor it into their strategy. The decision about who anchors the focus, and how, will determine the Chapter 9 outcome.

**Don Eusebio's confession is the arc's emotional midpoint**: Not the end of his arc — that comes in Chapters 8 and 9 — but the moment where he becomes, for the first time, a person the investigators can feel something other than contempt for. The story of Concepción should land quietly. It is not redemption; it is evidence that there is still a person inside the wreckage of his choices.

**Quirce's correction**: If investigators haven't caught it yet, they must catch it here. Give them the Spot Hidden check naturally, as part of the scene where Kuyen assembles the panels. If they miss it here, it becomes a crisis in Chapter 8.

**The entity's communication**: Don't play this for jump-scare. Play it for the quiet horror of being told, by something vast and indifferent, that it has accounted for you.

## EXPANSION NOTES — BRIDGING TO THE OVERVIEW FRAMING

The detailed prep above runs *La Víspera*: the night before the closing ritual at the burial pit, the confessions and conversations that crystallize the moral stakes. The `overview.md` framing of Arc 3 is **The Great Sleeper** — Andean cult routes, Zonda winds, locust hive-minds, Cursed Gaucho's family massacre, Doña Mercedes explaining the Necronomicon's necessity. Use the scenes below to layer overview elements over the existing pit-eve content.

### Reskinning summary

- The closing the campaign has been preparing for *is* an awakening attempt as well — from the entity's perspective. The ritual the players attempt to close, El Patrón has been arranging through the Great Wizard so that a *different* awakening (the Great Sleeper) lights up while the binding work peaks. This is the structural betrayal in the new framing: every closing is also a potential opening, depending on who is paying attention.
- **The Cursed Gaucho** (overview, central Arc 3 NPC) is introduced here for the first time. He arrives at the camp in the night, uninvited, and his backstory unfolds. He is not in the existing prep; he is *new*.
- **Doña Mercedes** is still in correspondence (Arc 2 expansion) but in this chapter, late, she arrives. She made the trip because Saráchaga (Arc 4) and the Buenos Aires Keepers told her to.
- **The Cult of the Great Sleeper** is glimpsed through environmental signs — Zonda wind, locust scouts — and through one captured priest the Cursed Gaucho brings to the camp.

### NEW SCENE — THE ZONDA AT THE PIT

Insert this in the early evening, before any of the late-night confessions. The Zonda — the hot, dry föhn wind from the Andes — should not be reaching this far east in this season. It does. It begins around dusk, a wind that carries no dust but carries *sound*. The fires bend. The leather panels Kuyen is preparing flutter.

**SAN Check**: 0/1 — for the wrongness of a Zonda at this latitude.

**Mechanical effect on the binding panels**: the Zonda is degrading the panels' edges. Kuyen has to weight them with stones from the pit's rim. **Survival or Natural World** roll: any character making the roll realizes the wind is *coming from the wrong direction* — not from the Andes proper, but from the cordillera spur that includes the locust fields and the cult shrines (overview). Something west of the pit is *broadcasting* the Zonda.

**Listen** roll on the wind, after midnight: faint, far, the sound of a chant. Not in any language the player knows. The chant is in *time* with the entity's vibration in the chest — the sound from the pit at 2 AM. Two rituals are happening, simultaneously, on the same beat. They are *coordinated*.

**Kuyen's reaction**, if anyone tells her: *(She stops working. She listens. Then:) "Hay otro sitio. Están haciendo lo mismo en otro pozo. Si los dos sitios cierran la noche del cierre, estamos cerrando con ellos. Si uno se abre y el otro se cierra, el que se abre gana."* — *"There is another site. They are doing the same in another pit. If both close the night of the closing, we are closing with them. If one opens and the other closes, the one that opens wins."*

This is the structural threat of the new framing: **the awakening attempt and the closing are running in parallel**. The Cult of the Great Sleeper is the entity that uses any *opening* the closing fails to seal. The players are now in a race with a second, distant ritual.

### NEW SCENE — THE CURSED GAUCHO ARRIVES

Insert this around 11 PM. A man rides into camp from the west. He is alone. He is not announced. The horses near the perimeter shy away from him; Galíndez's analogue here is Tomás (or whoever has been doing perimeter at the camp), and Tomás does not raise an alarm — he stares.

**Appearance of the Cursed Gaucho**:
- Mid-thirties. Sun-blasted, lean, his face cut at the jaw and the cut never properly closed — a scar that runs from earlobe to chin in a clean diagonal.
- Black *bombachas*, a *poncho* in *vicuña*, a wide-brimmed hat. Two knives in plain sight (a *facón* on his back, a *cuchillo de campo* on his belt). An old flintlock pistol hanging from the saddle. The pistol is unloaded.
- His horse is a *zaino* (dark bay) of unusual quality, well-fed but pushed hard recently. Two days' ride hard, possibly three.
- His eyes do not move when other people enter the firelight. He looks at one person at a time, completely, and then moves to the next.

**He says, on entry**:

> *"Estoy buscando a la mujer que cierra. Me dijeron que estaba acá. No vengo a cerrar — vengo a ver cómo cierra, para aprender."*  — *"I am looking for the woman who closes. They told me she was here. I am not coming to close — I am coming to see how she closes, in order to learn."*

He is referring to Kuyen. He has been hunting both cults — the Great Sleeper *and* anyone associated with El Patrón — for years. He needs the Tehuelche-Tobá-Salamanca tradition to do *his* closing, eventually, on a different scar in the cordillera.

### NEW SCENE — THE CURSED GAUCHO'S FAMILY

If the players sit with him, he tells the story. Around the fire, late, after Don Eusebio's confession and Nahuel's revelation. He does not perform the story. He just tells it.

> *"Tres años atrás, en un valle al pie de la cordillera, en lo que la gente de allá llama **Coypac**. Mi familia tenía un campo chico. Cabras. Maíz. Mi madre era de mapuche, mi padre era de gauchos del norte. Nací en ese campo. Mi mujer también. Mi hijo nació en una de esas casas con techo de paja, hace cuatro años."*
>
> *(Pause.)*
>
> *"Una noche llegó un grupo. Eran del Culto del Gran Durmiente — yo no sabía esa palabra entonces, la aprendí después. Decían que iban a despertar algo. Que el campo de mi padre estaba sobre una de las llaves. Mataron a mi mujer y a mi hijo. Mi padre se defendió y lo mataron también. Mi madre… ella no estaba en la casa. Estaba en el monte recogiendo hierbas. Volvió y los encontró. Me mandó a buscar. Yo estaba en otro valle, vendiendo cabras. Cuando llegué, el grupo se había ido. Mi madre estaba sentada con los muertos. No me habló. Esa misma semana se ahorcó."*
>
> *"Tres años. Voy buscando. Mato cuando encuentro. Aprendí a leer la geometría que ellos usan — no como Kuyen, peor que ella, pero suficiente para encontrarlos antes de que me encuentren. Sé que el que mandó al grupo no estuvo ahí. Yo también lo busco. Me dijeron que el lleva un nombre falso — *Dadálah*."*

Translation: *"Three years ago, in a valley at the foot of the cordillera, in what the people there call Coypac. My family had a small farm. Goats. Corn. My mother was Mapuche, my father was a northern gaucho. I was born on that farm. My wife too. My son was born in one of those thatch-roofed houses, four years ago. One night a group came. They were the Cult of the Great Sleeper — I didn't know that word then, I learned it later. They said they were going to wake something. That my father's land was over one of the keys. They killed my wife and my son. My father defended himself and they killed him too. My mother... she was not in the house. She was in the bush gathering herbs. She came back and found them. She sent for me. I was in another valley selling goats. When I got there, the group was gone. My mother was sitting with the dead. She didn't speak to me. That same week she hanged herself. Three years. I go looking. I kill when I find them. I learned to read the geometry they use — not like Kuyen, worse than her, but enough to find them before they find me. I know the one who sent the group was not there. I look for him too. They tell me he uses a false name — Dadálah."*

The name *Dadálah* is now in the campaign for the second time (first introduced in Arc 2 C6 expansion, through Quirce's mediumship). The Cursed Gaucho does not yet know what the players know — that *Dadálah* is El Patrón. That recognition can happen this scene, or in C8.

**SAN Check**: 0/1 — not for horror, for the way grief sounds when a man has carried it for three years and learned to speak it without inflection.

### NEW SCENE — THE CAPTURED CULTIST

The Cursed Gaucho did not arrive alone. Tied across the back of a second horse — left at the perimeter, half a kilometer away, still alive — is a captured cult priest of the Great Sleeper. The Cursed Gaucho captured him two days ago in the cordillera and has been bringing him east as proof that the cult is operational *now*.

The cultist, brought to the firelight under guard, is a thin man in his fifties, his beard ash-blond, his accent suggesting he was originally from the Banda Oriental. He is not from the Pampas. The cult is regional but not local. He is bound and gagged.

**Interrogation possibilities**:

- **Persuade / Intimidate**: He talks. He confirms a parallel ritual is underway tonight, two days' ride west, at a place he names *El Pozo de Coypac* — the same valley where the Cursed Gaucho's family was killed. The cultist is a mid-rank priest; he was in transit between sites when the Cursed Gaucho intercepted him. He carries no Necronomicón page, but he carries a list of *eight names* in a coded letter — Keepers, scholars, rural-police informants — being cultivated by the cult. Two of the names will be relevant in Arc 4 (Lacasa, Ana Bermúdez).
- **Hard Cthulhu Mythos**: His teeth show the same wear pattern as Saens's skull (Arc 2 expansion C5). He has been chewing — or being made to chew — a paste of *first people* origin during rituals. The paste is the cult's communion. Mercedes will want to study it.
- **Sanity Check** when he speaks of the Great Sleeper: 0/1D4. He is calm. He believes what he says. His belief is the unsettling part.

### NEW SCENE — DOÑA MERCEDES ARRIVES, LATE

Around 4 AM, after the entity has communicated with the Marked, after the Cursed Gaucho has told his story, after the cultist has been questioned, a small carriage arrives at the perimeter. It is Mercedes. She has come from Buenos Aires by post-coach and the last leg by hired *galera*. She is dressed for travel and tired in the way only old scholars can be tired.

She does not introduce herself dramatically. Kuyen meets her at the perimeter. Mercedes embraces Kuyen, briefly, and Kuyen — for the first time the players have seen — *closes her eyes* during a human gesture. They have known each other for thirty years.

**Mercedes' first action, before she speaks**: She walks the length of the fire and shakes hands with each player in turn. As she takes the third hand, she pauses — almost imperceptibly — and rubs her thumb across the cuff of the player's jacket. She nods once, to herself. She moves on. She does not name what she felt. **This is the dust runner (3b) at its most distant point in the campaign**: Mercedes, seven hundred kilometers from the pit, recognizes the dust on the players' clothing because she has read the manuscript that lists what the dust *is* — fine grey particulate from the original carving's limestone, ground over centuries by the geometry. The dust does not wash out because it is not, strictly, dust. Mercedes will not say this aloud. She filed it; that is enough.

**Mercedes' first words to the players, when she reaches the fire**:
