## PART 1 — PLOT HOLES & CONTRADICTIONS FOUND

### 🕳️ P8 — Two different Mapudungun chants (C7 cheatsheet vs C8/C9 chapters)

The Arc 3 cheatsheet names the counter-chant ***Newen mapu kelluiñ***; the C8/C9 chapter files name it ***Antu kishu, lan amukey*** (the line all NPCs are geometry-taught at Phase 5). **Fix that converts the contradiction into content:** *Newen mapu kelluiñ* was **Kuyen's working chant** — the mobile chant she sustained across phases. It died with her; nobody at this table knows it, and the felt silence in its place is canon-consistent with the C12 Destroyed note (*"the third line is unspoken; the chain has a missing voice"*). *Antu kishu, lan amukey* is **the geometry's own line** — taught to everyone in the ring at Phase 5, including the players at the table. One chant lost with the dead; one chant the land supplies. Updated cheatsheets reflect this split.

### 🕳️ P9 — Cheatsheet C8 "betrayal" is a different event than the chapter's (CRITICAL for session 8)

Cheatsheet Phase 4: Quirce *"attempts to redirect the closing into a binding-for-use… If not: outer ring breaks; **one Wrong Returned dies**."* Chapter: Quirce **crosses the ring to observe**, creating a structural gap; consequence is a phase restart, 1D6 SAN to a neighbor, deeper entity mapping — **nobody dies**. These are incompatible stakes. **Chapter wins** (it matches Quirce's C7 self-justification and his C9 *"I owe a debt"* arc — he is negligent, not a saboteur; the *Wizard's* line in the recopies is the sabotage). Updated cheatsheet rewritten accordingly.

### 🕳️ P10 — Saúl Carrasco is alive and dead in the same dawn (C8/C9 cheatsheet vs chapters)

Arc 3 cheatsheet (C9): *"Saúl Carrasco arrives, breathless, with a hand-copied page… Mercedes: 'Tenemos que ir.'"* Chapter C9: the dawn rider is explicitly **not Saúl** — *"he is dead, his body found by the cult and left at a paraje as a warning"* — the report comes by relay, signed **Saráchaga** ([handout H10](../assets/handouts-c8-c12.md#h10--the-coypac-relay-report-c9-dawn)). Chapter C8 has Saúl's *living* return between Phases 1–2 with the verbal intel. **Sequence locked in the updated cheatsheet:** Saúl returns alive in **C8** (twelve priests, two pits, the ochre man) → rides back out or stays behind the circle → dies off-screen → C9 dawn brings the anonymous relay rider with Saráchaga's letter confirming the partial failure *and* Saúl's death. The "hand-copied Coypac page" prop belongs to the captured cultist (C7) or is dropped.

### 🕳️ P11 — Concepción appears in cheatsheets at scenes she cannot attend (canon AND table)

In canon she is in **Azul** during Arc 3 (C7 confession: her cousin's letters); at this table she **died in C1**. Both beats are struck in the updated cheatsheet. Her presence survives only as Don Eusebio's confession (rewritten per the C7 `[THIS TABLE]` block) and the annual-dream epilogue, which the C12 `[THIS TABLE]` consequence already handles (village children in the long-form variant, since she did not live).

### 🕳️ P12 — Who compensates when the Cursed Gaucho breaks formation (C8 expansion)

The expansion scene: *"Kuyen is forced to compensate immediately or lose the phase"* + Kuyen's angry line. Kuyen is dead, and the general strike-all-Kuyen note doesn't assign a replacement for a load-bearing mechanic. **Fix (paste as `[THIS TABLE]` into the scene):** the western gap is held by **Marta** physically stepping half-into the broken position (her alignment substitutes for one round) while a **Marked PC calls the gap's angle** for her — the same call-the-direction mechanic from C9 Round 1. Phase 2 still restarts (10 minutes). The angry line goes to **Mercedes**, colder than Kuyen would have been: *"Si vuelve a salir del círculo, lo dejamos afuera. No tenemos a nadie para perderlo dos veces."* — *"If he leaves the circle again, we leave him outside. We have no one to lose him twice."*

### 🕳️ P13 — C8 contest difficulty double-penalizes this table

Scene 2's rule: +1 difficulty per failed condition — *"if Nahuel has support, if the asymmetry was corrected, if Quirce is not inside the ring."* At this table two of those are **structurally failed before the session starts** (Nahuel dead; asymmetry never corrected), which bakes in +2 to every phase contest on top of the improvised-leadership penalty. **Recalibration (paste as `[THIS TABLE]` under Scene 2):** the table-state conditions are already priced into the restructured ritual — do **not** count "no Nahuel" as a failed condition. Count only what the players could influence: (1) center anchor supported (someone with Don Eusebio / the PC anchor), (2) eastern gap mitigated (Warding **or** a human anchor on the eastern line — the [E3 preview](../02-la-salamanca/REVIEW-sessions-5-7.md#e3--session-7-guarantee-the-eastern-probe-paste-into-c7-between-scene-3-and-the-gm-notes) taught them this), (3) Quirce outside the ring, (4) bone-disturbance tier ≤1 (C5 carry-over). Each failed = +1. This keeps player agency the variable, not the table's grief.

### 🕳️ P14 — Does the center anchor die? (C9 cheatsheet vs chapter)

Cheatsheet C9: *"**Anchor dies**: Nahuel by default."* Chapter C9: Nahuel **survives** — pays in recovery time, nightmares, the corners going quiet. Contradiction. **Chapter wins.** At this table the anchor is Don Eusebio or a PC volunteer; recommended ruling: **the anchor survives but pays** — Don Eusebio's *"What do I owe?"* is the campaign's final moral question and lands far better with him alive to ask it (and his C9-expansion choice to ride east depends on it). If a **PC** anchored: SAN 1/1D6 + CON check at the seal (1D6 on failure) + the Nahuel-style aftermath (corners felt but quiet, months of recovery flavor). Death stays available only as the player's own dramatic choice, never the default.

### 🕳️ P15 — The 1810 lock: three different owners across the docs

Arc 4 cheatsheet: lock *"installed 1810, **by Quirce**, to fool the Keepers."* Chapter C11: installer **unknown**, private European maker's mark, Mercedes disturbed. Chapter C12, El Patrón: *"Tenía la llave hace cuarenta años"* — forty years ≈ **1781**, the year of Mercedes' copy, not 1810. **Recommended canonization (one sentence fixes all three):** the lock was installed in 1810 by **Tiburcio Lacasa** — the Keeper uncle from Mercedes' confession (Keeper until his death in 1812, archival access, *"flexible archival habits"*) — using hardware El Patrón commissioned. The "key" El Patrón has held for forty years is **double**: the physical key Tiburcio passed up the chain, and the *locational* key — Mercedes' copied section, which told him where the book was. This stitches the confession, the lock, and the Lacasa-family cultivation into one forty-year thread, and gives the C11 investigation a clean answer when players ask *"who installed it?"* → Mercedes, after a silence: *"Tiburcio. Tenía acceso. Y la familia siempre tuvo, digamos, hábitos de archivo flexibles."*

After 1D3 rounds the wedge withdraws — the entity is *testing*, exactly as it rehearsed in C7; the real exploitation it saves for Phase 5.
