# CHAPTER 11 — LA DECISIÓN

### _The Decision_

**Goal**: Force the move. Show ordinary containment failing. Expose the second compromised Keeper. Get the Cursed Gaucho's terms on the table. End the night with a transport plan, El Patrón aware of it, and the players knowing they are walking into his trap willingly. **Emotional arc**: Grief → Investigation → Cold resolve. **Key seed**: Every option is wrong. The right move is the least wrong one — and you make it knowing he is waiting at the end of it.

> **Keeper note.** This is the moral center of the arc — don't rush Mercedes' confession, Albarrán's _"Lo movemos,"_ Ana's face, the Cursed Gaucho's knife. And **El Patrón in the parlor is the boldest scene in the campaign**: run it slowly. He is not dramatic — he is courteous, reasonable, the most dangerous person these players will ever meet. The horror is in the manners, and _some players will be tempted by the offer._ That is correct.

## OPENING SITUATION

Saráchaga has been dead twelve hours. The Keepers convene at Mercedes' insistence, in the same room where the body lay (now moved to San Ignacio for a private vigil). Galíndez walks the city openly armed now, taking different streets to and from everywhere. The courtyard well's disc is **still hot**; Padre Albarrán has been at it since first light, praying, still without his answer. He will get it today.

Run three things **in parallel** — the players will split: the **investigation of Saráchaga's death** (which leads to the second compromised Keeper), the **operational planning of the transport**, and the **moral weight of the decision**. By dusk there is a vote; by midnight the plan is on paper; before dawn El Patrón will know the plan — because Ana Bermúdez will have been confronted, and whether she is a leak or a traitor will be answered by what she does in the next twelve hours.

> **The three threads, by scene — they all converge at the parlor.**
> - **Investigation → the second traitor:** [the vault](#the-vault-finally) (the wrong 1810 lock) → [tracing Saráchaga's last hours](#tracing-saráchagas-last-hours) (Lacasa exposed) → [Ana confronted](#ana-bermúdez).
> - **Operation → the transport plan:** [the docks](#the-docks) with Galíndez → recruit the [cordon at the tannery](#the-cursed-gaucho) (Cursed Gaucho's terms).
> - **Moral weight → what it costs:** [Mercedes' confession in the cloister](#doña-mercedes-alone); the vote settles when [Albarrán says *"Lo movemos"*](#ana-bermúdez).
> **Endpoint:** every thread lands at [the parlor](#midnight--the-parlor) at midnight — El Patrón waiting in Inocencia's chair. That scene closes the night; the hot witness-stone left on the bedside table starts the C12 clock.

## THE VAULT, FINALLY

Mercedes takes the players down at mid-morning (Galíndez and two men along; Albarrán stays at the well — _"No estoy listo. Lo voy a saber."_).

> _La escalera cerrada da a un corredor de ladrillo sin revocar que baja, primero suave y después empinado. El aire se seca; el mismo filo mineral del pozo y de La Salamanca. A los cien metros las paredes tienen marcas chicas a la altura del pecho — rayas de conteo en grupos de siete. Mercedes cuenta por lo bajo: "Trescientos cuarenta y cuatro. Esa es la cantidad de Custodios desde 1610. Cada uno hace una marca cuando entra por primera vez." El corredor termina en una sala circular de cuatro metros de ancho, de techo bajo, con paredes de la misma caliza importada que el tallado de las ruinas jesuíticas. En el centro: un cofre herrado, sencillo, tres cerraduras — dos viejas, una nueva, puesta en 1810. En el piso alrededor, un círculo de cinco metros más ancho que la sala, que se mete debajo de las paredes: el diagrama dual completo, tallado y retallado y vuelto a tallar, los surcos ahondados por siglos de cinceles._

> **Two dates, do not confuse them**: **1610** sealed the _vault_ (the room, the carved floor, the binding of the _place_ — it contains both book and El Patrón). **1810** added a _third lock to the chest_ — not by the Keepers, a private maker's mark, the same year the _cabildo abierto_ met upstairs. The chest was not opened in 1810; the lock was _added_, meaning someone had access to install hardware. **The lock is the question, not the year.**

> **The corridor is not the whole network.** This is a single sealed spur — Keeper-built or Keeper-appropriated generations ago, bricked off from the wider colonial tunnel system the players may have glimpsed in [C10](chapter-10.md#the-university-public-face) (the old archway, plastered over). The Keepers do not control, and have never fully mapped, what lies beyond that seal. **If a player asks whether the tunnels connect to anything else**, Mercedes, flat: _"Sí. No sabemos hasta dónde. Por eso lo tapiamos. No se puede vigilar lo que no se puede ver."_ — _"Yes. We don't know how far. That's why we walled it off. You can't watch what you can't see."_

**Handout**: the same [`dual-diagram.svg`](assets/dual-diagram.svg) from the Jesuit ruins. The chest sits at the **anchor** — where Saens lay, where the [bone-chamber center](chapter-05.md#the-bone-chamber) was, where a Marked PC may stand at the dock in [C12](chapter-12.md#chapter-12--el-libro). The continuity should be unmistakable.

**In the room**: the Marked roll 2d6, but a four-meter circular room has nowhere for a Hound to _not_ manifest — so **double 1 is a near-miss**: the chamber heats ten degrees, candles gutter, the chest's iron creaks, nothing manifests. The geometry holds, and the players feel it hold around them.

**The wrong lock** — the primary signal is **Mercedes' face, no roll**: she kneels, looks at the third lock three full seconds, goes pale, hand to her throat — _"Esa cerradura no la pusimos nosotros."_ Then quieter: _"Y no la pusimos en mi vida."_ A **Hard Cthulhu Mythos** adds the maker's-mark detail (a private European commission).

> **Who installed the 1810 lock.** **Tiburcio Lacasa** — the Keeper uncle from Mercedes' [cloister confession](#doña-mercedes-alone) (a Keeper until his death in 1812; archival access; _"flexible archival habits"_) — using hardware El Patrón commissioned. The "key" El Patrón has held _"hace cuarenta años"_ is **double**: the physical key Tiburcio passed up the chain, and the _locational_ key — Mercedes' copied section (~1781) that told him where the book was. One forty-year thread: the confession, the lock, the Lacasa cultivation. If players ask _"who installed it?"_ — Mercedes, after a silence: _"Tiburcio. Tenía acceso. Y la familia siempre tuvo, digamos, hábitos de archivo flexibles."_ (Corroborated by Lacasa's unsent draft, [H13](assets/handouts-c8-c12.md#h13--lacasas-unsent-draft-c11-if-the-satchel-is-taken), if the players take the satchel below.)

**Fresh dust** by the chest — a **Spot Hidden**, or Mercedes notices herself if missed: _"Hay polvo nuevo. Alguien estuvo acá hace menos de dos semanas. No fui yo. No fue Eladio. No fue Ramón. No fue Galíndez."_ A successful player roll adds that there are _two_ sets of footprints, three days apart — the visitor came twice.

**SAN Check**: 1/1D4 — proximity to the book itself (unseen, but felt). The chest does not radiate; it _absorbs_ — a hole the size and shape of a chest, in the world.

Mercedes does **not** open it — _"Lo abrimos para moverlo. No para mostrarlo."_ (If players push, Galíndez physically interposes.) When a player asks the natural question — _"if they have the key, why haven't they taken it?"_ — Mercedes answers, one hand flat on the lid:

> _"El candado lo puede abrir él. La bóveda no lo deja entrar. Toda esta sala es un dibujo, y el dibujo lo contiene a él tanto como contiene al libro. Mientras el libro esté abajo, dentro del dibujo, él puede tocar el cofre pero no puede llevárselo. El libro saliendo, atravesando la calle, es lo único que le permite completar el dibujo afuera y reclamarlo. Por eso necesita que lo movamos nosotros. Por eso lleva cuarenta años empujándonos a moverlo."_

The transport is not a security problem — it is **the condition El Patrón has engineered.** The Keepers move it anyway, because leaving it under a warm-going-hot containment is worse. They walk into the trap with eyes open, banking on the cordon, Galíndez's men, and the players' Marks to break the geometry _during_ the transport. **Galíndez**: _"Entonces estamos jugando su juego, pero con nuestras reglas."_ **Mercedes**, before leaving: _"El que abrió el candado de 1810 está vivo. Está en la ciudad. Lo vamos a encontrar antes de la noche."_

## THE DOCKS

Galíndez takes whoever's interested to the river docks. He has lined up the _**Carmen del Pilar**_ — a Genoese brig under **Capitán Ettore Borghi** (honest, mercenary, incurious if paid in silver), ready in 36 hours, bound for a Keeper-allied chapter house in Colonia — plus a **decoy**, the schooner _**La Niña Encantada**_, leaving the same hour with an identical-looking chest of bricks. The plan: chest leaves the vault at midnight in a closed _galera_, escorted by his fifteen men, transfers to a launch at the Riachuelo dock, rowed to the brig.

> **Galíndez's three weak points**: _"La salida de la Universidad, la esquina de Belgrano y Defensa donde la galera tiene que doblar despacio, y el embarque al bote en el muelle. Tres puntos. Ahí es donde nos van a pegar."_

**Player input matters**: recruit additional men (cordon walkers, the Cursed Gaucho's contacts, friends of Inocencia, _pulpería_ gauchos — Persuade/Credit Rating/Charm); propose routes (Galíndez adopts a defensible one); plan the dock transfer differently. **Every concrete element contributed _with reasoning_ reduces the [C12](chapter-12.md#chapter-12--el-libro) transport difficulty by one step at the relevant moment** — mark them.

## TRACING SARÁCHAGA'S LAST HOURS

The murder investigation yields the most dangerous information.

**The kitchen** — the maid **Luisa**, 19, terrified, saw a _visitante_ arrive at the second-patio entrance ~11 PM: a hat, a dark coat, and _spurred frontier boots_ — _"botas que estaban acostumbradas al campo."_

> **Failsafe (no roll, if Luisa is treated kindly)**: she volunteers — _"El visitante saludó al portero por su nombre. Dijo 'Bartolomé manda decir…' y después no escuché más."_ **Lacasa named by his own proxy.** The chain never stalls on one Track roll.

City boots rule out Quirce; the Cursed Gaucho has spurred boots but no motive (Saráchaga was the strongest voice for keeping the book where the Gaucho would want it). **Don Bartolomé Lacasa** — the absent Keeper, rural family, an estancia north of the city, spurred boots — fits.

**The well**: the recovered disc has cooled to warm overnight. Albarrán: _"La piedra responde a la presión. La presión bajó. Algo está organizando la presión."_ (El Patrón eased off after the kill — he wants the players to vote, deliberate, and _plan_. He wants the transport.)

**Lacasa's house** (Calle Reconquista): he's gone to _"el campo,"_ abruptly. **Track/Stealth**: his spurred bootprint at his own back door dates his departure to **11:45 PM** — fifteen minutes after the spurred _visitante_ reached Saráchaga's. He could not have been home. **Lacasa is the killer — not by his hand; he was the delivery vehicle. He brought El Patrón to Saráchaga.**

> **⚔️ The House Cleaners (scheduled).** Lacasa's office is not empty: two hired men ([escort block](assets/npc-stats.md#quirces-escort-3-generic)) are stripping it — drawers out, a satchel of papers, a fire in the grate. **This is why the letter is only _half_-burned.** They are a cleaning crew on a clock, fighting to **escape with the satchel**, not to win. If both escape, the players still get the half-burned letter and the strongbox deed. If the satchel is taken, it adds Lacasa's unsent draft — the only document naming _"el dueño del campo de Areco"_ as the man he answers to, and his uncle Tiburcio as his predecessor.
>
> **Where they run.** If the cleaners break off, they don't run for the street — they run for the **cellar**. A trapdoor under a rolled rug, brick stairs down, and a **tunnel mouth**, mortar chipped fresh at its edge: this house has had private access to the wider network since Tiburcio's day, and Bartolomé kept it clear. Players who follow (**Track/Stealth** to keep pace in the dark; a Marked PC feels nothing supernatural down here — this passage is mundane, older, and colder than the vault spur, no Hound roll) can run it as a **short chase**, 3 rounds, DEX or your Chase mechanic (penalty die without a light source): catch neither, catch one, or catch both. Whatever the outcome, **the tunnel mouth itself is the find** — proof the Lacasa family has moved under this city for two generations, and a physical thread the players can hand Galíndez for [C12's Weak Point 1](chapter-12.md#weak-point-1--university-exit).
>
> **If a cleaner is caught** (either one, alive): he doesn't know _who_ _el dueño del campo de Areco_ is by name — only that Lacasa paid him through the same faceless clerk as everyone else — but he's used this tunnel twice before this week, and can be made to describe where it surfaces: a disused well-house two blocks from the Manzana. **If both escape**: a **Spot Hidden** in the cellar, once the fight is over, finds what one of them dropped in the dark — the same information, different manner. Either way, hand out **[H17 — the tunnel sketch](assets/handouts-c8-c12.md#h17--a-hand-drawn-tunnel-sketch-c11-if-a-cleaner-is-caught-or-the-cellar-is-searched)**. The clue is never single-point — only how it's earned changes.
>
> **Keeper — scope.** The tunnels are not a way to move the chest. The transport has to trace the surface diagram — [moving underground breaks the city-scale drawing](chapter-12.md#weak-point-3--the-dock) the whole heist depends on. Their value here is intelligence and access, not transport. If the players report the well-house to Galíndez, he posts a man on it before dawn.

>

>

Sobre la fecha, trazadas con la misma tinta que la firma: tres líneas que forman una geometría parcial — el mismo blasón que vieron en la puerta del carruaje en Florida y San Martín.* **SAN 0/1.**

## ANA BERMÚDEZ

By late afternoon the players (or Mercedes, or Galíndez) connect Ana to Quirce. Mercedes confronts her quietly, players present if they wish: _"Ana. ¿Cuánto te dijo Quirce?"_ Ana goes white, does not deny, sits, lays both hands flat on her notebook: _"Me dijo que estaba estudiando los anillos sismológicos de la cuenca del Riachuelo… Yo le mandaba copias de las actas de las reuniones — pensaba que eran inocuas."_ She is not lying — she believed it, and is realizing in real time exactly what she did. Quirce paid her in books; she sent him six months of minutes; Quirce passed pieces to a third party (Lacasa → El Patrón) she never knew about. **A leak, not yet a traitor.**

**The decision** — Mercedes does not throw her out: _"Vas a ayudar mañana. Vas a estar ahí. Y después de que esto termine, vamos a hablar de qué eras."_ Mercy and a knife at once.

- **If players let her feed false intel** (route changes, fake timeline, decoy specifics matching Galíndez's plan): a real C12 advantage — **El Patrón's first attack hits the wrong location.**
- **If players exclude her**: held under guard through C12; the fake-intel option is lost; Mercedes is colder for the rest of the campaign.

**Padre Albarrán arrives** from the third patio, wet — he has been at (or in) the well. He looks at the room and says one sentence: _"Dios me contestó. Lo movemos."_ He sits. Galíndez nods once. The vote is over before it is taken. _(Note: he means move the book to the dock and re-vault it under fresh consecration — not put it on a ship. By the dock in C12 that answer finishes itself; see [the Hide foreshadowing](#doña-mercedes-alone).)_

## THE CURSED GAUCHO

Mid-evening, a runner: the Cursed Gaucho is at the Riachuelo tannery and will not come to the meeting house. He wants the players, alone, before midnight.

> _El olor pega primero — cueros curándose en cal y quebracho. La curtiembre es un galpón bajo de ladrillo con tres tinglados de secado. Él está en el tercero, en un banco de curtir junto a tres de sus hombres, afilando un cuchillo._ (And, at this table, the surviving **Wrong Returned** if any reached him.)

He has heard about Saráchaga, the well-stone, the planned move. His commitment:

> _"Mañana yo voy en la galera con ustedes. No voy a llegar al barco. Mi pelea es en la calle, no en el agua."_

He has chosen the place of his death and asks the players to _let him die there_ — and one promise:

> _"Si lo ven a él — el que tiene el campo en Areco con el otro nombre — no me dejen darle a alguien más mi pelea. Ese hombre es mío. Si no me dejan llegar a él yo, no me hacen el favor que vine a pedir."_ — i.e., **do not kill El Patrón before he can reach him**, and do not be in his way.

**If the players agree**, he lays a _cuchillo de campo_ on the bench — his family's blade, three generations old: _"Si yo no llego, este es para él. Hagan lo que tengan que hacer con esto, pero llévenlo a él."_ The knife is now an item carried into [C12](chapter-12.md#chapter-12--el-libro) (not magical — the family blade, the destroy-with-steel instrument if he falls). **If the players refuse**, he says nothing, goes back to sharpening, does not show at the carriage, and hits El Patrón his own way alone — depriving the players of his fifteen minutes of cover. Mark the difference.

> **The cordon, without Kuyen.** The cordon ritual (a moving city-scale binding, ten meters around the carriage, that El Patrón's surface manifestations cannot easily cross) is **the players' single largest C12 advantage — but improvised and diminished here.** No curandera, no tribal allies — only the **Wrong Returned** if they lived. **Marta** kneels over the leather panels: _"No sé las palabras que usaba Kuyen. Pero sé dónde tienen que estar las esquinas. Les puedo mostrar dónde sostener los paneles. Mi cuerpo sabe los ángulos."_ It will hold, but less stably — every complication at the three weak points matters more. A Marked PC whose [C10 grid-entry](chapter-10.md#entry-into-the-city) read the city as containment can steady her placement; a **Mandinga Warding** placed at one weak point hardens that corner to a full panel-leg's equivalent — the spell paying off in Arc 4.
>
> **Warding decision — ask it explicitly here, not in the street at midnight.** After Marta finishes: _"¿Usamos el hechizo de Mandinga en una esquina, o lo guardamos?"_ — put the question on the table now. Using it frees up a human walker (the cordon needs one fewer recruited man) and hardens that corner unconditionally; keeping it reserves the spell for a different use in C12. **If the Mandinga-deal PCs haven't been reminded they hold this spell since C3, surface it now** — Marta recognizes the deal-wound if she sees bare forearms.

> † _Canon note: the cordon is normally the curandera's working, walked by eight; the [C12 cordon worksheet](chapter-12.md#the-day-before-the-night) lists how this table fills the corners from the Wrong Returned, the Marked, recruited men, and a Warding._

## DOÑA MERCEDES, ALONE

Late evening, the plan set. Mercedes asks the player she has spoken to most to walk with her in the empty cloister of San Ignacio, and tells them what she has carried since Arc 2:

> _"Cuando yo era aprendiza, hace cuarenta años, copié una sección del manuscrito de La Salamanca del original que está en la bóveda. No era decorativa. Era una sección del Necronomicón. La copié por curiosidad. Por orgullo. Pensé que la podía estudiar y devolver."_ — She copied a section of the Necronomicón at twenty-four, for personal study; it traveled, in her hand, into the Salamanca manuscripts the cult later read. The Great Wizard's partial knowledge, and that the cult could attempt the awakening at all, is partly her fault. She has been atoning as a teacher for forty years, unforgiven, and does not expect to be.

**How it traveled** (she has rehearsed this answer for forty years): _"Cuando dejé el aprendizaje, le entregué mi cuaderno al archivo de los Custodios… Después alguien lo abrió y copió las páginas de mi mano. No lo quemaron, lo copiaron. Yo no me di cuenta hasta dos años más tarde, cuando una de mis frases volvió a aparecer en una carta… Era mi puntuación. Era mi forma de poner las comas."_ The agent, when pressed: _"El predecesor de Lacasa. Tiburcio Lacasa, su tío. Murió en 1812. Era Custodio entonces. No tengo prueba… Pero el cuaderno estuvo en su cuidado durante esos seis meses. Y la familia Lacasa siempre tuvo, digamos, hábitos de archivo flexibles."_ The chain is forty years long and runs through one family — El Patrón has been cultivating the Lacasa line as long as he has hunted Mercedes. **SAN Check**: 0/1 — the hard kind of intimacy.

> ⊕ **If the Antiquarian carries the vellum leaf** (their optional hook item — the dual-diagram fragment they bought in Europe): show it to her here. She goes still, traces the marginal hand with one finger, and says — quietly, without anger — _"Esta letra es mía. Más vieja, pasada por dos o tres manos, pero es mía. Compraste un pedazo de lo que yo dejé suelto a los veinticuatro años."_ The leaf is a downstream descendant of her 1781 copy, sold along the European trade the Antiquarian later bought from. For the seeker it lands hard: the object that _proved to them the geometry was real_ turns out to be a fragment of one frightened scholar's mistake — understanding and culpability are the same chain. (It changes nothing mechanically; it binds the Antiquarian's whole arc to Mercedes' in one beat.)

She asks one thing: _"No te pido que perdones. Te pido que cuando ese hombre nos diga mañana de noche que yo soy la que abrió la puerta, ustedes ya lo sepan por mí. No por él."_ (Inoculating the players against El Patrón's C12 reveal.)

> ⊕ **Antiquarian + Reader-path bridge** _(only if the Antiquarian is the player walking with Mercedes AND the C5 page-79 seed was never planted)._ The standard Reader-path (C5 seed → C8 recognition → this cloister beat) is not available to someone who only joined the party in the C5 whisper tunnels — past both page-79 readings (the ruins and the bone chamber). If Mercedes senses an active seeker beside her — someone whose scholarship is not faith but hunger — she pauses at the far end of the cloister, draws out a handout copy of [page 79](assets/handouts-c5-c7.md#h2--manuscript-page-79-the-readers-page), and says: _"Saens escribió esto para quien viniera después. Nunca lo escuchó nadie en voz alta. ¿Querés leerlo? Acá, ahora, para él."_ If the Antiquarian reads it slowly, in the silence of the cloister — the same threshold as the bone-chamber reading in C5 — **the Reader-path seed is planted now**, and the C12 Use ending becomes available if this beat and the [C8 Mercedes beat](chapter-08.md#mercedes-between-phases) both happened. The seeker finds, in Saens's voice, the limit of scholarship against the real thing — and stays anyway. **Only offer this if the Use path would otherwise be permanently closed; do not run it if a different PC already carries the seed.**
>
> **At this table this bridge stays shut — there is no Reader.** It is the one door left to reopen Use, and it does not open: the Antiquarian is the thematic Reader and may *want* it, but Mercedes does not offer the page (or offers and it does not take). **The seeker wanting the Reader role and being denied it is the intended beat.** The dock Choice is Hide or Destroyed.

**She gives the player a folded square of parchment**, wax-sealed with the Arc 1 binding figure inside-out:

> _"Si en la noche de mañana llega un momento en que tienen que decidir entre esconder, usar, o destruir — y tienen que destruir — ábranlo. Es de mi mano. Es la única forma que conozco de cerrar el libro entero, no solo la fuga."_ **Do not open it tonight — she asks them not to.** **What it is (Keeper-only, players learn in C12)**: _two_ joined pages, folded together. The outer page is the **destroy-ritual**. The inner page — seen only if a player fully unfolds and turns it over — is the **binding section of the Necronomicón itself, the use-ritual**, copied forty years ago, never used, never shown. **The only existing copy outside the vault.** If a player unfolds it all the way tonight, Mercedes only says: _"Si lo viste, lo viste. Mañana decidís qué hacer con eso."_

> **⭐ Reader-path beat — only if the [C5 seed](chapter-05.md#reader-path-seed-use-ending-c12) and the [C8 recognition](chapter-08.md#mercedes-between-phases) both happened.** Mercedes turns to the PC who read Saens aloud and speaks to them alone: _"Si mañana llega ese momento — el de leer, no el de destruir — abrís el papel del lado que no te dije, y lees vos. Yo no lo puedo hacer. La copia me salió perfecta porque no entendí lo que estaba copiando. Si mañana lo entendés y lo lees — vas a quedar como yo, pero al revés: vas a entender, y no vas a poder copiar nunca más."_ The **Use offer, made explicit**, in private, to the one PC who earned it. The cost is not just SAN — it is a permanent inability to ever be on the _teaching_ side of any geometric tradition again: they will _know_, but never _transmit_. The PC may share this with the table or never share it; both are valid. _If the seed was never planted, skip this entirely — the inner page is the only Use foreshadowing, and most players won't unfold it._

**The Hide foreshadowing** — before they leave, Mercedes will not let this go unsaid: _"Mañana Ramón Albarrán va a venir al muelle… Si la geometría se cierra alrededor del libro y de él, Ramón sabe la fórmula latina. La aprendió de Saráchaga en 1798. La fórmula no es magia. Es un intercambio."_ And: _"El 'Hic manet' ata el lugar al tiempo que le queda al hombre que lo dice. Saráchaga lo iba a decir él… por eso lo mataron antes de que pudiera. Ramón está en condición de decirlo ahora. No le voy a pedir que lo haga. Esa es una decisión que toma él."_ **The Hide ending is now a choice with a known cost**: asking Albarrán to speak the Latin is asking him to die. The player may keep this to themselves.

## MIDNIGHT — THE PARLOR

The players return to Inocencia's around midnight. Bread on the table. And a **visitor** in Inocencia's chair — a man they have never seen and have met in a hundred small forms across four arcs. Dressed simply, not large, his face unremarkable in a way that should be impossible (the eye keeps trying to find a feature to remember, and fails). He stands, bows slightly:

> _"Qué bueno conocernos al fin. Soy Don Eligio Dadálah. Ustedes me conocen como El Patrón."_

Alone, unarmed, here to **talk**.

> _"No me quieren matar. No tendrían tiempo. Y yo no quiero matarlos. Lo que quiero es que mañana hagan exactamente lo que están planeando. Mover el libro. Llevarlo por la calle. Dárselo, sin saberlo, a la geometría que vine yo a despertar. Hagan eso, y al final de mañana yo los dejo vivir. Si no lo hacen — si lo dejan abajo — voy a tener que romper la bóveda yo mismo, y eso va a costarles mucho más que un viaje de carruaje."_

He is telling the truth, mostly — he _will_ let them live if the move happens, and _will_ break the vault if they refuse. The choice is real. **The trap he doesn't name**: he needs the book _moving_, not stationary — that is what lets him take it. He will not threaten Mercedes here, name Lacasa, or promise specifics. At the door, to the player he addressed most:

> _"Doña Mercedes les contó esta noche lo que copió hace cuarenta años. Tomó coraje. La quiero, ¿saben? Es de las pocas personas que me han hecho trabajar. Cuando mañana terminemos, voy a pedirles un favor: déjenmela viva. Yo la voy a tratar bien."_

He bows again. The door closes on its own. **SAN Check**: 1/1D6 — a single conversation with the avatar of Nyarlathotep, in a boarding-house parlor, with bread on the table.

> ⚠️ **El Patrón = no roll — the silence tell.** Within ~20 m of him the Marks go **silent**: the city-hum the Marked have carried since the [C10 grid entry](chapter-10.md#entry-into-the-city) stops dead. The absence _is_ the detection — they feel him as a hole in the geometry, the one shape the grid cannot read; it resumes when the door closes. Retroactively, the [crested carriage at Florida y San Martín](chapter-10.md#the-streets) was him. **This pays off at the Stand in [C12](chapter-12.md#chapter-12--el-libro)** — the Marks go silent _before_ he rounds the corner, giving the Cursed Gaucho his three seconds of warning.

**After he's gone**: Inocencia is asleep at the kitchen table, head on her arms — she has been asleep since before midnight; no one heard him come in. She wakes with a small gasp at the closing door and does not remember him. On the chair where he sat: a single thin object — **a pencil**, new, recently sharpened. Not threatening. A gift, or a joke, or a calling card. The players carry it; nothing they do with it produces any effect. It is just a pencil. _(Don't explain it. El Patrón's small kindnesses are more frightening than his violence.)_

> _Mercedes, que llega a la una de la mañana, escucha a los jugadores describir la visita sin interrumpir, y después: "Bueno. Mañana hacemos lo que planeamos. Y cuando llegue el momento, decidimos si dejarlo vivo a él, no a mí." Un reloj en algún lugar de la calle Belgrano empieza a dar la una — y la campanada suena, y suena, y no para hasta diez segundos más de lo que una sola campanada debería durar. Después, silencio. En su cuarto, sobre la mesa de luz, encuentran un solo objeto que no recuerdan haber dejado ahí: el pequeño disco de bronce de una piedra-testigo. Está caliente._
