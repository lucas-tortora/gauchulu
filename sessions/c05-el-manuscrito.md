# CHAPTER 5 — EL MANUSCRITO Y LOS QUE VOLVIERON

### _The Manuscript & The Returned_

**Goal**: Understand the full history of the scar. Meet the Wrong Returned. Locate the original wound in the land — and learn what it asks of those who would close it. **Exit requirement**: the players leave knowing the ruins are the _teacher_ and the **pit at La Esperanza** is the _work_ — they must **return west**. **Emotional arc**: Comprehension → Weight of history → The horror of being chosen.

> **Keeper note — running this stretch.** The Wrong Returned are the emotional center: the most human element in an increasingly inhuman situation. Their composure is their own, not the entity's. The ruins are not spooky — there are no ghosts, no traps; the horror is geological. And the manuscript pays off _here_ — the party has carried it since [Ch 4](../chapter-04-el-patron.md#the-locked-study); give them time with it.

## OPENING & THE MORNING LETTER

The investigators have the [manuscript](../chapter-04-el-patron.md#the-locked-study) and the knowledge that [Don Eusebio](../REF-NPCs.md#don-eusebio-valdez) must stand in the closing ritual's outer ring. They met **[Héctor](../REF-NPCs.md#h%C3%A9ctor-r%C3%ADos) and [Marta](../REF-NPCs.md#marta-r%C3%ADos)** at the fence line last night ([Ch 4](../chapter-04-el-patron.md#the-wrong-returned--first-appearance)). [Silvio](../REF-NPCs.md#silvio-m%C3%A9ndez) stays at the estancia, watching the south pasture until he can't hold back — then walks south, toward the pit, not the ruins. Keep track of where Silvio goes.

>

A marginal note on page 47 of Saens's manuscript — which Quirce transcribed — caught my attention. If Quirce's reading is correct, that word changes a great deal. There is a second voice willing to help — not his. If you need a second opinion on what is happening on your lands, you may write to me at the address below. — M.S."_

_(The "word" Mercedes alludes to but does not write is **Necronomicón**. She writes around it on principle; she will name it only in person or in a sealed reply. ## THE WRONG RETURNED

### Morning after (or first approach)

If the party **already spoke with Héctor and Marta at the fence** ([C4 night beat](../chapter-04-el-patron.md#the-wrong-returned--first-appearance)), open with them falling in alongside the horses as the party leaves — no re-introduction. Use the dialogue below for questions not yet asked, or for sustained daylight conversation on the road. If the party **has not yet approached them**, they spent the night standing at the fence without sleeping; in the morning they are still there:

> _They turn to face you before you call out. Simultaneously. Not startled — they knew you were coming. Up close, they look exactly as [Rosa](../REF-NPCs.md#rosa) described: they eat, they answer questions, they stand in sunlight without apparent discomfort. But there is something in the focus of their attention that is wrong in a way that takes a moment to name. They are looking at you, and also looking at something that occupies the same space as you but is not quite you — a geometry of your position in the space, rather than your face._
> 
> **[ES]** _Se dan vuelta para mirarte antes de que los llames. Al mismo tiempo. No sobresaltados — sabían que venías. De cerca, son exactamente como los describió Rosa: comen, contestan preguntas, se quedan parados al sol sin molestia aparente. Pero hay algo en el foco de su atención que está mal de una manera que cuesta un momento nombrar. Te están mirando a vos, y también mirando algo que ocupa el mismo espacio que vos pero que no sos del todo — una geometría de tu posición en el espacio, más que tu cara._

**Appearance**:

- **Héctor Ríos**, 54. Former farmer from San Ruiz, wide-shouldered, graying. Was considered steady — not smart but reliable. Now answers questions with precise answers and no small talk; he has lost the social texture around his speech — the _"pos"_, the pauses. He just answers.
- **Marta Ríos** (his wife, no relation — the surname coincidence was a village joke), 43. Was the village teacher and midwife. Calm by profession; calmer now in a way that is not professional. The more present of the two — occasionally something that was Marta crosses her face and is gone.

**What they know**: not everything. They know what the entity showed them — geometrically accurate but not experientially interpretable. They speak in architecture and space, not sensation.

_"It isn't underground,"_ Héctor says. _"That was wrong. It's in the angles. It lives in the places where one surface meets another. The pit is just where the shape is biggest — where there's enough angle for it to reach through."_ — **[ES]** _"No está bajo tierra. Eso estaba mal. Está en los ángulos. Vive en los lugares donde una superficie se encuentra con otra. El pozo es solo donde la forma es más grande — donde hay suficiente ángulo para que pase por ahí."_

_"There are others like us,"_ Marta says (meaning Silvio, whom they have sensed but not met). _"Not many. The ones it touched but didn't take. We're still here. But we're also partly there."_ — **[ES]** _"Hay otros como nosotros. No muchos. Los que tocó pero no se llevó. Seguimos acá. Pero también estamos en parte allá."_

_"We came back because it's almost time,"_ Héctor says. _"We felt it from Tres Lagunas. The closing, or the opening. One of them is going to happen. We need to be at the geometry when it does."_ — **[ES]** _"Volvimos porque ya casi es la hora. Lo sentimos desde Tres Lagunas. El cierre, o la apertura. Una de las dos va a pasar. Tenemos que estar en la geometría cuando pase."_

**What they want**: to be at the pit for the final event — closing or opening, no preference. They are not aligned with the entity; they are anchored to the geometry. Their presence at the closing will matter ([Ch 9](./c09-el-cierre.md)).

**The question no one wants to ask** — what happens to them when the geometry is sealed? Marta, without distress: _"I don't know. If the shape closes, maybe we close with it. Maybe we come back. We decided it doesn't matter. We need to be there."_ — **[ES]** _"No sé. Si la forma se cierra, capaz nos cerramos con ella. Capaz volvemos. Decidimos que no importa. Tenemos que estar ahí."_

**SAN Check**: 0/1D3 (sustained conversation with someone who is and isn't a person).

**Possible Q&A**

_"Do you remember what happened to you?"_

> Héctor: _"I remember walking south. The night was very clear. And then it was like — like someone showed me a map of a place I'd been trying to describe without words. It was a relief, for a moment. And then I was back and it was morning."_ — **[ES]** _"Me acuerdo de caminar para el sur. La noche estaba muy clara. Y después fue como — como si alguien me mostrara un mapa de un lugar que venía tratando de describir sin palabras. Fue un alivio, por un momento. Y después estaba de vuelta y era de mañana."_ Marta: _"I remember it differently. There was no relief in mine. Only size. Something very large noticing something very small."_ — **[ES]** _"Yo me acuerdo distinto. En el mío no hubo alivio. Solo tamaño. Algo muy grande notando algo muy chico."_

_"Are you in pain?"_

> Héctor: _"No."_ _(Too quickly, too finally.)_ — **[ES]** _"No."_ _(Dicho demasiado rápido y demasiado terminante.)_ Marta: _(A long pause.)_ _"I don't feel pain the way I used to. I'm not sure if that's better."_ — **[ES]** _(Una pausa larga.)_ _"No siento dolor como antes. No estoy segura de que eso sea mejor."_

_"Can you be helped?"_

> Marta: _"You could close the geometry. Maybe that ends this. Or maybe this is what we are now and closing it just removes the sound."_ _(She touches her own face as if checking it's still there.)_ _"Close it anyway."_ — **[ES]** _"Podrían cerrar la geometría. Capaz eso termina con esto. O capaz esto es lo que somos ahora y cerrarla solo saca el sonido."_ _(Se toca la propia cara como comprobando que sigue ahí.)_ _"Ciérrenla igual."_

## EL POMBERO ON THE RIDGE

On departure, after Héctor and Marta have fallen in. The road east skirts a low rise — a _cuchilla_ the locals call _El Pelón_, "the bald spot," because no grass grows on its crown. Tomás doesn't look at it as you ride past; Don Eusebio's _peones_ won't speak its name. As the horses round its base, a small figure appears at the top — not climbing, simply _there_:

> _He is short. Brown skin, broad face, a straw hat too large for his head. He raises one hand and waves — not friendly, not threatening, just acknowledging that you are passing. He whistles. The whistle has more notes than there are notes a person should know._
> 
> **[ES]** _Es bajo. Piel morena, cara ancha, un sombrero de paja demasiado grande para su cabeza. Levanta una mano y saluda — ni amistoso, ni amenazante, solo reconociendo que están pasando. Silba. El silbido tiene más notas de las que una persona debería conocer._

**[El Pombero](../REF-NPCs.md#el-pombero)** is the guide-and-test. He does not _do_ anything yet — he whistles, waves, vanishes when looked away from. **Spot Hidden** while passing: the grass on _El Pelón_'s crown has the same ring pattern as the cattle field's rings — older, smaller, the same shape. _El Pelón_ is a _previous_ burial pit, ten generations old. **Do not open an entrance here.**

Tomás, if pressed, strangled: _"El Pombero. Le silba a los que están por entrar a algo. No me pregunten más."_ — _"El Pombero. He whistles to those who are about to enter into something. Don't ask me more."_

> **Roll for Hound** ([table](#roll-for-hound--c5)): if a Marked PC looks hard at the ring pattern, or the party camps in sight of the rise — both Marked PCs roll 2d6.

## THE ROAD TO THE RUINS

The Jesuit mission ruins are a day's ride east and slightly south; Saens's notes give landmarks (a forked river, a mesa that looks like a tooth) still accurate. The Wrong Returned walk alongside, requiring no water stops, keeping pace without effort.

> _The land between the estancia and the ruins is ordinary pampa — flat, dry grass, occasional low scrub. But if you travel with Héctor and Marta, you begin to see it through their attention. They note corners. Not dramatically — a glance when the road bends, a moment of stillness where a mesa casts a shadow in a right angle. They are reading the geometry of the land the way a cattleman reads grass for moisture._
> 
> **[ES]** _La tierra entre la estancia y las ruinas es pampa común — pasto chato y seco, algún matorral bajo de vez en cuando. Pero si viajás con Héctor y Marta, empezás a verla a través de su atención. Registran las esquinas. No de manera dramática — una mirada cuando el camino dobla, un instante de quietud donde una meseta proyecta una sombra en ángulo recto. Leen la geometría de la tierra como un hombre de campo lee el pasto buscando humedad._

**Optional encounter — the road**: during the night camp, a [Luz Mala](../REF-NPCs.md#la-luz-mala) appears to the south — not orbiting, stationary, ~200 m distant. Watching. It followed from the estancia. Héctor, without fear: _"It's a sensor. Not a threat unless you disturb the geometry."_ — **[ES]** _"Es un sensor. No es una amenaza salvo que toques la geometría."_ It tracks them all the way to the ruins.

### The merchant with tarnished copper (5b)

Late in the day's ride — the tooth-shaped mesa visible, the ruins an hour ahead — the party meets **Don Crisanto Larrea** before the stones.

> _A small cart is pulled off the track in the lee of a low rise, axle propped on a stone. A grey-bearded man in his mid-fifties is sorting through his goods with the expression of someone who has already lost an argument with the day. He looks up as you approach. He does not reach for a weapon. He reaches for his hat._
> 
> **[ES]** _Un carro chico está apartado del camino al abrigo de una cuchilla baja, el eje apoyado en una piedra. Un hombre canoso de unos cincuenta y pico está revisando su mercadería con la cara de quien ya perdió una discusión con el día. Levanta la vista cuando se acercan. No busca un arma. Busca el sombrero._

**Backstory (if asked)**: a traveling merchant on the Tandil–Buenos Aires road — _yerba_, copper _bombillas_, buttons, kettle-fittings, a few iron tools. His axle cracked two days east. He limped to the [ruins](#the-jesuit-mission-ruins), spent **last night** camped inside the broken walls, and was leaving this morning when the copper went wrong.

> _Every copper item has tarnished black overnight — not the green of oxide, black, evenly, as if held in flame for an hour. The bombillas are unusable, the buttons dull, the fittings ruined. The iron tools are untouched. The yerba is fine. **Only the copper, and only the copper on the side of the cart that faced the chapel floor** — the carved stone he slept beside without knowing what it was. The copper on the far side is normal._
> 
> **[ES]** _Cada objeto de cobre se ennegreció durante la noche — no el verde del óxido natural, negro, parejo, como si lo hubieran tenido a la llama una hora. Las bombillas quedaron inservibles, los botones opacos, los herrajes arruinados. Las herramientas de hierro, intactas. La yerba, bien. **Solo el cobre, y solo el cobre que estaba del lado del carro que daba al piso de la capilla** — la piedra tallada junto a la cual durmió sin saber qué era. El cobre del otro lado está normal._

Larrea is bewildered — twenty years a merchant, never seen this: _"Dormí al lado de esa piedra en el piso. No la toqué. Esta mañana el cobre del lado de la piedra estaba así. Es como si algo hubiera mirado el cobre toda la noche. ¿Saben? Como si el cobre hubiera estado mirándolo a él también."_ — _"I slept beside that stone. I didn't touch it. This morning the copper on the stone side was like this. As if something had looked at the copper all night. You know? As if the copper had been looking back."_

**Héctor or Marta** (if present): a glance at the chapel, then the copper — _"The stone is still doing work. So is the copper now."_ No more unless pressed.

> **What this is**: the [dual diagram](../assets/dual-diagram.svg) under the chapel floor _recognized_ the copper as metallically similar to [Quirce's](../REF-NPCs.md#aldao-quirce) brass markers and tried to extend through it overnight. A **Hard Cthulhu Mythos** roll identifies the same geometric-extension effect as Quirce's markers — but here, without markers, pulled from _below_. Marked PCs may feel it unprompted.
> 
> **What investigators can do**: buy the ruined copper at scrap price (Larrea is grateful). Brought to the [bone chamber](#the-bone-chamber) later, it _re-tarnishes_ on contact with the walls — confirming the link. [Mercedes](../REF-NPCs.md#do%C3%B1a-mercedes-sol%C3%ADs) will later recognize the signature.
> 
> **Larrea's onward role**: he limps east toward Buenos Aires once trade is done, arriving in the city before the players do, two months later. He tells the tarnished-copper story to the _vigilantes urbanos_ at the gate; it reaches [Saráchaga](../REF-NPCs.md#don-eladio-sar%C3%A1chaga), who writes Mercedes that _"the pressure is now visible at the perimeter, not just at the centre."_ This is one of the threads that brings Mercedes to the camp in [C7](./c07-la-vispera.md#doña-mercedes-arrives-late). Larrea is the campaign's first non-Keeper, non-curandera, non-tribal civilian witness to the entity's reach. He never knows what he saw. He dies of natural causes in 1843.

**Placement**: run on the **approach** (end of road) or as the **first beat at the ruins** — whichever fits pacing.

## THE JESUIT MISSION RUINS

> _The mission was abandoned in 1767 when the Jesuits were expelled. What remains: the foundation stones of four buildings, a broken bell tower still partially standing, walls that reach chest-height and no further. The stone is pale limestone, different from the local rock — it was carried here. Whoever built this chose this location deliberately._
> 
> _The vegetation inside the ruins is different — denser, greener despite the season. Things grow here that shouldn't grow this far south — a fig tree, improbably, in the corner of what was the chapel. As if the land here is more **fértil**._
> 
> _That word. That word again._
> 
> **[ES]** _La misión fue abandonada en 1767, cuando los jesuitas fueron expulsados. Lo que queda: las piedras de cimiento de cuatro edificios, un campanario roto que todavía se sostiene en parte, paredes que llegan a la altura del pecho y nada más. La piedra es caliza pálida, distinta de la roca de la zona — la trajeron hasta acá. Quien construyó esto eligió este lugar a propósito._
> 
> **[ES]** _La vegetación dentro de las ruinas es distinta — más densa, más verde a pesar de la estación. Acá crecen cosas que no deberían crecer tan al sur — una higuera, improbablemente, en el rincón de lo que fue la capilla. Como si la tierra acá fuera más **fértil**._
> 
> **[ES]** _Esa palabra. Otra vez esa palabra._

**Keeper notes**:

- The standing bell tower is the most intact structure. The bell itself fell and lies half-buried in the grass, inscribed **1749** — twelve years before the mission was built. It was brought from elsewhere.
- The chapel foundation has carved stones worked into the floor — flush with the others but different: the limestone has the geometry carved into it. These are the originals Saens found. The mission was built _on_ them.

### The carving

> _When you find the carved stone — and you will, because Héctor walks directly to it — it is both smaller and more precise than you expected. Perhaps sixty centimeters across, recessed into the chapel floor. The carving is deep and clean: the same angular-circular geometry, but here complete. Both the opening diagram and the counter-diagram, rendered simultaneously — one inside the other, interacting. Like a lock and key carved into the same stone._
> 
> _It is, without question, the most precise thing you have ever seen made by hand._
> 
> **[ES]** _Cuando encontrás la piedra tallada — y la vas a encontrar, porque Héctor camina derecho hacia ella — es a la vez más chica y más precisa de lo que esperabas. Unos sesenta centímetros de ancho, hundida en el piso de la capilla. El tallado es profundo y limpio: la misma geometría angular y circular, pero acá está completa. Tanto el diagrama de apertura como el contra-diagrama, al mismo tiempo — uno dentro del otro, interactuando. Como una cerradura y una llave talladas en la misma piedra._
> 
> **[ES]** _Es, sin lugar a dudas, la cosa más precisa que viste hecha a mano en tu vida._

> **Visual handout**: [dual-diagram.svg](../assets/dual-diagram.svg). Print large (A4 min, A3 if possible). This is the carved stone; what Marta finger-traces at midnight; what they will see in bone in the [bone chamber](#the-bone-chamber); what Buenos Aires _becomes_ in [C12](./c12-el-libro.md). Show it — do not describe it.
> 
> **Roll for Hound**: Marked PC looks hard at the stone, or page 79 read **aloud** — both Marked PCs roll 2d6.

**Skill checks**:

|Skill|Result|
|---|---|
|Occult|Not two competing symbols — one symbol. The opening and closing are two aspects of the same geometry, like a door and a doorframe. The opening is not wrong. It is **incomplete**.|
|Cthulhu Mythos|The geometry predates human habitation of the region by a very long time. Who carved it — before Saens, before the Tehuelche — has no answer in any tradition this character knows.|
|History|The limestone does not exist within 200 km. It was brought from the north. The stone was carved _elsewhere_ and installed here.|
|Art/Craft (Drawing)|The precision is geometric in the mathematical sense — the angles are _exact_. The tools of any human period should not produce this quality of line.|

**Héctor**, hands on the stone (the only one who touches it without hesitation): _"This is where the shape lives. The pit is just where someone tore a hole in it."_ — **[ES]** _"Acá es donde vive la forma. El pozo es solo donde alguien le abrió un agujero."_

**Marta**, from across the room: _"Padre Saens was right about one thing. It isn't the devil. But he was wrong about another. He said it was a wound. It isn't. It's a door. The wound is what we did to it."_ — **[ES]** _"Padre Saens tenía razón en una cosa. No es el diablo. Pero se equivocó en otra. Dijo que era una herida. No lo es. Es una puerta. La herida es lo que le hicimos nosotros."_

## DEEP READ: THE MANUSCRIPT

> **Saens notes**: [player handout](../02-la-salamanca/padre-saens-manuscript-player.md) · [keeper](../02-la-salamanca/padre-saens-notes-keeper.md)

Cross-referencing the manuscript with the carved stone makes additional passages legible:

**Page 71 (margin — illegible until the carved stone is in sight)**:

> _"The old site teaches the shape. The new wound demands the work. Return west with what was learned. To linger in the teacher's house is to leave the door open."_ — **[ES]** _"El sitio viejo enseña la forma. La herida nueva exige la obra. Volved al oeste con lo aprendido. Quedarse en la casa del maestro es dejar la puerta abierta."_

**Page 72 (water-damaged — now partly reconstructable)**:

> _"Antu-Rayo told me the ones who touch the shape and survive become anchors. They hold the geometry in place against distortion. Without them, the closing cannot be maintained against the entity's resistance. The people who went to the field at night and returned — they are not lost. They are necessary."_ — **[ES]** _"Antu-Rayo me dijo que aquellos que tocan la forma y sobreviven se vuelven anclas. Sostienen la geometría contra la distorsión. Sin ellos, el cierre no puede mantenerse contra la resistencia de la entidad. Las gentes que fueron al campo de noche y regresaron — no están perdidas. Son necesarias."_

This is the chapter's key revelation: the Wrong Returned are **structural components of the closing ritual**. Their presence at the outer ring stabilizes the geometry against the entity's pressure in a way trained humans cannot replicate. This changes their status from victims-to-be-protected to agents-with-a-role.

**Page 79 (final legible line)**:

> _"I could not close it alone. I put this where the next one will find it. God forgive the next one for what they'll need to do."_ — **[ES]** _"No pude cerrarlo solo. Dejo esto donde el próximo lo halle. Dios perdone al próximo por lo que habrá de hacer."_

**SAN Check** (reading this line): 0/1 — recognition of what Saens felt, and what it means that the investigators are the "next one."

### Reader-path seed (Use ending, C12)

The PC who reads **page 79 aloud at the table**, in the GM's hearing, has — without anyone explaining it, including the GM — taken the first step into the **Reader role** available in C12 (Use ending). The PC does not know this; the Marked PCs do not see it; **[Mercedes](../REF-NPCs.md#do%C3%B1a-mercedes-sol%C3%ADs) recognizes it later** ([C8](./c08-la-traicion.md)). From this point, when the reader looks at _any_ ritual text (Saens's manuscript, Quirce's notebook, Mercedes's parchment, the Necronomicón's binding section in C12), the words **stay in their head verbatim, in Saens's voice**. This is the campaign's quietest seed and pays off in C12.

> **Disambiguation**: if multiple PCs read page 79 aloud, the seed lands on the one who reads _slowest_, with the most weight, in the most silence — willingness to bear, not skill. **If no PC reads it aloud** (here or over the skull below), the Reader path is **closed** in C12: only Hide and Destroyed are available. This is the only campaign branch C5 choices can close. **Make a Keeper note about whether the seed was planted. Do not retrofit.**

_(Second and final chance to plant the seed is the [reading-over-the-skull](#reader-path-failsafe--reading-over-the-skull) failsafe in the bone chamber.)_

## THE NIGHT AT THE RUINS

_(optional)_

If investigators camp at the ruins, the night brings a single event. At ~2 AM, Marta wakes and walks toward the chapel ruin. She doesn't go inside; she stands at the threshold and traces the air with her finger — the complete dual diagram, from memory, in the dark, in three dimensions, eyes open but focused on something not there.

When she finishes, not to anyone, in the way [La Llorona](../REF-NPCs.md#la-llorona) spoke: _"Fourteen people went to the field at night. Seven came back changed. Five of those didn't survive the change. Two did. I'm one of the two."_ — **[ES]** _"Catorce personas fueron al campo de noche. Siete volvieron cambiadas. Cinco de esas no sobrevivieron al cambio. ## THE BONE CHAMBER

The shaft drops twelve meters to a roughly six-meter hexagonal chamber, walls of the same imported limestone as the Saens stone above, ceiling low enough that players bend slightly. The floor is **bones, in the dual diagram's pattern**.

> _The bones are arranged into the complete dual diagram, at chamber-floor scale. Perhaps two hundred individual bones — long bones, skulls, vertebrae, intermixed deliberately by hand. The skulls are placed at the diagram's outer-ring positions. They face inward. They are not human — too long, too narrow at the jaw, eye sockets too far apart. They are not from a species the players have a name for._
> 
> _In the very center of the diagram, where someone must kneel as the center anchor at the closing, is a single human skull. Old. Modern human. Its forehead unscarred. **It is [Padre Ignacio Saens](../REF-NPCs.md#padre-ignacio-saens-sj).** He came back here, alone, the year before he died, and laid himself on the diagram in place of the people he didn't have — opener, knowledge-holder, and counter-figure all at once — and bought what time he could. His body was taken into the geometry as substitute. The skull is the only piece left because the geometry needs a center, and a man's bone holds where parchment doesn't._
> 
> **[ES]** _Los huesos están dispuestos formando el diagrama doble completo, a escala del piso. Hay quizá doscientos — huesos largos, cráneos, vértebras, entremezclados deliberadamente a mano. ### The Bone Vigil (_De Vigilia Ossium_) — hidden rite

**Discovery**: a limestone niche the size of two fists in the **south wall**, easy to miss. **Spot Hidden (Regular)** or **Search (Hard)** after the diagram lesson, or when anyone searches the walls.

> _Inside: a roll of waxed cloth, stiff with age. Unrolled: three sheets in Saens's hand — a draft, not part of the bound manuscript. The ink is greener than the study folios. The title: **De Vigilia Ossium**. Figura I shows the closing ring walked clockwise; Figura II shows a kneeler beside a skull, not on it. A Mapudungun phrase is boxed: **Antu kishu, lan amukey**. At the bottom, in a shaking hand: Scripsi hoc postquam solus iacui. Alius vigilabit. — "I wrote this after I lay down alone. Another will keep vigil."_

**Handout**: [`bone-vigil-rite.svg`](../assets/bone-vigil-rite.svg).

**What it is**: a **closing-aspect** rite — counterpart to the opening-aspect [Graveyard Kiss](../01-la-tierra-mala/diagrams.md#the-graveyard-kiss-de-osculo-sepulcrali--eusebios-locked-study-page) in Eusebio's study. Saens drafted it after his first solo attempt failed, before he lay down to die. It closes nothing. It lets the geometry **answer one honest question** about the scar at La Esperanza.

**Héctor**, if read aloud: _"El cura lo escribió para quien viniera después. No para él."_ — _"The priest wrote it for whoever came after. Not for himself."_ He will not stop them.

**Requirements**: a complete dual diagram underfoot (the bone floor qualifies; a traced pit copy does not unless drawn at Hard Occult) · a **kneeler** beside Saens's skull (never on it, never moving it — Héctor intervenes if they try) · a **walker** with a long bone from the floor, touching each outer-ring skull once, **clockwise**, starting at the eastern station · kneeler speaks **Antu kishu, lan amukey** (Antu-Rayo's fragment — the line [Mercedes](../REF-NPCs.md#do%C3%B1a-mercedes-sol%C3%ADs) leads at the pit in C8) and holds **thirteen breaths** without moving · then **one honest question** about the La Esperanza scar, spoken aloud.

|Step|Detail|
|---|---|
|**Roll**|**CON (Hard)** for the kneeler; the walker needs no roll unless they disturb a skull|
|**Cost**|Kneeler: **1D6 SAN, 1D4 HP** (geometry presses inward) · Walker: **0/1 SAN**|
|**On success**|The chamber answers **once**, truthfully — not in speech: pressure, image, compass-pull, or a sentence in the kneeler's own voice from the wrong direction. Give a **true** short answer (east fails first, Eusebio must stand in the outer ring, Quirce's asymmetry, what the pit wants)|

## THE WHISPERING TUNNELS

Beyond the bone chamber, three tunnels lead deeper — ~2 m tall, smooth-walled, ceilings carved with the dual diagram's outer ring repeated at one-meter intervals. They are _acoustically tuned_: every word spoken in any tunnel is repeated at the entrance of all three, in the speaker's voice, at the original volume.

Practical effect: any roll involving speaking in the tunnels is **one step harder** — the speaker hears their own words returned in real time. **A Listen roll** identifies the tuning; silence works as a navigational tool.

The three tunnels lead to:

- **Offering chamber**: a stone table with a cup, bone needle, and a clay tablet in unreadable script. **Hard Library Use** identifies **proto-Tehuelche**, but the script is older than known proto-Tehuelche by perhaps a thousand years. A permanent campaign object — Mercedes will want it.
- **Flooded chamber**: the floor dropped; groundwater chest-high. Submerged: another dual diagram, same scale as the chapel stone — intact and _still active_. _Observable_: pampas grass dropped on the water won't sink; it arranges into a small dual-diagram on the surface within thirty seconds.
- **Sealed door** at the end of the third tunnel — the [Great Wizard's chamber](#the-chamber-of-the-great-wizard). El Pombero warned. Default: leave it sealed and move on. **If the players insist on opening it**, run the optional scene below.

**Marked PC in the tunnels**: speaking quietly, they hear their own words returned — with a _second_ voice underneath, faint, in the rhythm of the outer ring (twelve syllables, pause, twelve syllables). They will recognize this rhythm at the closing ([C8](./c08-la-traicion.md)) and at the dock ([C12](./c12-el-libro.md)).

> **Roll for Hound**: first spoken word in the tunnels under stress; Marked PC notices the twelve-syllable undertone; entering the flooded chamber with the active diagram — both Marked PCs roll 2d6.

### Keeper brief — who he is
