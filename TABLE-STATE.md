## THE GOAT THREAD *(table-specific element)*

The canonical chapter-02 text states: the goat lives in the well, does not eat, watches. If investigators try to rescue her, she walks back into the well and jumps in. She is a campaign-spanning witness; she is still there whenever players return to San Ruiz.

**At this table, this did not happen.**

The players extracted the goat — with two injuries and considerable effort — and she did not return to the well. She now follows the party.

### Why the well — the original placement

The goat has been in the well since before the investigators arrived. Not weeks — **months**. Since the gate started opening.

The well is a straight vertical shaft — no corners, no angles where two surfaces meet at anything but a right angle, and even those are four identical ones at cardinal positions. The entity lives in the irregularity of angles, in the geometry of complex intersection. The well is the one space in San Ruiz that offers it no purchase. Flat walls, flat floor, open sky above. Geometric minimum.

Mandinga placed her there when he first felt the gate opening — not to watch San Ruiz, but to **hide her from detection**. She is his territorial marker, his agent-in-place, and she had to remain invisible to whatever geometry the entity uses to map its surroundings. The well was the blind spot. She has been there, waiting, since the gate first cracked.

Rosa knew. *"La cabra está ahí"* — present tense, flat, the same register she uses when naming what the doorframe binding does. Not a guess. A fixed point in her reading of the village. As long as the goat was in the well, something was watching. When the chapel seal collapsed, the only remaining constant in her reading of San Ruiz was the goat.

The investigators removed it.

When Rosa next looks at the empty well, she will understand the seal is gone — not from being told, but from the absence of what was always going to be there.

### Now exposed

While the goat was in the well, she was invisible to the entity's geometry. The well was the one space it could not map. She was a blind spot in its awareness.

**She is not in the well anymore.**

The entity knows she exists now. Not what she is — but that there is something moving with the investigators that has a different quality of wrongness than the investigators do. Something that doesn't fit its geometry. Something that should not be in the open.

This matters because she will start to *act*. The deal, the Llorona headbutt, the way she is always already where the investigators are going — these are not new behaviors. They are behaviors that were always possible, now deployed because concealment is no longer an option. Mandinga put her in the well to wait. She is not in the well anymore. She is in play.

### She can harm the immaterial

The goat is Mandinga's creature: folk-devil matter, something that exists in the in-between space between physical and non-physical, in the territory between living and deal-bound. She has more presence in that threshold space than a person does. More than a weapon does. More than fire, which is the only other thing that forces the entity back.

**She can strike what normal matter passes through.**

Psychic remnants, echoes, displaced entities, the Hound's edge-geometry — she can reach them. Not destroy: **disrupt**. A headbutt from the goat doesn't kill a supernatural entity. It staggers it. It disrupts its form. It buys time and creates distance. Against La Llorona at the San Ruiz chapel — seen at this table when investigators depart — she headbutted the displaced echo mid-turn and sent it lurching east in a retreat it hadn't chosen.

This is the reveal. Players who saw it understand the goat is a weapon. Players who reason it out will understand she can be deployed.

**Tactical limits**: She acts on her own judgment. She cannot be directed. She will not headbutt the Hound on command — she will do it when *she decides to*, and she will decide correctly, and the timing will always be hers. Players who try to point her at things or hold her back will find she has simply already done what she was going to do. She does not take instruction from people who have not closed a pit with their hands.

**What this means for players who keep her**: The party has, at all times, one disruption of a supernatural entity available — timed by the goat, not by them. This is not nothing. Against an entity that cannot be killed and cannot be fought, a few seconds of disorientation buys everything. Let the players feel this weight. Let them protect the goat. Let them wonder when she will use it and worry that she won't.

### The Mandinga connection

The goat is a Mandinga marker.

Mandinga is the gaucho devil — the folk name for the Devil in the Río de la Plata. He runs La Salamanca, the underground school where forbidden arts are taught. He rides at midnight. He takes the form of black animals. He makes deals but can be outwitted by someone clever and lucky. He is the master of the morally compromised.

He is also being displaced.

The entity pushing through the pit is not Mandinga's kind of power. It is something so alien it doesn't acknowledge his territory exists. For perhaps the first time in the history of the pampas, Mandinga is not the most dangerous thing in the land. He knows this. The goat in the well — in the one space the entity's geometry cannot touch — is his marker. Not a neutral observer: a territorial claim. *This well is still mine. I am watching.*

The investigators reached into the one blind spot in San Ruiz and pulled out what Mandinga placed there. He is aware of them now. He is deciding what to do about it.

**The La Salamanca thread — Arc 2.** The Great Wizard of Salamanca believes he is working in the tradition of Mandinga's school. He is not. El Patrón has been steering him for forty years — working within Mandinga's domain without permission, using Mandinga's infrastructure to advance an agenda Mandinga would never have agreed to. Mandinga knows he has been used, and he knows by whom. The investigators walking into La Salamanca in C5-6 are walking into the territory of a displaced, furious entity who has more reason than anyone to want the elder god gone. He will not explain this to them directly. He does not operate that way. But the goat will be there.

**When to surface the connection**: La Salamanca is the right moment. Not before. Until then, the goat is simply wrong — a constant, a watch, a presence the players cannot read. Let it cook.

**How Mandinga might eventually move**: He will not fight the entity directly — he lacks that kind of power, and he knows it. He operates through deals, through the people already involved, through the things he can still claim. He will not offer the investigators a clean alliance. He will offer a transaction — something specific, something bounded — when the moment is right. What he wants: acknowledgment that his territory was violated, and the entity's removal from it. He and the investigators want the same outcome for entirely different reasons.

### How to run the goat going forward

She does not follow. She *precedes*. She is never behind the party — she is already where they are going.

**The core rule**: The goat is always already there. No one sees her arrive. No one hears her hooves. The investigators ride two hours ahead and make camp, and when they look up from the fire she is standing at the edge of the light. Not approaching. Already there. Waiting. The players will try to explain this the first time. By the third time they will stop trying.

**Specific beats to deploy across sessions** — use sparingly, one per session maximum:

- They tie her to a post at the last village and ride hard for an hour. When they dismount, she is standing by their horses. No rope. No sweat. Just there, looking at the gravely injured PC.
- The injured PC wakes before dawn to the sound of nothing. The goat is directly beside them, close enough that they should have felt her warmth. She is looking at their wound. She does not move when they move.
- The party passes through a gap in a fence that a goat could not fit through. She is on the other side.
- Someone looks at her for a long time — long enough to feel watched back. They realize, at some point in that looking, that she has not blinked. Not once. And she has been looking at them the entire time they have been looking at her.
- She is already facing the direction something is coming from before anyone else knows something is coming.

**She does not make sounds.** No bleating. No hooves on stone. No breathing that anyone notices. When she is in a room or a camp she takes up a specific quality of silence — a silence you notice when it stops, not when it starts.

**She does not react.** Horses rear — she stands. Guns fire — she stands. Fire comes close — she stands. Her pupils do not dilate. Her ears do not move. She is watching.

**The injured PC beat**: She does not protect them overtly. But once — once, timed to a moment when the injured PC is about to blunder into something dangerous in the dark (a ledge, a wrong step, a door they shouldn't open) — she is simply already there. Standing in the way. Immovable as stone. The PC goes around her. When they look back: she's gone. Nothing in the moment is explained. Nothing is called out. The players will remember it.

**She does not eat. Does not defecate. Does not bleed if cut.** If you press a blade into her flank, the blade finds no purchase — not supernaturally, just as if the geometry of harm keeps sliding past her. She turns her head and looks at the person holding the knife. That is all.

**Do not explain any of this until Arc 2.** Let it cook.

**Rosa, when she next sees the goat with them**: She looks at the empty well first, then at the goat. She understands what it means — that what was kept in the blind spot is now walking in the open. She will look at the investigators differently after this. Not with fear. With the particular attention of someone recalculating how much trouble they are capable of.

**The question this thread plants** — one the players should carry for arcs: *What did we take, and why is it always already where we're going?*

### The deal

When a PC is effectively dying — at the point where the dice have spoken and medicine isn't coming and everyone at the table knows — the goat makes her move.

She does not rush. She approaches the dying PC and gets very close: close enough that they should feel her breath, except she has none. She lowers her head until her eyes are level with theirs. The horizontal pupils are still. Not searching — already arrived.

The dying PC does not hear anything. The understanding comes whole, the way things come in fever or at the edge of sleep: not delivered, just *there*. An offer. Bounded, specific, not negotiable on its terms but real.

**The terms**: *You live. You close the pit with your hands. That is all.*

Not their soul — Mandinga said it himself, a soul is no use without pampas to spend it in. Not their loyalty, not their freedom. Just a placement: they must be present at the ritual closing and they must not obstruct it. What happens to them after is their own business. The deal is not a trap. It is a contract that happens to align with what the campaign needs. That is the disturbing part. Mandinga isn't offering salvation. He's hiring.

**The cost visible afterward**: The PC who accepts wakes with the wound closed — not healed cleanly, but *closed*, as if it was never the kind of wound that kills. Something about them is different in a way that is hard to name at first. Animals that would shy from a stranger don't shy from them anymore. The goat, from that point on, is never far from that PC specifically — not the injured one, but the one who dealt. She already knew who she was waiting for.

**What they carry**: They don't hear Mandinga's voice. They don't dream of him. But at certain moments — in the Salamanca especially, when the geometry is Mandinga's and the air smells of sulfur and old debts — they will feel the contract sitting in their chest the way a splinter sits under skin. Not painful. Present. A reminder that they owe a role in an ending and that something is keeping count.

**If they refuse**: The goat waits a moment, then steps back, and stands at the usual distance. The dying PC dies by whatever rule was already in motion. The goat does not mourn, does not react. She is already looking toward wherever the party is going next.

**If multiple PCs are dying simultaneously**: The deal is offered only once, to only one — whichever one the goat approached first. Mandinga doesn't run a clearinghouse. He needed one instrument. If he already has one, the others are on their own.

### Enforcement — if the PC breaks the deal

Mandinga is displaced and weakened. He cannot send agents or hunt the PC down. What he can do is structural: the life he gave was a loan, not a gift. The security on the loan is the action itself. If the action isn't performed, the loan is recalled.

**The wound returns.** Not all at once — gradually, over days. The PC can feel it. The closed flesh begins to remember what it was doing. No fever, no drama: just the slow recognition that the body is reversing. The timeline is roughly proportional to how far they are from fulfilling the deal. Running away from the closing accelerates it. Moving toward it slows it. This is not a curse. It is simply the debt becoming visible.

**The goat's quality changes.** She was patient before — ambient, creepy, waiting. Once the deal is being violated, the waiting becomes pressure. She is still always already there. But now her stillness has a different texture. The deal-splinter in the PC's chest tightens when she is near. It becomes harder to ignore that something is keeping count.

**Mandinga keeps them alive against their will.** This is the horrifying part, and the actual enforcement mechanism. He needs them at the closing — so he will not let them die of anything else before the debt is paid. Wounds that should kill close poorly but close. The body persists past the point where it should stop. If the PC tries to end things themselves, or walks into a situation designed to be fatal, they will find the body has opinions. It closes around injury. It refuses. He is not keeping them alive out of mercy. He is keeping an asset functional.

A PC who wants to die and cannot is a specific horror. Run it quietly and without explanation. The goat is there. The wound is returning. The body won't stop. That is all.

**If they default entirely — if the pit stays open**: Mandinga loses both his instrument and his territory. He cannot collect properly. The wound finishes its work and the PC dies — but not clean. Not free. The debt survives death. They come back wrong, the way the people who walked south at night came back wrong: sitting, eating, answering questions, not quite looking at anyone the way they used to. Wrong Returned, but Mandinga's version — compelled toward the pit they failed to close, unable to close it properly now, just drifting. The debt doesn't end. It just changes form.

This is not a guaranteed outcome — it is what happens if the deal is broken *and* the closing fails *and* the PC dies in the interim. It requires all three. But players should feel, by Arc 2 at the latest, that this is the shape of the cliff they are standing near.

**Do not explain the mechanics of the deal to the players at the table.** The dying PC's player understands an offer is being made — communicate that clearly. The terms arrive as understanding, not speech. What exactly is owed ("you must be at the closing") can be left slightly vague in the moment; the player will work it out as the campaign progresses and the obligation crystallizes. That slow crystallization is part of the cost.

**Tracking**: Two reads available on the trail east — one mundane, one wrong.

The other is moving heavier, the hoofprints pressed deeper into the soft clay at each strike. Not a heavier horse — a horse carrying more weight than usual. *(This is Kuyen's people returning to the encampment with Cata. Investigators who read this on the way out will arrive knowing someone was brought in. They will be looking for that person before Kuyen says a word.)*

### The eastern anchor — what investigators find

When investigators crest the bank and look down into the creek bed, the ritual is almost exactly as described in chapter-03. The fires. The ochre diagram. Tribe members at their positions. Nahuel at center.

And at the eastern gap: a girl.

She is kneeling on the line where the ochre arc ends, one wrist bound by rope to a ground stake on each side of the gap. The ropes are not tight enough to cut — they are tight enough to hold. She is young, late teens at most. She has been here long enough for the fear to exhaust itself; she is past crying, past calling out. She is looking at the approaching investigators the way a person looks at a door opening — not knowing if what comes through will be better or worse than what is already here.

The ochre line runs to each stake. Her body closes the gap geometrically. She is the anchor.

**Do not describe her as pitiable.** She is specific. She is a person. She has been waiting, alone, in a position she did not choose, while people moved around her doing things she does not understand, for however long it took the investigators to ride from San Ruiz. Let the players look at her and decide what she means to them.

---

**Kuyen does not explain this proactively.**

She goes through the diagnostic conversation (what did you touch, what's left, stone fragment, Rosa) while the girl is in the investigators' peripheral vision. She does not gesture toward the east. She does not acknowledge the question she knows is coming.

If investigators ask directly — *who is that, what is she doing there, why is she tied* — Kuyen answers without evasion and without softening:

> *"Necesitaba un ancla del este. Esto es lo que encontré."*
> *"I needed an eastern anchor. This is what I found."*

One sentence. She does not look away when she says it. She is not proud of this. She is not asking for absolution. She made a decision in the absence of better options, and she is doing what she decided.

If investigators push: *"She's unwilling."*

> *"Sí."*

If investigators say this is wrong:

> *"¿Encontraron otra manera antes de llegar?"*
> *"Did you find another way before you arrived?"*

Not defensive. Asking. She wanted another way and didn't have one until now. If the investigators provide one — a PC volunteers — she takes it immediately and without ceremony. The girl is unbound before anyone finishes the sentence.

**What Kuyen will not do**: apologize. Explain at length. Defend herself. She made a choice she knew was a choice, and she will not dress it as anything else.

**Who the girl is**

She has no connection to the geometry. She is not touched, not marked, not walking south at night. She is someone Kuyen's people found on the road two days ago — a farmer's daughter from a settlement west of here, displaced by the Zonda and the animal strangeness that preceded it, traveling with her father toward somewhere safer. Kuyen's people separated them. The father was told she would be returned in the morning.

She does not know what the ritual is. She knows she is in it against her will. She has had enough time to understand the fires, the symbol, the position she has been placed in, and to understand that something is expected to happen to her there. She does not know what.

Her name, if anyone asks her: **Cata.** Short for Catalina. She will tell you this in a voice that has been quiet for hours and is remembering how to be used.

> *"¿Qué me hicieron?"*

There is no answer that is adequate. There is no answer Kuyen will provide. If an investigator tries to explain: she listens, and then she says nothing, and then she asks someone to take her to her father.

**The Keeper should not resolve this.** Let it sit in the room.

---

**If Cata is freed and watches from the camp edge**

She sits beside the gravely injured PC. She does not speak. She watches the whole ritual — three rounds, the Hound, Nahuel going down and coming back, the moment the eastern line holds or doesn't. She watches a PC do what she was going to be made to do.

After, if the ritual succeeds:

She looks at the PC who held the east. She does not say thank you. She looks at them for a long moment with an expression that has several things in it at once — gratitude and something else, something more complicated, the look of someone who understands they are alive because someone else decided they were worth saving and does not know what to do with that knowledge.

She will make her way back to her father in the morning. The party will not see her again unless they go looking.

**Note for C8–C9**: If the party ever returns to the western settlements before the pit closing, they may encounter her father — a man who was told his daughter would be returned in the morning and never was. He is still looking. Whether Cata has gone back to him, or gone somewhere else, or is still trying to understand what happened to her — that is the Keeper's call. But the father exists. He is looking.

---

### Cata's escape attempts

She has been kneeling for hours and she is past fear and into something more useful: the specific, quiet determination of a person who has decided that if there is a moment she is going to take it.

**She attempts to free herself once per round, beginning with R1.** She is not waiting for the investigators to act. She has her own clock.

The attempts are not loud — she is not screaming or thrashing. She is twisting her wrists against the rope, using the weight of her body to try to work the stakes out of the clay, testing each binding systematically. Players who are watching the eastern position will see this. Players who are not watching will not.

---

**ROUND 1 — STR Hard (~25%)**

*Trigger*: The four tribe members die simultaneously. The people who were watching her are dead or have fractured under the SAN hit. The distraction window is real. She moves.

Her condition: hours of kneeling have restricted her circulation. Her wrists are partially numb. Her leverage from a kneeling position is poor — she cannot get her feet under her without risking the rope pulling tighter. She is working with what she has.

*On success*: One stake pulls free. She is half-unbound. She cannot run yet — the other wrist is still fixed — but she has a hand. See **partial escape** below.

*On failure*: The stake shifts but holds. She has moved it. Next round's roll improves.

**Kuyen's response**: If Cata visibly moves and no one stops it, Kuyen shouts two words at the surviving west-position tribe member: *"¡Aguántala!"* — *"Hold her!"* The tribe member moves to restrain Cata, reaching her end of R1. This costs the tribe member their position; the west arc weakens.

**Player options**:
- Help Cata (cut the rope, hold off the tribe member): she has a free hand, the west arc destabilizes, a PC must cover it or it fails
- Stop Cata (grapple, press the stake back in): opposed STR, she is fighting hard; this costs the PC their R1 action; she does not forgive it
- Do nothing: Kuyen's person handles it; Cata is restrained again; the attempt fails regardless of the roll

---

**ROUND 2 — STR Regular (~45%)**

*Trigger*: The Hound is testing the north arc. Kuyen has moved to cover it. The west tribe member is back at their position. Cata is now unattended.

Her condition: the R1 attempt loosened the stake — even if she failed the roll, the clay has been disturbed. She has better purchase now. She has been working at the rope with her free hand or with her teeth. She is making progress.

*On success*: Both stakes pull free or the rope gives. She is unbound. She is on her feet. **She runs.** See **escape** below.

*On failure*: The second stake is halfway out. She is close. She will try again.

**Kuyen's response**: Kuyen is covering the north breach. She cannot stop Cata herself. If Cata is about to break free and no one acts, the eastern position empties mid-working.

**Player options**:
- Help her escape: she's free, the eastern position opens, a PC must immediately fill it or the working fails at R3
- Stop her: opposed STR; she has both hands now and is fighting for her life; the PC is physically restraining a person in a ritual designed to harm that person; this is a real action with moral weight
- Do nothing: if she succeeds the roll, she goes; if she fails, R3 is still coming

---

**ROUND 3 — SAN 0/1D4 first, then STR Easy (~75%)**

*Trigger*: The Hound flows toward center, moving through the eastern space. As it passes through the gap — through Cata's position — the geometric distortion warps the clay immediately around the stakes. They shift outward, loosening, as if the ground itself is briefly unsure of their angles.

The Hound's proximity is also the SAN hit. She must hold herself together before she can act.

**SAN check first: 0/1D4.** On fail: she freezes. Not catatonic — she is looking at the thing in the gap with every muscle locked. She cannot move this round. The stakes, now loose, might as well be chains.

**On SAN success: STR Easy.** The stake is already shifting. She pulls. Both hands. Everything she has.

*On success*: She is free. She is at the bank and climbing before anyone can react. At R3, with the Hound at center, there is no one to stop her. She reaches the top of the bank. **She is gone.**

*On failure (even Easy)*: The stake goes back in. The clay re-settles around it. The distortion passes. Whatever was helping her is gone. She is still bound when the Hound reaches center.

**If she escapes at R3 with no PC in position**: The eastern gap opens at peak pressure. Kuyen cannot leave the symbol. The working collapses. See *failure outcome* below.

---

**Partial escape (one hand free)**

If Cata succeeds R1 but doesn't get clear — one stake out, one still fixed — she has a hand. She will use it:
- She can reach the second stake directly now, giving her +1 difficulty bonus on R2 (upgrade to Easy if her R1 succeeded)
- She can signal investigators — a specific, deliberate look, not a plea: an offer of information. *She will move when told.* She is not asking for rescue. She is coordinating.
- She can grab a passing investigator's arm as they move through the working. Not to slow them. To be seen.

---

**If she escapes — the angle**

She does not run blind. She runs east — away from the fires, toward the eastern bank, toward what she cannot see and does not know is there.

**When she crosses the ochre arc, she breaks it.**

Her body was the geometric closure of the eastern gap. While she knelt on the line, her physical presence held the arc's termination points in relation to each other. When she runs — leaning forward, moving at speed, one foot crossing the line and then the next — her body creates an acute angle between her trajectory and the ground at the exact point she breaks the ochre. A running figure leans at fifteen, twenty degrees from vertical. At the moment of crossing, that angle is sharp. Sharper than a wall corner. Sharp enough.

**The Hound does not enter through the empty gap. It enters through the angle her running body made with the earth as she crossed the line.**

The mist appears not at the gap but at her footprints — at the two dark marks in the ochre where she stepped through. The break is sharpest there. The angle was sharpest there. Ground level, instantaneous, egg-shaped and cold.

She has already taken four strides east. She does not see the mist behind her. She does not know what she did.

**She runs toward the eastern bank.** Straight east. The Hound's entry point is at the base of that bank. She is running toward where it came from.

---

**What investigators see:**

The mist at her footprints. Dense, localized, wrong temperature.

A Marked PC reads this geometrically without being told: the angle of her sprint across the line was the door. Her flight was the key. A non-Marked PC sees only the mist where she was and the empty dark east of the diagram where she is going.

**Kuyen sees it.** She does not stop working. She cannot. But her expression in the firelight changes for one beat — not surprise, not blame. Recognition. She knew this was possible. She did not have a better option.

---

**What happens to Cata**

The Hound was drawn to the ritual geometry. She is now outside the ritual geometry, running east in the dark. It does not pursue her — it does not pursue anyone. It was drawn to the diagram, not to her.

But the mist is at the base of the eastern bank. She ran through it. She was inside it for two strides while it was active.

**Whether she survives the bank is ambiguous. Leave it ambiguous.**

If investigators go looking for her after — in the hour after the ritual, in the following days — they may find her. Or signs of her. Or her father, still looking, still west-bound.

If she is found: she is alive. She does not have the Mark in its full form. But she has something — a quality of attention when she looks at corners, at the angles where walls meet floors, at the shadow-edges on flat ground. She notices shapes. She does not know why. She does not know what it means. She will for the rest of her life.

She does not know she opened the door. **Do not tell her. Let the investigators decide whether she ever knows.**

---

**What her escape costs now**

The eastern position is open. The Hound is inside the diagram. Whether the working survives depends entirely on whether a PC immediately takes the position — stepping into the space where the mist was, onto the broken ochre line, standing in what she left behind.

**The Keeper should not telegraph this.** Cata's R3 attempt happens simultaneously with the Hound flowing toward center. Both events in the same breath. Players have one beat to register what the mist at her footprints means before the geometry completes. If no one moves, the working collapses. If someone moves — onto the broken line, into the cold where the mist was — the geometry holds. The Hound is already inside the diagram. The anchor is standing in its entry point.

That is what the position costs at R3. Not just distortion. The PC takes the anchor standing in the Hound's door.

**CON check: Hard regardless of health.** Not because the ritual is harder. Because the Hound came through where they're standing.

### The working — modified for this table's damage state

The canonical C3 assumes the investigators might have the complete binding symbol from the chapel clay. **This table doesn't.** The eastern gap cannot be closed geometrically tonight. Kuyen knows this going in. Her plan: hold the working long enough to make progress without the full symbol — shorter exposure, controlled retreat.

**Nahuel** looks like a man who has been holding a door shut for three weeks without sleep. He looks at the investigators with the particular attention of someone who has learned to read strangers fast, and says nothing.

**Three-round structure — what's different**:

- No complete symbol → eastern gap cannot be ritually closed
- The only fix for the east: a human anchor. One PC plants themselves on the eastern line, holds it physically, takes CON check and SAN 0/1D6
- The gravely injured PC cannot do this — another wound is potentially fatal
- That leaves two eligible investigators

**Round 1 — Chaos**: Four tribe members at outer positions. Nahuel goes down. The Hound arrives at the eastern gap — larger than last time. The chapel collapse widened the gap.

> *No llega desde afuera. Llega desde el ángulo entre la tierra y el aire, exactamente donde termina la figura del este. Como si el mundo se hubiera doblado ahí un grado de más, y por ese grado extra cupiera algo.*

SAN 1/1D6 — first full manifestation.

**Round 2 — Pressure**: The Hound moves through the eastern gap toward the center. Kuyen is working. She shouts:

> *"¡El este! ¡Que alguien sostenga el este!"*

This is the decision point.

**Round 3 — The Anchor**:

If a PC plants themselves on the eastern line — no roll, pure decision:
- Entity's distortion field washes over them
- CON check: Hard if already hurt; Regular if healthy
- Failure: 1D6 HP from reality distortion
- Success or failure: line holds, Kuyen finishes the symbol, the Hound folds out of the space
- **Any investigator who takes damage from the Hound's distortion field and survives takes the Mark**

If no one anchors: Kuyen holds the east herself. She completes the working but takes the hit. Visibly damaged when it's over — not physically, but something in how she holds herself. She sits on the ground and doesn't speak for a long time. No Marks issued. Mercedes and El Pombero carry the perceptive load from C7 onward. Still works.

**The Mark — if issued, the dawn scene the next morning (run privately)**:

> *Te despertás antes del amanecer. Te pica donde lo tocó. Salís del campamento. Mirás el círculo del fogón. Es — algo. Está organizado. Las piedras no son un círculo exactamente. Están sobre tres radios de un arco que cerraría en un punto cuatro varas más allá. Esto no lo aprendiste. Lo ves. Es tan obvio como el color de la piedra.*

### After the ritual — what Kuyen says

After the silence, when she speaks:

> *"El ritual estaba casi completo. Ahora empezamos el cierre final desde acá. Un trabajo más, en el pozo. Si quieren ser útiles: métannos al pozo. El Valdez no nos va a dejar acercarnos."*

Then, looking at whichever investigator made the biggest call tonight:

> *"¿Saben lo que hay en el pozo? ¿Lo vieron?"*

She's not testing them. She wants to know what she's working with.
