# CHAPTER 5 — EL MANUSCRITO Y LOS QUE VOLVIERON

### _The Manuscript & The Returned_

**Goal**: Understand the full history of the scar. Meet the Wrong Returned. Locate the original wound in the land — and learn what it asks of those who would close it. **Exit requirement**: the players leave knowing the ruins are the _teacher_ and the **pit at La Esperanza** is the _work_ — they must **return west**. **Emotional arc**: Comprehension → Weight of history → The horror of being chosen.

> **Keeper note — running this stretch.** The Wrong Returned are the emotional center: the most human element in an increasingly inhuman situation. Their composure is their own, not the entity's. The ruins are not spooky — there are no ghosts, no traps; the horror is geological. And the manuscript pays off _here_ — the party has carried it since [Ch 4](chapter-04.md#the-locked-study); give them time with it.

## THE WRONG RETURNED

### Morning after (or first approach)

If the party **already spoke with Héctor and Marta at the fence** ([C4 night beat](chapter-04.md#the-wrong-returned--first-appearance)), open with them falling in alongside the horses as the party leaves — no re-introduction. Use the dialogue below for questions not yet asked, or for sustained daylight conversation on the road. If the party **has not yet approached them**, they spent the night standing at the fence without sleeping; in the morning they are still there:

> 
> _Se dan vuelta para mirarte antes de que los llames. Al mismo tiempo. No sobresaltados — sabían que venías. De cerca, son exactamente como los describió Rosa: comen, contestan preguntas, se quedan parados al sol sin molestia aparente. Pero hay algo en el foco de su atención que está mal de una manera que cuesta un momento nombrar. Te están mirando a vos, y también mirando algo que ocupa el mismo espacio que vos pero que no sos del todo — una geometría de tu posición en el espacio, más que tu cara._

**Appearance**:

- **Héctor Ríos**, 54. Former farmer from San Ruiz, wide-shouldered, graying. Was considered steady — not smart but reliable. Now answers questions with precise answers and no small talk; he has lost the social texture around his speech — the _"pos"_, the pauses. He just answers.
- **Marta Ríos** (his wife, no relation — the surname coincidence was a village joke), 43. Was the village teacher and midwife. Calm by profession; calmer now in a way that is not professional. The more present of the two — occasionally something that was Marta crosses her face and is gone.

**What they know**: not everything. They know what the entity showed them — geometrically accurate but not experientially interpretable. They speak in architecture and space, not sensation.

Héctor: _"No está bajo tierra. Eso estaba mal. Está en los ángulos. Vive en los lugares donde una superficie se encuentra con otra. El pozo es solo donde la forma es más grande — donde hay suficiente ángulo para que pase por ahí."_

Marta: _"Hay otros como nosotros. No muchos. Los que tocó pero no se llevó. Seguimos acá. Pero también estamos en parte allá."_

Héctor: _"Volvimos porque ya casi es la hora. Lo sentimos desde Tres Lagunas. El cierre, o la apertura. Una de las dos va a pasar. Tenemos que estar en la geometría cuando pase."_

**What they want**: to be at the pit for the final event — closing or opening, no preference. They are not aligned with the entity; they are anchored to the geometry. Their presence at the closing will matter ([Ch 9](chapter-09.md#chapter-9--el-cierre)).

**The question no one wants to ask** — what happens to them when the geometry is sealed? Marta, without distress: _"No sé. Si la forma se cierra, capaz nos cerramos con ella. Capaz volvemos. Decidimos que no importa. Tenemos que estar ahí."_

**SAN Check**: 0/1D3 (sustained conversation with someone who is and isn't a person).

**Possible Q&A**

_"Do you remember what happened to you?"_

> Héctor: _"I remember walking south. The night was very clear. And then it was like — like someone showed me a map of a place I'd been trying to describe without words. It was a relief, for a moment. And then I was back and it was morning."_ Marta: _"I remember it differently. There was no relief in mine. Only size. Something very large noticing something very small."_

_"Are you in pain?"_

> Héctor: _"No."_ _(Too quickly, too finally.)_ — _"No."_ _(Dicho demasiado rápido y demasiado terminante.)_ Marta: _(A long pause.)_ _"I don't feel pain the way I used to. I'm not sure if that's better."_ — _(Una pausa larga.)_ _"No siento dolor como antes. No estoy segura de que eso sea mejor."_

_"Can you be helped?"_

> Marta: _"You could close the geometry. Maybe that ends this. Or maybe this is what we are now and closing it just removes the sound."_ _(She touches her own face as if checking it's still there.)_ _"Close it anyway."_ _(Se toca la propia cara como comprobando que sigue ahí.)_ _"Ciérrenla igual."_

## EL POMBERO ON THE RIDGE

On departure, after Héctor and Marta have fallen in. The road east skirts a low rise — a _cuchilla_ the locals call _El Pelón_, "the bald spot," because no grass grows on its crown. Tomás doesn't look at it as you ride past; Don Eusebio's _peones_ won't speak its name. As the horses round its base, a small figure appears at the top — not climbing, simply _there_:

> 
> _Es bajo. Piel morena, cara ancha, un sombrero de paja demasiado grande para su cabeza. Levanta una mano y saluda — ni amistoso, ni amenazante, solo reconociendo que están pasando. Silba. El silbido tiene más notas de las que una persona debería conocer._

**[El Pombero](REF-NPCs.md#el-pombero)** is the guide-and-test. He does not _do_ anything yet — he whistles, waves, vanishes when looked away from. **Spot Hidden** while passing: the grass on _El Pelón_'s crown has the same ring pattern as the cattle field's rings — older, smaller, the same shape. _El Pelón_ is a _previous_ burial pit, ten generations old. **Do not open an entrance here.**

Tomás, if pressed, strangled: _"El Pombero. Le silba a los que están por entrar a algo. No me pregunten más."_

> **Roll for Hound** ([table](#roll-for-hound--c5)): if a Marked PC looks hard at the ring pattern, or the party camps in sight of the rise — both Marked PCs roll 2d6.

## THE ROAD TO THE RUINS

The Jesuit mission ruins are a day's ride east and slightly south; Saens's notes give landmarks (a forked river, a mesa that looks like a tooth) still accurate. The Wrong Returned walk alongside, requiring no water stops, keeping pace without effort.

> 
> _La tierra entre la estancia y las ruinas es pampa común — pasto chato y seco, algún matorral bajo de vez en cuando. Pero si viajás con Héctor y Marta, empezás a verla a través de su atención. Registran las esquinas. No de manera dramática — una mirada cuando el camino dobla, un instante de quietud donde una meseta proyecta una sombra en ángulo recto. Leen la geometría de la tierra como un hombre de campo lee el pasto buscando humedad._

**Optional encounter — the road**: during the night camp, a [Luz Mala](REF-NPCs.md#la-luz-mala) appears to the south — not orbiting, stationary, ~200 m distant. Watching. It followed from the estancia. Héctor, without fear: _"Es un sensor. No es una amenaza salvo que toques la geometría."_ It tracks them all the way to the ruins.

### The merchant with tarnished copper (5b)

Late in the day's ride — the tooth-shaped mesa visible, the ruins an hour ahead — the party meets **Don Crisanto Larrea** before the stones.

> 
> _Un carro chico está apartado del camino al abrigo de una cuchilla baja, el eje apoyado en una piedra. Un hombre canoso de unos cincuenta y pico está revisando su mercadería con la cara de quien ya perdió una discusión con el día. Levanta la vista cuando se acercan. No busca un arma. Busca el sombrero._

**Backstory (if asked)**: a traveling merchant on the Tandil–Buenos Aires road — _yerba_, copper _bombillas_, buttons, kettle-fittings, a few iron tools. His axle cracked two days east. He limped to the [ruins](#the-jesuit-mission-ruins), spent **last night** camped inside the broken walls, and was leaving this morning when the copper went wrong.

> 
> _Cada objeto de cobre se ennegreció durante la noche — no el verde del óxido natural, negro, parejo, como si lo hubieran tenido a la llama una hora. Las bombillas quedaron inservibles, los botones opacos, los herrajes arruinados. Las herramientas de hierro, intactas. La yerba, bien. **Solo el cobre, y solo el cobre que estaba del lado del carro que daba al piso de la capilla** — la piedra tallada junto a la cual durmió sin saber qué era. El cobre del otro lado está normal._

Larrea is bewildered — twenty years a merchant, never seen this: _"Dormí al lado de esa piedra en el piso. No la toqué. Esta mañana el cobre del lado de la piedra estaba así. Es como si algo hubiera mirado el cobre toda la noche. ¿Saben? Como si el cobre hubiera estado mirándolo a él también."_

**Héctor or Marta** (if present): a glance at the chapel, then the copper — _"The stone is still doing work. So is the copper now."_ No more unless pressed.

> **What this is**: the [dual diagram](assets/dual-diagram.svg) under the chapel floor _recognized_ the copper as metallically similar to [Quirce's](REF-NPCs.md#aldao-quirce) brass markers and tried to extend through it overnight. A **Hard Cthulhu Mythos** roll identifies the same geometric-extension effect as Quirce's markers — but here, without markers, pulled from _below_. Marked PCs may feel it unprompted.
> 
> **What investigators can do**: buy the ruined copper at scrap price (Larrea is grateful). Brought to the [bone chamber](#the-bone-chamber) later, it _re-tarnishes_ on contact with the walls — confirming the link. [Mercedes](REF-NPCs.md#do%C3%B1a-mercedes-sol%C3%ADs) will later recognize the signature.
> 
> **Larrea's onward role**: he limps east toward Buenos Aires once trade is done, arriving in the city before the players do, two months later. He tells the tarnished-copper story to the _vigilantes urbanos_ at the gate; it reaches [Saráchaga](REF-NPCs.md#don-eladio-sar%C3%A1chaga), who writes Mercedes that _"the pressure is now visible at the perimeter, not just at the centre."_ This is one of the threads that brings Mercedes to the camp in [C7](chapter-07.md#doña-mercedes-arrives-late). Larrea is the campaign's first non-Keeper, non-curandera, non-tribal civilian witness to the entity's reach. He never knows what he saw. He dies of natural causes in 1843.

**Placement**: run on the **approach** (end of road) or as the **first beat at the ruins** — whichever fits pacing.

## THE JESUIT MISSION RUINS

> 
> _La misión fue abandonada en 1767, cuando los jesuitas fueron expulsados. Lo que queda: las piedras de cimiento de cuatro edificios, un campanario roto que todavía se sostiene en parte, paredes que llegan a la altura del pecho y nada más. La piedra es caliza pálida, distinta de la roca de la zona — la trajeron hasta acá. Quien construyó esto eligió este lugar a propósito._
> 
> _La vegetación dentro de las ruinas es distinta — más densa, más verde a pesar de la estación. Acá crecen cosas que no deberían crecer tan al sur — una higuera, improbablemente, en el rincón de lo que fue la capilla. Como si la tierra acá fuera más **fértil**._
> 
> _Esa palabra. Otra vez esa palabra._

**Keeper notes**:

- The standing bell tower is the most intact structure. The bell itself fell and lies half-buried in the grass, inscribed **1749** — twelve years before the mission was built. It was brought from elsewhere.
- The chapel foundation has carved stones worked into the floor — flush with the others but different: the limestone has the geometry carved into it. These are the originals Saens found. The mission was built _on_ them.

### The carving

> 
> _Cuando encontrás la piedra tallada — y la vas a encontrar, porque Héctor camina derecho hacia ella — es a la vez más chica y más precisa de lo que esperabas. Unos sesenta centímetros de ancho, hundida en el piso de la capilla. El tallado es profundo y limpio: la misma geometría angular y circular, pero acá está completa. Tanto el diagrama de apertura como el contra-diagrama, al mismo tiempo — uno dentro del otro, interactuando. Como una cerradura y una llave talladas en la misma piedra._
> 
> _Es, sin lugar a dudas, la cosa más precisa que viste hecha a mano en tu vida._

> **Visual handout**: [dual-diagram.svg](assets/dual-diagram.svg). Print large (A4 min, A3 if possible). This is the carved stone; what Marta finger-traces at midnight; what they will see in bone in the [bone chamber](#the-bone-chamber); what Buenos Aires _becomes_ in [C12](chapter-12.md#chapter-12--el-libro). Show it — do not describe it.
> 
> **Roll for Hound**: Marked PC looks hard at the stone, or page 79 read **aloud** — both Marked PCs roll 2d6.

**Skill checks**:

|Skill|Result|
|---|---|
|Occult|Not two competing symbols — one symbol. The opening and closing are two aspects of the same geometry, like a door and a doorframe. The opening is not wrong. It is **incomplete**.|
|Cthulhu Mythos|The geometry predates human habitation of the region by a very long time. Who carved it — before Saens, before the Tehuelche — has no answer in any tradition this character knows.|
|History|The limestone does not exist within 200 km. It was brought from the north. The stone was carved _elsewhere_ and installed here.|
|Art/Craft (Drawing)|The precision is geometric in the mathematical sense — the angles are _exact_. The tools of any human period should not produce this quality of line.|

**Héctor**, hands on the stone (the only one who touches it without hesitation): _"Acá es donde vive la forma. El pozo es solo donde alguien le abrió un agujero."_

**Marta**, from across the room: _"Padre Saens tenía razón en una cosa. No es el diablo. Pero se equivocó en otra. Dijo que era una herida. No lo es. Es una puerta. La herida es lo que le hicimos nosotros."_

### Reader-path seed (Use ending, C12)

The PC who reads **page 79 aloud at the table**, in the GM's hearing, has — without anyone explaining it, including the GM — taken the first step into the **Reader role** available in C12 (Use ending). The PC does not know this; the Marked PCs do not see it; **[Mercedes](REF-NPCs.md#do%C3%B1a-mercedes-sol%C3%ADs) recognizes it later** ([C8](chapter-08.md#chapter-8--la-traición)). This is the campaign's quietest seed and pays off in C12.

> **Disambiguation**: if multiple PCs read page 79 aloud, the seed lands on the one who reads _slowest_, with the most weight, in the most silence — willingness to bear, not skill. **If no PC reads it aloud** (here or over the skull below), the Reader path is **closed** in C12: only Hide and Destroyed are available. ## DESCENT AT THE CARVED STONE

After the deep-read (and the night, if run). The players understand the dual diagram; Héctor or Marta shows them what it does.

> 
> _Marta camina hasta el piso de la capilla al mediodía — el único momento en que el anillo exterior del diagrama doble no atrapa ninguna sombra. Se arrodilla junto a la piedra. Apoya las manos en dos puntos específicos — los dos donde los arcos de apertura y de cierre se cruzan. Aprieta. La piedra no se mueve. La tierra alrededor de la piedra se mueve._
> 
> _Un cuadrado de tierra y pasto, de dos metros de lado, se hunde unos quince centímetros. Después gira — el cuadrado entero, alrededor de la piedra, noventa grados — y debajo aparece un pozo vertical. Revestido en piedra. Más viejo que la capilla. Cae hacia la oscuridad. El aire que sube huele al mismo filo mineral que la fosa, y a otra cosa que no se huele desde hace siglos: incienso frío, hecho de una resina que no crece en este clima._

**Héctor, if asked**: _"Esto es lo que llaman La Salamanca. No es una sola — hay varias. Esta es una de las antiguas. Una de las que se hicieron antes de que los Jesuitas hicieran su capilla encima."_

**Observable (before descent)**: a half-full canteen placed on the carved circle shows its water forming _standing waves_ in the dual-diagram pattern — the fluid reading the geometry the way iron filings read a magnet. The waves persist as long as the canteen sits there. No roll. Show the SVG beside it.

This is the **entrance to La Salamanca**. The shaft drops to the [bone chamber](#the-bone-chamber).

> **Roll for Hound**: when the square **rotates** and the shaft opens — both Marked PCs roll 2d6 immediately.

## EL POMBERO RETURNS

As the players prepare to descend, El Pombero is sitting on a ruin wall, whittling a stick — not seen since [El Pelón](#el-pombero-on-the-ridge). Without looking up:

> _"Abajo se baja con preguntas, no con armas. Si llevan armas, igual bajen. Pero no van a servir."_

If asked what is below: _"Lo viejo. Lo que estuvo antes de los curas, antes de los charrúas, antes de los tehuelches. La gente que vino primero. No queda nadie de ellos. Pero la geometría queda."_

Three warnings — all useful, none specific:

1. **[The bone chamber](#the-bone-chamber)** holds the dead of the previous closing attempt. Do not disturb the bone alignment; they are still doing work.
2. **[The whispering tunnels](#the-whispering-tunnels)** beyond it are not haunted, but they _amplify_ — every whisper is heard by all, in their own voices. Speak carefully.
3. **[The Great Wizard's chamber](#the-chamber-of-the-great-wizard)** at the deepest point has been closed for decades. Do not seek it this chapter. _"No están listos. Yo tampoco."_

Then, looking up for the first time: _"Abajo aprenden. Arriba deciden. Después **vuelven al oeste** — al agujero que ya abrieron. No se queden acá admirando la piedra."_

He vanishes between two glances. Marta: _"He has been here longer than the carving. Don't ask how."_

## THE BONE CHAMBER

The shaft drops twelve meters to a roughly six-meter hexagonal chamber, walls of the same imported limestone as the Saens stone above, ceiling low enough that players bend slightly. The floor is **bones, in the dual diagram's pattern**.

> 
> _Los huesos están dispuestos formando el diagrama doble completo, a escala del piso. Hay quizá doscientos — huesos largos, cráneos, vértebras, entremezclados deliberadamente a mano. Los cráneos están en las posiciones del anillo exterior. Miran hacia adentro. No son humanos — demasiado largos, demasiado angostos en la mandíbula, las cuencas demasiado separadas. No son de ninguna especie que los jugadores puedan nombrar._
> 
> _En el centro mismo del diagrama, donde alguien debe arrodillarse como ancla en el cierre, hay un único cráneo humano. Viejo. Humano moderno. La frente sin marcas. **Es el Padre Ignacio Saens.** Volvió acá, solo, el año antes de morir, y se acostó sobre el diagrama en lugar de la gente que no tenía — el que abre, el que sostiene el conocimiento y la contra-figura, todo a la vez — y compró el tiempo que pudo. Su cuerpo fue tomado por la geometría como sustituto. El cráneo es la única parte que quedó porque la geometría necesita un centro, y el hueso de un hombre aguanta donde el pergamino no._

**SAN Check**: 1/1D6 — for what Saens did, alone, ahead of the players, knowing it would not be enough.

> **Observable**: lanterns brighten ~30% at the chamber center (over Saens's skull) and dim ~30% at the outer ring. No draft. Lit cigars draw smoke _inward_ toward the central skull rather than rising — the geometry is breathing.
> 
> **Geometry points west** _(run this — do not wait to be asked)_: any compass brought underground **drifts west** — the bearing of [Estancia La Esperanza](REF-NPCs.md#don-eusebio-valdez) and the burial pit. Not broken; _pulled_. Marked PCs feel it as a tug in the chest. **This is the campaign telling them where the closing happens.** The teacher is here; the wound is west.
> 
> **Larrea's copper**: if bought, it _re-tarnishes_ on contact with the chamber walls — confirming the link to the pit geometry.

Run **proactively** if players are quiet or treating this as a museum; also if anyone asks what the bones mean, where the closing happens, or who stands where. Héctor and Marta do not lecture unprompted, but they will answer, and they read the floor the way a midwife reads a room.

### The Bone Vigil (_De Vigilia Ossium_) — hidden rite

**Discovery**: a limestone niche the size of two fists in the **south wall**, easy to miss. **Spot Hidden (Regular)** or **Search (Hard)** after the diagram lesson, or when anyone searches the walls.

> _Inside: a roll of waxed cloth, stiff with age. Unrolled: three sheets in Saens's hand — a draft, not part of the bound manuscript. The ink is greener than the study folios. The title: **De Vigilia Ossium**. Figura I shows the closing ring walked clockwise; Figura II shows a kneeler beside a skull, not on it. A Mapudungun phrase is boxed: **Antu kishu, lan amukey**. At the bottom, in a shaking hand: Scripsi hoc postquam solus iacui. Alius vigilabit. — "I wrote this after I lay down alone. Another will keep vigil."_

**Handout**: [`bone-vigil-rite.svg`](assets/bone-vigil-rite.svg).

**What it is**: a **closing-aspect** rite — counterpart to the opening-aspect [Graveyard Kiss](01-la-tierra-mala/diagrams.md#the-graveyard-kiss-de-osculo-sepulcrali--eusebios-locked-study-page) in Eusebio's study. Saens drafted it after his first solo attempt failed, before he lay down to die. It closes nothing. It lets the geometry **answer one honest question** about the scar at La Esperanza.

**Héctor**, if read aloud: _"El cura lo escribió para quien viniera después. No para él."_ He will not stop them.

**Requirements**: a complete dual diagram underfoot (the bone floor qualifies; a traced pit copy does not unless drawn at Hard Occult) · a **kneeler** beside Saens's skull (never on it, never moving it — Héctor intervenes if they try) · a **walker** with a long bone from the floor, touching each outer-ring skull once, **clockwise**, starting at the eastern station · kneeler speaks **Antu kishu, lan amukey** (Antu-Rayo's fragment — the line the geometry supplies to everyone in the ring at Phase 5, the final seal in C9) and holds **thirteen breaths** without moving · then **one honest question** about the La Esperanza scar, spoken aloud.

|Step|Detail|
|---|---|
|**Roll**|**CON (Hard)** for the kneeler; the walker needs no roll unless they disturb a skull|
|**Cost**|Kneeler: **1D6 SAN, 1D4 HP** (geometry presses inward) · Walker: **0/1 SAN**|
|**On success**|The chamber answers **once**, truthfully — not in speech: pressure, image, compass-pull, or a sentence in the kneeler's own voice from the wrong direction. Give a **true** short answer (east fails first, Eusebio must stand in the outer ring, Quirce's asymmetry, what the pit wants)|
|**On failure**|Kneeler cannot rest that night; **both Marked PCs roll 2d6** for Hound|

> **Keeper — walker and disturbance**: the walker's clockwise circuit is part of the rite. Touching each outer-ring skull once, in sequence, with the long bone is _intended contact_ — it does not count as a disturbance and triggers no bone-disturbance tier. Only removing a skull, knocking it out of position, or breaking the clockwise sequence constitutes a disturbance.

**Benefits**: if a [Tier-1](#if-they-disturb-the-bones) disturbance was triggered, then restored, then the vigil completed in the same scene → **no eastern penalty** from that disturbance. A kneeler who succeeded may treat **one** eastern-segment CON check at the pit ([C8](chapter-08.md#chapter-8--la-traición)) as **Regular** instead of Hard (once per campaign). **Cost**: like the Graveyard Kiss, each performance teaches **El Patrón** the performer's hand. Low-level, not free.

> _Do not push this rite. Let them find the niche. Make the answer worth the SAN — and make clear it is **one question**, not a hotline._

## THE WHISPERING TUNNELS

The passage beyond the bone chamber runs deeper — ~2 m tall, smooth-walled, ceiling carved with the dual diagram's outer ring repeated at one-meter intervals. It is _acoustically tuned_: every word spoken in the tunnels is repeated at the mouth of each chamber ahead, in the speaker's voice, at the original volume.

Practical effect: any roll involving speaking in the tunnels is **one step harder** — the speaker hears their own words returned in real time. **A Listen roll** identifies the tuning; silence works as a navigational tool.

Beyond the bone chamber the way **does not fork** — it is a single descending spine. Each room opens off the last; the only forward passage from each lies past that room's feature. They pass through everything, in order, or they do not go on.

## THE GUARDIAN — EL INVUNCHE

*Low-level encounter. The toll is the encounter, the claws are the punishment for skipping it. Stat block: [npc-stats.md — El Invunche](assets/npc-stats.md#el-invunche--guardián-de-la-salamanca).*

> *Algo está plegado en la oscuridad del fondo, donde sigue el pasaje. Se despliega — mal. La cabeza doblada del todo hacia atrás, mirándote por su propia columna. Una sola pierna; el otro miembro cosido o crecido al costado. Hace un sonido que no es palabra, porque la boca de donde sale no tiene lengua. No se abalanza. Se planta en el único camino adelante y espera, con paciencia de animal, lo que le vas a dar.*
>

**SAN 0/1D4** to see it. **The gut-punch (separate, +0/1D4):** the moment a PC registers it was **made, not born** — milk-teeth, a child's garment-scrap. Let it land in silence; let the players do the math. A **Psychology** read: it does not guard out of malice. It was *placed.* Being fed is the only kindness it has known.

**The toll — what it accepts, and what it costs:**

| Offering | Works? | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Bone needle + cup** *(offering chamber)* | ✅ Intended | A pricked finger: **1 HP** + a beat of dignity. No curse, no fight. |
| **Fresh meat / charqui / bled finger** | ✅ Always | A ration, or **1 HP** if PC blood. |
| **Milk** *(folkloric Invunche food)* | ✅ Best | Placates *and calms* it — no curse risk on a fumbled exit. (Goat is at La Esperanza; milk must be carried in.) |

| **A true name spoken aloud** | ✅ | In a whisper tunnel a name **carries** — risk it reaches the seal or wakes the warren. Marked PC's own name: Keeper's-option unease, log it. |
| **Empty hand / refusal / force** | ❌ | **The curse** (below) + the grapple→bite cycle. |

> **The curse (handout wording):** Refuse the toll or force past, and the Invunche lays its *maldición*. **Opposed POW 65 vs the target's POW×5.** On its success: **penalty die to ALL rolls until they leave La Salamanca**, and **−1D10 Luck**. It can cast this on a screaming, fighting party as its action — this, not the 1D6 claw, is how a careless fight actually hurts.

**Three ways through (all viable):**
1. **Pay the toll** *(intended)* — tribute from the offering chamber. No fight. The antiquarian flagged it.
2. **Fight it** — slow, no Dodge, weak-ghoul HP; the curse + grip make a loud brawl costly. **Killing it ends the toll permanently — and the Wizard will know his guardian is dead** (if still underground in C5, he is already at the slab when they reach his chamber; if they meet him first in C7, he will know the guardian is gone before they say a word).

## THE CHAMBER OF THE GREAT WIZARD

_(optional — run only if investigators force the sealed door after [Pombero's warning](#el-pombero-returns))_

> **Warn at the table**: this is the deepest human layer of La Salamanca; what they learn here cannot be unlearned; [C6](chapter-06.md#quirce-arrives) will feel different if they have already met the Wizard or read his desk.

**At this table the party looted the chamber entirely** — the pampa **maps** (La Esperanza, San Ruiz, and the Coypac mark), the ochre-edged **robes**, and his **geometry notes**: forty years of the Great Wizard's work, taken. **He retaliates with a [malón for the maps](#the-malón-for-the-maps)** — Ranquel raiders and two Coypac ochre priests who shadow the party home from the ruins and strike when [the closing begins](chapter-08.md#the-malón-strikes). Whether they faced him or stripped an empty room, he now knows them by their hand.

### Keeper brief — who he is

_Full NPC: [REF-NPCs](REF-NPCs.md#the-great-wizard-of-salamanca) · stats: [npc-stats.md](assets/npc-stats.md)._ Mortal head of the **Cult of the Great Sleeper** (the players don't know that name yet). A scholar-priest in late old age who built a school in La Salamanca's deepest chamber and believes he is recovering pre-colonial knowledge that will _re-found_ the _campaña_ outside the Spanish-Catholic frame. He is sincere, learned, and **not insane** — the campaign's proof that intelligence is not protection. Ash-blond hair (same cast as the Coypac priests); robes of undyed wool with **ochre** edging (his signature); fingertips black with ink and ash; moves slowly (DEX 35). He wants a successful _observation_ at the estancia closing — not to stop it, but to ride its geometry while the Coypac awakening lights up in parallel. He does **not** know he serves [El Patrón](REF-NPCs.md#el-patr%C3%B3n--don-eligio-dad%C3%A1lah), who has steered him for forty years; every "discovery" was placed for him.

**Relationships**: correspondent and unwitting courier _via_ Quirce (the Wizard rides his ink, El Patrón rides his voice — they do not coordinate); unknown rival of Mercedes (whose youthful 1781 copy is the ultimate source of his fragments — she confesses this in [C11](chapter-11.md#chapter-11--la-decisión)); known to El Pombero, who serves an older tradition El Patrón exploited.

### The door

The antechamber ends in a **slab of imported limestone**, flush with the wall, no handle. The dual diagram is carved into it, but the opening and closing arcs are **reversed** from the surface stone: a _seal_, not an entrance marker.

|Approach|Result|
|---|---|
|Brute force|Fails. The slab is not heavy — it is _anchored_. The geometry holds it.|
|Occult / Cthulhu Mythos|Recognizes a deliberate counter-seal; someone with full knowledge could open it from outside at the two intersection points, as Marta opened the shaft.|
|Marta or Héctor|Either can open it without a roll by pressing the two intersection points — then hesitating. Marta: _"This door was closed for a reason. The reason is still in there."_ If the party insists, they comply.|
|Wrong gesture (opening ritual traced on the seal)|The slab sinks and rotates like the surface entrance — but only 30 cm. A Hard POW roll or 1 MP: the opener feels the seal _recognize_ them as not the owner. Opens fully on a second attempt or with Marta/Héctor's help.|
|Marked PC|Feels the seal as a second skin over something that has been breathing on the other side for decades. **SAN 0/1** if they open it anyway.|

When it opens, the whispering **stops** in this tunnel. Silence is worse.

### The chamber

> 
> _La sala es más grande que la cámara de huesos — octogonal, el techo alto para pararse derecho. No es una tumba. Es un estudio. Estanterías talladas en la caliza sostienen códices, fascículos, mapas de la pampa con marcas en Estancia La Esperanza, San Ruiz, y un tercer sitio al este en la cordillera rotulado con una letra que no reconocés: **Coypac**. Un escritorio de tierra apisonada revestido en piedra. Lámparas sin combustible usadas hace poco — las mechas tibias. Sobre el escritorio: correspondencia en dos manos. Una es la cifra de Aldao Quirce. La otra es más vieja, más prolija._
> 
> _El aire huele a incienso frío y hierro. El piso no es de huesos. Es **ocre** — un diagrama doble completo en pigmento mineral, más fresco que todo lo de arriba. En el arco este, una línea está trazada **mal** a propósito: una corrección que solo haría alguien que conoce el dibujo desde adentro._

**Skill checks on the desk** (same content as the [C6 notebook scene](chapter-06.md#quirces-move) — if found here, don't repeat verbatim at the estancia):

## THE WARNING, WITHOUT KUYEN

> **SAN Check** (Marked PC feeling the pull): 0/1.

### The Malón for the Maps

This is the Wizard's retaliation for stripping [his chamber](#the-chamber-of-the-great-wizard). He is old and slow (DEX 35) and rides after no one — he sends word up the cult chain. What comes is a **malón**: a mounted raiding party of **pampa natives — Ranquel, not Tehuelche** — sent to take the maps and geometry notes back. Two **ochre priests of the Coypac cult** ride with them, directing; the warriors are the muscle (paid in trade goods and protection), the priests the purpose. **They do not strike on the road — they stalk, count, and wait for the closing.** ([The strike lands at the ritual, C8](chapter-08.md#the-malón-strikes).)

> **Keeper — name the distinction out loud.** These are **not** Antu-Rayo's people. The Tehuelche counter-tradition (Kuyen, the dead tribe) tried to *close* the wound; this malón serves the ochre men who want it *open*. Héctor or Marta says so if the table starts to conflate them: _"No son de los nuestros. Esos sirven a los de ocre."_ Do not let the campaign's grief about the tribe smear onto these raiders.

**The stalk — the ride back from the ruins.** The malón picks the party up as they leave La Salamanca and shadows them all the way west to the pit, never closing. They are reading the camp: numbers, watches, where the saddlebags with the maps ride.

**Spotting them — the stolen glass is the tell.** One warrior carries a PC's **catalejo (field-glass / "binoculars")**, lifted in an earlier run-in (table canon now — place the original theft wherever it fits your log). It is also what gives the tail away:

| When | Spot Hidden to catch the tail | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Morning** | **Hard** | low sun behind them, dust settled, careful riders |
| **Afternoon** | **Regular** | the westering sun **glares off the stolen glass** as the warrior watches — a flat wink of light on the horizon that does not belong |

> _A media tarde, allá atrás, un destello — un punto de luz que aparece y se apaga al ritmo de alguien que mira y baja el catalejo. Es el suyo. Los están contando._

- **The Marked feel nothing** — mundane men do not tilt the geometry, and that flatness is its own tell.
- **If the players catch the stalkers** (spot them, then hunt them, ambush, or parley) they can end it early. A priest, given a parley or a captured warrior: _"Devuelvan lo que le sacaron al maestro y se acabó. No vinimos por ustedes. Vinimos por lo suyo."_ **Return the maps → the malón melts away; break them on the road → no strike at the closing.** Either pre-empts C8 — and the party keeps real intel (Coypac's location; the Wizard's *corrected* eastern line, useful for reading the [eastern asymmetry](chapter-08.md#the-eastern-incursion)).
- **If the players don't** — the malón follows them home and **waits for the first chant.** Run [the strike at the ritual](chapter-08.md#the-malón-strikes).

**Stats (light).** Malón warriors — mounted skirmishers, **Ride 70, Throw (bolas) 60**, STR/DEX-forward, lance + facón; adapt the closest block in [npc-stats.md](assets/npc-stats.md). Ochre priests — Coypac cult, **far weaker than the Wizard**: POW ~80, Occult 50%, **Veil / Dread / Mental Suggestion only**; they will **not** spend themselves — and, crucially, *they know what the working does to anyone who crosses it.* **SAN 0/1** the first time a PC registers the ochre cult's reach runs *this* far from Coypac.
