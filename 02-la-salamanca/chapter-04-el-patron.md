## OPENING SITUATION

The investigators return to Estancia La Esperanza knowing what they didn't know when they left it. Don Eusebio hired them to do his dirty work. He watched from a ridge while four people died. He has the manuscript that started all of this locked in his study.

The estancia has changed in one visible way: the gates are closed and bolted. Two ranch hands stand watch at the entrance. Don Eusebio has not been seen outside the main house in three days — not since the investigators saw his palomino on the ridge.

**What Don Eusebio knows about the investigators now**: They helped the tribe. They witnessed the partial binding. They are no longer useful to him — they are a threat to the only cover story he has. He intends to deny everything and, if pressed, to discredit them (outsiders, drifters, possibly in league with the savages).

**What he doesn't know**: That they have the complete binding symbol. That they've read Dolores's message. That Rosa told them everything. He is still operating on the assumption that he controls the information.

## THE RETURN — FROM THE CREEK BED  *(THIS TABLE — opening scene)*

The chapter does not open at the estancia. It opens in the grey hour after the Hound, with the investigators scattered across the pampa, each surfacing from their own version of the night.

Two of them fled under the mind's own anesthetic — running without deciding to, or going somewhere behind the eyes while the body kept walking. One left because leaving was simply correct. However they went, they went *separately*. They come back to themselves at first light: cold, miles apart, the dew on them, the dust of the pit still ground into the seams of their clothes.

> *Volvés en vos despacio. No hay un momento en que se prenda la luz — más bien te das cuenta, de a poco, de que hace rato que venís caminando, y que los pies te llevaron para el lado del cauce sin preguntarte nada. El sol todavía no salió. La pampa está del color de la ceniza mojada. En algún lugar, atrás tuyo o adelante, los otros. No sabés a qué distancia hasta que los oís.*

**Regrouping**: Let the players find each other — no roll. The plain is flat, the morning is silent, and they have all been walking the same direction without knowing it: back toward the creek bed. A Marked PC knows why (the geometry there is now the loudest thing on the plain); the others just feel they should go.

**The creek bed, by daylight**: It is as they left it and worse for the light. The four cardinal fires are cold ash. The dead lie where they fell — Nahuel at the center, Kuyen on the eastern line where she went to hold it herself. No blood, no decay yet; just bodies arranged in ways that bodies are not. The ochre diagram is half-erased. **The goat is already there**, standing on the western bank, facing the ridge. She did not follow them in. She was waiting.

**SAN Check**: 0/1D4 — returning, sober, to what they fled.

**Eusebio on the ridge**: As the light comes up, a **Spot Hidden** (automatic for a Marked PC — they feel the watched-ness before they see it) finds a figure on the western ridge: a man on a **palomino**, motionless, a brass spyglass to his eye. Don Eusebio. He is looking at the creek bed. At them. For a long moment neither party moves.

Then he lowers the glass, turns the horse, and rides **west — toward the estancia**. He is not fleeing; he is unhurried, the ride of a man who has seen what he came to see. His trail is fresh and obvious in the dew.

That trail is the path home. West off the ridge, the only water and shelter on the line before the estancia is **San Ruiz**. The investigators follow the man who used them, and the road carries them back through the village they broke.

## THE CONFRONTATION WITH DON EUSEBIO

### Getting Past the Gate

The gate hands are loyal by debt and habit, not conviction. They don't want trouble. If investigators present any of the following, the hands will hesitate long enough to send word inside:
- A credible official capacity (police credentials, land authority documentation)
- Money (not much — they earn little)
- The simple statement that they have information Don Eusebio needs to hear before things get worse

Don Eusebio will agree to see them after a delay of 20–30 minutes. He uses the time to compose himself and decide on his approach: controlled denial.

### The Meeting

He receives them in the main sala, not the study. The door to the study is visible, locked. He is composed but not comfortable — a man who has rehearsed this.

His opening position: apologize for the confusion, claim the tribal threat resolved itself, offer partial payment for their work, suggest the matter is concluded. If investigators accept this framing, he will show them out and bolt the gate behind them.

**If investigators challenge him**:

He will hold his position through two rounds of pressure. He is a good liar and a proud man — he has lied to officials, to the Church, to his own workers. But he is not made of iron.

The cracks appear at:
- The mention of Dolores's name (his jaw tightens — he knew her)
- The mention of Rosa (he looks at the study door involuntarily)
- The mention of four dead tribe members (the finger taps twice — a tell that's become mechanical)
- Showing him the complete binding symbol (he goes absolutely still)

**Breaking point**: A **Psychology (Hard)** success, or the binding symbol placed on the table in front of him, or the statement *"The manuscript describes the same ritual. The Jesuit priest who wrote it died trying to close what he opened."* 

When he breaks, he does not break dramatically. He sits down — not collapses, just sits, as if his legs received a quiet instruction. He looks at his hands. He says:

> *"I heard it. Three weeks in. I was in the south pasture at midnight — the diagram had to be maintained, you understand, the lines redrawn after every rain — and I heard it. From underneath. Not an animal sound. Something… organized. Deliberate. I told myself it was cattle. I kept going for two more months before I admitted what it was."*
>
> **[ES]** *"Lo escuché. A las tres semanas. Estaba en el potrero sur a la medianoche — el diagrama había que mantenerlo, entendés, las líneas se redibujaban después de cada lluvia — y lo escuché. Desde abajo. No era ruido de animal. Algo… ordenado. A propósito. Me dije que eran las vacas. Seguí dos meses más antes de admitir lo que era."*

He will now answer questions. He will not lie about things he has already confirmed, but he will omit — strategically — the information about Aldao Quirce until directly asked.

## THE LOCKED STUDY

### Setting Description

> *The study is not large. A desk, a chair, bookshelves along two walls. A cot against the far wall — he has been sleeping here. The blanket nailed over the window makes the room feel sealed. The air is different: drier than the rest of the house, with that mineral edge from the pit. On the desk: the manuscript. Around it, on every available surface — loose paper, the backs of letters, a torn section of flour sack — diagrams. Dozens of them. The same angular-circular geometry drawn over and over, each with small variations. Some have sections crossed out. Some have red ink additions. One has been torn in half and taped back together.*
>
> *He has been trying to find a variant that would close it. He has been trying for months. None of them worked.*
>
> **[ES]** *El estudio no es grande. Un escritorio, una silla, estanterías a lo largo de dos paredes. Un catre contra la pared del fondo — ha estado durmiendo acá. La manta clavada sobre la ventana hace que el cuarto se sienta sellado. El aire es distinto: más seco que el resto de la casa, con ese filo mineral del pozo. Sobre el escritorio: el manuscrito. ### The Manuscript

**Physical description**: A handwritten journal, roughly 80 pages, bound in brown leather softened to almost fabric by age. The writing is in a formal clerical Spanish of the mid-18th century — legible but archaic. The ink is brown-black. Some pages have diagrams. The final pages are water-damaged, partially illegible.

**Author**: Padre Ignacio Saens, S.J. (Society of Jesus). Dates in the text range from 1761 to 1763. The manuscript was never published, never catalogued. It was hidden in a wall.

**What it contains (key passages)**:

*Page 1: The Discovery*
> *"In the region south of the river, following the old Tehuelche trails, I found carved into the bedrock a figure of such precision that I cannot attribute it to indigenous hand. The angles are exact in a way that requires instruments I do not possess to verify, yet the verification seems unnecessary — the eye accepts the geometry as true before the mind can question it. I have seen similar figures in texts attributed to the pre-Columbian period, but never the figure itself. I made a copy."*
>
> **[ES]** *"En la región al sur del río, siguiendo los antiguos senderos tehuelches, hallé tallada en la roca madre una figura de tal precisión que no puedo atribuirla a mano indígena. Los ángulos son exactos de una manera que requeriría, para verificarse, instrumentos que no poseo; y sin embargo la verificación parece innecesaria — el ojo acepta la geometría como verdadera antes de que el entendimiento pueda cuestionarla. He visto figuras semejantes en textos atribuidos al período precolombino, mas nunca la figura misma. Hice una copia."*

*Page 23: The First Opening*
> *"I used the figure in a ceremony of blessing for the mission's fields. The result was immediate and wrong. The crops grew, yes — but at night the earth spoke in a voice that had no source and no direction. Sister Beatriz reported dreams. I reported nothing to the Bishop."*
>
> **[ES]** *"Empleé la figura en una ceremonia de bendición para los campos de la misión. El resultado fue inmediato y aciago. Los cultivos crecieron, sí — mas de noche la tierra hablaba con una voz que no tenía origen ni dirección. Sor Beatriz refirió sueños. Yo no referí nada al Obispo."*

*Page 47: The Realization*
> *"The figure is not a symbol. It is a wound. Something passed through this location before our memory of it and left this shape as a scar leaves its mark on skin. ## NPC: SILVIO MÉNDEZ

**Role**: The ranch hand who came back wrong. Present since Chapter 1 — investigators may have glimpsed him in the background. He is now visible in a way he wasn't before, because he cannot be ignored.

**Background**: Silvio, 38, has worked at La Esperanza for four years. Seven months ago, during Don Eusebio's third ritual "maintenance" — when the lines in the south pasture had to be redrawn — Don Eusebio sent Silvio to check the outer markers at night. Silvio came back before dawn, resumed work at sunrise, and has not stopped working since.

**Appearance**: 38, but looking 50. Lean in a way that suggests he has forgotten to eat rather than having not eaten. His skin is dry and cracked despite the summer. His eyes are open a fraction too wide — the eyelids don't close fully even when he blinks. He smells faintly of the same mineral note as the pit. His shadow, in certain light, falls in the wrong direction.

**What He Does**: He works. He repairs fences, mucks corrals, carries water, mends leather. Constantly, without breaks, without apparent fatigue. He doesn't eat with the others. He doesn't sleep in the bunkhouse — he has been found, several times, standing in the dark outside the south pasture fence, facing inward. When spoken to, he responds to questions. He is not mute. But his responses are present-tense and slightly displaced — he answers what you asked three sentences ago, as if he is running slightly behind the conversation.

**What He Is**: Silvio was not taken. He was partially incorporated. The entity's geometry passed through him when he was in the outer ring zone alone at night, and something in the spatial structure of his person became partially aligned with the diagram. He is still Silvio. But there is also something in him that is listening to the geometry of the land, constantly, the way a compass needle listens to magnetic north.

**SAN Check**: 0/1 — the first time investigators closely observe Silvio's eyes or shadow.

**What Silvio Knows (if asked directly and patiently)**:
- He can feel where the geometry is. He knows its shape the way he knows where the fence lines run — as spatial fact.
- He knows that it is pulling harder now. *"The shape is getting louder,"* he says, if asked how he feels. *"It wasn't this loud before."* — **[ES]** *"La forma se está haciendo más fuerte. Antes no era así de fuerte."*
- He knows, in a way he cannot articulate, that there are other people like him. *"There are two others who can hear it. South. They're walking this way."* — **[ES]** *"Hay otros dos que la pueden escuchar. Al sur. Vienen para acá caminando."* (The Wrong Returned from San Ruiz — not yet arrived, but coming.)
- He wants to go to the pit. He holds himself back from it. He grips the fence post when he stands near the south pasture. His knuckles are always white on the side that faces south.

> **Dust runner — Silvio's recognition (3b)**: When investigators first stand near him — close enough that he could touch a sleeve — Silvio looks at the cuff or the saddlebag of one of them. He says, in his slightly-displaced way: *"Tienen el polvo. Como yo."* — *"You have the dust. Like me."* He does not elaborate. The investigator who carries the most pit-dust (the one who descended into the pit in C1 to scrape arcs alongside Concepción, or who has spent the most time near the geometry since) is the one Silvio addresses. **This is the third naming of the dust runner** (after Rosa in C2, Kuyen in C3). Silvio's recognition is the first time it is identified *as kinship* — he and the dust-carrier share something. Marked PCs noting this should feel a chill. They are slightly *more like Silvio* than they were before they came here.

**Roleplaying hook — Silvio**: He is one of the most effective horror tools in the arc precisely because he is not monstrous. He does his job. He answers questions politely. He is still, recognizably, a person. The wrongness is in the details — the shadow, the eyes, the grip on the fence. Let investigators decide whether he is a victim to be helped or a danger to be avoided. He is both, and neither fully.

## THE WRONG RETURNED — FIRST APPEARANCE

As investigators conclude their business at the estancia (or are being shown out), Silvio stops what he is doing. He faces south-southeast and holds very still for a moment. Then:

> *"They're here,"* he says. Not to anyone in particular. He sets down what he was carrying and walks to the fence. He stands there and looks across the south pasture.*
>
> **[ES]** *"Llegaron,"* dice. A nadie en particular. Apoya lo que estaba cargando y camina hasta el alambrado. Se queda ahí parado y mira a través del potrero sur.*

At the far edge of the south pasture — approximately 400 meters distant — two figures are visible. Standing still. Facing the estancia.

**The Wrong Returned**: Two former residents of San Ruiz. They walked back from Tres Lagunas — a journey of three days — without informing anyone. Rosa was right: they were changed by their nights in the south pasture, and they were sent away, and now they are back because the geometry is pulling them home.

**Appearance**: A man (Héctor, 50s) and a woman (Marta, 40s). They look healthy — well-fed from wherever they've been, appropriately dressed for travel. What is wrong about them becomes apparent only when they are close: they don't speak except in response to direct questions. Their eyes move in a way that tracks geometry rather than people — they look at the corners of rooms before the inhabitants. They cast shadows at angles that don't quite match the light.

**Behavior in this chapter**: They don't approach the estancia. They stand at the fence line and wait. If investigators approach them, they answer questions briefly. If asked why they came back: *"Something is finishing. We needed to be here for it."* — **[ES]** *"Algo se está terminando. Teníamos que estar acá para eso."* They cannot articulate more than this.

They are not dangerous. They are, like Silvio, pulled — and like Silvio, they are resisting the pull with what remains of their will.

**SAN Check**: 0/1 — seeing the two figures standing motionless at the fence line, and realizing what they must be.

> **Table pacing**: If the party approaches the fence at night, run the Wrong Returned encounter in low light. They do not enter the estancia. The party sleeps at the house; **C5 opens the next morning** with Mercedes' letter, departure east, El Pombero on *El Pelón*, and [Larrea's copper (5b)](./chapter-05-el-manuscrito.md#the-merchant-with-tarnished-copper-5b) on the road to the ruins — see [chapter-05 — *The Morning After* & Scene 3](./chapter-05-el-manuscrito.md#the-morning-after--estancia-la-esperanza).

## GM NOTES

**Don Eusebio is not redeemed yet**: This chapter he is frightened and partially honest. That is progress. It is not redemption. He still has not told investigators about Quirce's suspicious advance knowledge. He has not confessed to seeing the people walking to the pasture at night and doing nothing. Those truths come later, and they should cost him something when they arrive.

**The study is the chapter's center of gravity**: Get investigators in there. Let them take time with the manuscript. The horror of recognizing Saens's situation as a mirror of theirs — he failed the same way, for the same reasons, and died before closing it — is more effective than any monster description.

**Silvio should unsettle, not threaten**: Do not make him aggressive. The wrongness is in the ordinariness. He does his job. He answers questions. He grips the fence post. That is more frightening than violence.

**The Wrong Returned at the fence line**: End on the fence image or the night's conversation — Mercedes' letter at **C5 opening** (*The Morning After*); Pombero and Larrea's copper on the road to the ruins ([Scene 2–3](./chapter-05-el-manuscrito.md#scene-3--the-road-to-the-ruins)).

**Pacing note**: This chapter is slower than Chapter 3. It should feel like the eye of a storm — the violence of Arc 1's end gives way to something worse: comprehension. Everything investigators discover this chapter is information they almost had, and the gap between what they knew and what they should have known is itself a form of dread.
