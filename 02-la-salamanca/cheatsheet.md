## CHAPTER 4 — EL PATRÓN (1 PAGE)

**GOAL**: Confront the man who lied → break his denial → reveal the manuscript

**SCENE FLOW**

1. **The Estancia, Changed**

* Yellowed grass rings, cattle moving in single file, dogs that won't enter the south pasture
* Tomás won't meet eyes; Concepción gone (sent to Azul)
* Don Eusebio's right hand: ink stain spreading from index finger (C1) → full palm now

2. **The Confrontation**

* He interrogates *them* before they question him: what they saw, what Rosa said
* Psychology: he's deciding whether to double down or confess
* Cover story: "inspecting northern fences" — lie

3. **Silvio Méndez Arrives**

* Marked seven months; felt the pulse from Don Eusebio's pit
* Forces Don Eusebio's hand by *being there*
* Neither man wants to speak first

4. **The Locked Study**

* Light leaks under door at night; low continuous reading aloud
* Manuscript: Padre Saens, S.J., 1761–1763. Latin + Guaraní + Mapudungun marginalia
* Diagrams = same shape as pit, hide, chapel
* SAN 0/1D6 (reading the manuscript)

5. **First Wrong Returned**

* Appear at fence line at chapter end. Slow. Quiet. Watching.
* Faces of villagers from San Ruiz
* SAN 1/1D4

**KEY DIALOGUE**

* Silvio: *"Tienen el polvo. Como yo."* (the first naming as kinship)
* Don Eusebio: *"He works."* (about Silvio, flat, an admission)

**ATMOSPHERIC RUNNERS (3a/3b/3c)**

* Dust on the players' cuffs — Silvio recognizes it
* Low vibration in the locked study; intensifies when manuscript opens
* Tarnished copper runner: a merchant arrives the night before; copper items darkened by morning (5b)

**END**

* Wrong Returned at the fence; Silvio agrees to stay; Don Eusebio admits *some* of what he did

**GM NOTES**

* Don Eusebio is more hostile here than in C1; he's been outmaneuvered
* Treat the manuscript as the chapter's *object*, not an info-dump
* If players never enter the study, leak it: light, sound, manuscript pages on the wind

## CHAPTER 5 — EL MANUSCRITO (1 PAGE)

**GOAL**: Translate the manuscript → visit the Jesuit ruins → find Saens's bone chamber → plant the Reader-path foreshadow

**SCENE FLOW**

1. **The Wrong Returned at the Estancia**

* They walk in formation — geometric. Players notice or don't.
* They don't attack. They watch. They place themselves on the diagram.
* SAN 0/1D4 sustained presence

2. **The Road to the Ruins**

* Landscape changes: fewer birds, plants thinning, the air heavier
* Kuyen meets the party on the road if she lives; she will not enter the ruins

3. **The Jesuit Mission Ruins**

* Foundation stones, broken bell tower, walls chest-high
* Bell (1749) — older than the mission; brought from elsewhere
* Visual handout: `assets/dual-diagram.svg`

4. **The Carved Stone & The Descent**

* Spot Hidden / Cthulhu Mythos: the stone moves
* Bone chamber below: Padre Saens's body laid as the geometric center
* He achieved a *partial* binding — bought ~6 decades by becoming the anchor
* SAN 1/1D8

5. **READER-PATH FORESHADOW (2a)**

* Page 79 of the manuscript can be read aloud
* **The PC who reads aloud is the Reader.** Note the player.
* Don't tell them. Mercedes will recognize them in C8.

**MANUSCRIPT KEY FACTS**

* Names eight anchors, four directions, one warning: *the entity will let the ritual fail at peak pressure*
* Marginal note: *"…it is the same figure as the Necronomicón."* (Library Use, Regular)
* Pages 60–80: the binding section; pages 80+: the *opening* section (do not read)

**ATMOSPHERIC RUNNERS**

* Dust on the players still; Saens's bones have it on them too
* Vibration in the chamber; ladder rungs hum
* Observable consequence: candles bend toward the stone

**END**

* Mercedes's first letter arrives at the estancia: *"I have read parts of the Necronomicón. The figure is real."*

**GM NOTES**

* Failure tolerance on translation: partial reading still gives the eight anchors
* If players burn the manuscript: closing improvisation in C8–C9; survival becomes the highest achievable outcome
* If a PC reads alone at night: dream of Padre Saens, 1/1D10 SAN, deepest Don Eusebio understanding

## CHAPTER 6 — EL VISITANTE (1 PAGE)

**GOAL**: Meet Quirce → expose his agenda (without losing him as ally) → force Don Eusebio to commit → name El Patrón

**SCENE FLOW**

1. **Quirce's Letter Arrives** (5c)

* Elegant Spanish; Latin signature *sub signo magno*
* Latin speaker: colonial-era occult correspondence style
* He arrives the next afternoon

2. **Aldao Quirce — The Man**

* Late 50s. Soft hands. Walks like a scholar. Three armed men with him.
* Annotated copy of the manuscript. Brass markers (8 of them).
* Spot Hidden: one marker is *asymmetric* — northern panel, off by 3 degrees
* Cthulhu Mythos: this turns the ritual from a *closing* into a *binding for use*

3. **Quirce's Mediumship Moment** (3c — the vibration *stops* for ten seconds)

* He pauses mid-sentence. Different voice. Different cadence.
* Says: *"Gracias. — Eligio Dadálah."*
* Then the voice shifts again; the Great Wizard of Salamanca speaks one phrase
* SAN 1/1D6
* This is **El Patrón named for the first time.**

4. **Forcing Don Eusebio's Cooperation**

* Quirce uses the manuscript phrase: *"I invoke the anchor of the first opening."*
* This is *mechanical*, not social — Don Eusebio cannot refuse
* If players intervene physically: three armed men, this is not a negotiation

5. **Kuyen Confronts Quirce**

* If she's traveling with the party, she names the asymmetry without seeing it written down
* *"He's not closing it. He's keeping it."*

**KEY DIALOGUE**

* Quirce: *"I came because no one else would. The manuscript is not a closing manual."*
* El Patrón (through Quirce): *"Gracias. — Eligio Dadálah."*

**ATMOSPHERIC RUNNERS**

* Tomás / ranch hands mention Quirce moving furniture in the night
* Don Eusebio confirms the ten-second pause: *"Otra voz. No era la suya."*

**END**

* The party rides to the pit at dawn with Quirce, Don Eusebio, Silvio, Kuyen, Wrong Returned at the margins

**GM NOTES**

* If players kill Quirce in C6: betrayal vector shifts to Don Eusebio in C8 (entity offers him abundance; Psychology to hold him)
* Reward catching the brass-marker asymmetry — it makes C8 cleaner
* The mediumship moment must land. Drop the dialogue cold. No setup.

## ARC 2 SNAPSHOT

* **Theme**: Knowledge has provenance — and the provenance is contaminated
* **Emotions**: Doubt → Recognition → Dread
* **Seeds**: The Necronomicón named, El Patrón named (Eligio Dadálah), the partial binding (Saens), Mercedes by letter, the Reader-path planted
* **Rule**: The manuscript is the chapter's object. Geometry repeats. Voices borrow other voices. Trust no one's hand.
