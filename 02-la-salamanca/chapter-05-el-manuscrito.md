## THE MORNING AFTER — ESTANCIA LA ESPERANZA

Run this beat before the party rides out — not optional.

### A LETTER FROM BUENOS AIRES

[Tomás](../REF-NPCs.md#tomás-arredondo) finds the investigators at first light. He brings a letter that arrived yesterday; he has been sitting on it because Don Eusebio was shaken and Tomás did not know what to do with mail in the middle of a confrontation.

The letter is from **[Doña Mercedes Solís](../REF-NPCs.md#doña-mercedes-solís)**, Universidad de Buenos Aires, addressed to *"El Sr. [Don Eusebio Valdez](../REF-NPCs.md#don-eusebio-valdez), Estancia La Esperanza, Provincia de Buenos Aires."* It is dated three weeks ago.

> *"Estimado Sr. Valdez: Aldao Quirce me ha enviado parte de la correspondencia que él mantiene con usted, sin su consentimiento. Le adjunto copia. Me llamó la atención una nota al margen de la página 47 del manuscrito de Saens — Quirce la transcribió. Si la lectura de Quirce es correcta, esa palabra cambia mucho. Hay una segunda voz dispuesta a ayudar — no la suya. Si necesita una segunda opinión sobre lo que está sucediendo en sus tierras, puede escribirme a la dirección abajo. — M.S."* — *"Dear Sr. Valdez: Aldao Quirce has sent me part of the correspondence he is maintaining with you, without his knowledge. I am enclosing a copy. A marginal note on page 47 of Saens's manuscript — which Quirce transcribed — caught my attention. If Quirce's reading is correct, that word changes a great deal. There is a second voice willing to help — not his. If you need a second opinion on what is happening on your lands, you may write to me at the address below. — M.S."*

(*The "word" Mercedes alludes to but does not write is **Necronomicón**. Mercedes writes around the word in her own correspondence on principle; she will name it only in person or in a sealed reply. Players who found the [marginal note in C4](./chapter-04-el-patron.md#the-locked-study) recognize what she means; players who have not will be drawn back to page 47 to find it.*)

Don Eusebio has not replied. He hands the letter to the players. *"Si pasa algo y yo no estoy, escríbanle ustedes. Ella es de fiar. Quirce no."* — *"If something happens and I am not here, you write to her. She is trustworthy. Quirce is not."*

Mercedes is the campaign's eventual moral and intellectual center (overview, Arc 3 onward). This letter introduces her as a name early, well before her Arc 4 reveal. Players who write to her will receive her **reply** later this chapter ([Scene 9](#scene-9-doña-mercedes-reply)).

## SCENE 1 — THE WRONG RETURNED

### Morning After (or First Approach)

If the party **already spoke with Héctor and Marta at the fence** ([C4 night beat](./chapter-04-el-patron.md#the-wrong-returned--first-appearance)), open with them falling in alongside the horses as the party leaves the estancia — no re-introduction needed. Use the dialogue below only for questions not yet asked, or for sustained daylight conversation on the road.

If the party **has not yet approached them**, they spent the night at the fence line — standing, as near as anyone can tell, without sleeping. In the morning they are still there. When investigators approach them in daylight:

> *They turn to face you before you call out. Simultaneously. Not startled — they knew you were coming. Up close, they look exactly as [Rosa](../REF-NPCs.md#rosa) described: they eat, they answer questions, they stand in sunlight without apparent discomfort. But there is something in the focus of their attention that is wrong in a way that takes a moment to name. They are looking at you, and also looking at something that occupies the same space as you but is not quite you — a geometry of your position in the space, rather than your face.*
>
> **[ES]** *Se dan vuelta para mirarte antes de que los llames. Al mismo tiempo. No sobresaltados — sabían que venías. De cerca, son exactamente como los describió Rosa: comen, contestan preguntas, se quedan parados al sol sin molestia aparente. Pero hay algo en el foco de su atención que está mal de una manera que cuesta un momento nombrar. Te están mirando a vos, y también mirando algo que ocupa el mismo espacio que vos pero que no sos del todo — una geometría de tu posición en el espacio, más que tu cara.*

**Appearance**:
- **Héctor Ríos**, 54. A former farmer from San Ruiz, wide-shouldered, graying. He was considered steady — not smart but reliable. Now he responds to questions with precise answers and no small talk. He has lost the social texture around his speech: the *"pos"*, the pauses, the indirect approaches. He just answers.
- **Marta Ríos** (his wife, no relation to Héctor — the surname coincidence was a village joke), 43. She was the village teacher and midwife. Calm by profession, she is calmer now in a way that is not professional. She is the more present of the two — occasionally she slips into something that was Marta, a moment of expression that crosses her face and is gone.

**What They Know**:
They don't know everything. They know what the entity showed them — which is geometrically accurate but not experientially interpretable. They speak about it in terms of architecture and space, not sensation.

*"It isn't underground,"* Héctor says. *"That was wrong. It's in the angles. It lives in the places where one surface meets another. The pit is just where the shape is biggest — where there's enough angle for it to reach through."* — **[ES]** *"No está bajo tierra. Eso estaba mal. Está en los ángulos. Vive en los lugares donde una superficie se encuentra con otra. El pozo es solo donde la forma es más grande — donde hay suficiente ángulo para que pase por ahí."*

*"There are others like us,"* Marta says, meaning Silvio, whom they have sensed but not met. *"Not many. The ones it touched but didn't take. We're still here. But we're also partly there."* — **[ES]** *"Hay otros como nosotros. No muchos. Los que tocó pero no se llevó. Seguimos acá. Pero también estamos en parte allá."*

*"We came back because it's almost time,"* Héctor says. *"We felt it from Tres Lagunas. The closing, or the opening. One of them is going to happen. We need to be at the geometry when it does."* — **[ES]** *"Volvimos porque ya casi es la hora. Lo sentimos desde Tres Lagunas. El cierre, o la apertura. Una de las dos va a pasar. Tenemos que estar en la geometría cuando pase."*

**What They Want**: To be at the pit for the final event — closing or opening. They don't have a preference. They are not aligned with the entity; they are simply anchored to the geometry. Their presence at the closing ritual will matter (see [Chapter 9](../03-the-great-sleeper/chapter-09-el-cierre.md)).

**The question no one wants to ask**: What will happen to them when the geometry is sealed? Marta answers this if directly asked, without apparent distress: *"I don't know. If the shape closes, maybe we close with it. Maybe we come back. We decided it doesn't matter. We need to be there."* — **[ES]** *"No sé. Si la forma se cierra, capaz nos cerramos con ella. Capaz volvemos. Decidimos que no importa. Tenemos que estar ahí."*

**SAN Check**: 0/1D3 (sustained conversation with someone who is and isn't a person)

**Possible Questions & Answers — The Wrong Returned**

*"Do you remember what happened to you?"*
> Héctor: *"I remember walking south. The night was very clear. And then it was like — like someone showed me a map of a place I'd been trying to describe without words. It was a relief, for a moment. And then I was back and it was morning."*
> **[ES]** Héctor: *"Me acuerdo de caminar para el sur. La noche estaba muy clara. Y después fue como — como si alguien me mostrara un mapa de un lugar que venía tratando de describir sin palabras. Fue un alivio, por un momento. Y después estaba de vuelta y era de mañana."*
> Marta: *"I remember it differently. There was no relief in mine. Only size. Something very large noticing something very small."*
> **[ES]** Marta: *"Yo me acuerdo distinto. En el mío no hubo alivio. Solo tamaño. Algo muy grande notando algo muy chico."*

*"Are you in pain?"*
> Héctor: *"No."* *(Said too quickly and too finally.)*
> **[ES]** Héctor: *"No."* *(Dicho demasiado rápido y demasiado terminante.)*
> Marta: *(A long pause.)* *"I don't feel pain the way I used to. I'm not sure if that's better."*
> **[ES]** Marta: *(Una pausa larga.)* *"No siento dolor como antes. No estoy segura de que eso sea mejor."*

*"Can you be helped?"*
> Marta: *"You could close the geometry. Maybe that ends this. Or maybe this is what we are now and closing it just removes the sound."* *(She touches her own face as if checking it's still there.)* *"Close it anyway."*
> **[ES]** Marta: *"Podrían cerrar la geometría. Capaz eso termina con esto. O capaz esto es lo que somos ahora y cerrarla solo saca el sonido."* *(Se toca la propia cara como comprobando que sigue ahí.)* *"Ciérrenla igual."*

## SCENE 2 — EL POMBERO ON THE RIDGE

On departure, after Héctor and Marta have fallen in alongside the horses. The road east skirts a low rise before it opens onto the open pampa — a *cuchilla* the locals call *El Pelón*, "the bald spot," because no grass grows on its crown. Tomás does not look at it as you ride past. Don Eusebio's *peones* won't speak its name. As your horses round its base, a small figure appears at the top — not climbing, simply *there*, as if he had always been standing.

> *He is short. Brown skin, broad face, a straw hat too large for his head. He raises one hand and waves — not friendly, not threatening, just acknowledging that you are passing. He whistles. The whistle has more notes than there are notes a person should know.*
>
> **[ES]** *Es bajo. Piel morena, cara ancha, un sombrero de paja demasiado grande para su cabeza. Levanta una mano y saluda — ni amistoso, ni amenazante, solo reconociendo que están pasando. Silba. El silbido tiene más notas de las que una persona debería conocer.*

**[El Pombero](../REF-NPCs.md#el-pombero)** is the [overview](../overview.md)'s guide-and-test. In this chapter he does not *do* anything yet. He whistles. He waves. He vanishes when the party looks away. **Spot Hidden** while passing: the grass on top of *El Pelón* has the same ring pattern as the cattle field's grass rings — older, smaller, but the same shape. *El Pelón* is a *previous* burial pit, ten generations old.

Tomás, if pressed, will say in a strangled voice: *"El Pombero. Le silba a los que están por entrar a algo. No me pregunten más."* — *"El Pombero. He whistles to those who are about to enter into something. Don't ask me more."*

First folklore encounter; foreshadowing of an underground entrance this chapter will return to at the [Jesuit ruins](#scene-4-the-jesuit-mission-ruins). **Do not open the entrance here.**

> **Roll for Hound** ([keeper table](#keeper-roll-for-hound-c5)): if a Marked PC **looks hard** at the ring pattern on *El Pelón*, or the party camps in sight of the rise — **both Marked PCs roll 2d6**.

## SCENE 3 — THE ROAD TO THE RUINS

The Jesuit mission ruins are a day's ride east and slightly south — Padre Saens's notes give landmarks (a forked river, a mesa that looks like a tooth) that are still accurate. The Wrong Returned walk alongside the party. They don't require water stops. They keep pace without apparent effort.

### Landscape Changes

> *The land between the estancia and the ruins is ordinary pampa — flat, dry grass, occasional low scrub. But if you travel with Héctor and Marta, you begin to see it through their attention. They note corners. Not dramatically — a glance when the road bends, a moment of stillness where a mesa casts a shadow in a right angle. They are reading the geometry of the land the way a cattleman reads grass for moisture. The land is full of angles they have learned to see.*
>
> **[ES]** *La tierra entre la estancia y las ruinas es pampa común — pasto chato y seco, algún matorral bajo de vez en cuando. Pero si viajás con Héctor y Marta, empezás a verla a través de su atención. Registran las esquinas. No de manera dramática — una mirada cuando el camino dobla, un instante de quietud donde una meseta proyecta una sombra en ángulo recto. Leen la geometría de la tierra como un hombre de campo lee el pasto buscando humedad. La tierra está llena de ángulos que ellos aprendieron a ver.*

**Optional encounter — the road**:
During the night camp on the road, a [Luz Mala](../REF-NPCs.md#la-luz-mala) appears to the south — not orbiting, just stationary, about 200 meters distant. Watching. It has followed them from the estancia. Héctor watches it without apparent fear. He says: *"It's a sensor. Not a threat unless you disturb the geometry."* — **[ES]** *"Es un sensor. No es una amenaza salvo que toques la geometría."* He has known this since his night in the pasture. He learned it the same way he learned everything since then — from the inside.

The Luz Mala tracks the party all the way to the ruins.

### THE MERCHANT WITH TARNISHED COPPER (5b)

Late in the day's ride — the tooth-shaped mesa is visible, the ruins perhaps an hour ahead — the party meets **Don Crisanto Larrea** before they reach the stones.

> *A small cart is pulled off the track in the lee of a low rise, axle propped on a stone. A grey-bearded man in his mid-fifties is sorting through his goods with the expression of someone who has already lost an argument with the day. He looks up as you approach. He does not reach for a weapon. He reaches for his hat.*
>
> **[ES]** *Un carro chico está apartado del camino al abrigo de una cuchilla baja, el eje apoyado en una piedra. Un hombre canoso de unos cincuenta y pico está revisando su mercadería con la cara de quien ya perdió una discusión con el día. Levanta la vista cuando se acercan. No busca un arma. Busca el sombrero.*

**Backstory (if asked)**: Larrea is a traveling merchant on the road from Tandil to Buenos Aires — *yerba mate*, copper *bombillas*, copper buttons, kettle-fittings, a few iron tools. His axle cracked two days east of here. He limped as far as the [Jesuit ruins](#scene-4-the-jesuit-mission-ruins), spent **last night** camped inside the broken walls — shelter from the wind, he says. He was leaving this morning when the copper went wrong.

> *Every copper item in his cart has tarnished black overnight. Not the green of natural copper oxide — black, evenly, as if held in flame for an hour. The copper bombillas are unusable. The buttons are dull. The kettle-fittings are ruined. The iron tools are untouched. The yerba is fine. **Only the copper, and only the copper that was on the side of the cart that faced the chapel floor** — the carved stone he slept beside without knowing what it was. The copper on the far side of the cart is normal.*
>
> **[ES]** *Cada objeto de cobre de su carro se ennegreció durante la noche. No el verde del óxido natural del cobre — negro, parejo, como si lo hubieran tenido a la llama una hora. Las bombillas de cobre quedaron inservibles. Los botones, opacos. Los herrajes de la pava, arruinados. Las herramientas de hierro, intactas. La yerba, bien. **Solo el cobre, y solo el cobre que estaba del lado del carro que daba al piso de la capilla** — la piedra tallada junto a la cual durmió sin saber qué era. El cobre del otro lado del carro está normal.*

Larrea is bewildered. Twenty years a merchant; he has never seen this. He asks if they know what could have caused it. *"Dormí al lado de esa piedra en el piso. No la toqué. Esta mañana el cobre del lado de la piedra estaba así. Es como si algo hubiera mirado el cobre toda la noche. ¿Saben? Como si el cobre hubiera estado mirándolo a él también."* — *"I slept beside that stone in the floor. I didn't touch it. This morning the copper on the stone side was like this. It is as if something had looked at the copper all night. You know? As if the copper had been looking back."*

**Héctor or Marta** (if present): a glance at the chapel, then at the copper — *"The stone is still doing work. So is the copper now."* They will not explain further unless pressed.

> **What this is**: The [dual diagram](../assets/dual-diagram.svg) under the chapel floor — older and more direct than the [burial pit](../flowmap.md) at distance — *recognized* the copper as metallically similar to [Quirce's](../REF-NPCs.md#aldao-quirce) brass markers and tried to extend through it overnight. Larrea slept on top of active geometry without knowing it. **A Hard Cthulhu Mythos roll** identifies the same geometric-extension effect as Quirce's markers — but here, without markers, pulled from *below*. Marked PCs may feel it unprompted: the blackened copper and the carved stone *match* in some way they cannot quite name.
>
> **What investigators can do**: Buy the ruined copper at scrap price; Larrea will be grateful (he was going to lose the value anyway). The copper, brought to the [bone chamber](#scene-7-the-bone-chamber) later this chapter, will *re-tarnish* on contact with the walls — confirming the link. [Mercedes](../REF-NPCs.md#doña-mercedes-solís), seeing the copper later, will recognize the signature.
>
> **Larrea's onward role**: He rigs the axle well enough to limp east and rides on toward Buenos Aires once trade is done — or after the party leaves the ruins. **He arrives in the city before the players do, two months later.** He tells the story of the tarnished copper to the *vigilantes urbanos* at the gate. The story reaches [Saráchaga](../REF-NPCs.md#don-eladio-saráchaga). Saráchaga writes a letter to Mercedes saying *"the pressure is now visible at the perimeter, not just at the centre."* This is one of the threads that brings Mercedes to the camp in [C7](../03-the-great-sleeper/chapter-07-la-vispera.md). **Larrea is the campaign's first non-Keeper, non-curandera, non-tribal civilian witness to the entity's reach.** He never knows what he saw. He retires to a small farm outside Mercedes the next year and lives quietly. He dies of natural causes in 1843.

**Placement**: Run this on the **approach** to the ruins (end of Scene 3) or as the **first beat at the ruins** (opening of Scene 4) — whichever fits pacing. If the party camped on the road ([Luz Mala](#scene-3-the-road-to-the-ruins) optional), they meet Larrea the next morning at the ruin perimeter with the copper already blackened.

## SCENE 4 — THE JESUIT MISSION RUINS

### Setting Description

> *The mission was abandoned in 1767 when the Jesuits were expelled from the Spanish colonies. What remains: the foundation stones of four buildings, a broken bell tower still partially standing, walls that reach chest-height and no further. The stone is pale limestone, different from the local rock — it was carried here. Whoever built this chose this location deliberately.*
>
> *The vegetation inside the ruins is different from outside. Denser, greener despite the season. Things grow here that shouldn't grow this far south — a fig tree, improbably, in the corner of what was the chapel. As if the land here is more fértil.*
>
> *That word. That word again.*
>
> **[ES]** *La misión fue abandonada en 1767, cuando los jesuitas fueron expulsados de las colonias españolas. ## SCENE 7 — THE BONE CHAMBER

**Keeper context — what Saens actually achieved**: Visual evidence of [page 79](#deep-read-the-manuscript-chapter-5-additions): *"I could not close it alone."* The closing requires three roles (opener, knowledge-holder, counter-figure). Saens stood in for all of them. What he produced was a **partial binding** that held for ~6 decades, sustained afterward by [Antu-Rayo](../REF-NPCs.md#antu-rayo)'s lineage. [Don Eusebio](../REF-NPCs.md#don-eusebio-valdez)'s ritual eight months ago widened a wound already opening. **The bone chamber is not a finished closing. It is the cost of a brave, incomplete one.** The [diagram lesson](#explaining-the-diagram-marta-and-héctor) above is how the Wrong Returned translate that for the table without reading Saens's Latin.

**A Hard Cthulhu Mythos roll** on the non-human skulls: they are *"the first people"* El Pombero referenced — the species that carved the original geometry, before any human population reached the Pampas. Each transmission of the closing tradition (first people → Tehuelche → Saens via Antu-Rayo → Kuyen via her grandmother — see [TABLE-STATE](../TABLE-STATE.md)) has lost detail. The full closing was never available to humans alone. Strongest argument that **the Necronomicón may contain the full ritual** — the first people's knowledge survived in writing somewhere, and the most likely place is Buenos Aires.

**Observable consequences**: Lanterns brighten ~30% at the chamber center (where Saens's skull lies) and dim ~30% at the outer ring. No draft. Lit cigars: smoke draws inward toward the central skull rather than rising — the geometry is breathing.

#### The cataloguing pass *(survivable — not combat)*

Use this **instead of** a full Hound manifestation. The entity has been **notified** that the diagram changed; it comes to **read the edit**, not to feed.

**Read-aloud**:

> *The lantern at the disturbed station gutters — not wind. The angle where the wall meets the floor at that point looks deeper than it should, as if the corner receded six inches into the limestone. Something large is present in the periphery of that angle. Not in the room. In the **join**.*
>
> *It does not look at you. It looks at the bones you moved — the gap in the pattern — the way a clerk looks at a ledger line crossed out.*
>
> **[ES]** *La linterna en el punto alterado se apaga a medias — no es viento. El ángulo donde la pared se encuentra con el piso en ese lugar parece más profundo de lo que debería, como si la esquina se hubiera metido seis centímetros en la caliza. Algo grande está presente en la periferia de ese ángulo. No en la sala. En la **unión**.*
>
> **[ES]** *No te mira a vos. Mira los huesos que moviste — el hueco en el figura — como un escribano mira una línea tachada en un libro mayor.*

**Duration**: 1 round (Tier 1 on failed 2d6), 2 rounds (Tier 2–3), 3 rounds (grave sin).

**What players can do** (each round, each PC — pick one):

| Action | Roll / effect |
|---|---|
| **Hold still** | No roll. Safest. The entity finishes reading and leaves. |
| **Freeze on intact bone** | Stand on an **undisturbed** outer-ring position (Héctor/Marta can point). **Automatic** — the entity already has them on file. |
| **Replace what you moved** | DEX or Art/Craft, **Regular** — one bone back per success. Each success **ends the pass one round early**. Marta can coach (+1 bonus die if she helped with [diagram lesson](#explaining-the-diagram-marta-and-héctor)). |
| **Throw fire toward the angle** | No roll. Entity **retracts from that surface** for 1 round ([C3 rule](../assets/entity-hound.md)). Does not end the pass — it moves to the next disturbed gap. |
| **Run** | **CON Regular** — pampas rule: running manufactures angles. On fail: **0/1 SAN** and the pass **extends 1 round** (you became interesting). |
| **Attack** | Weapons pass through. Waste of round. |
| **Marked PC "looks hard"** at the entity | **Do not** unless you want escalation. Triggers raised 2d6 threshold + **POW roll** or brief spatial vertigo (**0/1D3 SAN**, lose next action). |

**When it ends**: Cold lifts. Lanterns return to normal breathing. Disturbed bones that were **left in the chamber** begin to **creep** — not animate, *settle* — toward their original positions over **10–30 minutes** (audible scrape, unsettling, not lethal). Bones **carried out** do not return.

**SAN**: **0/1** per PC present (Tier 1–2), **1/1D3** (Tier 3 or grave sin). Not gore — *being read*.

**Marked PC private read**: *It was not angry. It was updating. You were not the subject. The gap was.*

## SCENE 8 — THE WHISPERING TUNNELS

Beyond the bone chamber, three tunnels lead deeper. Roughly two meters tall, smooth-walled, ceilings carved with the dual diagram's outer ring repeated at one-meter intervals. **The whispering happens** because the tunnels are *acoustically tuned* — every word spoken inside any tunnel is repeated at the entrance of all three tunnels, in the speaker's voice, at the exact volume of the original.

Practical effect: any roll involving speaking in the tunnels is one step harder — the speaker hears their own words returned in real time. **A Listen roll** identifies the tuning; silence works as a navigational tool.

The three tunnels lead to:
- **Offering chamber**: stone table with a cup, bone needle, clay tablet in unreadable script. **Hard Library Use** identifies **proto-Tehuelche**, but the script is older than known proto-Tehuelche by perhaps a thousand years. Permanent campaign object — Mercedes will want to see it.
- **Flooded chamber**: floor dropped; groundwater chest-high. Submerged: another dual diagram, same scale as the chapel stone — intact and *still active*. A witness-stone (Arc 4) lowered here would read warm. *Observable*: pampas grass dropped on the water will not sink; it arranges into a small dual-diagram on the surface within thirty seconds.
- **Sealed door** at the end of the third tunnel — the [Great Wizard's chamber](#optional-the-chamber-of-the-great-wizard). El Pombero warned ([Scene 6](#scene-6-el-pombero-returns)). Default: leave it sealed and move on. **If the players insist on opening it**, run [*The Chamber of the Great Wizard*](#optional-the-chamber-of-the-great-wizard) below.

**Marked PC in the tunnels**: speaking quietly, they hear their own words returned — but with a *second* voice underneath, faint, in the rhythm of the dual diagram's outer ring (twelve syllables, pause, twelve syllables). They will recognize this rhythm in [C8](../03-the-great-sleeper/chapter-08-la-traicion.md) at the closing, and in [C12](../04-buenos-aires-and-the-heist/chapter-12-el-libro.md) at the dock.

> **Roll for Hound**: first **spoken word** in tunnels under stress; Marked PC notices twelve-syllable undertone; entering **flooded** chamber with active diagram — **both Marked PCs roll 2d6** ([keeper table](#keeper-roll-for-hound-c5)). Wizard chamber triggers: [separate table](#roll-for-hound-key-geometries).

### KEEPER BRIEF — THE GREAT WIZARD OF SALAMANCA

*Full NPC entry: [REF-NPCs — The Great Wizard](../REF-NPCs.md#the-great-wizard-of-salamanca). Stats: [npc-stats.md](../assets/npc-stats.md). Cast summary: [cast-one-pager](../assets/cast-one-pager.md).*

**Who he is**: Mortal head of the **Cult of the Great Sleeper** — though the players do not know that name yet. A scholar-priest in late old age who built a school in the deepest chamber of La Salamanca and believes he is recovering pre-colonial knowledge that will *re-found* the *campaña* outside the Spanish-Catholic frame. He is sincere, learned, and **not insane**. He is the campaign's proof that intelligence is not protection.

**What he looks like**: Ash-blond hair (same cast as the Coypac priests). Mercedes will confess this in [C11](../04-buenos-aires-and-the-heist/chapter-11-la-decision.md).

### OPTIONAL — THE CHAMBER OF THE GREAT WIZARD

Run only if investigators force the sealed door after [Pombero's warning](#scene-6-el-pombero-returns). **Warn at the table**: this is the deepest human layer of La Salamanca; what they learn here cannot be unlearned; [C6](./chapter-06-el-visitante.md) will feel different if they have already met the Wizard or read his desk.

#### The door

The third tunnel ends in a **slab of the same imported limestone** as the chapel stone — flush with the wall, no handle. The dual diagram is carved into it, but here the opening and closing arcs are **reversed** from the surface stone: this is a *seal*, not an entrance marker.

| Approach | Result |
|---|---|
| Brute force (STR combined) | Fails. The slab is not heavy — it is *anchored*. The geometry holds it. |
| Occult / Cthulhu Mythos | Recognizes a deliberate counter-seal. Someone with full knowledge of the dual diagram could open it from outside — but only at two intersection points, as Marta opened the shaft. |

| Marked PC | Feels the seal as a second skin over something that has been breathing on the other side for decades. No roll to know it is occupied — only whether they flinch. **SAN 0/1** if they open it anyway. |

## SCENE 10 — THE WARNING, WITHOUT KUYEN *(THIS TABLE — source of truth)*

Scene 10 exists to put two pieces of information in the players' hands before the closing: **(1) return west — the pit at La Esperanza, not the ruins, is where the work happens; (2) the eastern segment of the binding is the weak side, and it is already failing.** Kuyen would have delivered both. She is dead. The same information arrives through the ruins themselves.

> ⚠️ **[THIS TABLE — STEAMROLL]** Do **not** treat these as optional or pick-one. Run **all three deliveries below (A + B + C)** in order as the party surfaces from La Salamanca. Then run [**Departure — return west**](#departure-return-west). If players still hesitate, use [keeper steamroll](#keeper-steamroll-return-west) escalation. The chapter does not end with them comfortable at the ruins.

**Delivery A — The goat.** The [goat](../REF-NPCs.md#la-cabra-the-goat) that has followed the party since [San Ruiz](../01-la-tierra-mala/chapter-02-las-senales.md) has been watching the geometry all along. As the investigators emerge from La Salamanca / the carved stone with the dual-diagram knowledge, the goat is standing at the ruins entrance and will not move. A Marked PC who looks at her sees the six pupils (three per eye) tracing the same shape as the carved stone — and lingering on its **eastern arc**, returning to it, returning to it. She is reading the diagram. She has always been reading it. She holds still for perhaps thirty seconds, fixed on the east of the shape, then turns and continues following the party. No words, no explanation. The Marked PC understands they were just shown which side gives first.

> **SAN Check** (Marked PC only, watching the pupils track): 0/1 — not terror, recognition.

**Delivery B — Saens's late note.** In the [bone chamber](#scene-7-the-bone-chamber), among Saens's papers (already C5 canon), a Spot Hidden or a careful re-read surfaces a final marginal entry the investigators hadn't reached — a sketch of the pit at Estancia La Esperanza with the eastern segment circled twice, and beneath it:

> *"El nudo del este siempre cede primero. La tradición de los Custodios lo llama el lado de la apertura — el que permite que algo entre, y el que necesita refuerzo antes del cierre. Sin ayuda en el este, el símbolo no cierra."*
>
> *"The eastern knot always gives first. The Keeper tradition calls it the opening side — the one that allows something in, and the one that needs reinforcement before the closing. Without help at the east, the symbol does not close."*

This is, almost word for word, what Kuyen would have told them at the camp. It is now in a dead man's hand. The Mandinga-deal PCs reading this feel the obligation sharpen — they are being pointed, in Saens's voice, at the exact place their debt will come due.

> **SAN Check** (reading it aloud): 0/1.

**Delivery C — La Llorona as signal.** [La Llorona](../REF-NPCs.md#la-llorona), displaced from San Ruiz and moving east toward the pit ([C2](../01-la-tierra-mala/chapter-02-las-senales.md) outcome), passes near the Jesuit ruins during the investigators' time there. She cannot speak. She is not an ally and not a threat in this moment. But a Marked PC feels her passing as a **directional pull — east, toward the pit** — and in that pull the geometry of the creek bed and the burial pit lights up briefly in their perception, the eastern arc of it flickering like a flame that won't catch. No words. A map, and a warning about which line is thin.

> **SAN Check** (Marked PC feeling the pull): 0/1.

**After A + B + C**, the takeaway must be explicit at the table: *return to La Esperanza, days not weeks, east is the side that fails, Quirce is inbound.* No living teacher hands it to them — they read it off the world — but **you name it out loud in the departure beat** so nobody leaves fuzzy.

## GM NOTES

**The Wrong Returned are the emotional center of this chapter**: They are the most human element in an increasingly inhuman situation — people who walked into something they didn't understand, survived it differently, and are now choosing to face what comes next. Their composure is not the entity's doing; it's their own. Treat them with the dignity their situation deserves.

**The ruins should feel ancient and heavy**: This is not a spooky ruin — there are no ghosts here, no traps. The horror is geological. Something was here before the Tehuelche, before the Jesuits, before anyone. The carefully transported limestone. The perfect precision of the carving. The impossible fig tree. The land that is inexplicably more fertile within the walls. Something made this place a nexus and then left the geometry here like a road sign.

**The manuscript payoff**: Investigators have carried this document since [Chapter 4](./chapter-04-el-patron.md#the-locked-study). Chapter 5 is where it pays off. Give them time with it. The Saens passages should land as genuine revelation — not just exposition, but the experience of someone who failed where the investigators might not. The parallel is earned.

**Héctor and Marta as structural elements**: Make sure investigators understand, by the end of this chapter, that the Wrong Returned must be present at the closing. Not as sacrifices — as anchors. This changes their status from victims-to-be-protected to agents-with-a-role. It's more complicated than either the horror of sacrificial logic or the simplicity of rescue.

**Quirce is coming**: Don Eusebio mentioned Quirce at the end of [Chapter 4](./chapter-04-el-patron.md) with hesitation. Investigators who wrote to him, or checked in with the estancia, or had Tomás watching for arrivals should now learn: Quirce has been spotted on the road. He is one day away. This is the hook that drives them back toward the estancia and into [Chapter 6](./chapter-06-el-visitante.md).
