# CHAPTER 6 — EL VISITANTE
### *The Visitor*

**Chapter Goal**: Unmask Aldao Quirce. Force Don Eusebio's commitment. Move everyone to the pit.
**Emotional Arc**: Suspicion → Confrontation → The point of no return
**Key Seed**: The geometry drew not just an entity, but human attention. There are people who study these openings — and they don't want them closed.

---

## OPENING SITUATION

The investigators return to the estancia to find Aldao Quirce has already arrived. Don Eusebio let him in — out of some mixture of hope, anxiety, and the social training of a man who cannot turn away a visitor from Buenos Aires.

Quirce has been in the study for two hours when the investigators arrive. Don Eusebio is in the sala, drinking mate he isn't tasting, waiting.

The estancia feels different with Quirce in it. Tighter. More watched.

### Between-chapter hook (5c) — Quirce's announcement letter

Two days before the investigators return from the ruins (i.e., on a day they spent at the carved stone in C5), a courier rode in from the south. He delivered a single sealed letter, addressed to Don Eusebio. Tomás kept it on the sala mantelpiece, unopened — Don Eusebio has not been opening mail. The investigators may find it the morning of their return; or Tomás will hand it to them when they arrive; or Don Eusebio will, having read it, hand it to them.

The letter is short, in elegant Buenos Aires Spanish:

> *"Don Eusebio Valdez —*
>
> *Recibida su llamada de auxilio. Por la confianza que mantenemos hace ya varios años, considero apropiado hacerle visita en persona en lugar de despachar a uno de mis aprendices. Salgo mañana al amanecer; estaré en su estancia, salvo demoras, dentro de cinco días. Le pido que mantenga el sitio tranquilo y que no decida nada importante hasta que conversemos.*
>
> *Atte., A. Quirce*
> *sub signo magno"*

> *"Don Eusebio Valdez —*
>
> *Your call for help received. Given the trust we have maintained over several years, I think it appropriate to visit you in person rather than dispatch an apprentice. I depart tomorrow at dawn; I will be at your estancia, barring delay, within five days. I ask you keep the place quiet and decide nothing important until we have spoken.*
>
> *Sincerely, A. Quirce*
> *sub signo magno"*

> **What the players can spot**:
>
> - **Latin speaker** (Library Use, Occult, Cthulhu Mythos, or any character with seminary or university training): *sub signo magno* — *"under the great sign"* — is a colonial-era epistolary signature style used in **occult correspondence**. It is not a Catholic religious closing. It is not a guild closing. It is a closing used by a small network of European-trained occultists in the Americas in the late 18th century, modeled on the *sub sigillo confessionis* convention but secularized and *adapted to a different sign than the cross*. **Quirce is identifying himself, in a coded way, as a member of an occult network.** A player who recognizes this gains a significant Insight head-start for the C6 confrontation.
> - **Calligraphy**: The signature is dated. The phrase has been used in correspondence for at least two hundred years. Mercedes (in C7 or C8) will recognize it instantly if shown. *"Hace cuarenta años todavía firmaban así. Pensé que se había muerto la costumbre."* — *"Forty years ago they still signed this way. I thought the practice had died."*
> - **Ink**: A Hard Spot Hidden on the ink — Quirce mixes copper into his black ink (a personal eccentricity). The ink has *gone faintly green* on the paper since the letter arrived, despite no moisture. Investigators who saw Larrea the merchant's tarnished copper (C4→C5 hook, *5b*) will recognize the same effect at smaller scale. **The geometry has been at the letter all night.**
>
> Quirce knows nothing about the green ink. He believes himself a careful, controlled, nonbeliever-of-his-own-marginalia. He is wrong.

## NPC: ALDAO QUIRCE

**Role**: The other interested party. A man who studies geometric anomalies — but not to close them. The representative of a wider, darker world of knowledge.

**Appearance**: 48 years old. City-built — soft hands, good boots that have seen more cobblestones than field. Well-dressed in a way that reads as deliberately understated: a dark suit, no jewelry, a leather satchel of excellent make. His hair is neat, his mustache trimmed to precision. He has the face of a man whose profession requires him to seem trustworthy at first glance, and who has practiced that seeming for decades.

He carries a leather case that contains: brass measurement instruments (angle gauges, a compass of unusual precision, a small theodolite in its box), a notebook written in a cipher that resembles but is not Pitman shorthand, and a reproduction of the Saens manuscript — not a copy investigators have seen, but a *different version*, with annotations in three different hands and sections that are not water-damaged where the original is.

**Voice**: Warm, precisely modulated, educated Buenos Aires. He gives the impression of being delighted to meet you — genuinely, specifically delighted — in a way that after a few minutes begins to feel like a performance of delight rather than the thing itself.

**Mannerisms**:
- Introduces himself with the full title "Dr. Aldao Quirce, Faculty of Natural Philosophy, Universidad de Buenos Aires" — a real institution, a real faculty, but no one at the university would recognize his name in this capacity.
- When listening, he is completely still. Not polite-still — observationally still. He is measuring something while you speak.
- He has a habit of looking at the corners of rooms before the people in them. The Wrong Returned do the same thing. This is not coincidental.

**Who He Is (for the Keeper only)**:
Quirce is a member of a loose network of scholars, engineers, and wealthy enthusiasts in Buenos Aires and Montevideo who have been documenting "geometric anomalies" — sites like this one — for roughly twenty years. They are not a secret society in any dramatic sense: they have no formal structure, no hierarchy, no name they use for themselves. They are simply people who discovered that certain geometries do something, and who have been mapping them, studying them, and in a few cases, cautiously using them.

Quirce's goal is not to trigger a full opening at La Esperanza — he is not suicidal. His goal is to **observe the closing** — specifically, whether the geometry fully seals, partially seals, or rebounds. This information is valuable to his network. He believes a full closing will produce observable phenomena that will answer questions the network has debated for years. He views the four dead tribe members from Chapter 3 as "field casualties" — regrettable but scientifically significant.

He does not want the investigators to complete the closing before he has his instruments in position. He has been subtly managing Don Eusebio's cooperation to delay the timeline. He intends to do the same to the investigators.

**His Cover Story**: He is here to help. He has studied similar anomalies in the province. He can fill in the damaged sections of the Saens manuscript. He has instruments that will improve the accuracy of the ritual geometry. He knows more about this than anyone in the room.

All of this is true. It is not the point of his presence.

**What Breaks the Cover**: Several things, at different thresholds:
- A **Psychology** roll during any conversation with Quirce: he is performing warmth. Underneath it is a cold, focused intelligence that is tracking variables, not people.
- Asking about his annotated manuscript copy: he deflects smoothly but the deflection is too smooth — he had the answer prepared.
- A **Hard Occult** roll while watching him examine the study: two of his "corrections" to Don Eusebio's diagrams are subtle alterations that would change the direction of the geometry's closure — not block it, but redirect it slightly, creating a moment of maximum observable pressure before sealing. He is setting up an observation point inside the closing geometry.
- Confronting him about the manuscript copy: *"How did you know these sections weren't damaged in your copy?"* — he does not have a prepared answer for this. A beat too long before he speaks. **Psychology** succeeds automatically.

**Questions & Answers — Quirce**

*"How did you hear about the situation here?"*
> "Don Eusebio wrote to me. I'd published a paper on the colonial-era geometry sites of the province — he'd read it, apparently." *(True, but incomplete. He had been watching this site from the network's records before Don Eusebio's letter arrived.)*

*"What exactly is your expertise?"*
> "Field geometry. The study of how certain spatial configurations persist in land-use areas. Quite academic, I assure you." *(He says this with a smile that suggests he finds the word 'academic' slightly humorous in this context. This is an honest tell.)*

*"Can you help us close this?"*
> "Absolutely. That's exactly why I'm here." *(He believes this. He intends to help them close it — after his instruments are in position and his observations are complete. The moral weight of delay during an active incursion does not register with him as significant.)*

*"Why does your copy of the manuscript have sections ours doesn't?"*
> *(A half-second pause. Then:)* "I obtained mine from a different source — a private archive in Montevideo. The water damage on the original must have occurred after the copies were made." *(Plausible. Not true.)*

*"What's in the annotations?"*
> "Technical notes. Previous researchers' observations." *(He does not offer to share them. If pressed: he translates selected passages that are genuine but carefully chosen — never the passages that reveal the network's existence or his observation agenda.)*

*"Are you here to observe, or to help?"*
> *(A still moment. Then the warmth returns, calibrated.)* "I've come a long way to help, señor/señora. I hope my presence has not started on a suspicious note." *(He has not answered the question. Neither has he denied it.)*

## CONFRONTING DON EUSEBIO — THE CLOSING REQUIREMENT

This is the chapter's central negotiation: persuading (or coercing) Don Eusebio to agree to stand in the outer ring of the closing ritual.

The manuscript's requirement is clear. Don Eusebio has read it — he knew before investigators arrived that this would be asked of him. He has been building arguments against it.

**His objections, in order**:

1. *"There must be another way. Saens may have been wrong."* — The carved stone at the ruins refutes this. If investigators have been there, they can show him: the closing geometry in the stone shows an outer ring with human-scale positions. This was designed to require the opener's presence.

2. *"I don't know how to perform a ritual."* — He doesn't need to. He needs to stand in a position and not leave it. Kuyen handles the ritual. He handles his feet.

3. *"Something will happen to me."* — This fear is honest. The manuscript doesn't specify what. What Saens says is only that the geometry "knows" the opener. What that recognition does to a person standing in it — unknown.

4. *"I'll be arrested. Exposed. The land records, the workers—"* — This is the real fear beneath the supernatural one. Investigators with leverage (evidence, witnesses, the testimony of Rosa and Kuyen) can apply pressure here. The legal consequences of what Don Eusebio did are real. He knows them.

**Breaking point**: He agrees when confronted with Silvio, standing in the courtyard, holding the fence post, knuckles white. Don Eusebio looks at Silvio for a long time. Then:

> *"I told myself he chose to go out that night. That I didn't send him."* *(He did not send him. That doesn't help.)* *"When does this happen?"*

**Don Eusebio's condition**: He agrees on one condition — that Quirce comes too. He doesn't trust Quirce and he doesn't want to be alone with the investigators in a ritual circle. He wants a witness who is on nobody's side. This condition is double-edged: Quirce at the pit is exactly what Quirce wants. Investigators who realize this face a choice.

## SILVIO — THE DEPARTURE

Midway through this chapter — while investigators are dealing with Don Eusebio or Quirce — Silvio is no longer at the fence post. He is gone. His tools are laid neatly on the ground near the south pasture gate. He walked in.

The south pasture. The cattle field. The pit.

**Tracking him**: A **Tracking** roll finds his footprints going straight south, into the pasture, toward the pit. They do not loop or stop. They walk in a direct line to the pit's edge and descend.

**If investigators follow immediately**: Silvio is at the pit, standing in it. At the bottom, among the collapsed carcasses, looking at the geometry scratched into the earth. He is not in distress. He says: *"It wanted to show me something. I let it. I understand the closing now."* He comes back up without resistance. He will not explain what he saw.

**If investigators don't follow immediately**: He returns on his own, after dark. He is unchanged — or rather, he is what he was, but quieter. He says nothing about where he went. He goes back to work.

**SAN Check**: 0/1 (Silvio's purposeful descent into the pit, the casualness of it)

## QUIRCE'S MOVE — THE SETUP

This chapter, Quirce establishes himself as an ally and makes his preparations. He does not yet act against investigators — his moves are subtle and require specific skill checks to detect.

**What he does in the study** (while Don Eusebio meets with investigators):
A **Spot Hidden** check on the study afterward: Quirce has placed two small brass markers — the kind used for surveying — into gaps in the floor, aligned with the study's corners. They are designed to extend the ritual geometry's observable field toward the study position. He intends to observe from the study window with his theodolite during the closing.

> **C4 redundancy (clue retrieval)**: If players miss the Spot Hidden, **Tomás or one of the ranch hands** will mention, in passing the next morning, that they heard Quirce *moving furniture* in the study late last night — *"Como cuando se reorganiza un cuarto. Pero no movió nada visible."* — *"Like when someone rearranges a room. But he didn't move anything visible."* This is the players' second chance to find the markers. A Marked PC who walks into the study after this remark sees the *deflection* the markers cause (the string-deflection observable consequence in C6's D5 list) without needing the Spot Hidden roll at all.

**What he does with Don Eusebio's diagrams**:
He has "corrected" three of Don Eusebio's variant attempts. The corrections are accurate — they would work better. But a **Hard Occult** check reveals that one correction introduces a slight asymmetry in the outer ring that will, at the moment of maximum closure pressure, cause the geometry to "ring" — vibrate momentarily — before sealing. This is his observation window. It will not prevent the closing. It will, however, expose anyone in the outer ring to a concentrated moment of the entity's field. Which includes Don Eusebio.

**Whether to tell Don Eusebio**: Investigators who catch this face a choice. Exposing Quirce now may cause Don Eusebio to refuse to cooperate entirely (he invited Quirce; admitting he was manipulated is difficult for him). Waiting to deal with Quirce at the pit is riskier but may be more controllable.

## END OF CHAPTER 6 — THE MOVEMENT TO THE PIT

The chapter ends with the group assembled: investigators, Don Eusebio, Quirce, Silvio (who has walked ahead — he knows the way), Héctor and Marta (who arrive at dusk without having been summoned, as if they felt the gathering).

Kuyen is waiting at the pit. Nahuel is with her. His condition has worsened — he is functional but the circles under his eyes have deepened and he flinches at shadows in ways he didn't before.

> *The south pasture at dusk. The cattle field — the carcasses now collapsed and dried, the circle of them still present. The grass rings, the dead center. The pit.*
>
> *Something about the light is wrong here in the evenings now. The shadows fall slightly early, as if the sun is being bent around the geometry before it sets. Kuyen is standing at the pit's edge. She sees the group coming from two hundred meters away and her expression does not change.*
>
> *"You brought everyone,"* she says when you reach her. Her gaze moves across the group: investigators, Eusebio, Quirce (at whom she looks for a long moment without expression), Silvio, Héctor and Marta (at whom she does not look surprised).*
>
> *"Good. Tomorrow night."*

## GM NOTES

**Quirce is the most dangerous person in this arc** — not because he can fight, but because he is intelligent, patient, and morally certain that what he is doing is acceptable. He is not evil by his own lights. He is a researcher who has deprioritized the welfare of specific people against the value of knowledge that might help others in the future. This makes him harder to argue with than a villain.

**The confrontation with Don Eusebio is the arc's emotional payoff**: He has been a coward, a liar, and a man who watched people walk into something he opened and did nothing to stop them. The moment he looks at Silvio and says *"I told myself he chose to go"* is the moment he is no longer a villain — he is a culpable human being who has to live with what he did. That is, ultimately, worse.

**Don't rush the Quirce reveal**: Let investigators earn it. A player who catches the brass markers, connects them to the theodolite, and then looks at the corrected diagrams with fresh eyes — that player should feel the chill of it. Don't hand it to them; let them find it.

**Silvio going into the pit**: This should be alarming and then, when he comes back, more alarming in its calmness. The horror of "I went in and it showed me something and I let it" is not what he describes but what he doesn't describe.

**The final image**: Everyone gathered at the pit at dusk, the geometry already visible in the low light (blood arcs faintly present, the grass rings visible from above), Kuyen saying *"Tomorrow night"* — this is the end of Arc 2. The setup is complete. Everything that happens from here is consequence.

### Observable consequences (D5)

- **In Don Eusebio's study, near Quirce's brass markers**: A piece of string suspended from a finger above one of the markers will *not* hang plumb. It deflects toward the south-west window — the line of sight to the pit — by perhaps 8°. The deflection is constant. Move the marker, and the deflection moves with it. Marked PCs see the markers as small concentrated nodes of *the wrong direction*; unmarked PCs just notice the string.
- **During Quirce's mediumship moment**: Candle flames on the desk *bend horizontally* toward Quirce's mouth, then snap back upright when the second voice stops. There is no draft. The man across the desk from him will not have noticed because he was looking at Quirce's eyes.
- **At the pit at dusk**: Cattle on the estancia will not graze within sight of the pit. They have moved themselves to the far paddock without being driven there. Tomás notes this without comment when he brings horses around. (He has been trying to push them back to the south paddock for a week. They will not go.)
- **During the assembly**: A pampero cricket lands on Kuyen's pigment pouch and stays there, unmoving, for the entire duration of the gathering. When she walks away, the cricket flies — not in a random direction, but in a tight arc that traces the *outer ring* of the binding before disappearing into the grass. No one will believe a Marked PC who reports this.

## EXPANSION NOTES — BRIDGING TO THE OVERVIEW FRAMING

The detailed prep above runs *El Visitante*: Quirce's arrival, Don Eusebio's commitment, Silvio in the pit, the move toward the closing. The `overview.md` framing of Arc 2 wants the **Great Wizard of Salamanca revealed**, **El Patrón secretly manipulating the wizard**, and a **stronger Necronomicon reference**. Use the scenes below to add the new framing's beats over and around the existing ones.

### Reskinning summary

- **Aldao Quirce** in the existing prep is "scholar, observer, betrayer" — he can be played in this chapter with an *additional* role layered onto him: *he is the Great Wizard's correspondent*. The Wizard never appears physically in this chapter. He is *behind* Quirce, the way El Patrón is behind everything.
- **The Great Wizard of Salamanca** is, mechanically, not at the estancia. He is in his chamber underground (the sealed door at the third tunnel from C5 expansion). A second-tier antagonist. El Patrón is using him as a tool the same way he is using Quirce — except the Wizard *believes* he is in charge. Quirce knows he is not.
- **El Patrón** appears in this chapter as a *voice* — twice, briefly, through Quirce's body without Quirce's consent. The avatar reveal (overview, Arc 4) starts here as a tremor, not a confrontation.

### NEW SCENE — QUIRCE'S NOTEBOOK, A WORD IN ANOTHER HAND

Insert this when investigators pursue Quirce's annotated manuscript. The **Hard Cthulhu Mythos** roll on the cipher annotations reveals: most of the annotations are in Quirce's hand (his cipher, his Pitman-adjacent shorthand). **Three are not.** They are in a different hand — older, neater, written with a different pen. They are positioned at exactly the points in the manuscript where Saens wrote about the *Necronomicón* (the marginal note from the C4 expansion, plus two others the players can now find).

The three margin annotations in the second hand read, paraphrased and translated from the older Castilian:

1. *"This is from the third chapter of Alhazred. A copy survives. I have seen it."*
2. *"The closing as Saens has it is incomplete. The full closing requires the page across from this one in Alhazred. I do not have that page. I have asked. I will receive it or I will go where it is."*
3. *"If Quirce can be persuaded to leave the geometry alone for one closing, I can make a successful observation. The book is then closer than it has ever been."*

The hand is the **Great Wizard of Salamanca's**. Quirce is in correspondence with him. Quirce has been carrying the Wizard's notes inside his own annotations — possibly without realizing the Wizard's marginalia are the *purpose* of the document, and Quirce's cipher is the cover.

**Effect on the players' understanding**: Quirce is not the apex of the network. There is someone above him — someone who *names* Alhazred, who knows the Necronomicón intimately, who is willing to come to the closing in person if necessary. This is the **Great Wizard reveal** at the level of the document.

### NEW SCENE — EL PATRÓN SPEAKS THROUGH QUIRCE

Insert this twice during the chapter. Brief. Wordless on Quirce's part the first time; spoken the second time.

**First instance**: Mid-conversation with the players, Quirce stops mid-sentence. His face does not change. His eyes go *elsewhere* — not glassy, *focused on something not in the room*. For ten seconds. Then he resumes his sentence, in the middle of the word he was on. He does not appear to notice. **Spot Hidden / Psychology** roll: the players see what just happened. Quirce did not.

> **C7 redundancy (clue retrieval)**: **Don Eusebio is in the room** during the first instance. He sees it. If no player makes the Spot Hidden / Psychology roll, after Quirce leaves the sala Don Eusebio will look at the players and say, quietly, *"¿Vieron?"* — *"Did you see that?"* He does not name what. He does not need to. Players who answer "see what?" get *"La pausa. Diez segundos. No es la primera vez."* — *"The pause. Ten seconds. It is not the first time."* This is Don Eusebio confirming the mediumship without anyone needing a successful roll. Don Eusebio has been watching Quirce for two months and noticed the pauses before the players arrived; he simply could not interpret them. **He has the data; the players have the framework.** Together they have the answer.

**Second instance**: The same effect, longer, while Quirce is alone with one player in the study (looking at Don Eusebio's diagrams). His mouth opens. A voice that is not his comes out. It says, in conversational Buenos Aires Spanish:

> *"Ustedes están haciendo lo que yo necesitaba que hicieran. Gracias. — Eligio Dadálah"*  — *"You are doing what I needed you to do. Thank you. — Eligio Dadálah."*

Quirce's mouth closes. His eyes refocus. He is unaware. He continues looking at the diagrams.

**SAN Check**: 0/1D6 — for the avatar's first audible introduction, the casualness of the thanks, the casualness of the signature. *Eligio Dadálah* is the name in El Patrón's land deeds (Arc 4); the players are now hearing it for the first time in C6.

The players cannot yet act on it. Quirce will deny. But they have heard the name, and at least one of them now knows that the most polite person in the room has, for ten seconds, been someone else.

> **Third instance — the Wizard's whisper (C6 redundancy)**: Add a brief third moment, immediately after the second. Quirce, still unaware that El Patrón just spoke through him, leans forward to look at a diagram and *exhales* — a long breath, not quite a word. The player closest to him, with a **Listen** roll, hears within the breath a single word, not in any voice they have heard before, hoarser, older, distant: *"…Salamanca…"* That is all. Quirce shakes his head as if a fly had passed near his ear. He continues looking at the diagram.
>
> This second whisper is **the Great Wizard of Salamanca**, not El Patrón. **Two masters are using Quirce, and they do not coordinate.** The Wizard rides Quirce's marginalia (see *Kuyen's Confrontation* below); El Patrón rides his voice. The Wizard's whisper here is the players' first audible signal that there is a second hand on Quirce's strings — important when Mercedes' Arc 3 letter (C8) and Saúl Carrasco's Coypac intel start naming "twelve priests" and a tall man in ochre. The *ochre* is the Wizard. **SAN 0/1** for the player who hears it.

### NEW SCENE — KUYEN'S CONFRONTATION OF THE WIZARD'S WORK

Add this in the assembly at the pit at chapter's end. As Kuyen lays the leather panels for tomorrow's closing, she pauses on one panel — the asymmetric one Quirce "corrected." She stares at it for a long moment and then says, without looking up:

> *"Esta línea no la dibujé yo. La dibujó alguien que sabe el dibujo desde adentro, no desde afuera. No fue Quirce. Quirce no sabe el dibujo desde adentro."* — *"This line I did not draw. It was drawn by someone who knows the drawing from inside, not from outside. It was not Quirce. Quirce does not know the drawing from inside."*

Kuyen does not name the Great Wizard. She does not need to. **The asymmetry was the Wizard's correction, channeled through Quirce.** Quirce believes he made it himself. He didn't. He was a hand. The Wizard has been writing through him for months.

This shifts the players' understanding of Quirce: he is not a fully autonomous agent. He is closer to Lacasa (Arc 4) than the players currently know — a man being used who has not yet realized.

### NEW SCENE — THE NECRONOMICON, NAMED IN OPEN AIR

Add this after the assembly is complete and the players are setting up camp at the pit for the night. Mercedes' second letter arrives — by runner, fast, costly. Mercedes paid for an express rider. The letter is short.

> *"Si todo lo que ustedes describen es cierto, mañana se cierra esta abertura. Hay otras. La que más me preocupa es la única que no está en tierra: el libro. El Necronomicón está en una bóveda en Buenos Aires desde 1610. Si la cerradura abajo se está debilitando, está porque alguien ha empezado a tocar la bóveda desde acá. No vengan al norte. Cierren primero. Después escríbanme. — M.S."*  — *"If everything you describe is true, tomorrow this opening closes. There are others. The one that most concerns me is the only one not in the ground: the book. The Necronomicón has been in a vault in Buenos Aires since 1610. If the lock below is weakening, it is because someone has started to touch the vault from here. Do not come north. Close first. Then write to me. — M.S."*

This is the campaign's first clear statement, **outside the manuscripts**, that the Necronomicón is in Buenos Aires and that the events in the pampas are *connected* to a vault in the city. The players now have:
- A name on the avatar (*Eligio Dadálah*).
- A second voice on the network (*the Great Wizard*, secretly above Quirce).
- A target (*the vault in Buenos Aires*).
- A teacher (*Mercedes*) who will receive them when they survive.

The closing is no longer the campaign's final act. It is the end of the act they entered without knowing the campaign had four.
